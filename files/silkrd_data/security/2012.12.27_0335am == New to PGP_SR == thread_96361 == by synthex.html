I imported your key to my pgp client, and wrote the message below, you and I are the only ones that will be able to decrypt it.

I use a simple PGP client based on a forum members guide: http://32yehzkk7jflf6r2.onion/gpg4usb/

The guide is really easy and informative, Thanks Astor!.  Personally I like this client because it is simple, it doesn't e-mail messages it just encrypts and decrypts... you have to copy/paste/type messages in and out.  No install and easy, great for a USB drive!.  You don't need e-mail to communicate on SR, you can use the site messaging (encrypt the messages).  The guide covers how to get your private key with the app mentioned, not sure about yours.

The key to understanding PGP imho... only the people you select (aka use their public keys like you posted) can decrypt and read the message.  Knowing someones public key is obvious critical to writing a message.  You DO NOT have to have the public key of the sender.  As long as they encrypted the message with your public key you will be able to read it.

NEVER give out your private key

Not sure what the exact protocal is around here for creating a public key with a real/used email address even if its tormail... You could use a completely fake e-mail address in your public key if you wanted.  Because you put your public key on this site, I now might know your tor mail address.  You loose a bit of control of that address, right now I personally use a fake email in my PGP key... if I want someone to email me via my tormail account, I will send my address in an encrypted message:)

It's not a good idea to mention your exchange, or the fact that you're sending direct to an SR address. If you're buying coins under your real identity and you don't send them through a mixing service, that's a security threat. All bitcoin transactions are recorded in the block chain. If someone can identify that bitcoin address as belonging to an SR account, they can link it back to you.

If you haven't transferred coins to your SR account yet, you should send them through a mixing service. If you have already transferred coins to your SR account, I suggest you delete this thread.

You are an excellent student, synthex.  Good job!

You have accomplished two things with the last lesson:
1) Vendors will not curse you if they need to encrypt a message to you
2) If your vendor saves your key to their keyring they will also have your email address in case SR goes down

A few more tips for you, free of charge:  Research your vendors carefully.  Copy their public keys and their Tor mail addresses in case SR goes down.  Don't buy from a vendor who does not use PGP. Do not FE.  Use your real address when dealing with SR vendors.  The Road goes down from time to but so far, it always comes back up. Avoid panic, stay stocked up and don't abandon your IRL contacts.

Good luck, enjoy the Road!
