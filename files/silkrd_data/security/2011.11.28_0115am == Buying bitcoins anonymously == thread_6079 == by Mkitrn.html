Post by: monglobonglo on December 02, 2011, 09:23 am
1) bitcoin2cash.

they take letters full of cash.  The competing service, bitcoin4cash, appears to be dead or inactive at the moment.  Bitcoin2cash still maintains active communication,, though they have little or no reviews.

the guy who runs it is very focal on forums.  tough to tell.

services like this are sketchy at best, and even if he IS legit, its still very unsettling.

2) Bitcoin fog.  They are a "laundering/mixing" service.   I don't understand the exact specifics, but my understanding is that they do the following:

you deposit BTCs, which are then randomly transferred from one address to another, in random increments, multiple times, before being available to withdraw
this takes a pretty god damn long time, but part of that is intentional.   all of the transaction AMOUNTS are random.... so if you deposit 27 bitcoins, then exactly 27 bitcoins are not just jumped from one to another, which would be easy to follow.

that would be broken up into random increments, and possibly broken up further after each iterative step.
ultimately they are available for withdraw.
you then withdraw them into (ideally) multiple different bitcoin addresses, again in random increments, separated over a minimum of 6 hours.
thus the downside becomes:

the fog process itself takes a min of about 12 hours, and unless you have a wallet service that can take deposits from multiple different addresses, you will have to involve YET EVEN MORE bitcoin wallet/exchange services, all of whom have different operating principles (Virwox, for example must "manually verify" a transaction, which they say takes up to 48 hours).



3) the final one is the moneypak guy on SR.

that gives him the gold standard in my book.
