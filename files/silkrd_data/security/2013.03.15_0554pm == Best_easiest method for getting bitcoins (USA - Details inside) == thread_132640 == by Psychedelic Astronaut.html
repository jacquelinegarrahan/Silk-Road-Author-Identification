These are my personal prefs, with a little of others' experience salted in.  If you live near a Bank of America, you can use Bitfloor (clearnet bitfloor.com).  All you need is an email.  You might want to carry a fake ID with you when you make the deposit, but I've made over 30 deposits, and have never once been asked for ID, never heard of anyone being asked (just when you set up the deposit you have to tick a box that you'll agree to present one if asked).  You can also use Cryptocurrent at a BofA (clearnet cryptocurrent.com).  If not near BofA, you're probably near a Wal-Mart (or CVS, or half a dozen other retailers), and can use Bitinstant (clearnet bitinstant.com).  The fees are a little high (4% plus a $4 MoneyGram fee), but service is good, despite a recent outage that pissed off the community at large.  Bitstamp (clearnet bitstamp.net) is the European version of Bitfloor, and if you're into more than just buying drugs can be a great resource.  But, they do take Western Union/MoneyGram from the States.  I've never used them for that, so I can't speak to the fee, whether it's like a $4 fee or a $15 fee.  Other will probably chime in.

Others will read this and offer their own prefs, too, or just read through the forums.  We talk about this all the time.  In fact, jshizzle started a a pretty big thread for reviews of services a little while back.  Find it (I don't remember the name, but it's in all caps!!), and lurk awhile.  And, for the love of Thor, stay the hell away from places requiring you to give them a full set of fingerprints and a retina scan before they'll do business with you (Dwolla, Mt. Gox).  And, Cryptocurrent is great, but I wouldn't overnight cash, unless you really have no other options, just because Express Mail is so much more expensive than Bitfloor, for example.

Finally, I wouldn't make a habit out of buying directly into your SR account.  I know a lot of SR veterans do it, and maybe this is just me being overcautious.  But, if you don't want to pay for a service like Bitcoin Fog (http://fogcore5n3ov3tui.onion/), and it's a small fee, at least buy your coins into an instawallet or something, and then maybe bounce a third into another instawallet then to SR, then a third to yet another instawallet>SR, etc., over a couple of hours.  A poor man's laundry service, if you will.  Because we honestly don't know what the DEA knows about the Road, and while we have a certain amount of faith that SR encrypts the addresses it makes available to its customers, or generates them in a logically impossible way to trace back to it, we do know that once unencrypted, BTC addresses can be tracked.  So, routing your coins through a few addresses gives you deniability if someone ever tries to accuse you of buying off the Road. 

Just sayin'...
