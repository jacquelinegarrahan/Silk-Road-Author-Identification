Assuming you're in the US. BitInstant couldn't be easier. (there's a walk through right on the site)
Select Cash deposit, (Pay from?)from the left column, from the drop down menu pick the place you prefer to do the deposit. (I've found Walmart to be the most anonymous).
On the right side(Pay to?) choose either bitcoin to email, or bitcoin address. (I use bitcoin address) - Go to your 'account' page on SR and copy your BTC address, then go back to the Bitinstant site and paste it in there. -Then it will want your 1st name, Last name and DOB. - If you're paranoid, make it all up, they never ask for ID (CVS does btw), - I usually use my real name, and only alter my DOB, as I do for any website. Just remember what name, spelling, DOB you use, so if you're asked, you'll have it. Then it will take you to a ZipZap page, and ask you to put in your town. This is just to find the nearest location.  You can put a neighboring town. Doesn't really matter.
Then it will give you an "invoice". The info you'll need on there is a 4 digit pay code, and an account number. Also the name of the company ZipZap, and their location San Fran, CA.

That's it! Now go to Wal-Marts courtesy desk, and look for the Moneygram slips. Grab a Blue one, and go sit somewhere (Dunkin Donuts) and fill it out.
Here they want your name, address, and phone number. Again, if you're going to use a fictitious one, be sure to keep the receipt, because the first time, they put you in their 'system'- They'll sometimes say, "You're not in our system". - You can say whatever. "This is my first time using them" but the next time you go to do it, they'll simple put the phone number in, and not ask any questions, as you're already in their system.

The amount that says "PAY THIS AMOUNT" on the ZipZap form, is the amount you put down. - There is no Zip Zap fee, if they ask. They shouldn't though.

Hand the clerk the filled out Moneygram slip, and just wait for them to ask you for the money. They'll then pass you a receipt and ask you to sign it. Sign it.
They give you your copy stapled to the moneygram slip, and say "Thank You"

That's it!

-Always a good idea to grab a few extra moneygram slips, this way you can do it all at home, and save printer paper and ink!

Also the clerk may ask "are you sending money or paying a bill" - You're paying a bill.

That about covers it!

Been doing this for over a year now, and have only had a minor glitch here or there where the btc took longer to show up than expected. Never had any major problems.

Weekends it can take longer. - On weekdays it's in my SR account by the time I get home from Walmart.

Good Luck!
