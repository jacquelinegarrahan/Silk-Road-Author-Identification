Post by: awhiteknight on July 30, 2013, 07:16 am
You will need:

One stack of blank CD or DVDs
A USB flash drive. The faster the better (get a USB 3.0 one if your machine supports it)
A machine with an Internet connection and CD/DVD writer

Steps:

Download and install CDBurnerXP from cdburnerxp.se

Download the latest Tails ISO image from tails.baum.org. For additional security follow the instructions to verify the image if your government have slipped you a bad copy.

Use CDBurnerXP to write the image to a blank CD or DVD

Plug in the USB disk, put the DVD in the drive and restart your machine. 

At the black screen where it says "Press F1 to enter setup" or something, you press that button to get into the BIOS settings.

Look through the settings for "boot order" and set it to the following:
    First = CD/DVD drive.
    Second = Removable drive or USB drive.
    Third = Your hard drive.

Choose the "Save changes and exit" option. Your computer should now boot into Tails!

Once tails has loaded and you get to a desktop, click applications, Tails then Tails USB Installer and follow the on-screen instructions.

Choose the shutdown option from the power button in the top right corner, eject the disk from the DVD drive but leave the USB disk plugged in.

Turn your computer back on, it should boot into Tails again but this time from the USB disk.

When you get to the desktop, click applications, tails, configure persistent storage.

Create your storage with a really fucking long but memorable secret pass phrase that does not exist anywhere else, never has and never will. "Boopity boop bing a bleepity bloop a-ding ding ding, Bob!" is a good pass phrase, "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times" is not, while "FavePass123" is just negligent. Get this bit right.

Once storage has been created (might take a while) reboot again. This time enable persistence at the login prompt and type your pass phrase.

Go to applications, click Tails, Configure persistent storage. Tick everything and reboot again.

You now have a secure Tor environment where you can do all your illegal shit. If your USB disk is small enough to swallow or put in your arse that's a bonus. Set up a Tormail account, save your vendor public keys, set up encryption keys so you can chat, bookmark your black market sites, set up accounts across the web etc.

When you get a message balloon in the top right corner that says "this version of tails has security issues", download the latest ISO again, burn it to disk, boot into it then use the installer to upgrade your USB disk.
