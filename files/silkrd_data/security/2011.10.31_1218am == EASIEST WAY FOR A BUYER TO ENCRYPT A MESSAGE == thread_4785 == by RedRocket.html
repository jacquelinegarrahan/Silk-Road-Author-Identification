Title: EASIEST WAY FOR A BUYER TO ENCRYPT A MESSAGE
Post by: RedRocket on October 31, 2011, 12:18 am
EDIT: THERE IS AN EVEN EASIER METHOD THREE POSTS DOWN :D

if youre running tails its extremely easy to encypt a message,I only just figured this out, wish it was this easy when i first started...i also must say,imo you dont really need to bother about making a key to give to your seller...it just confuses things...the only thing that needs encrypting is your adress....sellers are already advertising drugs...they wont get into more bother with more plain text.lol...its not necesary in my opnion, and most sellers on here know that....im yet to find a seller that wont correspond in unencrypted text....the only thing that needs encrypting is your adress...if you are a buyer and you follow this way, there is no need to download programs,no need to  make keys,store keys, remember passphrases etc etc...on another positive note, if you are not running tails..then the simplicity of this method will hopefully encourage you to use it....


******************************************************************************************************************************************************
FIRSTLY:

-Highlight & copy a sellers public key (everything from the first hyphen to the last hyphen)
-paste it into the text editor(applications>accessories>gedit text editor)
-Make sure the public key is positioned to the extreme upper left hand corner with no open line above it,otherwise it wont save as a keyfile.
-save it to your desktop

THEN GO TO:
-system>preferences>password and encryption keys

THEN CLICK:

-File>Import>select your sellers key on the desktop then click open>next click the "other keys" tab to see your imported key>you can then exit out

FINALLY GO TO THE SILK ROAD MARKET PLACE:

-Go to where you insert a message
-insert unencrypted plain text(your address),then highlight the text
-select TOOLS at the top of your web browser
-Scroll down to FireGPG
-Then scroll to Encrypt
-Select your Dealers key
-and voila! your address is now encrypted and can be sent to your dealer.
