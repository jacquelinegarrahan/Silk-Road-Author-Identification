tottenham01 - i appreciate the dread of having to learn it, shit when i tried i needed a couple of aspirin before it was over - then someone gave me a basic "paint by the numbers" tutorial and then i found astor's tutorial - with either of those, it's a 15 minute affair

one thing to keep in mind, when you set up GPG4USB, you will end up generating a "key pair" consisting of a public key and a private key. When you comm with someone, they will need your public key to use to encrypt their msg to you, at their end - but only the private key you have in your computer will decrypt that msg. Similiarly, when you want to respond to them, you'll need their public key to encrypt the msg, that only they can decrypt at their end using their private key.

TAILS comes with all that crap pre-installed and pre-configured. Literally, all you do is:
Copy/Paste someone's PGP Public Key to a text file.
Open "Passwords and Encryption" and import said text file.

Now that you have their public key, to send someone an encrypted message:
Open "gedit" (the text editor)
Compose your message
Select "Encrypt" from the Edit Menu and choose their key.

You're done! No, seriously, that's it!
Use TAILS, it's easy and secure. Be smart. Play safe.
