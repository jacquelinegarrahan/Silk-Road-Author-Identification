Title: Re: Earn BTC-->Help Me Step Up my Game (Putting Tor + PGP on a USB)
Post by: wiggum on June 12, 2013, 09:07 pm
Tails is dead simple as long as you can follow instructions.  There are threads on here about setting up Tails and then setting up Electrum bitcoin wallet within Tails - just follow the instructions carefully and it will work. 

Tails has all the Tor and PGP support you need built into it.  So, with Tails+Electrum, you can have a setup where everything you'll ever need for SR is on a flash drive, and there will be no trace left anywhere else besides the flash drive.  Be sure to backup your Electrum wallet if you keep any BTC in there so that you can "destroy" the flash drive if you ever need to without losing money.

Tails will work on a relatively old POS computer that you could buy for next to nothing.  You don't even need a hard drive in it.

Post by: PerPETualMOtion on June 23, 2013, 10:31 am
I enjoy the feel of Liberte, but to each their own. In other words, I started with Liberte, and I like it. However, Liberte allows permanent storage of files, whereas Tails is very, very clean. Each boot on Tails is new, fresh from the last use--no history, no files, etc.

Allow me to change my position. For the ease of use and lack of footprints, Tails is probably the better option. As far as Tails goes, I have a blockchain account, in addition to bitcoin-qt (on another OS). Blockchain seems to be pretty solid. Alternatively, I have been sending my cash-->BTC straight to my SR acct, so there hasn't been much need for a wallet lately.

I actually had a few hiccups loading tails via Linux, so I used UUI (universal usb installer) on a Windows PC. After a few direct downloads and mistrials, I downloaded the torrent--much faster, in my case--than the direct download offered by tails.boum.org. 0.18 is the current version, and they are currently testing 0.19.

Hope that helps,
