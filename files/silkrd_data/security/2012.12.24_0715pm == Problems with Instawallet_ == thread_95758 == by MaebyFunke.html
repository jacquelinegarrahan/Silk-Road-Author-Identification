Not a bitcoin expert but from what I understand bitcoin transactions are sort of peer to peer (kinda like DNS) where they propagate through all the other servers.  Each server runs a confirmation of sorts and ups the number, this is to prevent spending of the same coin (say if you transfer it on server A, then quickly transfer it on server B before it got the record of transfer A).  Each receiving agent can actually deposit your bitcoin based on the confirmation number (its their risk).  Most do it by bitcoin amount, smaller chunks of coin are considered good with low confirmations, like 1 or 2.  Larger will take more.  From what I have read 6 is generally the accepted amount for most transactions.

This is why on instawallet, they will show deposit but say it needs more confirmations before you can move it again, instawallet is waiting for more confirmations to ensure it is good, like waiting for a check to clear.
