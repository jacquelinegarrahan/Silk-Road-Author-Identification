Title: Re: I gave virwex.com money,still havent got bitcoins!
Post by: biteme on January 05, 2013, 10:43 am
If you're in the USA. I recommend Bitcopia.com. Extremely simple and quick to use if you can go to a Wells Fargo Bank branch. You just create the order on Bitcopia's site and then you have one hour to make the cash deposit at the bank (the time limit is there because the exchange rate for Bitcoins can change significantly during an hour). It's completely anonymous and you can receive your coins at any Bitcoin address (including free anonymous temporary wallets like Instawallet.org). Then, if you desire, you can tumble your coins (at somewhere like Bitcoin Fog, for example) for extra security before you transfer them to SR. I like to even go through more temporary wallets between Bitcon Fog and SR. The more times you move the coins and the more different amounts and different Bitcoin addresses you use for the transfers, the harder it will ever be for anyone to trace the movement paths of the coins. It might sound complicated if you're new to it but once you've done it a couple of times, it will seem like child's play.

My advice for other newbies is to be overly cautious at first until you get the hang of it. Then you can decide if you're safe to cut out any of the steps because you think they're overkill. There's nothing wrong with being too careful if it makes you feel safe and eases your fears. The worst thing about it might be the few small service charges you could pay when buying or tumbling your coins. Plus you do have to wait for confirmations of the transfers so it can take some extra time for the extra steps.

I've pretty much given up using Mt. Gox and Bitinstant because these other methods are easier and more anonymous since they can be used with TOR and I'm never asked for a name or birthday. I have nothing against any Bitcoin vendors on SR or in these forums, but I personally prefer to buy my Bitcoins from a vendor who has no direct connection to SR.

I hope this advice helps. This community has been extremely helpful to me. I'm just trying to share what I've learned.
