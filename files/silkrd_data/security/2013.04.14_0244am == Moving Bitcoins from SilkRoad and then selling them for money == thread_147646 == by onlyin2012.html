Title: Re: Moving Bitcoins from SilkRoad and then selling them for money
Post by: radi8power on April 14, 2013, 05:20 pm
Quote from: onlyin2012 on April 14, 2013, 04:18 pm
What about just moving my SR bitcoins to my bitcoin wallet and then moving them to Mt. Gox sell them for money and then transfer the money to my bank that I have setup through Mt. Gox?  This way, I've changed my bitcoin address and any would be LE officials looking to track down bitcoins paths would be even more work on their hands?
I think this is a Reasonably Safe thing to do. In general, the more address hops between SR and your cash out, the better. These could be actual wallets on your local machine that you delete afterwards, or anonymous online ewallets. There are some popular BTC "mixing" services that charge a small fee to mix or tumble your coins (BitFog is one, there are others). Personally, I avoid these as they are all possible LE honeypots.

At the end of the day, you're going to have a cash deposit from a BTC exchange on your personal bank record. This isn't illegal or incriminating in itself (yet), but it will certainly look suspicious if you get in trouble for drugs/hacking/terrorism/whatever in the future. For this reason, I agree with the poster above (+1 donkeydong) that selling your coins locally for cash is the safest way to do it, even if not very fast or convenient.
