Title: Re: SSH Tunnel & VPN Service Suggestions
Post by: z3n on May 12, 2013, 10:12 pm
I'd rather go with an SSH tunel, get a cheap vps (check lowendbox.com for example) in the location you want with either a VCC or with a provider that accepts bitcoin, and then torify your connection to that box (torify ssh -ND port user@host) and use that as a socks proxy in your browser.

That way you'll have a clean, dedicated IP, and could stay totally anonymous, as you'd be connecting to the VPS through TOR...

It depends what you want to achieve though.. VPN -> TOR -> INTERNETS would make it so that your ISP can't know that you are using tor. TOR -> SSH SOCKS PROXY -> INTERNETS would make it so that the clearnet website you visit can't know that you are using tor...

Good luck :)

Quote
Does paying a VPN compromise your anonymity? It's a legal service, and the fact that your credit card information is used for purchase doesn't compromise the anonymity of your browsing, right? Am I missing something?

Yes and no. If you pay someone using a traditional form of payment, then you're right: it'll be known that you're using a VPN, when you signed up for it, how long your term is, and the exact type of service you signed up for. The solution? Pay with Bitcoin ONLY. Then there's no way anyone can ever find out who you are. Just make sure you use a Tormail address or something similar if you choose to leave any address at all.

That's why you always have to go on the VPN provider's rep. Have they ever cooperated with LE in any part of the world? Have they ever handed over information to anyone at all? Do they respect whistleblowers and tailor their service for them?

Trust is something that's earned, as anyone doing business on SR knows very well. There are a few VPN providers which have really earned the trust of their customers around the world. Search for them and you'll see. Either way, use Tor over OpenVPN and you'll achieve the highest level of anonymity that's currently known on the internet.
