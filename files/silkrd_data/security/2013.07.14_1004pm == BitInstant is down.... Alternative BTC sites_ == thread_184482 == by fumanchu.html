Post by: entreterra on July 15, 2013, 11:17 pm
Quote from: fumanchu on July 15, 2013, 07:36 pm
I've tried LocalBitcoins- there's not a seller within 200 miles of my location... and plus it seems like this place is just more scammy than the rest.

There does not need to be a seller near you to use this site. Many sellers offer cash deposits or numerous other methods. Seriously, this site is incredibly easy to use and so much less of a hassle than bitinstant. It's tor-browser friendly and many methods for making payments can be totally anonymous. Plus it totally exemplies the true power of the BTC, which is decentralization.

It may seem scammy because there is no middleman and you are just depositing money to a random person, but how is that any different than SR itself? Local-BTC have an escrow system, feedback system and admins who investigate disputes.

If you do a little searching and check the site several times over several hours, you will find numerous sellers popping up all the time with 100% feedback and several transactions under their belt. Sometimes the prices are a little steeper than market price, but the nice thing is the price you see is the price you pay, no extra fees like bitinstant.

Once you make a trade request, the coins are held in escrow and the seller can not cancel. You have a limited amount of time to make the cash deposit or whatever other method you choose to use, once you do and mark the payment as complete, the seller checks to see that you really did deposit funds, then they release them. If they don't release them from escrow, admins for the site will review the transaction and use things like your deposit receipt to prove you really made the deposit.

The misconceptions about this site are that you MUST meet someone in person, which is not true, or that it's easy to be scammed, which is also untrue, especially if you choose a seller with excellent feedback. Many of these people are just BTC traders trying to make a buck on the BTCs they have stored up.
