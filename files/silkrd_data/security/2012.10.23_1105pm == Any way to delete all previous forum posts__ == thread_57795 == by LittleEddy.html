Title: Re: Any way to delete all previous forum posts??
Post by: LittleEddy on October 24, 2012, 07:49 pm
After laboriously removing 60 or so posts, I made some observations. The first thing I noticed that each post taken by itself is not a big deal, but all the posts taken together are rather incriminating to say the least. A lot of posts can make a story about you. I didn't realize this until I had seen them again.

On the technical side, when you go to profile/summary/show posts, your posts are listed, but you must go to each thread to individually remove them. Only a couple that were listed gives you the option to remove it on the summary page (why??) so it takes awhile to get them deleted.

One big problem that you have no control over (that I'm aware of) is that there are many times where your post is quoted in somebody's elses post (and there it sits.)

Another problem, is with posts that you originate. You can't remove them--you can only modify them--i.e., remove all text, and change the subject line.

I wondering what happens when you delete the account?? Does it change anything other than just doing the obvious? Any advantage in deleting it??

One last thing, if you are as paranoid as I am about these kind of things and if you share the same sn's, you might consider doing this as well. If not, just don't post anything that is self incriminating. I know this is not the clearnet, but....
