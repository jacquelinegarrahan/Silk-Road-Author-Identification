Title: Long distance wifi + hotspot
Post by: shitashi9 on August 14, 2012, 11:54 am
Here is an idea I had for enhancing your anonymity beyond Tor.

1. Rent an office in a tall building.
2. Set up a parabolic wifi antenna + wifi amplifier of the type found here: http://www.radiolabs.com/products/wireless/wireless.html
3. Purchase pre-paid credit with a wifi hotspot provider, for cash. Needless to say, always purchase the voucher in a shop/newsstand far away from your office, and never in the same shop.
4. Point the wifi antenna at a hotspot approx. 1-2 km away.
5. Start tor hidden service.
6. Switch hotspot every 2 weeks or so.

Would this be feasible? How hard would it be to find the hidden service provider once the attacker has breached the Tor layer and knows the hotspot IP address?

Title: Re: Long distance wifi + hotspot
Post by: shitashi9 on August 14, 2012, 01:35 pm
The idea is to have a layer of physical anonymity on top of virtual anonymity. VPS are not a bad idea, but it's just more of the same type of anonymity. If someone has the resources to find you behind Tor, they certainly have the resources to find you behind a chain of VPS.

To break your physical anonymity on the other hand requires different tools and people with different skills.

Yes, renting an office is expensive, but if you are going to run a tor hidden service, it's a bad idea to do it from your home, so you're going to need to rent an office anyhow.  Running the web server itself on a VPS is also a bad idea, because the people working for the VPS provider can then see all your data.
