If there are any cases like this, it's unlikely that we'll hear of them, as this type of demand would likely take place pursuant to a National Security Letter (NSL).  I seem to recall reading about one fellow who ran an ISP, and was presented with an NSL. It took 7 years for it to be overturned in court, and during all that time he could tell NO ONE about it, without risking going to jail.  The man later commented that this prohibition on talking about it was not time-limited -- rather, it was for LIFE. If the NSL were not overturned by the court, he would have been required to remain silent about it for the rest of his life.

Who is going to risk going to jail to blow the whistle?
