Title: Re: Sending Bitcoin from one SR account to another SR account. Risky?
Post by: Duffman on April 21, 2013, 11:41 pm
If you're sending coins to a username on SR instead of a bitcoin address, then your transaction will never hit the blockchain.
It will be in SR's database though until it gets removed.
Also unless SR is ever compromised, nobody should be able to link an address to SR.
They're just like any other address, however that doesn't mean that you shouldn't try to obfuscate the link between your exchange and SR.
Lastly there's no distinction between small or large amounts as long as you send it to the correct user. ;)
