Title: Re: what exactly does encrypting your hard drive prevent?
Post by: raelag on August 19, 2012, 06:15 am
There is few solutions, but since I'd guess windows is in use - go with http://www.truecrypt.org/ (clearweb alert)

For starters, create some small partition(file container->standart, if you aren't sure), about 10-50 mb, hashes/encryptions would do by default.

Mount it and play a bit to get a hang (move  some files there, delete them .. you know)

Wipe this toy partition, decide on size of new one, AES-Twofish-Serpent or Serpent-Twofish-AES for enc, RIPEMD-160 for hash.

Oh, and to be completely serious about having safe storage - 16 or more characters for password, a's, A's, 1's and #'s. (Small and big letters, numbers and characters)
