Title: Re: First timer, would like verfication of the process
Post by: Aidoneus on October 10, 2012, 06:21 pm
Have to agree with Errl. As a vendor, it's actually kind of annoying to get encrypted messages for simple questions. I mean, we'd never turn someone away because of it - we understand the value of security.. but it just isn't necessary IMO unless, as Errl mentioned, your message contains personal info of some kind.

As far as obtaining bitcoins - Bitinstant bank deposit straight to SR is plenty safe enough. Some people are more paranoid and believe you should wash your coins first, but as soon as you send them to SR the trail back to you is gone anyway. To each their own.

Also, as has been suggested in other threads, just use fake information when setting up your bitinstant transaction. You'll never need to verify it so... yea. Just use a Mailinator.com email address for the process.

All said and done you could have bitcoins inside of an hour... or it could take up to 6 hours. The system really is pretty flawless so sometimes you just have to be patient. Your coins will come! :D

Getting caught buying bitcoins? It's 100% legal.. and there are a bunch of legit vendors out there who take them for products/services... so not really a big deal. Going the 'extra mile' and using false information is just a security buffer.

As far as fees - Bitinstant's fee is typically 3.99% + $1 I believe. Then TrustCash (the company you actually make the deposit too) has a fee of like $1.90 or something. You can go setup the transaction without actually confirming anything so you can see exactly how much money you'll get in bitcoin and what it will cost you.
