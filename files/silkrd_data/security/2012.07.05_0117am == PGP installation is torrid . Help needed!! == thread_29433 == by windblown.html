Title: Re: PGP installation is torrid . Help needed!!
Post by: Hungry ghost on July 05, 2012, 09:15 am
Yep I did recommend GPG4USB. I'm fairly new to this myself and pretty much went through the process you are undergoing.
Don't bother with thunderbird or trying to link with outlook or anything like that. You don't want these mail organising program's storing your messages unbeknownst to you!

GPG4USB is a stand alone program. You just open the folder and click "start windows" in your case and up will pop a GUI very similar to GPA but even simpler.

To decrypt a message just copy paste it into the window and hit decrypt. It will ask for your pass phrase
which you created when you made your key pair.

If you made your keypair in GPA already it would be simplest just to create a new one! However if you have a message that's encrypted with that key you CAN export your secret key. Open GPA. Select your key from the list and right click on it. A box with two options appears. I'm away from my computer do I can't remember but it's something like " key information" you select. If its the right one a large box of text about your key pair will appear. At the bottom will be option to "export private key" with lots of warnings to be careful. Do it asnd save the file. You should delete it later.
Now open GPG4USB. Select import key and choose "from file" or whatever. Browse to wherever you saved key file. This should now import your key pair.

Sounds complicated? It really isn't. Just have a play with GPG4USB you'll soon get head round it. You don't need anything else. Just use clipboard for everything for now. Import keys by copy pasting. Export your public key to clipboard to send it someone. Encrypt a message to someone then just copy paste it where you need it. And so on.

Honestly when I first started I tried all the same stuff trying to get thunderbird set up asnd everything. It's all unnessacary( I can't spell that word for some reason). GPG4USB is simple and does it all.  In a day or two you'll look back and think its really straightforward.
