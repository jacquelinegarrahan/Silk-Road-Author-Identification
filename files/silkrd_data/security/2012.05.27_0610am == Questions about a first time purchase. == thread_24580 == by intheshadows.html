Title: Re: Questions about a first time purchase.
Post by: PsilocybinTendencies on May 27, 2012, 06:29 pm
It sounds like you've taken every other security step possible.

So I wouldn't really be worried about using your real name.

It also sounds like you can afford a decent lawyer, that means in the unlikely event (given all the other precautions you've taken) that you do get pinned, if you just keep your mouth shut you're going to be fine. Nobody can really prove you ordered it. They call it plausible deniability.

I don't think I'd chance using the wrong name. I mean it's likely that the package will get through just fine, but you have no way of knowing if any of the previous tenant's mail has been denied (maybe only some gets through). It also may look funny because that name all of a sudden started ordering packages after getting nothing but spam for this long.

If the majority of your mail comes in your name, then the postman is less likely to notice a small package coming in your name (which could be ANYTHING). And at this point, the postal service is the only thing that could POSSIBLY get you busted (short of how you use the product, or the vendor keeping your name on a list, but those are beyond your mail receiving control).

No. It would be more likely to draw attention if you went and got a PO Box. You'd still need ID to register the box. The only safer method of receiving the package is to get a trusted friend to receive it for you, and take the blame if they get pinned.

They apparently (I am not a lawyer, and all the advice I've been giving is hearsay gleamed from the forums), do not have enough evidence to press charges based on intercepting a package alone (in the US), they would have to do a controlled delivery.

They are not going to fund a controlled delivery for that small of an amount (read the post from the former USPS intern, somewhere in this forum). They throw away tons of drugs everyday.

The idea is to make your packages blend in seamlessly with your other mail. So if you get more mail from your previous tenant than you do yourself, and have for years, then that might be the way to go.
