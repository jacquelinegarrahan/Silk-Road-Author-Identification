I had virtually the same thing happen a while back.  I was running Windows, started Tor, & vaguely remember there was a pop-up for a Firefox update.  I ignored it.  But apparently Firefox was updated automatically and the update broke the Tor config (that's my best guess).  Next time I restarted Tor I looked under Preferences/Privacy, and I could see my SR cookies still there.  Gaah!  That is not supposed to happen.

Quote from: Californeeya on September 05, 2011, 11:52 pm
...i had been logged out for over 3 hours, so i start up tor, head over to silk road and the first think that i saw was my profile. no white screen with the login and captcha or anything. my first instinct was to refresh to make sure that i wasnt actually still logged in. which I wasnt. when i refreshed it took me to the login page....
It sounds like your browser elected not to fetch a fresh copy of SR in favor of the copy sitting in memory or on your hard drive.  (Alarming thought, no?)  Once you forced a refresh, SR saw that your session had expired and asked for authentication.

The best (partial) answer, to me, is to *always* manually clear your browser cache *and* close the browser when you're done with business on any "sensitive" website.  There may still be information in both physical and virtual memory, but at least you're making it non-trivial for some one to figure out what you've been up to.

I've used Tor on Windows, Tor on Linux, and Tor from a TAILS USB drive.  Windows is the only OS that has given me any problems (like restarting Tor, going to SR, and automatically being logged in to SR).  The following is from the Tor Wiki on running a relay, but it applies here:
https://trac.torproject.org/projects/tor/wiki/doc/TorFAQ#WhydoesntmyWindowsorotherOSTorrelayrunwell

I think the following quote from that part of the Tor Wiki says a lot about Windows problems: "Most developers who contribute to Tor work with Unix-like operating systems.  It would be great if more people with Windows experience help out, so we can improve Tor's usability and stability in Windows."

I'm not sayin you have to dump Windows, but I feel a lot safer running Tor from either TAILS or Linux.
