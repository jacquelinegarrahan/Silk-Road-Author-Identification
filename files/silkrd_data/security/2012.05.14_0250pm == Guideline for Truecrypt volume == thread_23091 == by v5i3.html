Post by: Banjo on May 14, 2012, 03:34 pm
Quote
Note that this will slow down your computers performance.

Incorrect. Taken from TrueCrypt's FAQ page:
Quote
Can data be read from and written to an encrypted volume/drive as fast as if the drive was not encrypted?

Yes, since TrueCrypt uses pipelining and parallelization. For more information, please see the chapters Pipelining and Parallelization in the documentation.

OP make sure you have a blank CD or DVD handy when you're encrypting your entire HDD. Truecrypt forces you to create a rescue disk when you encrypt your entire HD, and it won't let you continue until it verifies that the disk has successfully been burned. Of course, I'd destroy this disk as soon as TrueCrypt has verified it, since it can be used to remove encryption in case you forget your password.
