Post by: Nellion on November 11, 2012, 05:33 pm
A thing to keep in mind when choosing your processing location with Bitinstant:

TRY TO PICK WHOLE IN THE WALL CHECK-CASHING JOINTS.

When you're finishing your ticket on bitinstant, it shows you a bunch of places that process their orders. I stay away from big corporate chains where employees fear punishment for not following guidelines. Granted, at Wal-Mart you will likely get a redneck who doesn't care, anyway, but I still like the small, independently owned check cashing places.

At the check cashing joints, they are mostly all owned independently and a lot of the times the owner or family member of the owner is working the register. And, the owners of these places are less likely to be as red about asking for ID.

What I like to do is this:

1.) Pretend like I don't know they're getting a small commission for processing the ticket.
2.) Before anything has begun processing, offer them an extra $5 ($10 if it's a larger amount) out of "gratitude".
3.) If in the rare case they ask for ID, either say you forgot your wallet or it was stolen. I like the stolen wallet excuse because then you can give them the whole "that's why I'm using this bill pay option because they don't require ID, blah blah blah". It invokes pity and they will appreciate the gesture of paying the xtra $5 so they will feel guilty if they turn you away for not having a damn ID,
4.) 9/10 times they will tell you not to worry about it and process your order.
5.) Also, they hardly ever accept the extra $5, and will tell you they get a small commission anyways.


But, fore me, I've been lucky enough to find an owner of one of these places that is a liberty-minded person (Ron Paul posters all over the place), and we've established a pretty solid relationship so I don't have to dance the dance any longer. I feel kind of bad, though, because he only knows me by my alias and he is a very decent human being (he's offered me no interest loans before), and I am basically manipulating him. But, I would rather feel a little guilty than compromise my anonymity :).
