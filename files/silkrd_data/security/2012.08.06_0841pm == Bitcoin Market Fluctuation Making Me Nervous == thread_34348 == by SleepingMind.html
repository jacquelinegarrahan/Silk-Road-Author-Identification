This is my first actual post since registering & I am actually in the same boat as you.  I lost a % of my investment while waiting for a purchase. 

Normally my advice (What I initially did) is to wait until you have found the product, get in touch with seller & make sure that if you spend half a day getting the funds in place that they will still be able to accommodate you - i.e. should their 1 time listing go down while you're still acquiring BTC, that they still have more of what it is you wanted. 

The first issue I ever had in regards to this was following the aforementioned, only to have what I wanted bought while I actually had it in my cart.  A gap of a few minutes cost me.  There wasn't more to immediately purchase so I had to play the waiting game - during which time the fluctuation nearly gave me a heart attack.

Basically make sure you dot & cross all those letters before actually putting the money in - make sure you can put it in & spend it before too much time passes.  Again, it's in your best interest to get ahold of the seller, tell them what you want & if there's more just in case what happened to me happens to you - then I recommend using bitinstant & making the transaction happen as fast as possible.

On a final note, if you're getting a custom listing from them, ask them to list it while you still have their attention, before depositing funds so you're good to go!  If not you may spend hours or the better part of a day just waiting to get back in touch with them and ask for that listing.  In my eyes that's valuable time for fluctuation in this incredibly volatile currency!

Title: Re: Bitcoin Market Fluctuation Making Me Nervous
Post by: pine on August 07, 2012, 02:39 pm
My advice is to dollar average your acquisitions over time.

e.g. You are spending 1000 USD, so use up $200 in five transactions in five days. That way you can ride out the downs and ups to an average. However in the long term, you are better off lumping it into one transaction and simply doing more transactions. But if you can't be sure about the long haul, then stick to the first approach.

I mean, in either case you should be swarming that 1000 USB in five or more packages in standard mail anyway, since it drastically decreases the chances of getting interceptions. Packages get exponentially more likely to be intercepted as they increase in weight/size. It's kind of counter-intuitive, but this is not done because of 'personal use' laws, it's just that swarming is an excellent long term risk management strategy on many levels, especially if you have multiple addresses to ship to like the pros do.
