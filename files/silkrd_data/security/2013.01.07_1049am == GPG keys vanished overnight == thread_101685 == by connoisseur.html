Quote from: CaptainMal on January 07, 2013, 11:02 am
Another thing you could do just to be sure is to make sure your PGP program is up-to-date.

While in general, this is good advice, I'm of the "if it ain't broke, don't fix it" school.  I recently advised someone on here who updated their GPGTools installation to the latest nightly build, only to find out that it broke something that they relied upon.  Luckily, they had a previous version stashed, and were able re-install that, so they were ok.  That's always a danger when updating software -- sometimes updating can cause more problems than it solves. You need to stress the fact that you always need to have an earlier copy you can re-install if the later copy causes you any issues.
