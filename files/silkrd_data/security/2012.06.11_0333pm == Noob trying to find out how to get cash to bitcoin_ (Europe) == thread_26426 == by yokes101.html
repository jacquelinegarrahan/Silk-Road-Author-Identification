Post by: hel on June 11, 2012, 04:35 pm
Hey,
I haven't been here very long either, but I guess you'd like a quick answer so I'll try...

If you buy bitcoins by any means linked to you, there's obviously proof that you bought bitcoins. Not illegal, but you'll be more interesting (SR=drugs in mail+btc; so if they find both they will get some ideas, right?). And I think btc can be tracked by more sophisticated investigators.

Bitcoins by cash you can buy from a vendor on SR called vlad1m1r, he has positive feedback, and is quite active in the security thread, so he might also help you with your questions. VCC I don't know. Maybe look at en.bitcoin.it (btc Wiki) to find merchants that accept them.

I haven't gotten deep into the bitcoin science, was just searching ways to get them anonymously, so someone else can explain you more.
As I understand the wallet is an address (combination of letters and numbers in fact)  linked to a number of btc that you can access, most merchant sites have so-called online wallets, you use it similarly to online banking (deposit/withdraw btc by sending them from/to another btc address). Please someone who understands this do explain it correctly, I just found it all worked, so I haven't read up on it (yet).

Again from newbie perspective: Bitcoinfog (http://fogcore5n3ov3tui.onion) splits up your transfers and mixes them with those of other bcf users, so it's much more difficult if not impossible to track them. If you use it to deposit into SR, your btc will come split up in small amounts over a few hours, min 6. Probably unnecessary when you've bought your btc anonymously in the first place.

Liberte and TAILS are live OS that run on your computer without leaving a trace. Your usual OS doesn't boot but instead the live OS is loaded into your RAM from a CD or USB stick. Shut down your computer - no trail of it anymore. It's recommended because the Tor browser bundle supposedly leaves traces on your HD that could point to SR.
I depends on what OS you have to make them work, this forum is full of guides how to use liberte/TAILS, and discussions on how secure they might actually be.

Hope this helps for a start.
