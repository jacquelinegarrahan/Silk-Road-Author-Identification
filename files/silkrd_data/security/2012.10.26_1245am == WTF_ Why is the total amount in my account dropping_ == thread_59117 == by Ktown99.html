What? I've read about the total limit cap of btc, but i haven't heard about the ammount able to be mined per hour being capped. Please explain. :)

Here's a quick version, you can read up a lot more at bitcoin.org or the bitcointalk forums on the clearweb.

Satoshi planned on a hard cap of coins to exist.  For the coins to be generated they need to be mined.  The algorithm used for miners adjusts the difficulty to keep the amount of bitcoins generated at a certain rate.  The more miners (higher hashrate), the higher the difficulty and vice versa.  Also within the algorithm when a certain number of blocks/coins are mined, the block reward splits in half until all coins are mined.  At the current network hashrate the reward will cut in half in December.  Once all coins are mined, miners will rely on transaction fees to continue operations but that won't be for a long time to come.

That explanation leaves a lot to be desired I'm sure, but it gives you the gist.

A lot of people will argue that the reward split is already taken into account on current pricing.  We can only hope they're wrong if you're holding on to any coins.
