Title: Re: help an idiot out with gpg4win PLEASE!!
Post by: astor on July 15, 2013, 06:31 pm
On a related note, your PGP encrypted address is rendered useless if the vendor sends you a tracking number in plaintext, because an adversary can use that number to find your address. Tracking numbers are actually worse, because addresses are deleted when the order is marked In Transit, usually within a couple of days, while tracking numbers will be stored in SR messages for 3 months, even after they are deleted.

If you encrypt your address, you should instruct the vendor not to send you a tracking number, but then again, they should be smart enough to figure that out for all of their customers who encrypt their addresses.

I agree that gpg4usb is the one to go for but if you insist on using gpg4win, here's a basic starter's guide I wrote to help get you started.  :)


1. Copy the Public Key of the person you want to send a message to.

2. Open a text editor, such as Notepad, and paste the Public Key. Name the file and save it to your desired location on your PC.

3. Open GPA (Gpg4win), click IMPORT, locate the file you just saved with the Public Key in it, and double click it. It will then show up in KEY MANAGER where it is stored for future use along with any other Public Keys you wish to keep.

4. Then click on CLIPBOARD, the 2nd icon in from the right at the top of the page. Type the message you wish to send to the recipient and then click ENCRYPT. Click on the Public Key of the person your sending the message to (should now be highlighted), then click OK at the bottom.

5. Then copy the encrypted message and paste it in a PM to the recipient and send it.

6. REMEMBER - Include your Own Public Key in this message if you wish to receive an encrypted response from the recipient. You can find this by RIGHT-CLICKING your key in KEY MANAGER, then copy/pasting it. :)

Tails has a rather elegant setup when it comes to using GPG.  In Tails, there is a editor plugin which can be used to encrypt/decrypt/sign/verify the contents of the document being edited.

Just use gedit to compose your message, and then select encrypt/decrypt/sign from the edit menu.

Naturally, to use GPG in Tails, you will have to have copied your PGP keyrings to the .gnupg directory, which is a sub-directory of amnesia.
