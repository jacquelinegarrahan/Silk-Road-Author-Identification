Post by: gaah on July 04, 2012, 11:11 pm
Don't belive the hype of the posters above...  mostly uninformed BS.

If you get a package siezed which is under a threshold (unknown what exactly the threshholds are), Dept. of Homeland Security Customs & Border Patrol will send you a separate "love letter" saying that they took your items and that you have a certain number of days to petition to get them back.  Sometimes the letter is sent via certified mail, sometimes not.
If you don't do anything in response you can expect the following:

1) International shipments to that address will be scrutinized.  Specifically if the name matches the seized package.  Sometimes other packages will make it through if they were sent around the same time... seems like it takes a bit for the system to update.

2) You may be stopped the next time you travel internationally.  They will take you aside and ask you a litany of questions and go through everything in your bags (both carry-on and checked).  You will also get special treatment when you arrive back in the US.  If you don't have anything incriminating on you they'll just let you pass.
If this happens, then you are on the DHS CBP watch list.  You can petition to get off via TRIP, but they'll just give you a redress number.  You can't get off the list.

If the amount seized was not a large amount then you really don't have much to worry about.  If the amount seized was large enough to warrant further investigation then they won't send you a letter, but instead will open a case on you and eventually bust you (Check out the story of Enelsyon from OVDB).  Sometimes they will even let the packages go through to observe you picking them up.

That said... I've a friend who has received at least 7 letters in the past 4 years (all for either tiny amounts, or pot seeds from NL) and nothing has come of it.

Watch yourself... stay safe... buy domestic from now on, and use a new address just to be safe.
