Title: Silkroad and Fight Club
Post by: onetimer1784 on March 24, 2012, 05:40 pm
I come from a background in the warez scene. We use similar methods to keep ourselves hidden as people use here. However, rule one (and two) is always do not talk about it. This keeps its exclusive and with exclusivity it becomes hard(er) for LE to infiltrate our sites. It also helps us to fly under the radar as much as possible which is important (LE agencies go after p2p primarily as we are harder to find and there's less of us by several million).

My point is: Silkroad is expanding massively. This is great news in the short-term for everyone as there's more drugs to go around but every new person here makes Silkroad a bigger target. The tor system it is based on is by no means infallible (look at paedo siterings that regularly get busted). The more exclusive Silkroad is (in terms of people knowing about it and also usage) the longer it will last. You can see the problem when you look at the number of noob posts in the forums asking how to use basic cryptographic techniques AFTER they made it this far. This new year is seeing silkroad become mainstream. Mainstream news organisations have run programs on this new phenomenon - fuck if a researcher from 5live can get here its too easy.

It's quite hard (and counterproductive considering it is a business platform) to limit silkroad's expansion. I guess the admins could limit the userbase (this would by no means get rid of all the LE/noobs currently on the userbase but it would stunt growth) but that is unlikely to happen. Things like moving the address of the site every couple of weeks would also be a good measure and slightly less intrusive. However, the easiest defense to employ is this:

DO NOT TALK ABOUT SILK ROAD
