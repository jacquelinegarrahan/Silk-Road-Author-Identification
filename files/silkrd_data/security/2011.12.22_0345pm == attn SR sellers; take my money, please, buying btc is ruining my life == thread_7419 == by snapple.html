No offense sugarmama. However as with all MoneyPak to BTC vendors be careful. Read the scammers list first. Check for any negative ratings. The first negative ratings on any BTC seller will be followed by a tirade of attacks on the noob. Then an experienced seller will get his coins. He/she may not realize that he/she is part of the scam. They will in good faith post to the board that "all is well". The reports will again trickle in and some people will wait hoping there is a glitch in the BTC matrix and the BTC vendor will come through. Then at the end of it all the BTC vendor just pull the big one and leave.

Also be cautious of vendors who claim they will not do small money pack transactions. There maybe limits on these cards however small is the way to build trust. Small is the way to avoid scams. Good luck trading for BTC on SR. I would however follow the advice of a lot of others and avoid BTC transactions on SR. There have been too many SunYunGai's.

There could also be a karma system on reviewers like slashdot. The bad reports if backed up by another good karma buyer should immediately sound off  vendor malpractice. Kinda like one transaction is bad, all buyers in the past 72 hours get a notice of this bad transaction and are encouraged to be pro-active. If there has been another bad transaction with two more buyers the account goes into lock down mode. Scam nipped in the bud is what SR needs. The trouble with financial scam on SR is that it's the most likely to be reported to the local news networks/ Law enforcement. It will threaten the very existence of SR.   I love SR but have no love lost for BTC transactions on SR.
Good Luck all noobs.
