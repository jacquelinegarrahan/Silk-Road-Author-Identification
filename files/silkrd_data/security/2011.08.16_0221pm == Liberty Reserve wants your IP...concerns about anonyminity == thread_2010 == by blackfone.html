Title: Re: Liberty Reserve wants your IP...concerns about anonyminity
Post by: CaptainSensible on August 16, 2011, 08:38 pm
"... they want your IP; which can be used to identify you."  You're damn straight on that one.  Even if you're piggybacking off of your neighbor's Internet connection via an unsecured router, the Internet service provider (ISP) is collecting data which can identify your computer.  The MAC address of your network card is a unique number that is probably being recorded by the ISP you use.

That said, it's not a crime to own Bitcoins.  Just remember that any clearnet site you've connected to has info that can be used to track you. So either stay away from clearnet sites or find a way to break the connection between Bitcoin transactions you've made and the transfer of those Bitcoins to/from SilkRoad.
