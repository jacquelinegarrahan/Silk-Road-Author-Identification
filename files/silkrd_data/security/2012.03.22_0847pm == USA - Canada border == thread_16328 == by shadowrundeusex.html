A friend of mine loves raves for some reason and gets all his info from meetup.com. There are "underground party" groups for my city and probably others. I have another amateur DJ friend on facebook who always plays at these things, so I get invites seemingly everyday.

But that doesn't really answer anything about the border. I don't have much experience. But here's one just for entertainment.

I was crossing the border via Greyhound and fell asleep on the ride. I woke up just as we got to the border station, where we all had to get off the bus and have our bags scanned and ID's checked and whatnot. I was really chill from just having a nice nap. This border cop saw how laid back I was and was like, "hey, this is serious you know!" He took me aside and asked me questions while typing shit into a computer. Meanwhile, everyone else is either back on the bus or getting their luggage scanned. The cop lets me go, but the funny thing was, they never checked my bag. I actually had a bunch of herbs (legal) in my luggage that would definitely have given them reason to turn me away, or worse. It was actually their suspicion of me that got me through.

Possibly useful: I tried to cross from Canada to the US again recently and they were again super suspicious of me for some reason. I keep a pretty clean nose, I don't know why my luck is so bad. I was kept there for about an hour while they did god-knows-what on their computers. They scanned my fingerprints 3 times. I had every piece of documentation they could possibly want, birth certificate, passport (brand new), ID. They turned me away because they wanted proof of where I got my income or something. So consider bringing your most recent tax return or the like.

In the second case I was again, very calm and cool, well dressed and groomed, it's almost like they pick on the ones who aren't afraid of them. They also searched my backpack, but didn't find the knife in the front pocket that I forgot I had (a friend left it at my house, I don't own any weapons)! All together, I'm not sure if I'm lucky or unlucky.
