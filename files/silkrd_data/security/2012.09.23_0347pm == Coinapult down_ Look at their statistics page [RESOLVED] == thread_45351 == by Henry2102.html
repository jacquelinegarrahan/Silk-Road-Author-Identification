Title: Coinapult down? Look at their statistics page [RESOLVED]
Post by: Henry2102 on September 23, 2012, 03:47 pm
So, I just went to make my first purchase of bitcoins, I bought them using Bitinstant and selected Moneygram as my method of payment and "Send to email" as the delivery method for the bitcoins. I went to my local duane reade and made the moneygram deposit ($35.95, $32+fees). Bitinstant uses Coinapult to deliver the coins by e-mail. I received a confirmation e-mail from Bitinstant stating "order executed" and giving me a coinapult url to collect my bitcoins. Upon clicking the coinapult url I was brought to my coinapult wallet, which showed a balance of zero bitcoins.

I was at first not too perturbed and figured that they just needed some time to process the transaction, but several hours later, still, zero bitcoins. So I sent bitinstant and coinapult each a message alerting them to the situation, and started reading over everything on the coinapult website to figure out what's going on, which is when I noticed that their chart on the statistics page shows a dramatic drop in bitcoin transactions over the last 48 hours going all the way to zero and staying there for about the last 12 hours (if I'm reading the graph correctly).

Now, I used my regular personal e-mail account that I've had for years, a gmail account, to receive the coins, so I'm open to the idea that perhaps my e-mail was compromised and the coins were stolen from me. However, coinapult requires a PIN to send those bitcoins, and that pin is not anywhere in the e-mail records, it exists solely in my head. All my internet traffic is torified, and the only place i ever entered the PIN was when I filled out the form on Bitinstant.com ...

Anyway, the chart on coinapults statistics page set off alarm bells in my head. And my $35.95 is gone, and I have no bitcoins. If it's simply because I failed to implement proper security measures (such as a secure email address or perhaps having Bitinstant send the coins directly to a bitcoin client on my computer instead of relying upon a third party) and the coins have just been stolen, I'm ok with that, I'll get some more money together and try again. But I'm mostly concerned that there's something wrong with coinapult and that's why my money's gone.

I would have posted this on the Bitcoin forums but I'm still a Newbie there and they don't allow Newbies to post so I figured I'd post here. Anybody have any idea what happened?
