The most common error that crops up when trying to import keys is the failure to have all 5 dashes at the beginning and end of the -----BEGIN PGP PUBLIC KEY----- and -----END PGP PUBLIC KEY----- lines.

If even one of the dashes is missing, the key will fail to import.

I just tried importing his key, and had no problems whatsoever, so his key is not malformed or garbled in any way.

This is a textbook example of what not to do. If you run into a situation like this, please stop immediately and ask for help! Please do not change any settings, and flail about trying to fix the problem -- believe me, you will only cause more problems than you solve.

As far as the keyservers go, a lot of vendors do not post their PGP keys to the keyservers, so the odds of success are minimal. The absolute best place to obtain a vendor's key is from their SR vendor page, or here on the Forums, if they happened to post their key here.

If I were in your shoes, I'd ditch Kleopatra, and use gpg4usb instead. You can download it from:

http://gpg4usb.cpunk.de/index.html
