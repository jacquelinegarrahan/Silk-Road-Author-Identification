This.

The original hackers from the 80s/90s laid the foundation for many of the popular security standard in use today, and that generation hasn't been involved with anonymous since the early days of /b/. They are the only ones with the knowledge and power to restore access to SR, and that's if the feds didn't physically seize the server and leave a new one serving a seizure notice in its place.

Many of us either have families or switched sides to law enforcement now, so hacking back SR from the feds is probably not going to happen. That doesn't mean we can't anonymously help revise all the dated algorithms, protocols, etc protecting online privacy and the ability to stay hidden. Thanks to the complacency of the early internet generation--the only ones that could have seen this coming from the start--the mythical "echelon" became a reality as soon as sniffing almost the entire internet became technically feasible.

Although at this stage it looks like SR fell as a result of DPR's incompetence, we can't rule out the involvement of the NSA or perhaps covert FBI abilities involving tor. Either way, tor is not a long term solution, and neither are current encryption algorithms.
