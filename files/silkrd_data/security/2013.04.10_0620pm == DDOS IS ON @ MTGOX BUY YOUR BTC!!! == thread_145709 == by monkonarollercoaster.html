Post by: ProudCannabian on April 10, 2013, 09:04 pm
BTC sellers control the price.  When you buy something, here or in RL the price is the dollar value of BTC. So it doesn't matter what you buy at, as long as you don't wait to buy your x dollar item.  Buyers always look for a deal, but will always take the lowest price offered, even if it is just above the average.
Selling is where you see competition - sellers trying to sell just a little less than someone else when they want to cash out, or hold until the average creeps up and their coins are at the bottom edge and get sold anyhow.  So if you have a shitload of coins (like maybe DPR) you can sit on that hoard of cash, at a higher than average sell, and expect to sell coins at that price here and there, or you can manipulate the price and "take what you can get" selling lower than average - and with that many coins bring down the average with you.

My theory is when you see "last price" waaay below the average, it's usually either someone cashing out a lump sum they didn't work for (bitcoin thieves) or someone intentionally fucking with the market (LE or speculators).

If someone with a lot of coins wanted to hurt SR, they could hold them until the price crept up (like right now where a lot of people are suddenly BTC "rich") then drop the price like a stone by selling the horde way below market value, leaving vendors with not much cash to replenish supply.

I think this is why it's very important not to freak out and sell below average unless you absolutely have to. The average was at 200 earlier, and some asshole just cashed out at 160 - desperate or devious?!
