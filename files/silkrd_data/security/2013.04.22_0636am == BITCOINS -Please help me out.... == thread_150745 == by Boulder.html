That depends on bitInstant's current exchange rate.
If bitcoins are $100/ea, you print an invoice out for $200 (+$4.95 fee), you'll get $200 minus fees, in my experience, it was about an additional %8.

If you assume an additional %8 from $200, that's $184 in bitcoins, and if the exchange rate is 1BTC:$100, then you'd have approximately 1.84 Bitcoins from bitinstant.

However, you'll lose some more when you ship it off to or from your wallet - depending on services used, and you could potentially lose another $5 or so.

In the case you describe, I would expect to end up with approximately $175 spending power on silk road. (Disregard how many bitcoins that is for a minute)

At the current rate, that's about 1.43 bitcoins, which is irrelevant to your spending power if you plan to spend it quickly.

People not accounting for these losses is precisely why the Spare Coins Thread exists.
