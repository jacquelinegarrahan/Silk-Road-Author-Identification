Post by: CaptainSensible on January 01, 2012, 08:27 pm
Not sure if you're talking about decrypting messages you sent or messages you received. So I'll answer this two ways: You can encrypt messages to other people if you have their public key.  But you can't decrypt them -- that would require you to have their private key.  Yeah, it's frustrating if you want to re-read the encrypted message you just sent someone.

If you can't decrypt a message sent to you from someone else (and intended for you) then the sender may have used the wrong public key to send you a message.
