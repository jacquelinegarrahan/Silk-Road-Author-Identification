Title: Re: Anonymous via cellphone network?
Post by: FarmerBob on April 24, 2012, 05:54 am
Even with GPS disabled the cell towers can often track your device location to within a few hundred yards or so.  It works by looking at your device signal strength as observed by all the cell towers (with their directional antennas) on the network and estimating where your device must be to generate that set of received signals.  Think of it as you hearing someone scream in the woods (your phone), you can point in the direction where you thing you heard the scream and guess how far away they were but it'd be inaccurate.  But if you had 15 guys (towers) within earshot of that scream and you knew their locations the directions they think they heard it from and their guess on how far away it was you could triangulate on a map the general area it must have come from.

They can always get your IMEI and SIM card info, but if you pay cash for a prepay phone that's no big deal. 

LE can pull your call record also, try to see if you called or texted a friend and then try to trace down who you are from there.

The good news is that the carriers tend to get so much data and usage that they don't have the ability to retain that info forever so it gets deleted after a while.

For very high profile cases the FBI and FCC (domestic) and CIA/NSA (international) have access to some fancy fancy communications tracking equipment that can locate phones anywhere based on signal time-of-arrival.  Lookup "emitter mapper" systems if you want to know more.  For those high profile cases the CIA/NSA can also emulate cell towers and networks, emulate app stores, etc and can occasionally push custom "software updates" to your phone.  Supposedly domestic spying is against the rules so I don't know if FBI has access to that technology but I wouldn't doubt it.

Anyways, if you're cellular internet won't buy you much security if you're using it at home.  But it's no worse than using your home internet.  Just don't be "high profile".
