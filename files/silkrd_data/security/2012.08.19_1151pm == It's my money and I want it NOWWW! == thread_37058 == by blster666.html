Title: Re: It's my money and I want it NOWWW!
Post by: vectorsalimundo on August 21, 2012, 01:39 am
For others new to this and reading this thread, as well as OP, look at it like this:

When you go to SR or similar sites you are traveling to a section of the internet with its own currency, much like traveling to a different country. When you travel to countries that use other currencies you have to buy the local currency when you arrive and buy back your home currency when you leave. This is equivalent to buying goods on SR and selling them, respectively. The nice thing about the real world's currency exchange rates is that they, relatively speaking, don't fluctuate that much during your trip to another country, because there is a large volume (liquid) and no big players can really affect the rate to their advantage, even central banks.

BTC on the other hand, while being very beneficial to this community due to its inherently difficult nature of tracing transactions to actual people, has downsides because it is a lot more volatile of a currency than GBP, USD, EUR, etc. Because of this the price can wildly fluctuate in hours or days and leave some people standing there with their dick in their hands. Also because of this, people that own a lot of currency can dump a bunch of it rapidly and drive the price down, then buy back when the price is low and make a nice profit.

Ultimately the way to protect yourself from this is to use fast sources of exchanging your currency to BTC and staying on top of it, like using bitinstant, as well as transferring your funds from an intermediary wallet to SR (or another wallet first, but each tumble costs time as the BTC blockchain has to confirm transactions) and place your order as soon as possible. When I buy bitcoin I always add some to account for the currency exchange transaction rates (usually 5% plus a little) and then add 1-2 BTC, as it can sometimes  fluctuate this much in the time it takes to place an order. Do these things and use trusted vendors and you will be fine. Sorry you got the shitty end of this BTC bear market and hopefully you don't get too deterred from it all.
