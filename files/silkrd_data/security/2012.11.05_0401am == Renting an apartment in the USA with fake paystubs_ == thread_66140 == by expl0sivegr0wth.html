Title: Re: Renting an apartment in the USA with fake paystubs?
Post by: thesearstower on November 05, 2012, 05:16 am
Faked bank statements are golden. I've personally seen falsified bank statements allow people to obtain commercial leases (5k/month, 10 years). It's also not usually something that they ask for, so when you volunteer it, you look prepared. It's also not possible for them to check whether the information is actually true. You can't just call up a bank and say, "Hey, what's the balance on so-and-so's account?"

Fake pay stubs run the risk of your employer calling your workplace to verify. Of course, if you're already faking the pay stub, it's probably small potatoes to slip in a fake phone number that forwards to you or a friend of yours. Or just give them a burner number, and tell them that it's your boss' work phone. Have a buddy record the VM message.

This fraud, as with most fraud, depends a lot on your ability to sell it. Experienced landlords have a pretty finely-tuned BS detector, and you can bet that they'll investigate more deeply if they catch a whiff of shit.

Also, if you're dealing with the landlord directly, and not a broker or rental company, try being upfront about your situation. All the landlord is trying to do is ensure that he/she doesn't get screwed. If you can convince them that you'll be a great tenant, then it might be as simple as putting down an extra month's deposit, or paying a little extra in rent every month.

I'm always a fan of Mark Twain's "If you tell the truth, you don't have to remember anything." strategy. Makes my life easier, for sure.
