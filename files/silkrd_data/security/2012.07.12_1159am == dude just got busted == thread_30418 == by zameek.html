July 12, 2012
Beckley man charged after drugs intercepted in mail

By Kara Van Pelt Register-Herald Reporter

A Beckley man is facing felony drug charges after a federal postal inspector and the Charleston Police Department intercepted a package mailed from Detroit, Mich., containing thousands of dollars worth of narcotics, Beckley Police report.

Reginald Turner, 54, of Beckley was charged with possession with intent to deliver after a search warrant executed on his home at 302 Wildwood Ave. uncovered prescription medication and marijuana, Detective Lt. Gant Montgomery said.

The search warrant was executed after Turner accepted the package, which postal inspector Josh Mehall had tipped Charleston Police to its suspicious nature and illegal contents after a federal search warrant for the package was obtained, Montgomery said.

The package contained 89 hydrocodone pills, and after Turner accepted the package at his home, a subsequent search warrant of the home resulted in the seizure of 20 Opana — oxymorphone — tablets, several ounces of marijuana and an undisclosed amount of cash, police added.

Beckley Police were contacted because an ongoing investigation into Turner was already being conducted. Police believe Turner has received previous packages containing illegal drugs through the U.S. Postal Service on a monthly basis, which Montgomery said is becoming a more prevalent issue in the war against drugs.

Turner maintains past drug convictions, Montgomery added, and additional charges are possible against other residents at the home. Turner was remanded to Southern Regional Jail where he was awaiting arraignment.

Cpls. Bryan Atterson and Will Reynolds, U.S. Marshall Shane Osgood, Parole Officers Dave Toiler and Doug Workman, and Charleston Police Detective Justin Hackney assisted in the execution of the search warrant, Montgomery said.

Anyone with additional information regarding this incident is encouraged to contact Beckley Police at 304-256-1708 or Crime Stoppers at 304-255-7867.
