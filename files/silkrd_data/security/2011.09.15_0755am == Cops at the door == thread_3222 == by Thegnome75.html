Post by: DwightEAnderson on September 15, 2011, 08:54 pm
Without an admission of guilt, they have NOTHING.  LEO's job in this case is to go fishing.  The reason they did not take you immediately was they did not have enough on you.  The other reason is to give you time to break down so they can get an easy confession. 

Unless the LEO has been watching you or you are a BIG timer, this is as far as most interactions in any country will go.  If you break down, you are busted.  If you are released, you were calm and in control during the interview. 

I would not send any goods to this address for quite sometime, perhaps never, just to be safe.  Could you if needed, IMO certainly but with a slightly elevated risk.  Just bear in mind that since they talked to you, dozens of other people have fucked up by trying to talk their way out it or just straight-up admitting it, thus going to jail and filling arrest quotas.  There are always easier fish to fry daily and most won't pass that interview you successfully endured.

Post by: DwightEAnderson on September 15, 2011, 09:00 pm
Quote from: ABC123nuthin on September 15, 2011, 06:16 pm
Why wouldn't you use your real name?

Makes no sense to me that all of a sudden your primary address starts getting shipments in random names. The feedback that I have heard from a lot of people is to make the postage appear as normal as possible. If I suddenly start to get mail to an address under the name of "Joe Bloggs", however I have been getting mail to the address under my real name for years -- to me that throws up a big red sign.

Depends how you do it.  I use a business name and have for years.  I write "Attention: MR. Anderson, and have never had a problem.  I change the Att: *name* all the time because 1) they have no idea how many employees I have, if any, and 2) if a LL is sent, they have a person to address the seizure letter to.

I have NEVER used my real name on ANY package.


Just wanted to add the sequence for USPS when sorting mail:

State
Zip code
City
Street address

Name (by far the LEAST important line)
