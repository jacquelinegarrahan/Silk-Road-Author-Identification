Post by: mdmamail on April 06, 2012, 04:52 am
Create a truecrypt container on the USB. Use it to store your GPG keys, passwords, email logins or whatever else.
Then you burn Tails or Liberte to CD and just plug in the USB and decrypt it whenever you want to use it. I used to do that with an encrypted IronKey until I started using a different system, but it works fine. I never installed Tails directly to a USB because that kind of defeats the point, I would've had evidence lying around unencrypted whereas the live CD is read only.

On the tails bootup screen, you can press tab and just type 'truecrypt' and press enter and it will be available when you boot up. Or just download it from the site and double click to install it while using tails.

When you first boot up tails (if you have no gpg key) you simply generate a new one clicking System->Preferences->Passwords & Encryption Keys in the top menu, then export both the private and public key (they're text files). You move them to your truecrypt container. Next time you nee d to use them (rebooting tails), just double click them and Tails automatically adds them to your keyring.

Can also just download Virtualbox, and any operating system .iso like debian, archlinux, windowsXP. It's a window on your desktop that loads a new operating system, so you have two comps at once basically. It creates a 2g (or however big you want) .VDI file and installs whatever operating system you want onto it. Now you have a seekrit computer to use. Install Tor browser, bitcoin program, ect. Then take a snapshot. Your virtual computer is saved and will reboot to that state evertime. When you're down, truecrypt the .VDI snapshot into a container. Nobody can access it now.
https://www.virtualbox.org/wiki/Screenshots

I use Virtualbox on a shitty 1G mem laptop running debian and it works fine for testing.
