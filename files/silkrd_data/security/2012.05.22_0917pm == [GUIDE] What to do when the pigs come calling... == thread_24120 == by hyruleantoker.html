Title: [GUIDE] What to do when the pigs come calling...
Post by: hyruleantoker on May 22, 2012, 09:17 pm
I've seen so many threads and posts in my short time here, regarding how to handle cops, among other things. Most of the time, the word has been to keep your mouth shut, and let the only word you utter be 'Lawyer'. This however, seems to only fit some situations.
I'm making this post as a sort of guide for that off chance you have an encounter with a pig from LE.
I'll be the first to admit that every situation is unique, and some call for different actions than others, but this should cover some basics. Ultimately, how you behave is up to you.

Situation 1: Signing for packages
You get a letter in the mail after ordering something from SR. This note tells you that you need to come to the Post Office to sign for a package.

What to do: Do not go in! Do not sign for any package. Signing means that you acknowledge ordering whatever is in the package, and you can be held accountable as such.
If you get such a letter, you're out of luck, and you're best bet is just to lie low for a short while (I would personally suggest ~2-3 weeks), and then try again.


Situation 2: Call from Police
You get a call from local police asking you to come in to talk. You've recently ordered something, and the package never arrived. The officer may or may not say why they want you to come in.

What to do: Go in! No matter what!
Option 1: If you have even a half-decent poker face, play the good, innocent citizen card. Play ignorance, and deny that you expected any package to come for you. MAKE SURE TO NOT USE THE WORD ORDER(ed). That will get you caught. Make the situation as light and friendly as you can, and act as a 'normal' person would. You have a job, you believe drugs are terrible, etc... If you can think of some reason someone else would send you a package with illegal things, have that up your sleeve if you need it. Local homeless kids trying to use your mailbox, some enemy you've made, a spiteful ex-girlfriend, etc... While keeping everything light and innocent, be mindful of what you're saying. Obviously take care not to say anything incriminating, and keep your mind sharp against any tricks the officer may try to use on you. If you sense that the interview has gotten heavy, and you may be getting into deeper mess, you MUST go to option 2.

Option 2: For when you don't have a poker face, and if option 1 has failed, you switch to this. What everyone else says to do: Don't say a thing! If you feel like LE already has solid evidence on you, or if the situation just feels too heavy, stay quiet, and demand a lawyer. Doing this makes you seem guilty of something, and that's why it's option two. You can and should tell your lawyer everything. They are not legally allowed to divulge any information that you give them. Once you fill your lawyer in, he/she will know how to handle things. Again, when in the presence of LE, be mindful of what you say.


Situation 3: Non-LE asking about package confusion
Perhaps not a common occurrence, but you've ordered a package, and some person, perhaps of the postal service, comes to your door with your package in hand. He's asking you about something, confirming that this package is for you, or something. Maybe you used a false name when ordering (Which you SHOULD NOT do by the way), idk.

What do do: Refer to option 1 of Situation 2! Play it easy and nice. You're a normal guy who didn't expect any packages to arrive. Make light conversation, and deny expecting the package.

Situation 4: Held in Customs
Your package has a tracking number, and you see that you're package has been held in excess of 5 days by customs. It is released, and it arrives at your house, or wherever you receive it.

What to do: While this may not always be the case, chances are your package was checked, and found to have illegal items within. The fact that you've received it anyway means that LE is likely watching it. I personally would not take the risk of even opening it, and would act as though it was not meant for me. So upon finding it in your mail box, or on your front step, take a marker and write 'RETURN TO SENDER' on the package, placing it back in the box, or taking it to the post office shortly/immediately after finding it.
The last thing you want is to take it inside and open it, only to have LE pigs knocking down your door 30 minutes later while you're trippin acid or lighting up your pipe.


Hopefully that will answer some questions many worried buyers have. If you disagree with any of these points, feel free to post, and we can have a discussion! ^_^

Cheers,
~Toker
