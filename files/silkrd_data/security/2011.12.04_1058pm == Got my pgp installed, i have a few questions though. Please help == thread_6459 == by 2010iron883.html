You're getting there: just takes a lot of study.  On PGP, if you haven't already, be sure to download this documentation, and go thru it: it's not that long, and I used it myself to learn how to use it: http://www.gpg4win.org/documentation.html  (That is also good site for any pgp downloads, for beginners)

What we are talking about, usually, is just each other's *public' keys.  Once you get your pgp app installed, it will ask you if you want to create your own key pair, and then you will have a private and a public key, just like we do.  You can then give others your public key, so they can encrypt messages to you.  They we will give you our public key, so you can encrypt to us.  Your 'private' key, is used so you can decrypt the messages we send you. 

If you are buying on the main site, and they give you their public pgp key, then you can encrypt your address to the vendor.

My own preference for btc wallet--and tumbler--is instawallet.org.  Just go there, and then be *sure* to save the address, as that is your online wallet.  YOu'll see, if you go there.

Kleopatra is the GUI of PGP.  You work within her (pun intended ;)

So open up Kleopatra File -> New Certificate
Enter whatever for name/email
Enter cryptic passpharse
Chose the Paired PGP Keys
Then create a backup of your PRIV & PUB key if you like
Click Finish
Then viola Cert created.  Right click it -> Export Certificate to get your PUBLIC PGP Key
