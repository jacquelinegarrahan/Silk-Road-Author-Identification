Title: Re: How to turn my bitcoins into cash?
Post by: sbaxter on July 08, 2012, 09:59 am
Unless you are in Canada, I recommend one of those "IBAN reloadable prepaid debit cards", usually VISA electron from a large Poilish bank (yes, I am selling those myself, but there are other vendors here I guess who also sell them).

You can open an intersango.com account (they don't ask any awkward questions, no ID etc.), transfer your bitcoins to your account there, change (trade) them to EUR or USD (the fees are very low), then withdraw to your debit card and then withdraw cash via ATM using your debit card. It is really quite easy and straight-forward if you read the instructions carefully and follow them ;)

IMO this is one of the cheapest and most secure (at least against scammers) method for small amounts. (you can only withdraw 150 UR or 200 USD per day via ATM, weekly limit 750EUR/1000 USD, yearly limit 2.5 times that I think).

Be sure to order the correct card, they are issued in EUR, USD and GBP, if you choose GBP and withdraw USD (for example) you will have to pay additional fees and it generally just is more confusing regarding limits etc.

Or, if this is too much hassle for you (though it should only be a matter of a few hours to buy the anon card, create the intersango account and do the withdrawals), there are trusted vendors around here who offer cash in the mail for your BTC, this will be faster I guess, but probably more expensive (after all, they also have to pay some fees and want some money for their work). Of yourse there are some who speculate on the BTC exchange rates and give you a really good price, but I doubt that currently.

cheers,
sb
