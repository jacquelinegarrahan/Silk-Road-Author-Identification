If you meet in person to pay cash for coins, noone needs feedback as the deal gets done right then when you meet. You have to worry they will grab your cash and run, but, I don't think that is at all common. Meet in a public place and do a small trade to get to know the seller. You can set up more than one trade and make sure the first one works before moving on to a larger one. The coins go into your Local wallet quickly and there is a confirmation code that the seller will only know if they released the coins to you. Be sure they "funded" the transaction at least an hour in advance so you know they really have the coins and can release them quickly when you meet.

If you do a deal online thru a bank or cash in the mail or whatever, feedback/reputation is crucial (since you will not have any as a Local newbie) and will likely have to send your part first. There are escrow protection options and vendors with solid reputations. You just have to be careful who you choose.
