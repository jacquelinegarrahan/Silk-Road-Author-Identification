Title: Re: a few questions about pgp that guides will not answer
Post by: CoolGrey on October 27, 2012, 11:27 am
Quote from: SRtester on October 27, 2012, 10:55 am
What does "signing" the message actually do?
When you sign a message, you attach a cryptographic signature to it.

You can verify a signature if you have somebody's public key. If the signature is good, you can know for sure that the message came from the owner of that public/private key, and that it was not modified before it reached you.

If you want to get a little technical: When you sign a message, you encrypt it with your private key. Then somebody else can verify it by decrypting it with your public key. If that second step is successful, the person knows that it was actually encrypted with your private key, and therefore, must have come from you (because nobody else has your private key).
