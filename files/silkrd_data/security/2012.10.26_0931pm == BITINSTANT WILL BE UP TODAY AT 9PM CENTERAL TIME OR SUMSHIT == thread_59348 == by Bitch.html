I'm pretty new to btc (this was just my second btc transaction), and honestly I had a similar feeling about mtgox (though just based upon one SR member's post I read a few days back). But honestly it was not materially different that bitinstant>SR address.

I set up a mtgox account, which gave me an account number there. I used that account number to create a transfer order at bitinstant: CVS>mtgox. I took that information to CVS and used the moneygram phone to complete the transaction and paid for the order at the register. I went back home, and the correct dollar amount showed up in my mtgox account right away. This is new to me, but I guess since mtgox is an exchange, you have to purchase btc's from other members? So I used the USD that was in my mtgox account to purchase btc at the current rate of $10.15/btc (that's the main reason I didn't wait to see if bitinstant would add back the option to send directly to an address, I wanted to lock in this low price). The btc immediately appeared in my mtgox account. I created an instawallet account, and transfered the btc from mygox to instawallet. They showed up immediately; there was a 20 minute delay before they were verified and ready to send on to my SR account. After about 20, I sent them on to SR, and they showed up about an hour later.

I didn't have to provide any personal information to create the mtgox account. There is an option to get "verified," but that was not necessary for what I did. My transaction was rather small, so I would read up on that to make sure you don't HAVE to get verified to transfer larger amounts.

So, all in all a smooth transaction. Really glad I didn't have to wait days like some SR members recently. But hopefully bitinstant will add back the option to send directly to an address, just because a few less steps are involved.
