If you're looking for the easiest option, blockchain.info is the way to go. Bank transfers have a low daily limit, so you will want to install Barclays Pingit on your smartphone, which is an app that lets you make payments to blockchain.info with a much higher daily limit. It works with any UK bank account, not just Barclays.

When sending the coins to SR, send them through the mixer to 'anonymise' them. There's a 0.5% fee.

The best option is LocalBitcoins as it's both more decentralised and more anonymous. However, it's not worth it if you can't find anyone locally who has a reasonable exchange rate. (I can't comprehend why would anyone pay the ridiculous 9% fee to buy with cash in the mail from Bitcoin Nordic.)

Virwox is euro based and takes paypal as well as all the usual suspects.  the problem is you don't just buy a bitcoin, you have to buy Linden Dollars first (it is primarily a LD to real currency exchange) and then can place a bitcoin order.  Getting it out is easy but as I found, your first transfer out will be held for up to 48hrs as they process that one manually.  The coins are a higher price than on mt gox and you will pay a high commission if you're only buying a few but it's an easy way to do it.  I've been using virwox for years and have never had the slightest trouble with them.

localbitcoins I found that not all of the people advertising coins for sale are actually still active.  If you're going to do it then look for a trader who has got recent reviews, otherwise you may find that your offer is ignored.
