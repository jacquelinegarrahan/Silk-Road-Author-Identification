Title: Re: Is buying Bitcoins through my bank account really that dangerous?
Post by: caffeine_me on December 01, 2011, 06:17 pm
It does seem like a lot and it is normal to feel like you will never understand it.  The route I discussed above is the quickest and easiest way I have found that keeps me anonymous enough for my comfort level. 

When I first started out, I just did Dwolla (Takes forever and connects to my identity) ------->Mt Gox (Can't use TOR and connects to my real identity)  --------> ewallets (Got a bunch of money stolen from mybitcoin) ----->Tumblers (Cost money and don't trust they do what they are supposed to do)--------->SR

Then if you have the product sent to the same address with the evidence of the purchase, i.e., TOR, Browser, bitcoin, etc  all on your computer.  It is one more nail in the coffin.  So that is why I recommend a thumb drive. 
What I described is probably over kill for a few grams, but after getting money stolen and having time to think about how LE would go about building a case on me........I spent the time to learn a few more steps.

Key note about running your bitcoin client from a thumb drive.  Even if you export the file to the say Z: drive, it still wants to put your wallet on your C: drive (which is evidence).  So don't forget to make a short cut from the .exe file and in the properties put :  Z:\Bitcoin\bitcoin-qt.exe -datadir=Z:\Bitcoin

And download the block chain over TOR (Takes a long time so do it before you want to make a purchase).  With the new client I had to un check the "Map port using UPnP" while checking the connect through SOCKS4proxy
