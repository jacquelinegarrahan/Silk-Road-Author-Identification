But since a members personal shipping details/names, ect are deleted immediatly upon being shipped, that shouldn't be too much of a concern. And they're not gonna go after buyers who may have bought a '10-strip', even yesterday. They know, as well as we do, that it most likely would be long since ingested (err...deleted), and with no shipping trail, is probably nothing to be concerned about. ESPECIALLY if you encrypt your details to the vendor using GPG.

Using a Privacybox.de allows ANYONE to do just that, whether you are still learning GPG or not. You only need the vendors public key to use GPG to encrypt your personal info to them. We feel every vendor should add one to their page (if you trust the vendor), to offer a "one-way" method for ANY member to auto-encrypted a GPG message (and/or shipping info) to them. But you're on your own if you want a GPG encrypted reply without having GPG installed.

Although, if you search for our recent "Countermail removing private key from server" thread, there 'may' be workarounds. The only weakness with CM is Java, but I've read elsewhere there are friends of us all working with CM trying to get an Opensource applet to be be released. Time will tell I suppose?

But using locally installed GPG (preferably on a Truecrypted flash drive), is of course, still preferable. Just a 'possible' option. Especially since CM doesn't log IP's, and actually forces you to to keep your public key private from the start. You must 'manually' upload it to a keyserver (or preferably just send it only to those you choose?). I like that. The usb key option they offer is nifty as well.
