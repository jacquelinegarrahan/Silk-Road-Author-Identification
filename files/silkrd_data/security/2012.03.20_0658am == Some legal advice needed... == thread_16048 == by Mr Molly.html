What you should have done, and I'm not sure if this pertains to Australian law as it would in my country, is try to strike a plea bargain instead of going to trial.  In my country if you go to trial the prosecution will "take you to the box" and gun for the maximum sentence as punishment for wasting the taxpayers time and money on a trial.  If you agree to plead guilty, they will usually offer you a lesser charge or reduced sentence.  If you flee and become a fugitive you will be looking over your shoulder your entire life.  When you finally DO come to trial you will be convicted for sure because the jury will see your fleeing as a sign of guilt.

Use the next month to talk to your lawyer or public defender if you have one.

While i have a law degree, i would recommend you seek professional legal advice from a reputable  lawyer/solicitor and retain counsel (a barrister). Do not cheap out here.

I would also look at the conditions of your bail. Most bail is subject to conditions such as the forfeiting of your passport or staying within a specified jurisdiction and/or reporting to police. It is my personal recommendation to you - even if you are not prohibited from travel outside the relevant state jurisdiction, it will do NOTHING in your favour in court if you choose to travel overseas. You would potentially be doing yourself a LOT of harm in that regard.

Armed robbery (whether an accessory or not) is a very serious offence and often results in a lengthy custodial sentence. I'm sure that you are aware of this fact.
