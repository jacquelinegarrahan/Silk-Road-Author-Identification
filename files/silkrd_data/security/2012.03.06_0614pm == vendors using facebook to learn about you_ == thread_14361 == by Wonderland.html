Title: Re: vendors using facebook to learn about you?
Post by: THConnoisseur on March 06, 2012, 09:48 pm
Just one more reason not to use real information, despite sellers prompting you to. You have to remember that using your real name is strictly protecting the seller and does little for yourself (real name=delivery=they get their money). How much will fake information will help you with denial? No one can really say for sure, only guess, I think there are too many variables to say.

Even SR guidelines/rules suggest NOT using a real name/info, a seller asking you to do this is asking you to ignore SR guidelines, created to help keep you safe. Same with sellers asking to finalize early. Do buyers demand sellers' real information? Of course not! Is either at more or less risk/punishment if found than the other? Impossible to know!

If you have even just a half a brain, getting it delivered so that none of your real info is given out is possible.

Only give real info to sellers you have been doing business with for a bit your real information. You can never fully trust someone to not do something stupid with your information so this trust really needs to be earned, NOT expected. Who knows what they are doing with it, and in the wrong hands, it can be than just your name or where you live (ex. facebook). Real trust is built over time through experience, it isn't something you just all of a sudden 'have a feel for' by talking with someone.

Your facebook can be locked down so it does not even show up in a google search. However, that does not stop a seller from trying to find your info on google or facebook and that can be dangerous because your info is still being searched and tracked whether they find your facebook or not.

I have see too many threats from sellers on SR regarding info, and seemingly too much toleration for it- so much so, that I think it's a joke sellers request real information.
