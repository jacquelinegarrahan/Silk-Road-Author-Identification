=====The following might just be conjecture=====
Anyway Guru, I have to disagree with you about tossing the machine. The most aggressive virus will fuck with the hard-drive in a very direct way to try to hide itself, and of course we've all heard of rootkits and MBR-viruses...

None of those are actually that hard to clean off, last I checked. The first step is to consider that your OS is now bunk and accept the need for a reinstall. Also a good idea to format the drive, of course. But it's not like a virus can run when it's not an "active computer file", so even a quick-format works on most viruses. Then there is the MBR problem, of course. With an MBR-virus... still cleared by the format, really. Same with rootkits. A few viruses will actually change up your partitions... that's just a re-partitioning to fix, I'm pretty sure.

And that covers all of the broad-stroke viruses. Anything else that can squeeze into a computer in a way that won't be fixed by a format/new hard drive, is very specifically targeted to the hardware in such a computer.
=====Conjecture-end=====

But... seriously. Guru, the only thing that shouldn't be trusted is the hard drive, at most. And it's a rare virus that can get through the hard-drive in a way that formatting won't fix.

Unless I've missed recent developments in the virus-coding scene or something, I suppose. As far as I know, the above should be accurate.
