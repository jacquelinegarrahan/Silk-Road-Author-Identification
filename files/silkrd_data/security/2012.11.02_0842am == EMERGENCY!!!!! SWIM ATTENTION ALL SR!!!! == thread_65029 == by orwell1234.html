Post by: Leapfrogger on November 05, 2012, 05:42 am
Here's a little anecdote from "SWIM" about "remaining silent" in practice. For purposes of narration, I shall write it in the first person. ;)

During an especially tumultuous time in my life, I was pulled over on my way home from a 12-hour drinking binge. I was actually blacked out when I got pulled over- I "came to" as the officer was questioning me at my car window.

He asked me to take a field sobriety test. I politely refused.

He asked me to take a breathalyzer. I politely refused.

"Have you had anything to drink tonight?" No answer. If I'd said "just a couple of beers," they could have nailed me; I "appeared intoxicated" and even admitted to drinking! By staying silent they had no real evidence that I was drinking that night.

"Where are you coming from?" No answer. I could have made something up, but I was coming from a bar, and if they could somehow figure that out (credit card records maybe), they would have caught me in a lie, which is never good. (The "Don't Talk to Police" video mentions a similar scenario.)

The only question I answered that night was whether I had anything in my pockets that would stick him, because that's the only thing he actually needed to know. (The answer was "no", by the way!)

The police department hated my fucking guts because I was completely uncooperative (with the exception of actually physically resisting arrest.) In fact, they hated me SO much that they waited until just five minutes before the trial to offer me a plea bargain that brought the DUI down to a minor traffic offense. :)

I somewhat regret taking the deal and not going to trial- had I gone to trial and won, I wouldn't have even had to pay the fine for the reduced "charge" (essentially a ticket.) Even my lawyer wanted to give it a shot. It's quite possible that they wouldn't have even bothered trying me and just dropped everything. But, as the prosecutor knew, if you give someone a deal that guarantees they're not gonna get a DUI, they're gonna go for it- even if they're assholes like me.

So yeah, I more-or-less got away with a very stupid thing (don't drive blacked out, kids!) by keeping my mouth shut, and I have that fucking YouTube video to thank for it.

The reason why staying silent is better than trying to talk your way out of/around something is because you're walking in a fucking minefield. You don't know what they know, so you don't know how far you have to bend the truth. You run the risk of lying too much or too little.

Let's say you're questioned about a confiscated order. They ask you if you've ever purchased Bitcoins. You think "shit, I used my real name on that Instawallet deposit once. They probably already know about it." So you say you've bought Bitcoins, sure. "Why?" Um, because you're really into decentralized, pseudonymous cryptocurrencies. They're fun. "What did you buy with the Bitcoins?" Amazingly, you're able to think of an item off the top of your head that you can buy with Bitcoins and isn't drugs. "When did you make this purchase?" About a year ago, you don't know. "Who did you purchase the item from?" Um, you can't remember. Some website. "Do you still possess this item?" No...

Meanwhile, it turns out they didn't know anything about Bitcoin acquisition and never would have found out about the Instawallet deposit. But now they know that you've bought Bitcoins. Is that enough to convict you? Maybe not, but they just have a few more questions...

If you think it's worth the risk, you can try to smooth talk your way out of stuff- hell, I always attempt this with minor traffic offenses. Worst case scenario they write you the $90 ticket anyway, but at least you tried. But if it's a serious charge, I would stay quiet. The officer might yell and stomp his feet, but when they end up dropping the charges at the last minute for lack of evidence (or never even bring charges), you'll know it was worth it.
