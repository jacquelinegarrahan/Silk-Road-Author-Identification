Title: in USA, BCs can be purchased anonymously with cash via BitMe.com
Post by: TitsUp on November 19, 2012, 05:12 pm
I've now done 3 transactions with BitMe.com.  You simply have to have a Chase Bank branch near you, and you can get coins with no private information provided whatsoever.  The service requires no personal information to sign up.  Then, you just go into any Chase Bank branch and provide BitMe's account number and name, as pre-provided by the service.  I'm 3 for 3 with Chase never asking a single question about who I was.  I handed them the cash and the account information, and they handed me back a deposit receipt.  No drama whatsoever.  And the deposit is free...100% of your deposit will be available in your BitMe account.

Once your funds show up in your anonymous account, which has always happened within a few hours for me although they warn up to 24 hours, you can either buy bitcoins on their exchange.

The one downside of BitMe is that they're new, and therefore small, and therefore their exchange is much less liquid than MtGox.  So you tend to pay about $.20 more per coin than the current MtGox going rate.

If you can find an good enough deal on coins elsewhere to make up for the transfer fee, then using BitMe still gives you a way to get $ into the system anonymously.  You pay 2% to make an transfer of $ via VouchX.  Since MtGox, for example, accepts that method of transfer for no fee, you can get money into MtGox anonomously for 2%.  But since the 2% fee has always balanced out against the better MtGox pricing, I've never bothered.

Once you've bought coins, you can transfer them to any other BC wallet for a very nominal (talking pennies) fee.

Hope this helps some of you.
