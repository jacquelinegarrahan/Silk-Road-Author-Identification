It won't, I mean, it already is, sort of. If anything it's lately been trying to come above ground from the underground.

They can't really stop a mathematically based currency. They can't make numbers/cryptos illegal. They can try to ban "bitcoin", but then somebody will just start a new currency(like litecoin).

All bitcoin is, is data. We are trading data when we trade bitcoin, The exchange of numbers cannot be banned. They can try to regulate it, but that is a task that is near impossible.

They try to regulate drugs, which are physical things we try to exchange for currency, etc. Even due to the solid nature of drugs, and the regulations they have in place, we still manage to smuggle and exchange them. Now if they try to regulate bitcoin/data... it will simply be impossible. Numbers/data are infinitely times easier to exchange than solid matter. If they ban 'bitcoin', or 'mathematic currencies' which I certaintly doubt they will do the latter... somebody will just try to find some way around it. They can just disguise the 'currency "data" ' are some other legitimate data, which would be infinitely easy to do.

Simply, they cannot ban the exchange of data, the exchange of information, without banning technology/society. Information exchange is the foundation to the corporate world. Unless they ban all trading of any information/product of any and all kind, then they will never stop a bitcoin like currency.
