Post by: Aussie bob on July 18, 2013, 06:55 am
  If you're paying the contact in cash, either face to face or bank over the counter, then you could send it straight to SR I suppose :-\ Definitely not if you're paying by online transfer or anything linking back to you.

   Seriously, though, why take the risk of being connected to bitcoins going to Silkroad. Cops find drugs in your mail you can deny any knowledge. They find evidence you've used Tor recently, even evidence of visiting SR, you can say you were curious about all the media fuss. They find drugs, tor/SR and can connect you with bitcoins being sent to an SR wallet it's looking like a more solid case.

   Transactions are recorded in the blockchain forever, tor wallets are easily identified as such. Just use and dispose of new online wallets every time. The fees are miniscule; 0.0005 of the total amount as standard. If you were transferring $40000 worth of btc you'd pay a $20 fee... Small price for completely closing down that avenue of investigation by law enforcement.

   Best wishes, AB.
Title: Re: Sending BTC from Localbitcoins directly to SR?
Post by: Railgun on July 18, 2013, 07:05 am
Bitcoin tumblers are so quick an efficient that there's no excuse in not using them.

Bitcoin fog is a must for any SR user IMO: http://fogcore5n3ov3tui.onion/

Free to register and use (small fee)
1) Deposit coins in fog from LocalBitCoins-- I normally split the deposites to look even messier on the blockchain.
2) Set up a withdraw.  Very easy. Copy paste the address.
3) Set timer
6 hours is default, but that CAN be traced if they want to go gun-ho. If you are a MAJOR vendor/buyer (e.g. buying Kilos and shit), I recommend using the max of 48 hours.

They will transfer your coins in and out of so many wallets, delete those wallets, etc that tracing will become almost impossible.



All your transactions are recorded on the blockchain, so if that address were to be ever able to be linked to SR, there'd be a trace of you using SR. Now because of the BTC tumbler in SR, they might have trouble finding out what you spent money on, but its best to not even have them know there's an SR link.
