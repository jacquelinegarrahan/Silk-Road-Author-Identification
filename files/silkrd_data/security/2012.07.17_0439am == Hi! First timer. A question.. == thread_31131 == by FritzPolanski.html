Basically the way this stuff works is that you will have two sets of keys - a private key and a public key.

As the name implies, a public key is a key which is advertised to the general public, because this is the key that other people will need to use to encrypt messages they want 0to send you. The private key is a key which you keep very close to your heart because it is the one you use to decrypt any messages addressed to you.

So say you want to send a message to your vendor. You write your message lock (encrypt) it with YOUR VENDOR's public key. Not your key. Your vendor's. Now once the message has been encrypt it, only the vendor can decrypt and read it. Not even you can decrypt it at this stage. The vendor will use his PRIVATE key to decrypt his message.

The reverse is true for someone sending a message to you - they encrypt it using your public key; you decrypt it using your private key. Simples.

Step-by-step instructions:
So now that all is said and done - what you need to do is go to the locksmith's and tell him you need a public and private key. He'll know what you mean, but keep it on the down low. Don't ask him in front of other people or he'll act like he doesn't know what you're talking about. Let everyone leave and then approach him quietly. If he asks you whether you want them done by hand or with a key machine - this is a trick question, lol. Everyone knows that encryption keys are digital.
