Post by: CrunchyFrog on November 15, 2011, 01:17 am
Quote from: dd60iluv
...I gonna probably use my actual living address and name because I can see no other alternative, how safe is that?...
In my view, quite safe as long as you're not asked to: 1) sign for delivery, and/or 2) take delivery at the post office desk.  Either of those things could indicate a controlled delivery is about to take place.

To me, it's all about minimizing potential suspicion.  And what's the most common thing that goes into your mailbox nearly every day without incident?  Stuff addressed to your real name or Resident, most likely.  (And parcels addressed to Resident or Occupant aren't at all common.)  The only downside I can think of to using your real name is that it *could* turn up in your seller's records if s/he hasn't practiced good data security and later runs afoul of the law.  That could be of consequence if you're on parole or probation.  (Although your real address will likely be in there, too.)  Also, a fake name could help if your seller is also an identity thief, or has their computer hacked by one.

Practically all the mail I receive that's sent to my address, but with a recipient of different surname, has a big (sometimes red) question mark written on the front.  Clearly, my particular letter carrier notices -- and cares about -- name differences.  (Yours may not.)   So, I have everything sent to my own name as that appears to arouse the least suspicion.  I can always claim ignorance should I be questioned about or asked to sign for a particular item: "Gee, I know I didn't order anything recently.  I'd better not sign or else I might get charged for whatever it is."

Still, I'd guess that roughly one-quarter of the people here use a fake name -- or that of a former occupant -- along with their real address.  Maybe one of them will let you know their reasoning on that.
