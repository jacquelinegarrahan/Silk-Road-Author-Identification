Post by: CoolGrey on October 18, 2012, 10:34 pm
I'm not going to share personal information, but I can tell you about the way I access the deep web:

I use Linux Ubuntu. It is the best OS in my opinion because it is open source, the developer cannot secretly sneak in any back doors to spy on me. With Windos and Mac, theoretically this is possible.

With Linux, the risk of a virus is way smaller. And it's compatible with many security software. GPG is installed by default on many Linux distributions, just like many hash function and shredding commands.

I store as little information as possible, but for the things I do I use encryption. My HDD has full disk encryption, and for USB sticks I use TrueCrypt.
