Post by: trigger on February 19, 2012, 12:36 pm
That seems like a reasonable way to buy BTC in Australia.  The exchange rate is a little steep, but on the flipside you just walk into your local NAB and hand over the cash, no need for ID.  If you use other options you might get a better exchange rate, but the larger exchanges usually demand that you provide identification, or that your money is deposited from a real bank account, etc.

It says on their website they won't charge you extra, I assume the fees are just absorbed into the extra 10% they're taking on the exchange, so you won't need to put in extra cash for that, but keep in mind that the value of bitcoins fluctuates (like any currency), so if you're looking at something today for 50 BTC, it might be more or less expensive when you're ready to buy.


As for your other questions, it's probably pretty important that you read up on these things before you start throwing your money around.  Best to have the security fundamentals in check so you know what the risks are and how you can best minimise them.

Good luck.
