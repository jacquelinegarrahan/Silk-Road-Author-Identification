I use VirWox time to time with a credit card through PayPal. For the first few transactions you will need to wait 48 hours for them to clear, but once you are verified you can purchase instantly.

Paypal through localbitcoins

Title: Re: How does VirWoX Work when buying with a credit card?
Post by: offbeatadam on August 22, 2013, 08:50 pm
I have used them with their other one. Sorry the name of the processor slips me, it starts with an S though. I refuse to give paypal any money, even a pittance in fees anymore after what they've done to some charities.

VirWoX is a little misleading, and I only used them a few times after figuring it out because bitinstant had gone down and I didn't have other avenues.

VirWoX is actually NOT a bitcoin exchange persay. You deposit USD through one of the processors, and then you must trade it for second life dollars. From there, you can then convert it again to bitcoin. You can then withdrawal it.

Unfortunately this means that you take double the hit. The markets fluxuate so quickly on both "dollars" that you very easily could end up with a double loss. In other words, you could deposit your money thinking you get x amount, but instead you take a hit when you convert to SLL because its value changed, and then you take a hit again because its value against bitcoin changed.

VirWoX is nice because of the fact they take credit cards. They are useful in that end. For a time, this was the only place I could use a credit card to get bitcoins, especially a credit card that I use with cash. At this point though, I've had to reexamine my methods and take an option that provides me a much greater rate of success, at the cost of a little identity discovery. I use BTCQuick now.

Title: Re: How does VirWoX Work when buying with a credit card?
Post by: offbeatadam on August 23, 2013, 03:34 pm
VirWoX verification period is a little interesting. The time periods are actually a little different than you might expect.

When you join, you have limits imposed. Those limits last for quite a while - you have a limit of how much you can do per month. I think it was $180, I could be wrong though. That is in USD, and they do keep track of conversion rates. No way around this one I'm afraid.

In addition to that, the first couple of times you do a BTC withdrawal, it has to be verified by a real human admin/moderator. The site states this process can take up to 48 hours. The first one I did, took 47 hours and 30 some odd minutes. The next one was a bit faster, but not much, it was closer to about 36 hours. I've not yet done one that didn't require human intervention.. by my third transaction I'd moved to BTC Quick.
