Title: Latest book by Bruce Schneier - Liars and Outliers
Post by: vlad1m1r on May 19, 2012, 01:31 pm
I have just started on Security Guru's Bruce Schneier's book on the subject of trust - both in social and technical terms, it's been a very interesting read so far so I thought I'd upload a copy to Torland for anyone who wants it, please find the link below.

There are some parts highly relevant to many of the issues we face here on SR to do with transparency and accountability and I'd particularly recommend this to new buyers and sellers on the Road.

Quote
We don’t demand a background check on the plumber who shows up to fix the leaky sink. We don’t do a chemical analysis on food we eat.

Trust and cooperation are the first problems we had to solve before we could become a social species. In the 21st century, they have become the most important problems we need to solve—again. Our global society has become so large and complex that our traditional trust mechanisms no longer work.

Bruce Schneier, world-renowned for his level-headed thinking on security and technology, tackles this complex subject head-on. Society can’t function without trust, and yet must function even when people are untrustworthy.

Source : https://www.schneier.com/book-lo.html

For anyone interested in computer security, Schneier's e-magazine Cryptogram which has been around since 1998 will give you all the latest in the world of e-security straight to your inbox. His website is also a great place to learn about the basics of encryption algorithims (which might have something to do with the fact he helped to build a good number of them!)

Happy reading!
