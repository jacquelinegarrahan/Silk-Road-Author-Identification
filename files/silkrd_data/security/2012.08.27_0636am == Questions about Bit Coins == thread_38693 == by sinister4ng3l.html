Title: Re: Questions about Bit Coins
Post by: elivance on August 27, 2012, 06:53 am
If you're in the States, you can try bitfloor.  You can take cash and deposit in their bank account at either Wells Fargo or Chase.  There is no charge to deposit money this way and you can purchase coins from them.  (Essentially a 0% way to get BTC.)

They will have a high-def video of you making the deposit at the bank but as long as you aren't a serious vendor (in which case you'd be more worried about how to move money the other way anyway) you are fine.  (Wear a flower shirt and real big sunglasses like I do.  Sure, it doesn't stop anyone from recogonizing you, but it is an excellent reason to talk to the cute girls behind the counter.)

My advice, purchase coins this way and pay the %1.5 - %3.0 for BTC laundry right after.  (e.g., TORWallet or blockchain.info.  To be clear, I don't trust either of those two but I have moved a few hundred through them with no problem at all.)
