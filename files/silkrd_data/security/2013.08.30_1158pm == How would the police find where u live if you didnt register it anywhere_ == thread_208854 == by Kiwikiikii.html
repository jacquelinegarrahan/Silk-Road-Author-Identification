Lots of clearnet articles recently about how LE can use however many cameras they want to track you.  Cop cars have cameras, lots of intersections have cameras, most stores have cameras, and they can all be checked and if you are seen on one or more of those cams LE can track your physical movement in vehicle or on foot or bike or rickshaw or whatever in the USA fully within the constitution according to supreme court.

Post by: johnwholesome on September 01, 2013, 03:38 am
If they are actually looking for you they'd more than likely ask your bank for help. The bank may have your old address on file, but the cops can ask the bank to flag your debit/credit card, sooner or later you will use it with a cop around the corner close enough to get a hold of you. They got quite creative with that shit.

If you use any sort of social media they can sift/analyze/correlate that shit too, quite efficiently so.

There's a myriad of ways to track someone these days, and it's only getting worse. Plenty of European metropolitan cities are experimenting with facial recognition systems on their vast CCTV networks, heck one of them is even attempting to "predict" criminal behavior (I think it was London), so just loitering next to a pricey car might prompt a closer look by an officer. I think the next logical step in that department is to get live streams of the on-board security cams inside public transportation vehicles.

All in all, times are getting rough for the wanted ones...
