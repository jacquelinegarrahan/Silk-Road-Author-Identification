Title: Re: Ukash To Bitcoins [UK]
Post by: zipp on December 08, 2012, 03:41 pm
If you want to exchange UKash to BTC, I recommend creating a Neteller account.  You can deposit UKash coupons into your Neteller account for a 3% fee.  You do not need to verify your account or anything, the daily deposit limit for an unverified account is still fairly high (around $250 I think).  Now you need to create an account on VirWox.  On VirWox, you can deposit from your Neteller account for 4.9% (no deposit limit).  Note that while VirWox allows you to directly deposit UKash into your VirWox account, this isn't recommended, as the fee is an outrageous 20%.  Doing it through Neteller first will result in a lower fee of 3 + 4.9 = 7.9% (approx.).  Once the cash is in your VirWox account (it can be USD/EUR/GBP) you actually have to convert it to SLL first, then convert the SLL to BTC (SLL is another sort of virtual currency, but it's not important).  This is done in a fairly similar manner as most exchanges, with market orders and limit orders available.  However, the fees start out high (2.9%).  I only use the UKash>Neteller>VirWox method if I need BTC fast, since this can all be done immediately once I have the UKash voucher.  I can get UKash vouchers at the Esso down the street, or I can even get them online paying through PayPal on websites like pcgamesupply and onecard.  Again, on top of all the other fees you have to pay, these websites take around a 10% commission themselves.  Expensive stuff.  If you have PayPal, you can deposit directly from PayPal into VirWox for a lower fee (3.4% vs 7.9%).  The only letdown is that the deposit limits start fairly low.  So if I need BTC immediately now (and since bitinstant online bank transfers are down) the first option is PayPal through VirWox, but if the deposit limit is reached, the next option is UKash through Neteller through VirWox, in which there is no deposit limit.  If I had time, I would use a website that allows direct cash deposits into their bank account to avoid fees.  These websites, however, usually only accept cash from the one country that their website is based in, so you'd have to find one for your particular country.
