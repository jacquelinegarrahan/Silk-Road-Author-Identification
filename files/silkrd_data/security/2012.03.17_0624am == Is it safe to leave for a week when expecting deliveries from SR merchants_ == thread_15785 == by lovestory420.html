Title: Re: Is it safe to leave for a week when expecting deliveries from SR merchants?
Post by: coptcha on March 21, 2012, 05:56 pm
Well, sort of?

Canceling/forwarding would probably attract more attention than just letting it pile up.

On the other hand, the longer anything sits in one place, the more the odds of something happening to it go up.

Sometimes it can't be avoided (family emergency, mis-timed delay at customs, etc), but if it does I would err on the side of either letting it sit in the mailbox or having a very very close friend pick it up for you (and you better tell them what they are picking up, so they can decide for themselves if they want to take the risk).

Judgement call though. If your mail goes in a slot in your door and then sits in your living room until you get home, you are pretty darn safe from anyone noticing. If it sits on your front porch and your neighbor is a cop who lets his drug sniffing dog run out to take a dump on your lawn, it's a very unsafe situation. If your mailbox tends to rip letters when three or four days' worth of mail is crammed in after it, it's unsafe. If your mailbox just drops new mail on top of old mail and nothing gets mangled/torn open, it's pretty safe. If you live with your parents and they tend to open your mail, it's a really unsafe situation. So on and so on and so on.
