You can still use them on SR, it just would be a very good idea to mix/tumble the coins first.  blockchain.info has a wallet with an anonymous send option, it costs 1.5%, this will send "different" coins essentially masking your identity from the ones you purchased to the ones the blockchain sends (for the most part).  So send your coins to Blockchain.info wallet, then anonymous send to your SR wallet.

bitcoinfog is another mixing service, it costs 1-3% (random fee) and based on their FAQ is a very good mixer but can take longer.  When you send they ask how long to spread it out, they send random BTC amounts during that time so LE can't just search 10 BTC sent on this day to this account etc.   Personally I think this is a bit overkill, learn to use blockchain.info to search your BTC transfers and check taint (Search for from address).  Do a mock send, aka send to another wallet like instawallet to test your process and chain taint.

The cash to BTC services are good because your identity is hidden 100% (cash) and it is fast, getting coins directly to your SR wallet in hours (some people still mix but many do not).  Though this costs more, usually ~5%.  Where as using a more official method like mtgox and coinbase takes longer but is cheaper (up to 1% using coinbase) AND I would say REQUIRES mixing.
