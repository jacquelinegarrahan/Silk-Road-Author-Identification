Anyone can report inaccurate whois information to their domain registrar. The domain registrar is then supposed to contact the domain registrant and have them confirm their identities and update their contact information. If the domain registrant doesn't do this within a certain amount of time (usually about 15 days), the domain registrar is then supposed to lock their domain, making their website inaccessible.

Since they're registered with Melbourne IT, you can send an email complaining about inaccurate whois info to: abuse@melbourneit.com.au (source: http://www.melbourneit.com.au/contacts/index/).

Be aware that they may require personal info about yourself (name, email address & phone number) to actually do anything about your inaccurate whois complaint.

So, if you're willing to share that information (should be fine as they have no way to trace you back to this forum), you could potentially get this domain taken down, or at least investigated and on their registrar's radar.
