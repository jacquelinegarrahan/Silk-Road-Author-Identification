Title: Re: newbie here question about GPG
Post by: phubaiblues on October 28, 2011, 03:22 am
If the vendor provides a public key, I use it, especially for address, and I always include my public key for the vendor, giving him the option, if he wishes, to encrypt communication.  I think it's good to learn to use it...I'm not totally sure it's 'vital' at this stage of the game, but it can't hurt, and who knows what the future holds.

If you are using windows, this is probably easiest setup: http://www.gpg4win.org/ but there are good threads/stickys showing the various ways of doing this.  I'd download and try again, make sure you have a private and public key, and just practice with somebody on here, a time or two, to get the hang of it...I found the documentation with gpg4win to be excellent...just needed to take the time to get it figured out...then I'd save my keys somewhere, either online, swissdisk or wuala, and, again, practice with somebody...

We never know for *sure* what we need, as this is all so new...we just protect as best we can, and hope it's enough...Like I tell all new people, best thing is go to 'Security' forums, and start from when this forum was started, back in june, I think...and there are plenty of good threads showing how to do this...takes a bit of determination, but well worth it...once you get the 'aha' moment and 'get' how pgp works, it's kind of cool...
