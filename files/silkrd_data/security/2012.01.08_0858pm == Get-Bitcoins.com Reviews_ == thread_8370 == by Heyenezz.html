Well.  It sounds as if you've discovered a downside to having BTC sent from the vendor to someplace *other than* your own local wallet first.  You are now the middle party in a three-way conversation; in between Get-Bitcoins and Silk Road.  Needless to say, one of the other parties (GB) needs to be kept ignorant of the identity of the third (SR).  Plus, you're unable to tell SR what sending address to look for unless you ask GB for it.

Quote from: fgthn1
What do I need to download? I've downloaded bitcoins software and it's synchronizing to network for about 40 minutes already(downloading blocks of transaction history). What else do I have to do before I get that damn bitcoins?...
1) Your local BTC client will take about six hours to pull down the full blockchain -- starting from scratch -- based on what I've observed a couple of months ago.
2) Having an up-to-date *local* copy of the blockchain isn't going to help you in this particular instance, but may be useful to you in the future (so let it finish the download).
3) Even with a fully current copy of the blockchain, the BTC you ordered will never show up in your *local* wallet because you had them sent someplace else (SR).  It's SR that needs the current blockchain in this instance, which I'm sure they already have.
4) Probably the first thing to do is wait for a reasonable time (24 hours?) to see if the BTC are credited to your SR account.
5) If not, you'll likely want to ask GB for the sending address that they used and pass that information along to SR in a PM explaining what's going on.
6) In the unlikely event that GB inquires, you could tell them that the receiving address was that of an online wallet site that's now helping you track down your coins.

Alright, well essentially it's basically the same. Now, when you submit the order form online you will receive a confirmation email as normal, however, no you also need to wait for the email from trustcash. SAVE YOUR GET-BITCOIN TRANSACTION NUMBER. Once you've received that email, go to your nearest BoA (not the one they tell you to go to) and deposit into the account, making sure to note the different account number and the state the account was opened in. The amount you deposit will be slightly different, ie, if you want to deposit 200 USD you'll deposit 201.18. This is so they can ID your transaction faster and get your money quicker. Once you deposit, you'll receive an email from coinapult, trustcash, as well as get-bitcoin (I think). From there, you just go to the coinapult link they email you, and put in the BTC address you want the coins to go to, and the last 4 digits of the transaction number get-bitcoin gave you.
