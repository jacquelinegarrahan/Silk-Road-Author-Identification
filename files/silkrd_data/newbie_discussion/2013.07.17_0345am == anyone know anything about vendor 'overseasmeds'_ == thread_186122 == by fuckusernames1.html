Post by: magenta296wok on July 20, 2013, 11:50 pm
I ordered a 3 x 30mg sample of were called "Oxycodone Copies." I assumed this meant they would be pills that resembled a generic 30mg oxycodone pill, and contained oxycodone. The next day the listing was changed to "Oxycodone Copies/Counterfeits/Fakes" and a disclaimer on the page (not there when I made my order) said the pills would not contain any actual oxycodone, but rather "the strongest pain medication overseasmeds can get there hands on." All of which is still up on the product page.

I messaged him with my concern about what this "medication" was, and that I felt I had been mislead by his wording. After days of unprofessional messages, containing an incredible amount of spelling and grammatical errors and nonsensical rambling, he finally answered my question. According to overseasmeds, the "Oxycodone Copies" contain 15mg of Codeine and 15 mg of Tramadol. The order arrived, and to the vendor's credit the pills look nearly identical to an actual 30mg Oxy. Visually, they would pass to most people on the street as a pill containing 30mg of Oxycodone. You can look it up on something as simple as Google based on shape, color, imprints, and scoring. It will match. Chemically speaking, I think calling it a "copy" is a joke and incredibly misleading. The three chemicals mentioned are similar in that they are all pain killers, but the potency of one of these "copies" is not even remotely close to the real thing, as Oxycodone is roughly 20 times more potent than Codeine, mg for mg. Tramadol is nearly negligible to almost any opiate user, certainly when doses are generally no less than 50mg for mild-moderate pain. At the dosages overseasmeds gave to me, you would have ZERO doubt you were scammed no more than half an hour after ingesting the pill.

For the record, I did finalize my order. I was sick of trudging through his messages to find any real information, and due to the codeine content and appearance, decided the pills could be called "low quality counterfeits." I also felt the cost was worth the opportunity to reveal the pills' contents on the product page.

These pills are being sold (most likely) so that dealers on the street can rip people off by selling these Oxy look-a-likes as the real thing for a huge profit. I do NOT agree with this practice whatsoever (why would you want to buy a pill that looked like the real thing but had no actual effect? Or are you greedy enough to sell snake oil?). TSR is a truly free market; therefore, overseasmeds should be allowed to sell his/her/their wares. The market, through reviews like this, will decide his fate.

UNLESS YOU WANT BUNK PILLS, DO NOT BUY FROM OVERSEASMEDS
