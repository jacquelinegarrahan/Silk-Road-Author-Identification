Title: Re: Bad time for SR?
Post by: thagoat on February 13, 2013, 06:24 pm
So much noob in the OP...

First of all, there are plenty of legit vendors who are only here to do honest deals. Like other people have said, read through the Rumor Mill extensively.

Also, if you plan on making your first purchase a large bulk deal, you'll be the one looking "sketch". Use common sense and place a few small orders first. You need to build a strong buying history.

Most importantly, when you make your first purchases DON'T EVER FINALIZE EARLY (FE). 

Find a vendor you want to make your first purchase from and read their ENTIRE vendor page. Do you see a section saying that they require FE? If you do, forget about that vendor for the time being. There are so many good vendors out there that you have reason to FE starting out. But what if you really like this vendor's product/prices/username? Too fucking bad. If you think it's acceptable to FE on your first order, be prepared to never see your drugs or BTC again. Just send the coin to me and save yourself the time and hassle.

Moving on, now that you've found a vendor who lists a smallish amount without requiring FE, you'll need to send the vendor a message. Just A MESSAGE, don't spam them with silly shit. You will be ignored. All you've gotta say is something like this: "Hi (vendors name here), I'm a new customer just starting out and I have no buying history. I like how your listing looks and I'd like to make my first purchase with you. Since I'm just starting out, I want to stay in escrow only. I don't want to FE. Please let me know if we can do business. Thanks".  If they say no, move on to another vendor. If they say yes, congratulations.

This is all you have to do.  It really is that simple.  When messaging a vendor, keep it short and sweet. Type your shit out like you passed high school English.  You'll be amazed at how much further you get by sounding polite and educated.

Above all, don't be a fucking retard.

Post by: thagoat on February 15, 2013, 03:06 pm
For people living and ordering outside of the US, I can understand why somebody would consider FE'ing. There just aren't a lot of vendors for certain products in certain countries. I guess I should've mentioned that my previous post mainly applies to Americans; it shouldn't have been such a blanket statement.

That being said, I'm still vehemently against FE'ing. When you do a drug deal in real life and you're unfamiliar with the dealer, you wouldn't hand over your money without seeing the drugs first, right? When you FE, you're just handing over your money to the vendor and hoping that you get some drugs for it. The SilkRoad is a wonderful marketplace that provides the buyer with multiple levels of protection through feedback, the forums, and most importantly, the escrow system. This escrow system is what sets SR apart from shady dealers on the street corner. But when you FE, you're basically telling SR to fuck off and that you don't need them to look out for you. This is a choice that I would just never make.

Regardless, choosing to FE is a personal decision based on personal circumstances. Just understand  that under any circumstances, you will be fucked 99% of the time when something goes wrong and you've already FE'ed. There are multiple ways a vendor can make it look like your order was sent after you've FE'ed.

If you FE, make sure you can afford to lose all of that money. Think long and hard.
