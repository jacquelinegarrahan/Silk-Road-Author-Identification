Post by: sleepyeyes2k2 on February 09, 2013, 05:29 am
Once it converts from USD to Bitcoins, it becomes anonymous.  There is no mechanism for seeing what entity did what with who's address.  Even if there were, SR routes payments through so many different addresses that it becomes impossible to determines the end point, not entirely unlike how the Tor network itself works.  It's why Bitcoin is complained about sometimes by senators as a means to launder money.  It's quite safe.  When I do the cash transfer at Wal-Mart, they just think I'm paying a bill.  So does Moneygram, who is actually handling the transfer.  Bitinstant knows it's going to a bitcoin address, but after that - poof! - it's gone.
