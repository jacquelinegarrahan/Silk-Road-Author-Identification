Hi - vendor speaking here, so take what you read with that in mind.

I believe "quality" issues can not be raised if purely subjective (you don't like it)...but if you are credible and can prove through test etc then have a discussion. I believe SR generally does not consider "quality" a reasonable grounds for rebate but if your delivery is absolute shocking bad - or perhaps not even what you requested, then I personally feel you have grounds for complaint.

If you have FE'd then DON'T...!!!! I'm a pretty new vendor, though have settled near to 200 transactions now in less than two month by being decent with people and supplying good gear...it's quite simple! FE is not needed if the quantity small, and the vendor confident of the sale. I personally never ask for FE.

Any vendor worth his salt should deal professionally with any issue arising and discuss openly. The resolution should be amicable.

It boils down to some perhaps not being as straight as you'd hope and I'm sorry you've experienced that.

Good luck in the future - there are good vendors out there...

Cheers

Title: Re: Confronting reputable vendors concerning quality of products
Post by: frank-butcher24 on July 01, 2013, 07:00 pm
Leaving negative feedback without first contacting the vendor is pretty poor etiquette in my book. I've never received bad product on this site, but I think my plan would be not to accuse anyone of anything because at the end of the day there's not a lot you can do about it.

I would:

Contact vendor telling him I think there may be something wrong with this batch, can he confirm I got the same as everyone else, and if it's a new batch is there a chance of a partial refund.

Then wait to see how it gets resolved. If you agree some resolution then leave 5/5 with a comment that the product didn't live up to your expectations but seller resolved amicably.

If he's an arse about it, tell him you intend to leave honest feedback about your disappointment. Then leave feedback accordingly, and note the good points if any too e.g., good stealth, speedy shipping etc. And leave a short, polite but to the point post in his feedback thread on here.

Then go elsewhere in future, and if you're at all bitter about the situation, keep reminding yourself how you're far more likely to be ripped off on the street than on here.
