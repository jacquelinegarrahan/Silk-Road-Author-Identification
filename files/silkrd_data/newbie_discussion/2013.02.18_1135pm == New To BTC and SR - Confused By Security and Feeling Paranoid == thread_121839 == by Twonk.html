Title: Re: New To BTC and SR - Confused By Security and Feeling Paranoid
Post by: onetwothree on February 20, 2013, 04:16 am
I disagree with what has been said so far... from what you've described, your transaction history should be relatively easily traced. The only layer in the entire process severing you from your real identity (your bank account no less) is SR's tumbler. As far as I know, SR's tumbling methods/sourcecode is not public, so you just have to trust that it works well.

Having said that, one small purchase is not likely to draw any attention and you're almost assuredly safe. However, I personally would improve your anonymizing efforts in the future. You said your wallet is with blockchain; they offer a great anonymizing service for what I think is a reasonable fee of 1.5%. You can receive anonymously at 1.5% and send anonymously at 1.5% and then it goes to SR's tumbler, so you're getting 3 anonymizing services for 3%. You'll have to decide if that's something you want to pay, but if you're using your bank account directly, I think it would be wise.

For your current blockchain wallet, if you want to be as safe as possible, I would never use it again (assuming it's at 0.0 balance). Just generate a new one which has no association to any account or any other wallet, and make your transactions wisely, even if you don't use the anonymizer.

For what you've done so far, you're all good. Welcome to SR. Stay safe.
