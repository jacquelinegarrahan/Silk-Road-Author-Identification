Post by: Vinospear on May 16, 2013, 04:00 am
I've used Coinbase, which is low-cost and viable for US residents - you just can't be in a hurry.

1. You need a real bank account, which they take 2-3 business days to verify.
2. After verification, there is a 10 BTC limit for daily purchases and 50 BTC for sales - this goes up to 50 BTC per day for purchases after 30 days following your first transaction.
3. When you buy BTC, it will take 3-4 business days for them to show up in your account - generally, the price is whatever MtGox is at the moment you buy. If they got too many orders, they will tell you you can still buy, but it will be at the price when your transaction completes vs. now. You can then choose to cancel at any time before completion.
4. On heavy trading days, they will run out of BTC and tell you to come back later (rolling 24-hour window). This is less frequent now that things have settled down a bit. 
4. Once BTC hit your account, transfer out to any address is immediate (plus time for receiver to get the required number of confirmations).
5. Cashing out BTC also takes a few days as well - funds then go into the same bank account. 

So if you don't mind waiting a while for your BTC, I found it easy and painless to use. Keep in mind that the powers-that-be will definitely have a record that you bought bitcoins, and I would imagine they also retain a record of the addresses you sent BTC from when you go to cash out.

Personally, I think FinCen and other authorities will keep cracking down on anonymous BTC purchases since that's where most of the high-value targets are for LE. Most people up to really bad shit will not have their name on anything. But as long as you are careful to mix/shuffle/tumble/etc between Coinbase and your ultimate use of BTC, I think it's less risky than most of the other options for BTC/fiat transactions out there. Coinbase also recently received $5M USD in a venture capital round, so they'll be around for at least...a month :)
