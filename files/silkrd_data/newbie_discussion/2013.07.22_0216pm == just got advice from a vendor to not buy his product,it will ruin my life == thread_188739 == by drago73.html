Yes you can still get a buzz from methadone after doing H, and vice versa.

Methadone only 'blocks' you from getting a buzz if you are doing so much of it that your brain's opiate receptors are used to being jam packed full of opiates from taking methadone all the time.  Methadone lasts a really long time, and if you did it every day your opiate receptors would be pretty full and other opiates wouldn't have as much effect because you would be used to having that methadone in your system, so you would need to do much more H to get past the methadone.

Post by: AirshipAdmiral on July 22, 2013, 02:56 pm
To limit or reverse opiate or opioid tolerance, keeping naltraxone, bupropion and methadone handy is something I'd highly suggest.

Methadone is commonly used to avoid severe opioid withdrawal symptoms by replacing something like herion.

It's used to "step down" serum levels of opiates so that your u-opioid neural receptors have a chance to get used to not being glutted with heroin or whatever. u-opioid receptors do eventually bounce back from a heavy tolerance, but you have to give them a chance to do so. Methadone provides a temporary opioid to gradually reduce in dosage until you return to having a low tolerance to opiates.

Naltraxone is a u-opioid antagonist, also known as an opioid blocker. It stuffs u-opioid neural receptors without actually activating it, taking up the space opiates usually key into to activate opioid neurons which provide the orgasmic pleasure and pain eradication effects associated with drugs like heroin and other opiates.

Bupropion is a dopaminergic antidepressant that is also commonly used to help cigarette addicts to stop having uncontrollable cravings for nicotine and other addictive substances.

Bupropion, by activating the same dopamine pleasure circuits at a lower level  stimulation to reduce the utter lack of stimulation that causes intense cravings.

Combining methadone, naltraxone and buproprion can help to give your body a chance to recover a low tolerance so you get more out of the same amount of herion, saving money in the long run.

With smart usage of methadone, naltraxone and bupropion, it is possible, at least in theory, to have the same level of orgasmic transcendental experience as your very first time using heroin, "catching up with the dragon" so to speak.
