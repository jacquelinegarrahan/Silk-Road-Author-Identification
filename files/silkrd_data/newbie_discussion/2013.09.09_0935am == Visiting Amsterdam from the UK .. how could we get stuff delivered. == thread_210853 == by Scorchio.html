Title: Re: Visiting Amsterdam from the UK .. how could we get stuff delivered.
Post by: The Bubble Dreamer on September 10, 2013, 08:15 am
I was playing with these ideas, not sure how feasible they are or if vendors would go along with it but:

- Ask the vendor to put the package in a locker in one of the major train stations. These lockers use a PIN code. The vendor leaves the package, sends you a message with the location of the locker and the pin code, and you pick it up and finalize. The problem is that this implies a risk for the vendor, as you could be LE. But if your buyer history is good and since he can choose time and place and there is no way LE is going to watch all lockers in the major train stations 24-7, maybe he will accept the risk.

- Use the lost-and-found service of the dutch railways. According to their website, lost-and-found objects will remain for 5 days at the railway station where they were handed in. There you can pick it up without any fee. (After that they will take it to a central depot, and the procedure becomes a bit more difficult). So, the vendor hands over a bag at one of the railway stations in Amsterdam (he can just buy a very cheap bag somewhere) with some random stuff in it, including your stealthy package). A couple of days later, you go there and ask for it (you will have to give an accurate description of the bag and it's contents). Just don't say: "it's the bag that contains coke" ;-)

I guess it all depends on how worthwhile this is for the vendor. Offer him a reasonable price for the effort.
