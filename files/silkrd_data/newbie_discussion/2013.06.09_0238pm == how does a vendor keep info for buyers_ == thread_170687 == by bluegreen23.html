Title: how does a vendor keep info for buyers?
Post by: bluegreen23 on June 09, 2013, 02:38 pm
I don't mean addresses.  100% unequivocally NOT ADDRESSES

By this I mean, as a vendor I would prefer to keep tracking #s to myself. I feel that it is a tool that is used as proof of mailing, proof of receiving and possible looking into delays IF AND WHEN there are delays.
I am not fond of the idea of simply handing it over without any need. I feel that a person who is tweaking might decide he/she needs to check every 5 minutes. This was brought up in another thread and it seemed the consensus was that providing a tracking # was not required, and could be giving if the vendor wished but also that the vendor better have one to avoid any "it didn't arrive" BS.

So you send it off, you have your tracking #. all is well in the world. day 3 arrives and the package hasn't. but it should have. the buyer gets antsy and starts msging. You reply as soon as possible to assure the buyer you're here and available and want to make everything right in this persons world. you will be in touch shortly yadda yadda yadda. So... you think, "well maybe it's a slight delay, maybe it's lost, maybe it's been delivered, maybe a lot of things. time to find out by punching in the good old tracking #" oh but wait you pump out 10-50 orders a day and with not keeping any records you're really not sure which order belongs to this buyer.

so how do you solve this problem?
