That would be a very preferable option. I'll run through the preferred platform and utilities.

You have the right idea going for netbooks. Easy to conceal, transport, and (if it comes to it) destroy. Most are powerful enough to run all the software required for a TOR server (I use a cheap laptops for my own anonymous TOR nodes and server).
You can also use a tablet type, but these are more expensive.

So many netbooks though.

Gerneally, you want one that is running at least a Intel Atom Processor. Keep it within laptops from 2010 and later. At least 2Gb RAM.

One of the most important thing is to get a SSD hard drive, or solid state. You'll see why in a second, but they have a much faster read/write and can be formatted instantly. They are expensive compared to disk Hard Drives, but only in terms of cost/stoarge. So, for $40 extra bucks at Newegg.com you can get a 12-20gb SSD drive.

Now, you have a fast laptop that's small. Now what.

Get truecrypt. Once you have your OS installed (I'm guessing Windows) install truecrypt. Then encrypt the OS partition and create a hidden partition. If you are ever forced to reveal your account, you can type a secondary password to the fake partition. Probably put some embarrassing pornography or furry stuff. Not implying anything, that's just what you would expect to find on this level of encryption. That and TOR.

I prefer AES256 + Blowfish encryption.

Then you can simply use windows like normal. Shut down, never hibernate. Don't worry about shutting the power off instantly. SSD drives can't get damaged like that.

By the way, other than the laptop is all free except the OS.
For that just torrent Windows XP or Windows 2000 (two very secure versions). 2000 will have the benefit of being clean, some copies of XP can have Trojans.
