Title: Re: Scales, scales and scales
Post by: turtle134298 on August 01, 2013, 04:22 am
Hello all.  The problem with all the scales mentioned here, except one, is that they utilize strain gauge technology.  What's wrong with strain gauge technology?  Nothing, as long as you are loading your weight quickly and not gradually.  However, they can lack accuracy and more often have problems with precision and sensitivity.  Precision is how consistently the scale reads the same result for the same weight.  For example, I was weighing "something" today and on my strain gauge scale I got 3 different readings, 0.4g, 0.5g, and 0.6g.  Not very precise eh?  So what do you do if your going to take a dose of something powerful, what is the correct weight???  Also strain gauge scales are better at measuring large (grams and up) amounts of weight and due poorly measuring lesser amounts (e.g. milligrams).

At this time, I would like to congratulate the member who named Mettler-Toledo balances.  These high quality advance balances solve many of the shortcomings of strain gauge technology by employing magnetic force restoration technology.  Other quality brands utilizing magnetic force restoration technology include: some Sartorius balances (the ones using magnetic force restoration), Mettler-Toledo, Ohaus, Cahn, Denver instruments, A & D, or Shimadzu.  Here's the problem though.  Almost all of these balances cost a thousand dollars of more.  Your best bet, which has worked for me, is to find a used one, albeit a used one that works and functions properly.  There are a few sites on the web you can check into and ebay frequently has quite a few, just be careful of scammers and check the condition of the scale thoroughly after unpacking it from being shipped to make sure it works.  For much more information and guidance on balances see Stimulant's post at www.drug-forum.com/forum/showthread.php?t=93149.  Have a good one, and get that shit weighed right.
