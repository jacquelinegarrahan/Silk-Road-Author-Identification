If you're trying to sleep after being up all day, one thing that works well for me is melatonin.  It's a hormone already naturally in your brain. It's the brain's "time to go to sleep" signal, it regulates your circadian rhythm.

You can buy it at just about any health store, drug store, or grocery store in the health supplement section. It's pretty safe to take, as in not a toxic chemical (the ld50 is ridiculously high, 3250 mg/kg in rats, which is up there with table salt).

I take it to help me flip my schedule back and forth between awake all night at work and awake during the day on my non working days when I'm home with my family
