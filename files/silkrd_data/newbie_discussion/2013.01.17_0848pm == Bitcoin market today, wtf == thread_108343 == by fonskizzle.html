The FX(currency) markets and BTC/emoney markets have almost nothing in common. There are two main factors that influence exchange rates in the real world. The first is interest rates. If interest rates move up 1 percent in UK then it is reasonable to assume that the USD/GPB rate will move up 1%, likely more but for this discussion we'll leave it at that. The reason being an increased return. If you can move your $100mil out of your US treasuries and into UK treasuries that are now yielding 1% more than you were getting in the states you will make the move. The second is future expectations or what traders believe governments and economies will do over the net 3, 6, 12, 24 months.

So in effect there is zero reason to believe that there is a connect between the internet market and FX market. The only slight influences will be exchanges that offer USD/GBP, USD/EUR, EUR/GBP as well as currency to BTC. In my opinion as a trained economist and trader, this is a market that can be cornered. With lack of supply for inflating demand anyone with enough money in their pocket can essentially drive the BTC price through the roof by only purchasing. Their only risk would be a relaxation of mining rules and therefore an influx of new currency. In my mind it really wouldn't be worth the time as the transaction costs for BTC are exorbitant. But then again in the long run if the currency mint isn't ramped up you would be sitting on a pile of money. Unlikely though, fiat currency can be created by anyone and the BTC network is a brilliantly simple yet effective one that can be recreated.
