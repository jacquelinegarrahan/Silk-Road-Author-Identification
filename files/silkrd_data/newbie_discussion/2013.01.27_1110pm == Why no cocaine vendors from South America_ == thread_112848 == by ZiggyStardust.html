Title: Re: Why no cocaine vendors from South America?
Post by: bokchoy on January 28, 2013, 06:34 am
Most South American countries are known suppliers of cocaine, therefore when a package comes from places like Columbia customs will already assume there might be cocaine in there and look a little harder for it. It's not very likely your cocaine will show up if ordered from a known cocaine producing country.

Besides, as soon as cocaine hits US soil its value goes up tenfold (or something like that, maybe more I don't know), why would they sell it in a place where it's worth less? For the most part, prices on Silk Road reflect the country that the drug comes from, not where it's going.
