Post by: sinister-breaks on August 22, 2013, 08:38 am
Here is a pretty simple method:

Goto: https://www.igolder.com/pgp/encryption/

Top box add the Vendor PGP (Include the -----BEGIN PGP PUBLIC KEY BLOCK----- & -----END PGP PUBLIC KEY BLOCK-----

Middle Box add your postal address

Click Encypt and it spits out the PGP you need to send to the vendor.

Some will argue this is not the safest method (being a web based service), but its the easiest way I know to generate a PGP key with little knowledge and will get the ball rolling for you
