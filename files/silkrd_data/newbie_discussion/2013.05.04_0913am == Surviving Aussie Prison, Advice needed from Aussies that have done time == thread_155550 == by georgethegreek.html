Title: Re: Surviving Aussie Prison, Advice needed from Aussies that have done time
Post by: Barack on May 04, 2013, 10:02 am
Don't accept anything from anyone. Be weary of anyone being nice to you, especially if they are offering you something. You will have to pay it back with a high tax or pay it back with some type of favor that you do not want to do, possibly sexual favors. Gambling often leads to trouble. Try hard to blend in with everyone and don't stand out. The guards will mess with you if you stand out. The nail that sticks out gets hammered down. It's better to get your ass beat than to pussy out from a fight. If you pussy out then you will be known as a bitch to the whole compound. Word spreads fast. If you are a bitch or you let someone take advantage of you, everyone will do the same thing and take advantage of you. All kindness will be taken as weakness. If you share something with someone, they will be back for more constantly. That includes food and non-food items. Don't make eye contact with other inmates unless you are in a conversation with them. That is a very easy way to get in a fight. You will hear lies all day, every day. Every inmate thinks they are right and will argue with you even though they are wrong. I can't think of anything else.

Post by: narcocapitalist9 on May 05, 2013, 02:23 pm
I can't really offer any advice about actual prison because I've never been done that way. I got raided due to a couple of people I knew informing on me. One was this guy who was a former major mdma cook in brisbane and the other was this crazy russian woman who I got far too infatuated with. They showed up expecting to find meth and weed in reasonable quantities (maybe an 8ball and an ounce) as the second informer, the crazy bitch, was told by me of what I intended though she swears black and blue that she told nobody. I don't believe her. I would have saved myself a lot of trouble forgetting about her but you know how it is when you get a thing for someone.

Anyway, they raided me, and I didn't actually have any drugs, but I had a bunch of poor-man's labware, a 12 pack of demazin tablets, about a gram of red phos and a little hand pipe I'd forgotten about. I coughed the stuff straight up for them, and they made a big song and dance about how 'dangerous' all that labware and tiny amounts of a common chemical found on products in most households was. ZOMG TURPS ZOMG SHELLITE ZOMG HYDROCHLORIC ACID. oh yeah, cunts stole my computer, that I'd literally just the day before got a pair of 320gb HDDs for (so they were still unformatted) and a nice set of triple-beam 20mg accurate scales. anyway, I said in court that I was cooking it up for myself and that I had ADD and a sleep disorder, I showed them the diagnostics for both, fully legit, and I got off with 18 months probation.

It still totally sucked ass. I spent far too much of that 18 months drinking like a fish and too nervous to even buy a stick off a long time trusted friend.

I absolutely hate how vicious and callous detectives are. You really are better off being very careful about what you admit to. I only rolled over so extensively because I was staying with my mother at the time and I didn't want them busting holes in walls and that sort of nonsense, or turning the place upside down. If I'd been living by myself I'd have just sat back in the couch and said 'well you're gonna do it anyway, i don't see why i should help you'. they say that they will be 'nicer' to you if you cough up, but that's bullshit, and only applies a little bit when you are a first time 'offender'.

Anyway, I hope things go ok for you. I think that all things considered unless you have priors 12 months jail is unlikely, especially if you are younger. I would think that the sentence will be more like 6 months with a year suspended or maybe even just a 12 month suspended or 18 month probation. I was in queensland at the time, and those pigs are extra vicious, so if I was able to get off that lightly on a petty manufacturing charge, I'd say for a petty dealing charge you should be able to do about the same, if you are smart and don't believe a word they say to you either in the interview room or in the courtroom. good luck!
