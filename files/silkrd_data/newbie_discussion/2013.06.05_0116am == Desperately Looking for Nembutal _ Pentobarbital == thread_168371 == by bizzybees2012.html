If it's cyanide you guys are after, it's actually ridiculously easy to make. The only equipment you need is a vacuum flask and some tubing (I made it a few times back when I was studying chemistry).

Get ahold of some Potassium Ferricyanide. This can be purchased on Amazon, or from photography suppliers (it's a nontoxic dye, and dirt cheap).

React it with phosphoric acid over heat, and use the stoichiometric equivalents shown in the equation below:

K3[Fe(CN)6] + 2 H3PO4 = 6 HCN + FePO4 + K3PO4

Bubble the HCN into a solution of KOH or NaOH. You'll be left with a solution of NaCN/KCN and some leftover hydroxide. You can separate them with methanol (Cyanides are insoluble in it, but hydroxides are).

Have fun.
