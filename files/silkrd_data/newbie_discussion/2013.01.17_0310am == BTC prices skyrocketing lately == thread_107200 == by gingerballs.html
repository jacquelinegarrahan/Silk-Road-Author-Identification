Quote from: beadvised on January 25, 2013, 03:07 am
... I'd be willing to bet on this, I just don't know enough about the supply of btc to feel like I should be investing in them.

Okay. To start, the current total number of Bitcoins in existence (as of this writing) is 10,698,425. Because of the architecture of the Bitcoin currency, there can only ever be 21 million Bitcoins in existence. So we're about halfway there, but not in terms of the amount of time it takes which I'll get to in a second. Enthusiasts have calculated that all of the Bitcoins will have been generated at or near the year 2140.

This link is a graph of the total number of Bitcoins (in millions) generated over time by year.

https://en.bitcoin.it/w/images/en/thumb/e/e3/Total_bitcoins_over_time_graph.png/740px-Total_bitcoins_over_time_graph.png

So you can see that in 2010 there were around 3 million. You can see a pretty neat parabola. The infrastructure was built to mimic the mining of actual precious minerals, which because of their scarcity, maintain their value well. The system basically generated a lot of Bitcoins at first, and then less as time goes on. So if you're a Bitcoin miner, you're mining less Bitcoins per kWh (of electricity spent on running a mining rig) in January 2013 than you were in 2010, and you'll be making less next year as well. Thus the invention of ASICs, the industry name for specialized chips designed for Bitcoin mining, which you can read about here:

http://www.technologyreview.com/news/508061/custom-chips-could-be-the-shovels-in-a-bitcoin-gold-rush/

Quote from: #slothrop on January 25, 2013, 03:45 am
I'm no expert but BTC seem to be to be especially volatile. Something I'd stay away from even if I wanted to invest.

While you're correct in that BTC is "especially volatile" for a currency, it's not a bad investment by any means. With the amount of Bitcoin being spent or otherwise transacted every year, all of the existing businesses that are beginning to accept Bitcoin as a form of payment such as Wordpress, and especially with the black market appeal of SR, I don't think it's likely to see very many more "big" crashes like in 2011 when it went from $30 to $2. It's pretty widely understood in the Bitcoin community that as they are accepted by more businesses, their value will stabilize. Check out this link for more on that:

https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/FAQ#Where_does_the_value_of_Bitcoin_stem_from.3F_What_backs_up_Bitcoin.3F

Quote from: allhercontacts on January 25, 2013, 06:03 am
... but reading all that stuff about bitcoins really helps me understand why it's thrived when other e-currencies haven't

I think the main reason why Bitcoin has thrived is because it's based on public key encryption, the same type of encryption we use to hide our address when we send it to vendors. The public keys are published in the blockchain which is public. But the private key, which is what gives you ownership of the coins and allows you to sign transactions as authentic, stays safe and hidden. In hindsight, it seems like an obvious idea. One of those "Why didn't I think of this before?" deals. PGP was invented by a guy named Phil Zimmermann in 1991, but it took until 2009 for somebody to think of creating Bitcoin. And nobody has any idea who the inventor is. It's a total fucking mystery. That's my favorite part of the whole story. The guy invented a currency under a fake name, provided technical support for a year and then left in the middle of 2010. No trace.

If you're curious about how secure PGP is, devices containing PGP-encrypted files have been intercepted multiple times by a multitude of different law enforcement agencies. The more widely-publicized cases indicate that no law enforcement agency has the ability to decrypt these files without the private key. As of about 2007 police have resorted to using legislature to force people to provide their passwords/keys. In the UK for example a man was jailed for 9 months for refusing to provide police with his encryption keys.

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pretty_Good_Privacy#Security_quality
