Title: Re: Just ease my mind..
Post by: ByronLegosi on March 16, 2013, 12:32 am
Reasons for vendors to ask for FE (I am a vendor btw) legitimate and not so:
1) The user has a high auto-finalize rate
2) The user has never ordered anything before, is brand new and has started with a $500+ order
3) It's a raffle
4) They aren't very confident in their stealth ability
5) lots of packages have gone missing
6) The buyer has needed a resend several times

Reasons not to FE for the vendor:
1) Buyers can trust them and feel secure
2) You don't have to chase up the buyer and your stats look better


Reasons to FE for the buyer:
1) They want some freebies from the seller
2) They know the vendor well

Reasons not to:
1) THEY WILL GET FUCKED OVER


Basic conclusion:
Don't do a deal where you're required to FE unless you have shit stats. I won't ask you to, but if you have crap stats I won't ask you to FE, I'll just cancel the order.

Thanks ByronLegosi for the breakdown! What's the best way to start out on the right foot with good stats, so that vendors won't need to ask for FE? Or is it best to do a few very small orders first and FE them early, so your stats are decent and you can afford to not have to FE? (I'm interested in both buying and selling, btw)

I think you can usually make a few orders, especially domestically, that are relatively small and the vendor won't ask you to FE. Once they've done and you've finalized legitimately a few orders then you can start to expect the vendor not to ask you to FE. If they do, either refuse, ask why or cancel the order yourself. You will almost certainly find the same product from someone else who won't ask you to FE.

As a vendor, firstly, don't start with listings that you can't afford to lose. I've started small with ones that (and it's looking like I may have to) I can afford to either resend or refund, that way you don't need to FE. Once you get a good rating as a vendor then you may ask for FE on larger orders at the buyers risk rather than yours but in my honest opinion, if you have chosen to be a vendor on Silk Road then you should be prepared to ALWAYS use escrow.

The exceptions of course having been listed above.
