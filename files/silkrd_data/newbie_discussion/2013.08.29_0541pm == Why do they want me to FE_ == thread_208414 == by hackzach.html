Title: Re: Why do they want me to FE?
Post by: prettyboyfloyd on August 29, 2013, 07:18 pm
Escrow has your funds so the trust bit on the vendor side should decide them on whether to accept your order or not in the first place. If they want to have a fail-safe on whether they're going to be ripped off, they can always send out tracked mail. That gives them evidence if it goes to resolution and they'll get their cash.

I guess some vendors working on a tight balance may argue they need the funds to get new stock. But if they're making that your problem you may as well be a shareholder ;D (I've read some vendors offering a discount on FE, maybe for that reason?).

If you're FE'ing because you trust the vendor then why not - you're making an active decision that's based on (presumably) solid information and an informed choice. But if you're good enough for them to pick up your order, then ou have to ask yourself why they've apparently become worried straight after you've made the commitment on your side.

I guess new vendors have to start somehow, but if they can stump up the cash for the bond and make a bulk purchase in the first place, it seems pretty unbelievable that they urgently need your little portion of retail cash.

Anyway that's my 2 cents.
