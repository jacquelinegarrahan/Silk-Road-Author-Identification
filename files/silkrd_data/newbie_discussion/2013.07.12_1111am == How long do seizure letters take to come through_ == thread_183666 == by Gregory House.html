Title: Re: How long do seizure letters take to come through?
Post by: Chip Douglas on July 12, 2013, 07:35 pm
The only one I ever got, the vendor and I figured took an extra 2 days. That was from Amsterdam. It's amazing that anything get's out of that city, with that postmark.

The very same day it arrived, another one from the same post ofc came! - Talk about a lump in the back of your throat!!! :o

The vendor was amazed, and I guess this is when the 'better stealth' light-bulb went on over his head.  :D

We chatted back+forth about commonly shipped items. I used my many ebay examples. He wanted to see a scan of the letter, which I did. He said he believed me, he just had never gotten one, and wanted to frame it proudly! ???  :) Crazy guy. - Turned out to be a lot of fun to correspond with.

Also I'm happy to report, his 100% improved stealth made it to me the following week. Not only that, but it had been opened, and stapled hastily back together. (Bubble wrap parcel envelope). We figured it was on the NL side, because they'd do it that way according to him. Also the fact that they opened it, but failed to find it was laughable. I don't want to give any more away, but let's just say it just about hiding in plain site!

I checked back on his listings as the months went by, and noticed a big improvement in sales, and his rating. With lots of comments on the excellent stealth!  I'd like to think I can take a little credit for that one! ::)

The particular product I wanted he didn't have all the time, and as months passed, I found my regular Vendors, with less notable cities as the post office.  ;)

I wish I could give him a shout out, but I'm sure he'll read this, and remember.  It's been about a year. So much has changed. I went right along ordering, figuring, if they've got me, they've got me. Though if you saw the letter, it's really quite dissapointing, I mean if you were hoping to show someone and say LOOK!  For shock value or whatever.

A very poor photo copy of a form letter. Had to at one time have been copied using an old fax machine. If you know what I mean.
Add another 3 generations of  re-copying it, along with poor alignment, and it might as well have been an "out of order" sign in a laundromat!

Thanks for indulging me that trip down memory lane. Definitely a growth spurt there.  :-X

Chip
