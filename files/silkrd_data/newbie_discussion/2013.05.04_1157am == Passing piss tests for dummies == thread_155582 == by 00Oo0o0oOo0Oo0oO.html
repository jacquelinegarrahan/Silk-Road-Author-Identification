Post by: shuvool on May 04, 2013, 12:34 pm
I used to collect urinalysis for the military when I was in, and the random drug tests that we gave were the standard 10-panel gc/ms tests. On a positive (ug/dl above cutoff point: below the cutoff you could still tell the substance was there, but it had to be reported as negative) result, the sample is re-tested 2 more times and if it fails either of those times, the report back is tested negative for drugs.

Thing is: that shit is expensive, so what most places that randomly test employees often do is they gather batches and test random samples in the batch. I've personally seen guys get caught on urinalysis failure that had been using during periods that they had passed other tests, simply because their sample was never tested.  Now obviously for pre-employment screening or for probation or anything that is singling you out, your sample gets tested every time, but the message I'm trying to get across is that you can't necessarily rely on past success to ensure future success when it comes to beating a drug test (unless you're actually clean).

Now, there are substances that aren't tested for.  Read up online for more info, but the 10-panel is the most common, 10 panel meaning that they only test for 10 things.  The 10 things tested for are all "recreational drugs", but at additional expense they can test for other substances (it's really expensive for some of them)
