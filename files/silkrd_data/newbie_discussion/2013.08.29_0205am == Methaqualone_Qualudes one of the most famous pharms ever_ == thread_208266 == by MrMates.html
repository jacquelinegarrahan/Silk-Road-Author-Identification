Title: Re: Methaqualone/Qualudes one of the most famous pharms ever?
Post by: Nakorx on September 07, 2013, 09:40 pm
Qualudes, or Mandrax as they were called in the UK, are a great drug but very difficult to get hold off. A brief timeline is shown below:

1955       Methaqualone was first synthesized in India by M.L. Gujral and was soon being marketed as a non-addictive sleeping pill. 1/ 
1960       Methaqualone is sold by the Boots company under the names Melsed and Melsedin and by Marck as Renoval. 1/
1965       Methaqualone is sold by Roussel labs in combination with an antihistamine under the name Mandrax. 1/ 
Late 60s       Methaqualone becomes a commercial success in the United States where it is sold under the name Quaalude. It also becomes a popular street drug. 1/
1971       Methaqualone becomes regulated in Britain under the UK Misuse of Drugs Act due to its increasing popularity as a recreational drug.   
1973       Methaqualone is placed in Schedule II in the United States making it difficult to prescribe and illegal to possess without a prescription.   
Early 80s       Methaqualone is withdrawn from the market in Britain due to its addictive qualities.   
1984       Methaqualone is moved to Schedule I in the United States, making it illegal to buy, sell, or possess.   

1/ Reference: Rudgley R. "The Encyclopaedia of Psychoactive Substances" by Thomas Dunne Books, 1998.
 Basically the only country that manufactures and markets them is South Africa, although I expect a few smaller labs are working on it!

And to think that I had a prescription for Mandrax, to help me sleep, from a private doctor in Brixton Lane(???) South London, before it was banned. What great Friday nights we used to have, it wasn't called Randy Mandies for nothing - LOL :)

 Have never met a vendor IRL with over 30 years of looking although there are quite a few scammers out there, including the ones on SR, so unless they come from South Africa and have all the correct packing, patients leaflet etc, then forget it.

I expect the upcoming generation of young "research" chemists to take another look at this drug, as its potential for making money is vast if manufactured in bulk  and it does not cost a great deal to acquire the necessary precursors.

There is a stack of information about  it out there - try using "startpage" and see for yourself. Good luck people! :)
