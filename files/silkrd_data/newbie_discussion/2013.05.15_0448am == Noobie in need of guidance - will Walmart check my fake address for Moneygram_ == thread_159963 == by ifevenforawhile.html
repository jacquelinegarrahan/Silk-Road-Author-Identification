Title: Re: Noobie in need of guidance - will Walmart check my fake address for Moneygram?
Post by: offbeatadam on May 15, 2013, 04:53 am
Moneygram policies are actually pretty clear. There are value amounts that have required levels of identification. As long as you stay below those amounts, you'll be fine. Like payday loans, I think these amounts are regulated in some places/states - so the value may be different than what I tell you. That being said, from the ones I've used, anything over $900 requires an ID and a verifiable address. I've used moneygram a LOT at walmarts around the country, and I've never once been asked to provide ID. I don't normally do values more than around $250 for this exact purpose.

Walmart and Moneygram both put this information very easy to find at the desk where you go to actually do the transaction. Its always on the moneygram form - read it carefully to understand what may constitute the requirement.

Also this is important, and I'm making another post for that reason...

If you are using something like BitInstant, make sure you use the same name that you used at bitinstant. You don't need to use your name there either, but they MUST match. If you don't, there is no guarantee that you will receive your bitcoins. This is not a process that is actually performed by bitinstant - it is through an external company that they utilize to receive the cash.
