Not quite sure how AUS post works regarding the security of receiving your mail. But in the US, its as easy as opening a PO BOX online, yes you can use real information. Be sure to sign up for some free newsletters or free catalouges or magazines under a non suspicious person (most likely someone real in  your area from whitepages). This will start letting the postal carrier learn that the person name (Fictitious name) is possibly receiving mail at your po box address which is fine because anyone can use your box, friends, family etc. Now once you establish and let the postal carrier learn that user and it becomes apparent in there system and will not raise future flags or suspicion you can actually have a package or parcel sent to that name to your PO BOX. Also another good method I've used numerous times when a customer doesnt include correct receiver name, I use default sender information which is:

Current Resident
PO BOX 420
QUALITY, CA 42000

I have never had an issue with this method for sending or receiving as when I dont trust a vendor with my information I tell them to use Current Resident and use your real address even. If the package makes it to you and it doesnt look like it was tampered with then you can accept the package and dont even open it til you are far away and the coast is clear! Trust me I have dealt with many different postal services and carriers in many locations and this works every time! I have even had packages sent to my real address and say my name is Gary Coleman, I sometimes would change it but keep it the same to not arouse suspicion, ie. Gary Cole and if they every ask you play the stupid card obviously. You cant get in trouble for a package showing up in your mail box.. You can only get in trouble if you KNEW that the package was coming and admitted to knowing what was in the box.. Otherwise it is not your job to prove your innocence but it is there jobs to prove your guilt.. which we are all innocent so you can just drop all worries ;)

When in doubt, play stupid and act dumbfounded. If they catch you after you opened the package and with the product then you go further into your stupid act as in.. ie. well a package came to me in my box and was unsure if it was addressed to myself or another based on the unclear name on the package and it was in my mailbox so I decided to open it and check for myself, is that a crime? "I had just started looking on the internet on what to do if drugs arrive in my mailbox" as to get this to the proper authorities!

Never admit to guilt as they cant prove you knew what was in the package nor who sent it and for what reason. Just stay calm and act like you have not a care in the world, never speak without an attorney present! That means don't even converse with the cops arresting you or questioning you in hopes to get released as this will only fuck you further. Play dumb take your ride to the station, await your attorney and tell him your sob story and how this is ridiculous and then you get charges dropped.. (if any are filed with DA, doubtful!) Title: Re: Strategies for receiving domestic shipments
Post by: ozdealerdownund3r on August 25, 2013, 12:38 pm
I have now had quite a bit of experience getting packages here in Australia.

First always choose a vendor from the US/UK with good stealth. Cannot guarantee that you will get it but your chances are much higher then the Netherlands for example, I learnt the hard way.

Second, buy a master key. Go to your local apartment block (with new letter boxes) and try your luck with this master key late at night. This works on about 80% of the boxes for me.
http://silkroadvb5piz3r.onion/silkroad/item/b18be0a843
I bought a key then got it cut 3 times so I had multiple copies as I once thought I lost it. Not good when you are expecting a package.

Third, make up a name to use. You don't want to be opening the mailbox and spending 5 min to work out which one is yours. A few vendors recommend to actually use the name of the person living there but I have found no difference. Trust me you will know when a package arrives.

Finally look at what times letters are dropped for that apartment block in your area. If you have a 9-5 job you will find this difficult but you don't want the owner of the apartment to have access to the letter before you!

As a thought I have had a few letters that never arrived. What I found is that during this period regular letter drops for that address decrease. Not sure if the post office get a note to flag a certain address, then all of a sudden boom the mail starts again. Suggest to use a vendor with the least lead time as possible and select multiple addresses. This way you don't spend up to one month going back to this mailbox!

Enjoy and party safe.
