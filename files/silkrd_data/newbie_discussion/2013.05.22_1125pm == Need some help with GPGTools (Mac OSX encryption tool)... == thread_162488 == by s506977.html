Title: Re: Need some help with GPGTools (Mac OSX encryption tool)...
Post by: DoctorFate on May 22, 2013, 11:49 pm
I think GPG tools is based off of GNUPGP which is what I use but their GUI's are worlds apart :/

It will be hard to direct you specifically but I would do some google searches for stuff like gpg tools tutorials

I found one right on their website, it's nice tutorial that shows setup and exactly what you need to do at around 2:00 minutes in.   

Go to
http://support.gpgtools.org/
Then click Getting Started

Once gpg is all setup and you've imported your vendors public key then you open a text editor, type in some text then right click and encrypt it from an option on the menu.  I believe the video shows how to set this all up in the beginning.  Just ignore the stuff about encrypting emails it may just get confusing.

FYI you don't need your own key to encrypt messages, only your vendors public key.  You need your key if you want to have your vendor send you something encrypted.    So technically you could just open the program, paste in your vendors key, open a text editor, type text, right click encrypt, it should be that simple but you never know.
