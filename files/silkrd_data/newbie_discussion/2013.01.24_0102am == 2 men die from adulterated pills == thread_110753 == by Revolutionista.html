Most of the 'danger' of drugs like MDMA comes from their legal status, which means dodgy suppliers, unknown dosages, and damaging adulterants. Exactly how an argument for keeping alcohol banned during prohibition was the 'dangers' of methanol-adulterated supplies turning you blind, as if that's somehow proof of the danger of alcohol itself. Some day, I hope that all drugs will be legalised - not just decriminalized... unless they can be manufactured and sold legally on an industrial scale, the dangers of contamination are still there. It really does sadden me that politicians these days, that obviously experimented with cannabis in their youth, and know full well how harmless some drugs are (and are smart people, so must at an intellectual level be able to appreciate the benefits in harm-reduction that would come from legalisation), still support the obviously broken status-quo. In thrall to the tabloid newspapers, afraid of losing votes, outright admitting that they follow (misinformed) popular opinion rather than evidence-based science... sigh. No wonder David Nutt resigned.
