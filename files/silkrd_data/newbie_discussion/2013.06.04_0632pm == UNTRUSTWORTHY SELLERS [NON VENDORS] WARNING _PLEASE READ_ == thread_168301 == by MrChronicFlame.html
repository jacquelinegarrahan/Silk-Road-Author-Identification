Title: UNTRUSTWORTHY SELLERS [NON VENDORS] WARNING *PLEASE READ*
Post by: MrChronicFlame on June 04, 2013, 06:32 pm
                                                                                                                                Everyone who is new, sorta new, returned, or the daily roader's...

                                                                                                                      DO NOT GIVE ANY INFORMATION OUT TO NON-VENDOR USERS OR FORUM THREADS.



                                            Things to look for, (RED FLAGS)

     1: If it seems to good to be true, it most likely isn't.
     2: Check to see if the seller/vendor has a review thread, with active reviews. (Check some of the reviewer's to check their legitimacy)
     3: Never give anyone information, BITCOIN's,- to an unknown seller/vendor (even for shipping fee's/Samples)
     4: Check if its domestic, how long he's been apart of SR, Dig info up.
     5: Never give any address's, name's, numbers, etc to Unknown seller/vendor's.
     6: Search the user, on the store page not the forum I recommend (80) or higher for better results in buying.
     7: Always think with your head.

                                          Things to look for, (GREEN FLAGS)
     1: Seller has a vendor page, with over (80) point's.
     2: Seller has a decent thread on reviewing product's.
     3: Is a known SR Vendor, Friendly, but with a'little attitude.. (Sometimes the nicer one's are the ones that F*** you)
     4: Check for product papers/pictures (test's/pictures)
     5: See how many FE's and failed orders Vendor has.



I'd love for an admin to sticky this, If anyone wants to improve this and copy it, PM ME so I can update it with an EDIT if its good.

I had to make this due to the increase of unknown sellers and scammer's in the Newbie place, its just way to much... to many roader's are in danger...

I will add something...it's a life lesson too.

You can pick up a great deal from a persons word and deeds...so just speak to a vendor - test the water...see if you like the person you're interacting with...how does your instinct spark...? Initial gut feeling (even online) can be very powerful and give a good indication of the quality and integrity of the person.

Do you like them? Do they seem to have a sense of decency? Do they give a shit about your questions, concerns or just engage well in a friendly but professional manner.

That's who I deal with - if I get anything else I know there's plenty more to trade with, banter with...life's short so don't waste it dealing with people you'd "much rather kick in the eye" (Morrisy/Smiths for those who know!)

Good luck and be safe
