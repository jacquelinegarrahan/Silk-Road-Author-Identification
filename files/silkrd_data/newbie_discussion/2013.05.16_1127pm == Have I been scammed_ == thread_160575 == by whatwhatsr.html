Bad luck man, sounds like youve fallen victim to a scam. I think the only possibility of doing anything about this, is to contact WU and explain that you have sent someone money for a product and they have stolen your money. A big no-no would be to mention bitcoins, personally I would come up with another believable story, take your pick which. A person must have photo ID to collect a WU payment and (I think) also proof of address. Maybe its possible for WU to track down the person and do something about it. But I will say again - do not mention bitcoins and come up with another story. Even though bitcoins are not illegal, the chances of WU and/or police doing anything about it if you mention them are very slim

Also, in case you want another way to buy bitcoins, which is pretty much 100% safe is localbitcoins.com. Ive used it and can recommend ti highly

You sign up and numerous vendors selling bitcoins in your area become visible. When you agree to deal with someone, they then send the agreed amount of bitcoins to an escrow system within the localbitcoins.com website. It will be indicated to you that the bitcoins for the transaction have been funded into the escrow system, by the seller

You then send money to the seller. This can be done by meeting in person and giving cash, a bank transfer, a WU payment, cheque..... etc

When the seller receives the money, they enter the release code for the transaction, into thelocalbitcoins system and the funds will be sent to you

Sellers have feedback there, you can see that they are legitimate, and the escrow system offers you full protection
