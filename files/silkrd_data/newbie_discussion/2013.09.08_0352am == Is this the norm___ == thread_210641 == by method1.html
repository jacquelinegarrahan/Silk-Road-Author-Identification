The in transit time thing is kind of funny because it's somewhat of a guessing game.  There are vendors who will mark in transit early yet put the package in the mail later.  Then it can be the other way around where package is in the mail but vendor didn't mark in transit (less likely than the previous IMO).  Every vendor is different and since some of the vendors you ordered off of have that policy stating they'll have it out in 48 hours, it probably wouldn't hurt to just shoot those particular vendors a quick message and ask for a status update on your order.  Tell 'em why you need it soon and see what they say.  If a vendor doesn't have it written up though regarding how long it'll take to ship or mark in transit, I wouldn't message them.  They do have I believe 4 days (or maybe 5 can't remember) to mark it in transit before you have the option of hitting cancel.  If cancel becomes available, I'd send a message at that point saying you'd like to know where the order stands otherwise you'll cancel.  Don't be threatening but that's what I'd do.  And then finally I'd tread real lightly with the reload in the future.  If you've sent the code to more than one vendor this is how people get scammed.  I hope you did your homework as to which vendor is reliable and truth worthy because the first vendor you sent the number to might take the money and then the new vendor you're waiting on might check the number and find there is zero dollars on the card.  I'm not saying that to scare you but it's a risk.  I have had a bitcoin vendor take 24 hours but usually they def. say aprox. how long it'll take for them to get you your coin.  Also if the other feedback says people are getting there coins super fast and it's say 48 hours and you haven't gotten them I'd be messaging the vendor.
