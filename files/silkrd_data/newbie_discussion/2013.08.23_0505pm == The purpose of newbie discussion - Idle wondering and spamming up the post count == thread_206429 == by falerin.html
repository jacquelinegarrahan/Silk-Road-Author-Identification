Title: The purpose of newbie discussion - Idle wondering and spamming up the post count
Post by: falerin on August 23, 2013, 05:05 pm
A thread for general navel gazing and idle speculation. Rhetorical questions in their finest form of mental masturbation.

I must profess as an experienced community moderator in other forums that the purpose of this forum and indeed the entire behind it seems rather counterintuitive to me.

Are we worried that the quality of the spam elsewhere will go down unless we are certain that our new posters are adequately capable of spamming the forums enough to get member status?

In all seriousness I am assuming that the idea is that only a real human will take the time to make 50 posts and therefore it will filter machine posts

Has that been an issue historically for  forum? Is this mechanism preemptive?

And more to the point is the mechanism even working given that we encourage spamming to get post count up rather then quality posts and all that this is doing is encouraging scammers to target the newbies who may be less informed. As has been shown by at least one person repeatedly spamming the newbie boards with junk links to scam sites today.

I am against trial by fire in a general sense, and I find it particularly odd here.

I am not sure I tend to get a market structured around an MMO style You  N00b! approach to business.

Such an approach is likely simply to drive clientele off.

Mind you I am sure that given this market environment there are always new customers but hostility toward new customers seems a rather counterproductive business model to me.
