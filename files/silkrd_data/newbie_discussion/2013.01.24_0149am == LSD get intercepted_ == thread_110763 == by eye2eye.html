Here's something I learned from a man who trains police dogs.  He said dogs are mainly trained to do one thing.  So it's not like they train every dog to be able to sniff out explosives, coke, meth, weed, etc.  There are dogs for explosive sniffing and then there are drug dogs which are trained by the police officers stuffing drugs into the dog's chew toys.  The dogs are essentially always looking for their toys.  That's why they go nuts when they walk by my car.  They think their toy is in there but in actuality I just have an oz. of weed. 

True story, one time I got pulled over literally seconds after smoking a blunt.  They sent dogs all up in my car to see if they could find my stash that I had hidden in a good spot.  The dog was too confused and overwhelmed by the blunt I had just smoked to find it.  And it was not like inside the engine or tire or something it was in the "cockpit" if you will.  They ended up finding one solitary seed and almost took me to county.
