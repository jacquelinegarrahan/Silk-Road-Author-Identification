Title: Re: Legit Seizure notice?
Post by: foxen624 on June 23, 2013, 07:06 am
I was really curious on this one because some years ago, a friend of mine received a so called "love letter" from the Customs and Border Protection Agency, and it didn't look anything like the pic that the OP showed, but it did look to the best of my recollection exactly like the pic in the link from Attacksia.  But some of the wording in the one received by the OP was very close to the more familiar letter size Customs seizure notice, yet the CFR (Code of Federal Regulations) number was different.   So, I went and looked up the CFR in the OP's notice [19 CFR 149.59], and sure enough it definately is about items being seized in US Customs.   Matter of fact, there is an in depth explanation of it should you want to take a look at it.  Here's the link:

http://www.ahpa.org/portals/0/pdfs/10_0115_19cfr149_complete.pdf

Unfortunately, your notice is probably legit, although I don't see how that would indicate any sort of scam on the part of the vendor.  Also, as long as it's the first/only such notice you have ever received, don't worry too much about it as I've heard of many others who have received one (besides my friend whom I personally knew) and apparently they don't follow up past the second letter ...   in that I doubt that you need to worry about the feds showing up at your door with a warrant or anything like that...  although I understand that it must be pretty disconcerting to have gotten it at all..  :{
