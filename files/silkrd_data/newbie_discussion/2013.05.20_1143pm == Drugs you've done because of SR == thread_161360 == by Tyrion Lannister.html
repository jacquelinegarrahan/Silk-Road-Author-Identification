^ With K, it hurts. The drip is really bad with K too, and it can leak down into your stomach and make you nauseous.
You have to do a lot of K to get to a K-hole. MXE is easier, feels better and gives a greater euphoria with a deeper hole. You also have to do a lot less.

As for what I've done cuz of SR:
MXE: Instantly addicted. My first real drug was DXM so I loved dissociatives already.. but MXE was so good. So much deeper. Mixes incredibly well with a psychedelic to 'flavour' the trip.
n,n-DMT: Wasn't mentally ready for it. Made me suicidal until it wore off.
2C-E: Had a bad night, fought with my mother, gf, don't remember much about the drug.
25C-NBOME: Took it irresponsibly, had a bad trip in public on about 1800ug. Also, did some crazy shit on it. There was a KFC down the road I used to always buy a Zinger burger off when I was fucked up on NBOMEs. I actually went in there once with a Guy Fawkes mask in the middle of the day and purchased a zinger, they asked me what I was on and I'm like Bombs from the internet and walked off. I must have looked fuckin blasted to them haha. I never went back, I'm so glad I skipped town!
25i-NBOME: Took it responsibly, 1200ug but realized I didn't like phenethylamines at all. I feel insane on them, like I'm losing control of everything and the world is crumbling. It lasts way too long for my taste and I couldn't sleep.
5-MEO-MIPT: Interesting, I'm on it now, in the first hour after taking the capsule I thought the vendor had sent me MDMA by mistake. Then the spirals came. I think I'm peaking now because I can see the carpet's pattern pulsing. It's quite mellow and I think I would even be able to sleep on it. It's a comfortable psychedelic, I don't feel the same on NBOME.
Ketamine: nauseating, holes aren't that deep, feeling of being 'fucked up' rather than 'im having a profound experience'. I like it when I'm feeling depressed because it's like alcohol in a way, you forget about your problems and escape into it's comfort. But it's not a good drug for having experiences with.
