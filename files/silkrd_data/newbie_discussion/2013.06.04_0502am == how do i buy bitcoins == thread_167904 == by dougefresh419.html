Post by: Scintillation on June 04, 2013, 05:22 am
If you're in the US, the simplest way to buy BTC is to use Coinbase since it accepts bank transfers and debit cards without a long wait like mtgox.  However, it is not very anonymous.

mindlessWarrior mentions Bitinstant.  Do not buy BTC with bitinstant.  Instead use their service (if you choose to) to transfer USD to mtgox or another exchange and buy the BTC yourself.  Bitinstant capitalizes on spread and will get you a bad price.  Also, if you do choose to buy BTC with bitinstant do not send the BTC directly to a SR address.

Bitcoins are not anonymous if an address can be tied to you.

Using IRC and out of band transfers is worth it (for anonymity) and relatively safe if you go about it correctly and carefully.  Even buying locally from someone is probably better than having official records on your transactions and video footage of you sending money at Walmart or equivalent.

Look into mixing services.  There are some reputable ones with zero or close to no fees.

Mining is also a good way to generate BTC anonymously.

So, in regards to bitinstant getting you a bad price, the USD/BTC price is set by supply and demand on exchanges.  As I'm sure you know, it fluctuates.  If you buy BTC with bitinstant, you are asking them to put the order in somewhere for you.  Since this process isn't immediate, they can exploit this to choose a high USD value per Bitcoin.

Let's say you place an order at some price.  Then you go to Walmart and send the money.  Then you receive your BTC sometime after (let's just say an hour on average after you send the money).

If the USD value of BTC goes higher at some point before they send, they make money because they send you less BTC.

If the USD value of BTC goes down, they can just select the price it was either when the order was placed or when they received your payment.

They do this to make additional profits, and it has been documented, although they don't admit to it.

If you buy USD with bitinstant, they can't exploit this.  Bitinstant with a fake name -> mtgox with an unverified account -> trustworthy mixer -> SR is not ideal but I'd trust it (and often do.)  Recently mtgox started cracking down on unverified accounts and are requesting ID's (before this was just if you were wiring money directly to them - not if bitinstant or equivalent were sending money in.. you'll have to look into it).
