Title: Should I be honest and critical in my feedback?
Post by: TuggingOnSupermansCape on February 21, 2013, 02:20 pm
I blindly did an order for my first time that actually went better than i thought. The vendor communicated with me, and i had the items in my hand within 3 days of ordering. I was happy (naturally), so i decided to spend a little more on SR, and went with 3 orders from two of the '100 rated' trusted vendors.

Both vendors had excellent selection and decent photos of some amazing looking items. Both also had 'i ship every day of the week' listed prominently in their shop message - what more could i ask for? I ordered Sunday. It's now Thursday and the cancel option is about to come up and neither one of them has marked the order as in transit. I sent messages to both, one responded Tuesday and said he was having a 'IRL emergency' and would send Wednesday - that never happened. The other one never responded. I thought i was choosing wisely, it appears i was not (despite both vendors having amazing 5/5 ratings and great comments about their product).

Even if the items come in and are literally the best i've ever seen, i still don't feel honest in leaving them a 5/5, or even 4/5, as neither have kept their word or self imposed shipping deadlines. Heck one vendor has missed it twice now. I know some people have the mentality of 'be glad you got something, that's worth 5/5 alone" - well to me, its not. I keep my word on finalizing when i receive the product and writing a great review, and you are supposed to keep your word about the product you send and the deadlines you maintain. My only fear is that my honest review will somehow 'blacklist' me from other vendors.

Any thoughts/comments on this issue? Am i being a drama queen or are my concerns valid?

Post by: SycoPunk on February 21, 2013, 08:57 pm
Keep in mind these vendors are people and shit happens in life. Some of them have so many orders they cant possibly process them all daily. You wont be able to cancel after he ships if he marked it shipped.. once the vendor marks that he shipped it you can no longer cancel it. your only option then is if you still dont get the product you can click "resolve" after like 17 days (it gives enough time for product to come before you can do anything) but if the product doesnt come in a while just contact the vendor first... they will usually offer a refund or reship it if you did not get it. Onle use resolve if you dont get the item and vendor doesnt respond to messages.

If you click cancel now though.. in regards to what youde get back it depends. Look at the "Account" tab. It will tell you what is in escrow, as sellers have the option to hold the funds in btc or usd. If it says you have usd in escrow and the price of bitcoin went up since you ordered you will get less bitcoin but the bitcoins will be worth the same ammountr in usd as they were when you ordered it.

If it says its in btc then you will get it back in btc, therefore giving you more money (usd equivalent) back.

So technically if you wanted you could cancel and re order it for less btc than before and "save money" if your in escrow with btc but i dont recomend that as its not very nice plus then you might have to wait longer for your order again.
