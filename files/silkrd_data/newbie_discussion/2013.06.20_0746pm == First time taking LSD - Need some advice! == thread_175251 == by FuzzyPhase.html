Post by: CITVVTIC on June 21, 2013, 04:26 am
I highly  recommend reading this beforehand - http://www.luminist.org/archives/session.htm - although I do not suggest taking everything in there as gospel, I think it provides a useful framework for thinking about tripping and the ways in which you can turn your trip in an unpleasant direction. Also the effects graph is very useful. It definitely can't hurt to have read it and it is written in a kind of matter of fact, common-sensey tone that I think is quite reassuring as it grounds the reader. Lots of other material on psychedelics has a more spiritual/metaphysical bent, and while these things are interesting to think about and issues of this sort might be very real and important to you before/during/after tripping, it is also useful to be reminded of other things that might be very real and important to you before/during/after tripping like having a good time and not pissing off your companions and embarrassing yourself.

Another thing you might want to think about is what you want to get out of the experience - do you want to connect with nature/meditate and explore your mind/listen to some of your favourite music/listen to some brand new music/hang out with your mates/watch films/watch bill hicks/shatter your ego? Different things may be more or less fun/possible on different doses so you should take that into account when planning your trip. You should also take into account the fact that you might find that what you had planned to do might not be as appealing once you are tripping and so, especially for your first few trips, it would be useful to have contingency options. So, for example, if you are tripping with friends it might be good to have a place where you can go to be on your own for a bit if the mood takes you that way, if you plan to listen to music (definitely a great idea) it would be useful to have a selection of things you can change to if something unnerves you, if you plan to trip indoors it would be useful to be able to go outside into a garden for some fresh air.

Anyway, I would say the less control you have over the environment the lower you would want to do dose to begin with. So more people around = lower dose. But I would also say that there can be a disadvantage to dosing too low - the possibility that you will be in a nearly-there state which can lead to anxiety as you are waiting for something to happen and it never quite happens. I think really you have to take at least a full dose and dive into it, as you are kidding yourself if you think you can control the experience precisely by controlling the dosage. Not that that matters as you are always in control anyway  ;) Eat as much as you can afford!

Title: Re: First time taking LSD - Need some advice!
Post by: mrmilleripresume on July 02, 2013, 02:28 pm
I used to do lots of acid. When I say lots of acid, I mean I ate it like candy.  Five hits would be average for me, and aside from driving or operating heavy machinery or rock climbing, there isn't a whole lot that I would not or could not do, and do well.

But that's me.

As a first timer, my best advice to you would be just to make sure that you are with a small group of trusted friends. Just one really good friend is even better.  No outsiders or people you don't know well, and no unstable types. If you have a friend that's reckless and does stupid shit, that is not the type of friend you want to have around because he will be prone to doing even dumber (and sometimes just plain weird and disturbing) shit than normal when he's tripping. Only trip with well grounded individuals, and only trip in an environment that you feel safe in.

Make sure that you don't have some issue on your mind, or that you don't suffer from any kind of complex when you drop. If you do, you're gonna have a bad time. Sometimes, when you drop acid even in the best of situations, your mind will go somewhere unpleasant. There's a reason for that. I found for myself that LSD beats the hell out of talking to a therapist. I've seen others though that can't handle it. LSD is not for everybody. If that happens, just know that the drug will wear off and you will be fine. You are not going to die from it, and your mind will not be eternally fucked from one hit of acid. ;) On that note, one hit is all you need to be doing if you've never had it. Wade in, don't jump straight into the deep end with LSD.  There's always room to add down the road once you've gotten the feel for it.
 
STAY AWAY FROM LARGE CROWDS until you have experience. After I got used to tripping, I enjoyed crowds. If you've never had acid before, chances are that a crowd is going to make you anxious and paranoid. You don't want that, especially on LSD.

That's about it. Just research. Set and Setting....that's the key to having a good time, but there are NEVER any guarantees with LSD. You drop your hit and then you're on the ride and there's no getting off of it. Having a valium around might not be a bad idea, but I've never needed it.

I'm editing to make one more point. A lot of people will tell you to have a "sitter." That is someone who is not taking LSD to sit around and watch you to make sure you don't do anything stupid. I don't like the whole sitter concept. Unless there's something wrong with you in the first place, LSD is not going to make you dangerous.

I highly  recommend reading this beforehand - http://www.luminist.org/archives/session.htm - although I do not suggest taking everything in there as gospel, I think it provides a useful framework for thinking about tripping and the ways in which you can turn your trip in an unpleasant direction. Also the effects graph is very useful. It definitely can't hurt to have read it and it is written in a kind of matter of fact, common-sensey tone that I think is quite reassuring as it grounds the reader. Lots of other material on psychedelics has a more spiritual/metaphysical bent, and while these things are interesting to think about and issues of this sort might be very real and important to you before/during/after tripping, it is also useful to be reminded of other things that might be very real and important to you before/during/after tripping like having a good time and not pissing off your companions and embarrassing yourself.

Another thing you might want to think about is what you want to get out of the experience - do you want to connect with nature/meditate and explore your mind/listen to some of your favourite music/listen to some brand new music/hang out with your mates/watch films/watch bill hicks/shatter your ego? Different things may be more or less fun/possible on different doses so you should take that into account when planning your trip. You should also take into account the fact that you might find that what you had planned to do might not be as appealing once you are tripping and so, especially for your first few trips, it would be useful to have contingency options. So, for example, if you are tripping with friends it might be good to have a place where you can go to be on your own for a bit if the mood takes you that way, if you plan to listen to music (definitely a great idea) it would be useful to have a selection of things you can change to if something unnerves you, if you plan to trip indoors it would be useful to be able to go outside into a garden for some fresh air.

Anyway, I would say the less control you have over the environment the lower you would want to do dose to begin with. So more people around = lower dose. But I would also say that there can be a disadvantage to dosing too low - the possibility that you will be in a nearly-there state which can lead to anxiety as you are waiting for something to happen and it never quite happens. I think really you have to take at least a full dose and dive into it, as you are kidding yourself if you think you can control the experience precisely by controlling the dosage. Not that that matters as you are always in control anyway  ;) Eat as much as you can afford!
