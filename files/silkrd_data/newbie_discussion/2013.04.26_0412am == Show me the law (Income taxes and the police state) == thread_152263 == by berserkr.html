"There has got to be lots of small licensed companies that only bring in minimum wages that forgo filing"
-----Yes. And that's OK legally. There is a minimum threshold for taxation. If you fall below that threshold, you aren't required to file at all. Most LLC's profits are filed on an individuals return (i.e. 1040); they're not filed as a corporation generally.

But if you ACTUALLY OWE taxes, you're in trouble IF you haven't paid them and IF you get caught. You can file your return late, but you MUST pay your taxes on time to avoid harsh penalties.

LLC owners shouldn't complain about taxes because they aren't double taxed as corporations can be. A CEO pays income taxes and his corporation pays taxes on the same money by paying him. LLC's profits as personal income.

And if your business is incurring losses, you should definitely be filing taxes. Even if the tax credit isn't refundable, it can be carried over and applied to the next year's tax bill.
