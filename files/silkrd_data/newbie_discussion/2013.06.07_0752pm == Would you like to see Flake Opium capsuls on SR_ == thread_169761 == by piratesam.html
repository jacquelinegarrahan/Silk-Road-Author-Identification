Title: Re: Would you like to see Flake Opium capsuls on SR?
Post by: simplemechanic on June 09, 2013, 03:57 pm
Opium is opium. If CFO (cooked flake opium) is done right, you won't be able to tell a difference unless you are using analytical equipment or are a hard-core opium connoisseur (and I mean daily for years hard-core). The trick is in how well it's made. Likely the CFO that the OP is talking about is poorly made because it doesn't smoke well. This is because pods contain various molecular sized contaminates that don't effect the high when eating, but they don't vaporize properly (they will actually burn before the opium vaporizes) and will give you a nasty smoke with a small high (since you can't get it hot enough to vaporize the opium). Once upon a time I made some high quality CFO, but it took a lot of care and time (note, I have a BS in chemical engineering and access to a fully stocked lab) and you wouldn't be able to tell the difference - other than CFO is flaky and almost completely dry while regular opium has some moisture to it. The relative strength of smoking vs eating was the same as any other opiate - meaning that there weren't enough contaminates to mess up the smoking process.

Long story short, opium is opium, regardless of what you start with. You just have to process and purify it differently depending on what your starting materials are.

Also, putting it in capsules just makes it easier to take orally, assuming the OP uses generic geletan 00 capsules, you can always break it open and smoke up what's inside.
