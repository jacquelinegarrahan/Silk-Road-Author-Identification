Bongs? Ok so you will need the following items;

your bong (dirty and gutter hash blocked as much as possible!)
A supply of hot water, the hotter the better but be careful of scalding yourself and also of the gutter hash staining... Porcelain in the bathroom WILL stain but the stainless steel one in the laundry won't..
Laundry powder.... Omo seems good but really anything with 'active enzymes' or whatever will work a treat too

So its a simple process ....

First rinse the bong in the hottest water you can manage with and keep rinsing until it has removed the flaky sludge and you are left with just the 'bath ring' style stuff that is left behind. A trick is to have the hot water flowing straight down the stem so you don't have to burn your fingers with the hot water. When done right you should get enough pressure to force the water to flow out the carb (shotty, bung etc) leaving the top cooler to tip the bulk water out between rinses. Next take a scoop of the laundry powder and drop into the bong coating the walls as much as possible. The bong should not have water in at this stage just the damp sludge left behind and the powder coating you just applied.. hear that?listen close and you can hear the fizzle of hassle free cleaning.. leave for a few seconds or more and genly fill the bong with HOT water... the hotter the better and if done right you will get all the sludge first time .I usually fill till just below the mouth piece stem and swirl the water a little bit to get it all rolling along.

Take 5... the longer you leave itto bubble away  the better and the odd swirl or 2 every few mins will speed it along too.. you will be watching the crap on the walls inside go a lighter brown and float away from the pyrex/glass... its kinda awesome.. but anyway ..... so when your happy to just  rinse with , you guessed it, hot water and you will be looking at your bong shining like its brand new again! I run some warm water as a last thing and run my hands over it all to rub away the slippery film that gets left behind and. the 'shadow' the carb (shotty, whatever you call the hole in the mouth piece stem) leaves is either a ruler and bit of wash cloth or whatever or you can block the hole and fill with water above the shadow and the process will be as effective in removing it too.

They are really good at breaking down oily, dirty organic stuff from your cloths in its day job so its just more of the same for in
