Title: Re: New and Eager for first buy
Post by: PowerToCharm on July 14, 2013, 03:09 pm
It seems that the larger anonymous btc traders are all closed, either permanently or temporarily. An alternative is localbitcoins.com, a p2p site where individuals can advertise to buy or sell bitcoin. You can meet in person if a seller happens to live around you, or exchange funds in some other way. Many sellers will accept cash deposit into a local branch of a national bank. So you stick money in the seller's account, and then the agreed upon number of coins are released to your localbitcoins wallet. You can then send them to SR or anywhere else. Escrow through the site protects both buyers and sellers. You will need to create an account in order to use the site, but you can fill in any information you want, real or fake, and no sort of id verification is required.

Once you place an order, the seller may wish to communicate with you via text message, so have a disposable phone standing by if that's an issue for you. Also, expect to pay 5-10 dollars per coin more than you would at a mainstream exchange. Also, many sellers are small-timers who only have two or three coins for sale at any one time. Also, once you place an order you may have only 60 or 90 minutes to get to the bank before the order cancels automatically, so be ready to roll before ordering. Overall, localbitcoins seems to work well, though I still have hope for Blue Sky to get back on its feet.
