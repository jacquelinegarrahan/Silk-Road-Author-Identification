Title: Re: best place to get bitcoins?
Post by: offbeatadam on May 14, 2013, 12:49 am
Quote from: anonymouse123 on May 14, 2013, 12:42 am
i'm in the usa. was looking into using bitstamp or btc-e. bitinstant seems like a good option. are there any other fee's besides the 3.99%? how do you deposit the money do they use moneygram?

So, yes, in a way. They use an external service (its called CashPayment, http://www.cashpayment.com <-- clearnet). You specify the transaction you want, ie: cash to bitcoin address. In the list of "cash" there are a few places you can select, like Walmart or CVS. It'll give you a receipt that has the information that you use when you go to the counter in order to send money. If you use Walmart, for example, it'd be moneygram. The company that handles it - cashpayment - has a few methods that are dependent on location and where you go to actually do the money transfer.

The 3.99% is the fee. If you look on the bitinstant site, there is a spot that gives you fees and location information - if you scroll down to the ZipZap section, that is the one that deals with Cash. ZipZap is the parent company that owns and runs CashPayment.

Post by: offbeatadam on May 14, 2013, 01:17 am
You should also remember that using the cash service includes both the 3.99% fee from bitinstant/zipzap, and the fees from the service you use, ie moneygram. So, if you do say, $200 - it'll cost you $212 to send at moneygram in walmart, plus 3.99% fees on the bitinstant transfer, so you are going to end up with $192 (4% of 200 is $8) plus the $12 you spent at money gram. Thats $20 total, so you hit like 10% at that point, if you consider the original amount you wanted to send.
