Title: Re: DCNs over
Post by: pestlepete on February 09, 2013, 01:31 am
The barcode on flat rate envelopes is a UPC, and is the same on every package of one type (I'm about 90% sure on this). I believe they will be printing an additional QR or bar code onto the package upon acceptance at the PO, perhaps with an ink hand stamp right at the counter. Regardless, no slips and no tracking from blue drop boxes (at least once all the old slips are out of circulation). Sorry if I'm just rephrasing what you guys meant, but this is my understanding after visiting the PO recently. I personally believe a small part of the reason this change is to target people like us by making us walk into the PO (along with efficiency, competitiveness, etc.).  I am not sure how I am going to compensate for the new tracking yet; I'm open to ideas!

There is some new info on DCNs, credit to USAShroomzz in this thread:
http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?topic=120821.msg828431#msg828431
in which he posted this link:
https://store.usps.com/store/browse/productDetailSingleSku.jsp?categoryNavIds=catGetMailingShippingSupplies&categoryNav=false&navAction=jump&navCount=0&productId=P_LABEL400&categoryId=catGetMailingShippingSupplies

This is good news, however these labels are not intended for Priority mail. On the FAQ for this service, it says all Priority mail is ineligible. However, this might mean it's simply ineligible for purchasing the service because it's already included; and I'm fairly certain they wouldn't reject a package for that reason. The question is whether or not it will look odd to the people handling it. I asked a clerk about them and he said he didn't have any; I would have to order them online. He also said that they were for First Class and Media Mail packages, but mentioned that you could use them on Priority if you're applying your own postage (not paying at the counter). He didn't say it convincingly enough for me to discern whether or not this will be considered "normal". He didn't specify whether the DCN is still free with Priority when using this label, but I can only guess that it should be. I plan to go to another PO to ask about it soon, as the branch I visited is very small.

This thread looks like it has some good info: http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?topic=112044
