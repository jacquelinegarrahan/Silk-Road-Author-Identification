Post by: wolf2wolf on June 02, 2013, 04:13 pm
I've been using MDMA since late 80s. Prior to the test kits being available, quality was always a total crap shoot, unless you happened to know the cook personally or made it yourself. If you look at the ecstasydata stats, http://www.ecstasydata.org/stats.php, you can see that in almost every year since they started testing, over half their results show adulterated or bogus pills, with 2004-2009 being particularly bad. I'm not sure if quality today is really any worse than it's been in the past, if anything, the stats imply its getting better, but it's hard to say how representative ecstasydata is of the overall market (I'm guessing people tend to send in more dodgy pills than the ones they like).

Regarding the test results, there are several different reagents. The following FAQ describes typical results for each: https://www.erowid.org/chemicals/mdma/mdma_faq_testing_kits.shtml. BTW, dancesafe recommends Mecke as the best one to use for MDMA, if you can only pick one.   As the FAQ says, a darker result can mask a lighter one, so it's definitely possible to have a mixture of MDMA and something else show a positive result, as you surmised.  The home test kits do not guarantee quality, unfortunately. You need a real lab for that.

My own experience is that most MDMA I've tested does not fiz or smoke, but I usually use Mecke.  I have occasionally seen the Marquis bubble a bit if I use a larger sample size.

Really pure MDMA should be white, dry, crystalline solid. Other colors and consistency result from some impurities, although it's totally possible to have a batch of brown "MDMA " be cleaner than a batch of white "MDMA".

Having said all of that, the quality of MDMA on SR seems quite good relative to what you are going to find on the street, so if you pick a reputable vendor here, you should be quite happy with what you get.
