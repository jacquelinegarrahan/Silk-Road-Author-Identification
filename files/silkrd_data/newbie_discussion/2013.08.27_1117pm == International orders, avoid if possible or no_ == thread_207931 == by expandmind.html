Title: Re: International orders, avoid if possible or no?
Post by: unbeknownst on August 28, 2013, 12:04 am
The risk of seizure is substantially higher. Domestic packages are rarely searched, but customs control in the US and especially AUS, NZ, Scandinavia are fairly tight. That's why many vendors up and won't ship to these countries. International can work, it all comes down to the strength and variety of stealth that the vendor uses. Once LE seize one package in the mail, they will be on the lookout for that particular stealth profile and single it out. Packages from certain countries are also scrutinized very closely, i.e. Netherlands, Germany, anywhere in Latin America.

You need to do your research on vendors and the effectiveness of their stealth, and success rate in shipping from their country to yours. There should be enough information in the forums, stats, and feedback to make an informed decision. If there isn't don't risk it. You will find the success rate varies enormously, it all comes down to how creative and careful the seller is with their packaging; it is one of the main things that makes the difference between mediocre and great vendors.  I have had many SR transactions, all but a few domestic, and the only one that didn't work out was from NL. Canada to US or vice versa is considered fairly safe and I have had several successful shipments from CA.
