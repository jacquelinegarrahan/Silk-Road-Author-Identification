Just to address this part, if you lose your password, all that happens is that you can't log in and your orders are auto-finalized. The only way that you lose money is if you had funds left in that account or if one of your orders either doesn't arrive or is bogus. Otherwise you're fine.

Like Scout has said, it's best to just create a new account if you forget your password. In fact, that used to be the only way when I started out here. I didn't know they were resetting passwords now until I read Scout's post.

If you forget your pin, then you can't make any purchases or transfer any coins to any other member. That's it though.

That happened to me when they first implemented the PINs (they didn't always have  them). In that case, you have to use the maximum number of attempts to guess and let it lock your pin. Then you have to email support 7-10 days later and ask them to reset it for you (which they do promptly -- there's info on this on the SR site -- this is to the best of my memory as to what happened to me over a year ago).

Losing your PIN is more of a PIA than anything. I THINK (not 100% sure) that if you lose your password and start a new account, they can move your buyer's stats to the new account if you can provide enough information on the old account to support.

All of this is in the wiki I believe.
