Title: Re: What are your favorite songs to trip/roll/get high to?
Post by: gorrillahunter on June 03, 2013, 08:17 pm
Most important thing here is to have a good sound system that will make you feel sound even when you're sober. It doesn't have to be too fancy I use my car which has only 1 12" kicker L5 attached to a 500 watt rms sub d amp and with all aftermarket interior speakers recieving ~100 watts rms each from a 4-way amp.  If you don't access to such a system, the second best option is a high quality set of cans. :D

Instructions: It's probably a good idea to have tell a buddy what to do in the event that you are tripping too hard to remember what to do next. Take a heavy dose of lsd- 250 mics at least. I like 500 or more personally. But then I do enjoy going super deep. The more the merrier. Wait a few hours until you're peaking. Then smoke a bowl or two of quality herb (sativa or a sativa dominant strain works best). This is the point where a buddy might have to step in because you should be at that point where planning goes out the window and trying to find anything, especially a song on your device, or even the name of the song you're looking for should be almost impossible to do. Now, play the song called "Relocation" by Feed Me that you can find in their album aptly named "Escape from Electric Mountain" album.

Observed results: I've blasted through time and space and lost all sense of reality while I was simultaneously being ripped apart cell by cell, atom by atom, with each atom being enveloped in waves of euphoria. That's me though, but since my discovery, I have made all of my friends experience this song and I have seen them cry with happiness, squirm and jump with joy, and even the most atheist of them made remarks that would have made any passing stranger think that they were having a life-changing religious experience. Results may vary, but everyone that has done everything as described above has said things like "oh my God," and "holy shit." Afterwards, you will understand what Feed Me meant when he once stated, "I don't even make music anymore." Trust me, It will be worth your time.

Title: Relocation
Album: Escape from Electric Mountain
Artist: Feed Me
