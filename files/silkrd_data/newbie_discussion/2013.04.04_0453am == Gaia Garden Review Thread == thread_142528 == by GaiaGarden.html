Here's my review of the samples I received from the new cannabis vendor Gaia Garden. GG sent me very generous samples of two different strains- Alaskan Ice and Prophet. He is a cool guy to talk with and is definitely very knowledgeable about what he does. Judging from the quality and generosity of the samples he provided, it's safe to say GG is fully committed to his customers' satisfaction and to building a successful business here on SR. I'm happy to be one of the first to give him the praise he deserves. I have a feeling there will be many more to come.

On to the review...

Shipping (5/5) - I placed my order for the samples late Thursday night/early Friday morning. GG informed me that he would put the package in the mail first thing that morning. It arrived today, Monday, so he was definitely on top of it. No worries in this department. You should have no issues with shipping/response time when dealing with GG.

Stealth/Packaging (5/5) - This was very well done, as well. The outside of the package was inconspicuous and discreet, with printed labels and clean, professional packaging methods. No sloppy tape, rips, etc. On the inside, it was similarly well done. I had asked GG to label the samples, which he did, and they were double vac sealed with plenty of visual barrier on all sides. The visual barrier also doubled as a clever way to add a little more weight to the package, making it seem like something else entirely. Not a bit of smell until breaking the last seal on the buds. Again, no worries in this department. GG has you taken care of.

Product (5/5) - The first sample that I tried was the Alaskan Ice. Clearnet pic -----> http://i.imgur.com/eLE3yVb.jpg

I am somewhat familiar with this strain, but it has been quite some time since I have had the chance to get my hands on some. I was quickly reminded of what a great strain this is. Upon initial inspection, I was greeted with a pungent, spicy aroma. The undertones were almost skunk-like, but not quite; I would describe them as piney. It was sort of like what you would envision an exotic rainforest to smell like; just really bright and clean. The buds are a mixture of light and mid green with bright orange hairs crawling all over. These buds were given a nice trim job, as well. Properly cured, with a nice density, but just the right amount of "give" when giving the bud a gentle squeeze. The bud is coated with a thick layer of sticky trichs. And I mean STICKY. It is so sticky that it is almost a bit of a chore to grind up, but nevertheless it ground up pretty well and, as expected, produced a very nice amount of kief. I can't imagine trying to break this bud down by hand.

The Alaskan Ice has a taste that is similar to its smell. There is more of a fresh, earthy undertone on inhale, while the exhale provides the pleasant spiciness that is present in the scent. After vaping a good sized bowl, the high crept up and set in quickly. The sativa dominance was apparent almost immediately as a warm, euphoric state of mind took over. Just a bright, clean high with a nice alertness and sense of awareness. Best described as a "soaring" type of high, in my opinion. The indica traits are noticeable as well, although obviously less so. But you still get a nice bit of stoniness that sets in later on in the high and provides a bit of a mellow, relaxed feeling. However, the cerebral effects are on full display at all times and are clearly the redeeming quality of this strain. A very enjoyable strain to experience.

I moved on to the Prophet a few hours later. Clearnet pic -----> http://i.imgur.com/aNReoHO.jpg

This was my first experience with this strain, and it definitely did not disappoint. This strain had less of a pungent aroma than the AI, but it's still worthy of noting. The scent was slightly more musty, although not overpowering, with floral hints. Also a bit of a hash undertone. The bud is a bright lime green with reddish-orange pistils curled around it. A bit more leafy than the AI, but still well trimmed, and covered with glistening trichs. The Prophet was quite a bit more dense than the AI as well. These are some rock hard nuggets. They were clearly well taken care of during every step of the growing process.

I broke out my glass for this strain since my girlfriend had commandeered my vape. The inhale was smooth, but potent. The taste was quite the contrast to the AI, with the main difference being the dominating presence of kush. Upon exhale, a bit of citrus is noticeable, along with the undertones of hash that are present in the scent. A nice lung expander, for sure. The bud was flushed properly and burned to a light white ash. The high was also in direct contrast to the AI. This is an indica dominant strain which provides a mellow, peaceful full body experience. The heady buzz is still there in nice balance, but the relaxing, somewhat narcotic effects are what this strain is all about. I sank into the couch after smoking and was just in one of those familiar trances. Fairly long lasting effects, as well, when compared to many other modern strains. Another very enjoyable experience.

Overall (5/5) - I am very impressed with Gaia Garden. He delivered in every aspect of the transaction, and I would certainly not hesitate to do business with him in the future. As I stated earlier, he was very generous with his samples. Thanks for that, GG. I fully expect him to be successful here on SR if he continues to provide product and service of this quality. Thanks again GG, and I look forward to dealing with you again sometime soon.
