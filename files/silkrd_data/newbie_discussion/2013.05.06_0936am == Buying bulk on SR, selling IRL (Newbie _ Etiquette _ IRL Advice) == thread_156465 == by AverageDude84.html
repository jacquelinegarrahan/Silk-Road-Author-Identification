So far the most popular stories of people being caught for using the SR were bulk buyers and afk sellers.. Just putting it out there.
If you insist on dealing after all here's a few tips:
a) Don't be open about it.
b) Be aware whenever traveling with stock but also play cool.
c) Have the middleman refer to you as "a friend".
d) Introduce yourself whenever needed with a fake name (make it sound real.build a story around it just in case you can't dodge questions)
e) Be straightforward. The more of an authority you present the more people (partners/customers) behave themselves. (it's not about being an asshole it's about being professional)
f) Don't sell to junkies.. That's just miserable.
g) Safety/Anonymity>Ease of trading. Put high standards and keep them, whatever that might include.
h) If you feel unsure about a deal/transfer/w/e simply don't do it. Getting busted/killed/w/e is not worth the potential bucks (also known as opportunity cost).
i) Get to know how the local police force works.
j) Split money/stash/people's perceptions. "The shade" is your friend.
k) Take in account other dealers, their markets, their whereabouts.(competition becomes dangerous)
l) Apply all of the above but take in account your local factors which i may not know of to warn you about.(Warn yourself!)
I'm sure there are people who know much more than me and that most of this advice is pretty much common logic and there certainly are more things to cover.
But i think that by considering these you'll have a solid beginning (don't rush it)

This a tough thing to give advice on. Depends on a lot of different factors.

Whats been working for me is buying in bulk and having a a couple close friends go and work it for me, since i can toss em some for a good price and still make money, while they're out there on the street working it.
Helps reduce a little risk on my end, and everyone's happy.

Of course it wouldn't really be a good idea for you to jump into that, I've been working this stuff for a couple years now starting from the ground up, and its from all that experience going up the ladder while being cautious that I've learned what I know now.


The only solid advice I feel like i can give you,
1. Save your money. Don't put all your eggs in one basket. In the words of Wu-Tang "Diversify yo bonds nigguh" I've started from getting 2-5g at kept putting all my money into it until i could get 20g with all that i had and lost it all. Don't do that. Always make sure you have at least a small fund to get you back on your feet if things go wrong (this could also include an "incaseofemergency lawyer fund")
2.BE FUCKING CAREFUL. This means keep shit off the phone. Fuck texts, and be careful when you call. Don't deal with shady fuckin' kids who are naive and dont know what they're talking about. Even if they pay good money for a product, bad customers can bring down a whole operation, especially if they know a little more than they should. Try to keep your clients as cool as possible. All the people I sell to are people that would come over and hang out if I had a barbecue or something, they're people I can consider good friends.
3.Take a break. The only dealers IRL that I've known to make bank and be able to spend the money outside of prison are those that stopped selling drugs. This is an illegal market with law enforcement agencies getting lots of funding to bring people like us down. They have more resources than most of us. Unless your smart as fuck and willing to go to great lengths to hide your illicit operation behind a legit one, take breaks, or set a goal to reach so you can eventually stop. I have a buddy that was selling lbs. of dispensary weed for a few months. He made thousands on a weekly basis, and once he had enough money to pay for a cheap house and fill it with new furniture, he stopped and got a new place.
4. Watch your greed. Everytime I've made a mistake it was cus i was desperate for inventory and was willing to ignore my gutfeeling that said WHAT ARE YOU DOING?!!?!
if something doesn't feel right, its usually because its not. Only buy/sell to people you are comfortable with and know that it will go smooth.


Some people are cut out for this line of work, others just aren't.

Post by: maple on May 08, 2013, 05:16 pm
Lots of good stuff here.

I would keep in mind the possibility of having to dump everything. Ideally, store the bulk of your product away from your house, only keep enough to cover any reasonable demands, like maybe an oz of weed or 20-50 tabs, but not so much that you cannot quickly dispose of it if the cops come. Without any drugs to point at, their ability to convict you will be greatly reduced.

I would just sell to friends. If they want to introduce someone new, that person has got to become a friend first, and be very selective about who you trust to be a friend. This will help weeding out the shady customers although it will not protect against a customer turning snitch, that is pretty much gut luck and planning on a case by case basis.
