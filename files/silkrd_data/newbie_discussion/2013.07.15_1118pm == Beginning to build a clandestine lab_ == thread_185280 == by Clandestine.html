this might give you an idea - look for an old chicken farm. Back in the 70s i bought a small 15 acre farm with a shit house (siding was roof shingles made to look like brick) but it had a huge chicken barn - about 150 ft long X 25 ft wide, with full electric svc (there were huge electric radiant ceiling heaters, so the place was already wired with decent svc.

I ended up moving up to canada for a few years and gave it to a realtor to rent for me - found out when i got back, some kids had rented it, and boarded up the huge chicken coop - chicken coop from about 4 ft off the ground up to about 8' ft mark was just chicken wire for ventilation. Anyway they were growing maryjane, industrial scale. The problem, and their stupidty, they might as well have put up a sign saying look at me - they put a huge gate on the driveway with "NO TRESPASSING" signs on it, and had german shepherds patrolling the property  - they got bustef. Point of story is blend in, neighbors notice odd activity.

Reason you'll stand a good chance to find one - i had looked into raising chickens  commercially- everything is done under contract with one of the big chicken processors, like perdue - and they fuck you in the ass bad - you agree to buy the feed from them, price on feed will fluctuate, and agree to sell them the meat on a pre-agreed on price. If feed price, utility costs go up you're screwed - if chickens get infected with whatever bird flu strain, they have to be destroyed. Long and short of it, there are a lot of small chicken farms that go under

if it helps
