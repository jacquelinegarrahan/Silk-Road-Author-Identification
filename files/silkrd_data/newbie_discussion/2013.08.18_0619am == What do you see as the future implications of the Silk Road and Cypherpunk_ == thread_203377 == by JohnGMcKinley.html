Post by: Donna Freck on August 18, 2013, 07:42 am
There's some hope, sure, but right now, unless things change, it's a quiet, shadowed hope. Hope for closets, anonymous back rooms, maybe dark apartments after everyone's removed their cell phone batteries and tossed everything in the refrigerator. Silk Road and its attendant technologies are absolutely positive developments, but I wonder for how long they'll continue to work (strong cryptography will get stronger and likely be around for a while, naturally---but side channel attacks are always a risk). There will be more monitoring: across the United States there will be a marked increase in public CCTV surveillance. New York's the laboratory, London (and elsewhere) the example. And something like the Chinese internet may be in our not-too-distant futures, who knows. I'm rambling, headached, stringing together a fistful of half-formed thoughts.

I don't want to reside in East Germany, perpetually living out the dissident playwright's life in The Lives of Others, speaking honestly to a select few and even then not knowing whom to trust.
