Ok here's what I do.  I order lets say for example 100mg fent--it comes in a little baggie, its fluffy white powder.

I have a glass nasal spray bottle with a plastic top.  I find this kind is best: (clearnet)
 
http://www.cheyee-cn.com/products/03_mist_spray_bottles/01_nasal_spray_bottles/02_PET_Nasal_Spray_Bottles.JPG

(end clearnet)

 Just google around til you find some company who sells them; or alternatively you can go to the drug store and buy a bottle of saline or other kind of nasal spray and just empty it out and clean it and use that (you want to avoid stuff that has a lot of weird ingredients or mentholated stuff in it IMO).  I prefer transparent bottles so I can see what Im doing, how much is left, etc. 

The most important things in choosing a bottle are a)the bottle's top/spray mechanism can be screwed off and removed entirely, so you can clean it and fill it, b) the bottle releases measured doses (this is why I like the kind of bottle above), and c) The capacity of the bottle, and the number of sprays per bottle, is CLEARLY printed on the box so you know exactly how many fl ozs, or ml or whatever measurement of liquid, it holds.

While you're at it buy some saline.  I just use the kind for contact lenses, without preservatives, but you can make your own with salt and water.  You need to use mostly saline because plain water gets really uncomfortable in your sinuses (remember getting water up your nose in the pool as a kid?), and saline is much more comfortable.

The important part: mixing your spray.

The safest way to do this is just to empty the whole packet into the bottle, then you'll divide the total mgs by the amount of sprays in the bottle and that will tell you what the dose is per spray.  So 100mg in a bottle with 200 sprays is 500mcg per spray.

If you want to buy bigger amounts of fent in the first place (cuz its cheaper in bulk) or use a smaller amount per spray, either buy a bigger bottle (100 mg divided by 400 sprays is 250mg per spray) or use a smaller amount of fent in the bottle to begin with. 

You can do the latter by measuring out a smaller amount from your bag of fent (unreliable without a really expensive scale); or by eyeballing it (also very unreliable, but after doing this for awhile you'll get a sense of what 25mg or 50 mg looks like, and because you are preparing something with 200+ doses if you are off by even 10mg it wont really affect each dose in a huge way. 

But I still wouldn't eyeball until you have done this for awhile and know what kind of tolerance and dose leeway your body has.  If you're new to this method just buy the amount of fent you want to put in the whole bottle.  A good place to start IF you already have an opiate tolerance is 125 or 250mcg per spray.

If you dont already have an opiate tolerance, just don't do this at all.  Buy the blotters or the mannitol mix or something.  You need to have enough of a tolerance where if you're off by a few hundred micrograms per dose it wont kill you.

Finally, once you've figured out the measurements its time for the mixing itself.  I fill my bottle about halfway with saline, drop the powder in carefully, put just a little bit of super hot water in there, and then fill the rest with saline.  The hot water helps with dissolving, I've found, and you can add up to 10-15% water in the bottle without hurting your sinuses if the rest of the bottle is saline.

Then screw/attach the spray mechanism (hopefully with its cap still on) until its totally on there tight, and shake it up until you cant see any particles floating around anymore.

That's it!  Let me know if you have any Qs.

PS with this method I find I need at least 5 mins to let it kick in all the way, and the first dose sometimes more like 10.  Subsequent doses kick in faster.  But either way when they do kick in their kick in hard, with a nice warm glow and relief from pain.  So don't overdo it and respray until you're sure enough time has passed. 

Also I have been doing this with citrate and I just tried it with the HCL, which works well except it takes a bit to dissolve fully (maybe an hour or so total, with intermittent shaking).

The main difference with the HCL is not only is it stronger but it seems to kick in WAY faster and harder, and its easy to underestimate and do too much.  I would therefore recommend using the citrate for most people, unless your tolly is really sky high and you need each spray to be as strong as possible.  Either way start with the citrate until you get really comfortable with the whole process, both making and dosing.
