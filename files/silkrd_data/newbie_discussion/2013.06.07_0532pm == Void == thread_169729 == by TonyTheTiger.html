On the contrary, it does. If for example your order is domestic and you don't receive the package while the vendor insists that he sent it and you received it, if the vendor cannot PROVE that he really sent it and you - or someone other for you - got it (as with a DCN) then you, as a buyer, will get a full refund.

If the SR admins decide you are not worthy of a refund then you can be sure the fault is yours somewhere (as in the aforementioned case someone else took the package for you), because the burden of proof is always on the vendor, not the other way around.

Believe me, as it is now it is very difficult to get scammed if you follow the rules. If you don't FE the probability you will get scammed are practically 0.
