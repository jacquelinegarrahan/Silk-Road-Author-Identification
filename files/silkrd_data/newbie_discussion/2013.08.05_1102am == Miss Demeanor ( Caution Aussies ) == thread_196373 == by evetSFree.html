From my experiences warning signs to look out for:

- Insist on FE

- Customer reviews will often indicate that seller has not specialized in the product and/or Australia. But the seller will have over 100 transactions, almost all successful and generally 6+ months on SR

- Seller is generally slow to get orders out because this buys time because the scam is over when the first or more people reach a point that doubts turn into complaints, reports to SR and forum postings.

Nothing to do with customs.

- Don't be fooled by seeing a seller was be a vendor for 6+ months.  There trend for dormant vendors to sell their account with 1+ years on the road for a negotiable fee.  I have nothing against sellers having the opportunity to sell their business as a going concern, just don't like the idea of this being used for scams.

- The other signs are claims that tracking would require them or you to sign for the package. This is true for Couriers, USPS Express and above.  But USPS first class and/or priority, no signature is required.  It works similar to Australia Post where the seller can print the parcel labels and customs forms.  They do a normal mail drop, but they have basic tracking.  Tracking that will at least confirm the package was sent, and the date it leaves America.  Unfortunately after that point, you just wait, but from my experience it will only take 2-5 business days after leaving LA.  Better than nothing and shows the seller is not just a complete scam artist.
