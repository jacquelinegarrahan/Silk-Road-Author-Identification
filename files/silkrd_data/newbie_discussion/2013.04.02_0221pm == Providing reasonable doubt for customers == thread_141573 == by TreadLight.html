Title: Providing reasonable doubt for customers
Post by: TreadLight on April 02, 2013, 02:21 pm
When I start selling my shrooms I want my prospective customers to be as safe as possible. I know the rule of thumb is a buyer should simply act as he didn't order the package if he gets pinched. Perhaps set it aside for a couple days before opening it. I have a couple ideas for some extra insurance.

Idea#1: Say I ship powdered shrooms to look like herbal supplements or any other number of things. What if I include a fake invoice inside, for the said supplements. And what if the TO address on the invoice was a completely different name and address than the one the package was shipped to? Would that be an extra weapon in the defense attorney's arsenal?

Idea#2: What if I also include in bold letters a statement at the top of the invoice that says something like “In the event you received this package in error please contact the following email address for dispensation instructions”. And if someone emails that address they will receive an automatic reply stating something like “This is an automatic response. The package you inadvertently received contains a controlled substance. For your own safety do not consume it. I periodically ship such packages to random addresses to throw off law enforcement from my illicit activities. Feel free to dispose of the package in any way you see fit or to turn it in to law enforcement. Sorry for the inconvenience.”

Alright, I admit that might be overkill but imagine LE does catch your customer receiving the package. Wouldn't that be more than enough reasonable doubt to get anyone off? Is there a downside to either idea?
