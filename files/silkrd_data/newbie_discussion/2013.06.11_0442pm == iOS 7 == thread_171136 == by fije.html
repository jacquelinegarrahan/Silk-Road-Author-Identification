- The font can be too thin for viewing in direct sunlight
- Same for the white/greyness of the folders
- The default apps icons are too bright and contrast too much on each other
- The blur used for the effect on the task bar and other overlays is too blurred and generally makes most backgrounds too muggy compared to the rest of the background

- The pop-up overlay quick config thing looks messy
- The drop-down notifications screen is much darker than the quick config pop up and the rest of the system, which feels like a spare wheel
- On the lock screen, it says "Swipe to unlock", however there's an swipe up icon below this text (for the quick config screen). That is a direct contradiction when your making very quick decisions. I keep trying to swipe up to unlock the device despite having used an iPhone for years now
- The system prefs are basically as long-winded and messy as they were before
- NO DEFAULT APPS!?!? What were they thinking

Phew.. glad I got that off my chest.

The only improvement I can see if the do not disturb feature can now be set to block calls when you're using the device also, and you can now block people via individual contacts.
