As I understand it, SR will automatically tumble(I.E. pass through multiple addresses) your transfer, according to the buyer's GUide:

Quote
Just when you thought Silk Road couldn't be more secure, we went one step further. The tumbler sends all payments through a complex, semi-random series of dummy transactions, each with a new, one-use receiving address, making it nearly impossible to link your payment with any coins leaving the site. The quantity, frequency, and number of transactions are all varied chaotically in a way that mimics the transactions of the Bitcoin economy as a whole.
Title: Re: A few questions after doing some research, please help a noob!
Post by: ripthesystem on August 08, 2013, 08:36 pm
Where you buy the BTC is really just personal preference, and most of the services have little difference. There is no real danger using a bank account because there is nothing illegal about buying BTC and ideally you cover the trail anyway.  If you want to be on the safe side then it is indeed wise to ''tumble'' the BTC through one or several other wallets before sending them to your SR wallet. Also to be safe, use PGP encryption (you can find threads explaining how) when sending info like your address.

All in all, be cautious, but do not worry too much. Small amounts for personal use are not generally the target of law enforcement.
