DPR brought up the possibility of allowing buyers to revisit and raise feedback, but not lower it. That idea sounds like a solution to the problem of accidentally leaving a lower rating than intended.

The reasoning behind having feedback final is to avoid some emotional buyers that get impatient and leave low ratings because their product is still in transit, which hurts a seller's potential business during the time said buyer's purchase is in the mail. With set in stone feedback I'd think there would be less of this, i.e. buyers would wait to receive the product to leave feedback, and there would be less emotional rating bashing because a buyer wouldn't want to be blacklisted.

Just my .0002 BTC :)
