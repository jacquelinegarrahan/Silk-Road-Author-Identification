Quote from: mrmoonman on July 17, 2013, 02:41 am
do you still get charged if that happens? Like what happens if they seiz it? Come arrest you whenever they want?

Sounds like we're talking about the U.S. here. In which case, if they seize the package, what happens is that you receive a "love letter" in the mail, which is customs letting you know that a controlled substance was attempted to be sent to you via the postal system. They'll dispose of it. You can trash the letter and do nothing, and usually nothing else happens. It's my understanding that you might get an offer to "appeal" to try to accept the package and appeal your reason for ordering the substance, but this is essentially an admission of guilt and unless you have a really solid LEGAL reason for being sent the substance, this is where you might be charged since they now have evidence that you attempted to purchase the controlled substance.

So if you get a letter, do nothing, and the majority of the time the issues end there. But your address will likely be scrutinized a bit more by the post/LE so don't use it to order illegal things for awhile - I'd likely never use that particular address again.
