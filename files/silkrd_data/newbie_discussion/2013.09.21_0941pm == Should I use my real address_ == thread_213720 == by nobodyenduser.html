Title: Re: Should I use my real address?
Post by: DaTherapist on September 22, 2013, 01:18 am
Everyone has their own preferences.  Personally, I like to use either my own address or po box with a business name on it, or send it to a friends place and put a business name on it.  This way, if you get a slip on your door to pick it up at the post office, you can always use the "I wasnt expecting anything.....I just got a slip saying I missed a package", if LE decides to be there waiting for you.  But if your name is on it...it's kind of difficult to use that excuse when the name on your ID matches the name on the package (sure...someone other than you could have sent a package to your name but REALLY?  Rare. Much more likely to send to the wrong address by messing up the numbers accidentally).  I like the real address / fake name or business name method.  But it's all what you're comfortable with that you should do.

Title: Re: Should I use my real address?
Post by: DaTherapist on September 22, 2013, 01:36 am
I have never had a problem with delivery using my real address and business name.  And then I have any one of my "receivers" go pick it up at the post office if a slip is left.  Been doing this for a looooong time.  Over a decade.  And yes it works in apartment buildings, my receivers have all lived in apartments.  The only issue is if your vendor gets the address wrong.  Then things can get a little tricky, because you have to go to the post office and pick it up with your tracking number if it says a slip was left, meaning that you were looking for a package vs a slip being left at YOUR address and you going to pick it up with the slip, saying you were NOT expecting a package, if anyone questions you.   It's rare to have anything go wrong though where they do a controlled delivery.  I've had numerous letters sent from US customs, but never a controlled delivery in over 10 years.
