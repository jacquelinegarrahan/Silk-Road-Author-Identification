Hargenflargen,

Without knowing which reagent tests you performed and a more detailed description of the progression of the color change that you observed there is absolutely no basis upon which anyone could offer you even a wild ass guess. There are alot of testing reagents out there, so it is impossible this point to make any guess whatsoever about the identity of the substance.

Less is definitely more whenever your performing a reagent test, and a few tiny grains of the substance that is just big enough so that you know where to place the drop of reagent from 4 inches or so away is just the right amount. A little residue will actually give you much more information that a small little pebble.   I take a clean clip from a Bic Pen, and simply rub it against the sample a few times until I see a little powder on it and then gently tap it onto a plate.

Why don't you try performing your tests again, and posting your observations for each reagent.

We all have our first times doing something, however testing your drugs before ingesting them is the smartest thing you can ever do, so lets work at this until you are comfortable performing the reagent tests.
