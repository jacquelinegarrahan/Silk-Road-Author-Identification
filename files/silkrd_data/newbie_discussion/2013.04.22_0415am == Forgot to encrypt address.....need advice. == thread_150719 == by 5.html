Post by: BlueGrippelrunt on April 22, 2013, 04:22 am
Hi! My first post as well! Haha

Anyhow, to the issue...
The system is tuned so the only ppl to see your address, even unencrypted, is you and the vendor. I think there's something in the wiki that explains the protections in place already to the address 'hand-off' system.

Encrypting it w/ PGP is a step you can take to make 1000% sure that it can't be intercepted. I think that the SR system is pretty ironclad as is. I wouldn't stress too badly, just be sure to encrypt it from this transaction forth for the peace of mind. :D
-------------------------------------------------
EDIT - Further reassuring info...

Here's the info direct from SR (see link below). According to the wiki (like you mentioned) from the point you submit an order till the vendor reads it it is encrypted by the site and NOT EVEN SR STAFF CAN READ IT. Once the vendor confirms the item has shipped the address is irretrievably deleted. So like I said before, the system is damn secure so you needn't worry too much about sending it as is. Just PGP encrypt it in the future so you can sleep a bit better at night. lol

http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/wiki/index.php?title=Buyer%27s_Guide#Receiving_address

P.S. Also, be careful with FE, lots of people say never to do it, because from what I hear reputable vendors shouldn't require it. I have heard many stories of people being screwed by FE. I guess if the vendor is super reputable it might be ok, as I've also seen very reputable vendors ask FE of everyone. Just be careful, FE makes you vulnerable to exploitation.
