Quote from: dfgdfgdfsgdsf on June 28, 2013, 09:27 am
Now the question is, how securely is tor connection encrypted. I mean can my provider listen the e-traffic and provide proofs that i applied  to this web site made order and gave the address where to send? ...
How can they find out which computer was used to connect to this web site?

Your tor connection is strongly encrypted. But most importantly it's anonymous, meaning your tor traffic can not be tracked back to your home IP. At all. (Unless you are using one of those clearweb -> tor proxies. Don't use those.)

Quote from: dfgdfgdfsgdsf on June 28, 2013, 09:27 am
Should i destroy the Ethernet adapter in this computer... they have my ethernet adapter and prove that it was MAC-address on this computer.

Your MAC address never leaves your local network. Meaning only your wifi router will know it. You can also change it at any time. So it is totally irrelevant. Even if LE were running a website that you connected to they wouldn't have your MAC address. And even if they did, you can change it any time you like with software - it proves nothing.

As others have said, if this story were true (which it likely isn't) the reason for a cop takedown will be the package stealth, or possibly a fake vendor. Tor will not be the weak point.

Quote from: dfgdfgdfsgdsf
Can someone explain if tor is enaugh secure why people use pgp than?                                                                 

You're conflating two different things. Tor is secure and anonymous - nobody SR can see what you're doing on SR, and nobody at all can tell that it's you who is connecting to SR. PGP is used to ensure that even the SR operators can't read a message, only the intended recipient can. Given how sensitive addresses are, this makes sense, in case of a compromise of SR. PGP also allows secure messaging outside of SR if necessary, and again the messages can only be read by the recipient, regardless of what mail server or whatever they sit on.
