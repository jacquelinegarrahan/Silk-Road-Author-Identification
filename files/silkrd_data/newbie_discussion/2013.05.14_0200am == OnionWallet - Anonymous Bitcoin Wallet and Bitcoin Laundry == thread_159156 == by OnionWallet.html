Live and learn. Don't let it ruin your experience entirely. Reading and learning is good, but I strongly suggest you ask questions before you do it the first time. Get a reality check. It is very easy for someone to see what they want to be true, and then do it based upon that easy belief. The one thing you can be sure of here, is if you've justified a stupid decision in your mind, we will tell you about it :)

My suggestion is to keep it simple, especially if you aren't as familiar/skilled with this sort of technology. Get your own wallet locally - the bitcoin wiki on clearnet has many suggestions on how to do this. Always send your bitcoins there. Hosted wallets all suffer from the same potential issue: you are trusting someone else with your money for one, and for two you never know when/if they're going to be shut down. If they get taken offline, your money is as good as gone. Use the bitcoinfog link found earlier in this thread to transfer from your personal wallet to SR. Making a wallet locally, is easy, and there is no limit to how many you can make. You can make a new one for each transaction, each and every time, and you can burn it afterwards.
