Two weeks should be enough time. Just be sure your address is exactly correct to prevent delays:
                                                                                                 John Doe
                                                                                                 1234 Anywhere Ln.
                                                                                                 City, State Zipcode
                                                                                                 USA

If you have 14 days until you move, you should be fine. However, delays can occur, so it may be worth waiting until you move. If you order, it does not arrive, and then you move, you could just have all mail forwarded to your new address the second you move. However, if the letter is already in transit, I don't know how likely it would be that they would return to sender if perhaps you move, the mail is delivered and then it sits in your old mailbox for a few days until the postman takes it back after a few days of mail piling up. You should contact the post office to find out how they would handle the situation.
