Points of contact with LEO's

buying - Just like buying on the street, you need to be informed in order to buy safely. On the street, you rely on word of mouth, reputation, and respect when determining if it's going to be a safe buy. Utilizing the feedback system and vendor reviews from other users is the key to staying informed. Also being active on the forums is another great way for you to connect with the vendors and speak with them on a personal level. After you select a vendor and complete your purchase, you will need to send some money to the vendor(or LEO!!!). Use your bitcoins! And if you're extra paranoid like me, you can use a bitcoin mixing service as well. (Read about mixing here hxxp://www.bitcoinfog.com/) This(the buy) is going to be the first instance where your pseudo-identity and real identity will cross paths. The vendor has the upper hand here as he is the ONLY one who can remain completely anonymous. It's just the nature of the beast. The vendor is going to need your real address and your real name so they can mail your product to you. People use fake names and addresses all the time. Some times this works, most of the time the package is undeliverable. My only advice here is to use extreme discretion when selecting your vendor because the product in which you're buying will eventually touch your hands no matter how much cloak and dagger (you utilize. This is normal but still risky. Use gpg to encrypt your message to the vendor by using their public key. You can read more about gpg and how to use it here hxxp://gpg4usb.cpunk.de/ ) At the top of the newbie section, there's a gpg practice area with loads of helpful people that will have a short encrypted conversation with you if you want some practice. A decent vendor should destroy your records after the transaction is complete.

Accepting Delivery: Have it delivered to your house or you can get a fake id for about $150-200 and use that to open a po box. Do some research. This part isn't that big of a deal. Just make sure you never sign for any packages, and any package that gets delivered to you should have "return to sender" written on it IMMEDIATELY and be placed by your door. Let it cool off for a day or two. Most of the LEOs are trying to get you to throw away your plausible deniability(i swear your honer, i had no idea what was in the box. some asshole must have sent this to me just to get me in trouble.
