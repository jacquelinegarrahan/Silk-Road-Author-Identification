Title: Re: Noob security questions
Post by: Hitch on August 13, 2013, 02:36 pm
   Yes, SR tumbles coins on the way in and out. Individual bitcoins, and their decimal divisions, are untraceable, but the process of transferring is forever recorded in the blockchain. If you know a wallet address you can track the transactions it's involved in. Tumbling is a means of obscuring the chain of transactions.

   You can use bitfog, for a fee, but not necessary if you ensure that who/where you purchased coins from doesn't have any trace of your identity. You can then create a couple of online wallets, each from different providers, always using tor and switching identities at each stage. Jump your coin through the wallets and then onto SR, spend with confidence :)

   For example

   Log onto tor

   Visit online bitwallet site A and create anon throwaway wallet 1, record the password, username, wallet address

   Switch tor identities

   Visit online bitwallet site B and create anon throwaway wallet 2, record the password, username, wallet address

   Switch tor identities
   
   Visit localbitcoins.com

   Purchase coins anonymously from vendor. Once released into your localbitcoin wallet transfer to wallet 1

   Switch tor identities

   Login to wallet 1 and transfer coin to wallet 2

   Switch tor identities

   Log into wallet 2 and transfer coin to SR wallet

   Switch tor identities

   Log into SR and wait for coin to hit, then create a new SR wallet address for future use. Never use those throwaway wallets again.
