Consider if we use spendbitcoins.com or similar we pay 10% commission, the vendor then loses 8% to SR comission, and then i suppose they lose another 10% cashing out bitcoins to cash? So say a vendor gets close to 30% less than how much it costs us to buy (please update me if this is correct.

So for a listing costing $260, it actually costs us $260 + 10% = $286. Vendor would get $260 - 18% = $210 approx?

$210 does not seem TOO outrageous to me, but perhaps as buyers are paying the 10% commission buying the bitcoins it seems fair that the vendor can cover the costs of cashing out bitcoins (10%) in their margin, and both vendor and buyer go halves in the SR commission?

If the above was the case, buyers would be paying $210 + 14% = $240, and vendor would cashout $210 - 14% = $180.

I think the vendors are charging buyers all the extra costs at the current listings prices....perhaps with a bit more vendor competition we would see vendors willing to cover some of the commission costs? At this stage tho, I think the high prices really are reflecting the fact that buyers are covering all the lost commission costs.
