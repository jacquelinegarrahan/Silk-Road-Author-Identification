Title: Re: valium or something
Post by: SourGrapes on June 22, 2013, 09:01 pm
valium/diazepam (valium is a brand name for diazepam pills) is the longest-lasting of the benzos, and takes about 1.5hrs to fully kick in. it also builds up in your system, so if you're taking it daily, after a while you can lower your dose. this is not the case with other benzos.

xanax/alprazolam (again, same thing) is much more short-acting. i believe generally it's the most abused benzo, the one people tend to like to drink with (though it's dangerous and blackout-inducing) and when i've had prescriptions for it among other benzos, the one people most want to buy from me. it lasts 3-4 hours or so. if i take it when i dont need it for anxiety, though, xanax is the most likely benzo to make me just fall asleep.

lorazepam/ativan and klonopin/clonazepam are mid-range in the half-life, last around 4-6 hours. klonopins always made me feel a little more buzzy than ativan.

there's also etizolam, a short-acting benzo analogue, which is kind of weird to me... makes me feel a bit drunk on its own, and induces a little too much amnesia for me in higher doses, but in lower doses it has a bit of a mood lift and a silliness to it that i really enjoy on occasion.

for the record valium is my favorite, but not to drink with -- i use it for general anxiety. i'd say if you're dead-set on drinking with benzos (which i will perfunctorily say is a bad, bad idea) i would stick with a short-acting one like xanax so you can gauge the effects more quickly and not be zonked out in the morning. start with 0.25mg or less.
