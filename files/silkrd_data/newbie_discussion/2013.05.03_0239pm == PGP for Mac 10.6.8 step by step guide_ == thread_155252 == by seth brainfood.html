Post by: TemaculaSunrise on May 03, 2013, 02:46 pm
Hey I replied to your message on the PGP newbie thread.  You've done nothing wrong, when you create a new key pair there is a public and private key.  The public key is what you can put out there, keep the private to yourself or delete it or something, your PGP program keeps the private key as part of the trusted certificate.  You can always re-create a certificate too if you are paranoid and want to delete your certificate after you send or something, but its not necessary.

Again, your install is fine, nothing is wrong with what you're doing, post your public key here and I'll respond to it.  Check the menus at the top of your PGP program, there's either a clipboard within the program, or the tool creates a system icon that you can use as a shortcut for clipboard encrypting and stuff.  To export just your public key, there should be an option for "export keys' and not "export private keys'.

Nothing wrong or illegal about having a PGP program and its not sending out your private key to the internet so don't worry.  Again, here's my public key for you to try and import and send a message back:

Title: Re: PGP for Mac 10.6.8 step by step guide?
Post by: TemaculaSunrise on May 03, 2013, 07:16 pm
@gratefulheadofdead

So when you open the PGP program, it'l ask for a username/email and then password.  Write this password down, this is how you decrypt messages sent to you.  When you've created your key pair, it will be represented as a trusted certificate in your PGP program.  There should be a button or dropdown somewhere that says "Export key" which will create a file somewhere with your public PGP key.  This is what you send to others so they can send you private messages.

On the other side, to send encrypted messages you import someones public PGP key either through the clipboard or a file, in the PGP program there is an option for "import key" where you'll import the ----PUBLIC PGP KEY address.  Then, when you write your message and encrypt, you'll put the recipient as the name you have imported and send it out.

For a good test, create your own certificate and encrypt a message to yourself.  Take this PGP block and then decrypt it using your password from the beginning.  If you can see your own text then it works as intended.  Try to write me a message from my public key posted a few above, try to post your public key as well and I'll send you a message.
