Title: Re: How is one suppose to find the right SilkRoad address in the first place??
Post by: jekyll on June 08, 2013, 03:32 am
You would determine it the same way that you should evaluate any information that you find online.  If you google 'Silk Road drugs', you get the correct address in the first two search returns.  After you've seen the same address in a few different places, like when searching ofr other information, you know that it's probably correct.  It's more of a probability game than an exact science.

Phishing works by having you input your credentials (login/password) and then, instead of taking you to the real site, just steals your credentials.  So when a site allows you to register, you know that it's not a phishing site. So if you get to the SR page, or the forums page, and can't register, but can login, you might want to investigate further.  But a newbie needs to register.  If you went to the phishing site, you wouldn't be able to register because new users aren't useful to phishers.*

*There are other ways that you could be scammed by a fake site that let you register and then make deposits, but it would require a much more substantial effort be to spoof an entire site replica.  Phishers just spoof the login page.
