Title: Re: How do I transfer bitcoins from my Mt.Gox account to my SR wallet?
Post by: iliketurtles on February 17, 2013, 12:07 am
This one confused me for a day or so as well! Some googling did the trick for me :) I'll run you through it though.

Log into MtGox, then click on Funding Options on the left (under Trade and above Account History). Once there, there should be 3 options - Add Funds, Withdraw Funds and Redeem Codes -- you want Withdraw Funds. Click on that, then head on over to your Account page on Silk Road. It should have a line of text there with random letters/numbers -- copy and paste that (since it's your first time, it should tell you that it's your deposit address) into the Bitcoin Address on the MtGox/Funding Options/Withdraw Funds page, put in the amount you're transferring across and hit confirm! I pay the 0.005BTC fee, and have only done two deposits and both times selected it so I couldn't tell you the difference in time sorry. It took a couple of hours for my funds to appear in my SR account.

Hope this makes sense! :)

Title: Re: How do I transfer bitcoins from my Mt.Gox account to my SR wallet?
Post by: Remediless on February 17, 2013, 12:53 am
Guys you may want to 'tumble' your coins by transferring them through a couple of instawallets before you send them to your SR wallet. https://instawallet.org

MTGOX does not like the idea of money going from them direct to SR.

Use a couple of wallets before you send the money to your SR wallet as that way it can't be traced.

WRITE THE INSTAWALLET NO DOWN as if you forget it they can NOT help you get your money back.

Instawallet is free, anonymous and requires no signup.

The URL is the key to your wallet. If you lose it, nobody can be certain of which wallet is yours and the coins will remain unclaimed.

Properly backup your Instawallet address or key before receiving coins.
Only share your bitcoin address, NOT the wallet URL or key, with the public.
Be aware of who may have access to your browser's history or cache.
Never trust shared computers such as the ones found in internet cafes.
Please consider yourself warned: If you lose wallet's key or URL, your Bitcoins are lost, no matter the circumstances.
