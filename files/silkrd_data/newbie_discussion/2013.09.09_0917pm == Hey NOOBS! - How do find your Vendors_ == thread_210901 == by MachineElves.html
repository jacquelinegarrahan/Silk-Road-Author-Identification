Title: Re: Hey NOOBS! - How do find your Vendors?
Post by: gmanuk2k on September 09, 2013, 09:55 pm
INTERESTING QUESTION.

Replies to this will be useful for all "New Vendors" who are trying understand the though process of potential buyers.

Honestly, I think everyone will differ in their attitude and some will ALWAYS just play it safe, only sticking to high rated vendors and never "taking a chance on something new".

Personally, I love to try lots of varied products but what sways me is always the "branding", the profile and the initial communication.

I love to message new vendors and ask them a few questions, essentially asking them why i should buy from them!

It's their responses (their vibe) that generally sways a purchase of something new.

And on the question of locating the vendor, it's been a mixture of stumbling across a vendor in the forums or by searching by category and seeing something that jumps out......normally the image at first.

Hope this helps and would like to hear others comments  8)

Title: Re: Hey NOOBS! - How do find your Vendors?
Post by: Winky Doodle on September 12, 2013, 01:57 pm
With SR, I don't want to take any chances. I only buy from people with only 5 ratings, with very few exceptions. I don't want to be the one that gets the shitty packaging or the cut product, and if the vendors ratings are all 5 I don't have to worry about that.

I will only buy domestically unless I am really desperate to find a good deal. I don't want to risk anything with customs as I don't really know the process for sending packages to other countries.

The thing that really turns me off is high shipping, I have to say I've avoided a lot of vendors I would of bought products off of because of the high shipping.

I also find my vendors from either the best selling section or the lowest price listings (has to have good ratings) if I'm making a purchase and I have an extra .1-.3 Btc

I would be more inclined to buy off a vendor if say for every $400 you spend on their products, you get a free gift or % off you're next order.

Close up pictures don't really do anything for me, as long as it has good ratings I could care less what the picture is.

I would never buy from a new vendor. The best way to get me to buy from a new vendor would be giving out free samples or putting a reduced price on their products.

The last thing that gauges my interest in a vendor would be their listing has to be legit. If there's poor grammar and spelling mistakes everywhere and a shit title, I'm going to expect the vendor isn't that smart and has more of a chance to fuck up my order by either not packaging it right, trying to rip me off, or just being negligent.
