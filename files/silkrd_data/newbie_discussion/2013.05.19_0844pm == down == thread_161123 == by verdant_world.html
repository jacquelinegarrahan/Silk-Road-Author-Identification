Using that website is actually less safe than just putting you adress unencrypted. When browsing on an onion website, traffic is encrypted end-to-end so nobody can eavesdrop on your communications. Using PGP to encrypt your address is good in case SR's servers are compromised, which is why it's recommended, and it's pretty easy to use anyways. When you use a third party website, that website knows who you're communicating with and your address, which is NOT GOOD.
For example, the website would see the key belongs to "Marcel Ketman - ketman@tormail.org", and linked with your address, they pretty much know what you're up to, and have your real identity thanks to your address.

A good easy to use PGP software is gpg4usb.
