Post by: isallmememe on September 29, 2013, 12:15 pm
if you click on account at the top of your silk road page it will give you a long address that is just a series of lower and upper case letters and numbers. this is your silk road wallet address. you paste that code into your wallet on localbitcoins, put in how much you want to transfer and click send. it will show up in your silk road account usually in around an hour, sometimes a bit longer tho. as long as you make triple sure that you put the exact sr wallet address in then it will turn up. copy/paste to be sure but also best to check it letter by letter before you click send. otherwise you will send it to someone else and won't get it back.

there was a guy posting on here the other day saying he had another wallet address copied to his clipboard and put that in by mistake. reckons he lost quite a lot of money because of it. so always check before you click send

Post by: isallmememe on September 29, 2013, 04:04 pm
i'm not really familiar with sepa, but a lot actually say cash deposit, however most of the ones that just say bank transfer will also allow a deposit.

and no you can't give them your sr wallet. if they don't deposit into your lbtc wallet then they have no proof with lbtc that they finalized the deal. you would be able to make a dispute and win if they did that. besides when you open a transaction their btc to the value you are asking for go into escrow. lbtc release them after the seller confirms the deal is done.

transferring from your lbtc wallet to sr is easy tho.

finally you can never be 100% that something won't go wrong. but if you stick with sellers that have a good rep then it is very unlikely they are going to want to ruin that rep and lose on sales that would make them more money than what you've just sent them. but like with sr sellers, always check their history, rep and feedback to get an idea if they are a legitimate and consistent seller.
