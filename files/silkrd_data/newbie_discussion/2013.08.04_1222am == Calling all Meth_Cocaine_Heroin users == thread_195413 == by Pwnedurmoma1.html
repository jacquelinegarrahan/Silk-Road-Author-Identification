Title: Re: Calling all Meth/Cocaine/Heroin users
Post by: 5p1c4 on August 04, 2013, 02:28 am
I'm a recreational coke user who's also tried Meth and Oxycodone. You need to be on your guard when using these drugs not only because of their addictiveness, but also because of the withdrawal symptoms. I personally don't use Heroin or Meth because the combination of the intense high followed by unpleasant withdrawal symptoms make these drugs too difficult to manage, so it's easier just to stay away. Cocaine addiction is much more insidious, so I set limits ahead of time as to how much I'll use over the course of a month. A couple of times I exceeded those limits, and stopped using for a few months. Having the self-discipline to walk away from the drug is ultimately what separates the addicts from the recreational users.

Post by: james frazer on August 14, 2013, 02:29 pm
IMHO to become addicted a certain amount of dedication is needed. Curiosity alone isn't enough. If life isn't going well then the danger is much greater. Also people who are younger are more likely to get swept away by the excitement of it. If using stuff intravenously, the actual act of injecting becomes a powerful habit in its own right. As cryngie said earlier, taking an addictive or strongly habit-forming drug three days running is not a good idea, because it easily becomes four, then five... and, if the supply doesn't dry up, within a couple of weeks it's likely to have become a religion. Probably I was lucky coming across top quality psychedelics early on; my loyalties were already established! But with the ones you mentioned, it's not the addictiveness of the drug which is important; it's the addictive personality of the person taking it.
