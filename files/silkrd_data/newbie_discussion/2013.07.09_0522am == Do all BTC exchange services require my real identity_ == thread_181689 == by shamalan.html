Title: Re: Do all BTC exchange services require my real identity?

Quote from: shamalan on July 09, 2013, 05:22 am
Am I not able to purchase BTC off of prepaid debit cards with USD value?

AFAIK, places that sell btc generally won't accept payment in any form that can be cancelled (checks), have the charges reversed (debit/credit cards, Paypal), or in any other way allows for the purchaser to recant their payment as soon as they get their btc.  And just as the companies protect themselves by not accepting these types of payment, you also would not want to use any of them, as they all require your REAL info including your SSN...   besides, apparently besides having the US government to keep track of all our online communications, they also track our credit card and probably debit card transactions as well....   

Stay safe, Stay anonymous, get BTC with cash transfers under a fake name, address, etc...  or find someone who will accept a moneypak that you can only pay for in cash and are available at most stores - as trade for BTC.   Alsoe, , while checking the forums and site listings for someone who will trade their BTC for a moneypak, you will probably also find people who will sell you btc for cash in the mail.  This is probably a little riskier, so if you go that route, make sure you check the feedback that the cash in the mail for BTC vendor has, as well as how long he or she has been a vendor in the same place.  That can help you to determine how trustworthy is the person.  You should check those things anyway whenever you buy anything from a vendor for the first time on SR.

Best of Luck :)
