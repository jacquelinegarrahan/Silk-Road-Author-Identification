That being said if a buyer buys something off of me and they never receive it, they could always dispute the payment anyway right? So new or not, if they didn't have their product they'd be covered.

That being said all my packages would be sent with tracking (as I do with ebay) so that all parties can see when a product is delivered and to avoid scammers - even ebay has plenty of those.

If a vendor wants to dispute a buyers claim that they did not receive the product, the vendor is likely to get some of the money in dispute resolution. Going that rout, however, is bad for buyer and seller reputations. Some may try to take advantage of buyers and especially new vendors who care about their reputation.

Checking tracking can flag a package, especially if done from Tor (rumor). If the package is intercepted and you track it from your own computer, I believe there can be concerns there as well. So tracking good, giving out and using tracking both deserve some further stealth thinking.
