Title: Re: couple of FE questions from a newbie if you would be so good...
Post by: wishihada2JZ on August 12, 2013, 01:20 pm
When a product is shipped, as long as you haven't FE, your bitcoin remains safely in escrow in the event the seller is trying to scam you. Basically you pay your BTC to SR, which holds on to the funds, until you finalize the order. Ideally, you finalize your order when you receive your product, and SR releases the funds to the seller.

Many times you'll see "FE trusted seller" because the buyer might be sitting on the package for a few days before retrieving it. Some vendors might not appreciate the product reaching it's destination and still not receiving their payment.

Don't take this as a sign that just because you buy from someone for a very long time and you trust them that you might not get scammed. tony76 man. Look into that story. The short of it is he was a very very well trusted and major vendor on the road, one day announced a huge sale, and requested all of his customers FE on their purchases. Lo and behold, no one ever received their products, and because every order was FE, they couldn't get their BTC back. Maybe he took the money and ran, maybe he got arrested, who knows.

Moral of the story, never, ever, ever FE. There are plenty of vendors that do not require FE for new buyers. One more tip, no such thing as reading too much. You can never spend too much time reading the forums for new information and arming yourself with knowledge.

Hope this helped :)
