Title: Re: Sellers rank
Post by: farmer1 on August 13, 2013, 07:02 pm
The feedback ranking is how a vendor has been rated by buyers when they leave feedback.

The percentile ranking evenly distributes all SR vendors across a 1-100% range depending on a number of different factors. Essentially it tells you who is doing the most business.



You could have a small time vendor with low volume, but very happy customers. They may have a 100 next to their name but only be ranked in the top 50%.

A large vendor doing tons of business may be in the top 1% but less customer satisfaction, only earning a feedback ranking of 90.



I would advise you to put more weight on the feedback rating then the percentile ranking.

Title: Re: Sellers rank
Post by: Hitch on August 14, 2013, 12:12 am

   I think scanning through feedback is a better method for deciding whether a vendor is worth a try. The percentile score is calculated via a pretty complex algorithm with a whole bunch of variables that can skew ranking.

   For instance buyers with long history, great stats and heaps of coin spent effect the percentile score more than other buyers. So a vendor who has an almost perfect run, but then gets into a dispute with a solid buyer who gives the vendor a 1/5 will see the percentile rank drop quite a bit.

   Then there's those scammy vendor fucks who, after building good rep, do a mass fe scam, which can take a while to reflect in percentile ranking, but is usually pretty quickly obvious in feedback.
