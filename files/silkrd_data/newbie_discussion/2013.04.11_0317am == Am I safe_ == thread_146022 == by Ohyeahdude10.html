Post by: wsd23 on April 11, 2013, 03:30 am
You're fine. Buying BTCs isnt a crime (yet) so all anyone would be able to see is a record of you sending money to bitinstant, nothing illegal about that :). Not using PGP is a problem, especially because it links you (your address) to a drug buy with no guarantee that only the vendor is seeing it. PGP is such an easy thing to use and only takes about 30 seconds to use each time.

I would stop visiting clear-net sites, especially bitinstant on TOR as many exit nodes are known and that is a huge red flag to anyone that something 'suspicious' is going on. If you are truly paranoid about just buying the coins, find a open Wifi spot and buy it from there.

No one is ever 100% 'safe' as in the end you have to trust the vendor with your address, but really there hasn't been much LE attention (yet) on SR and BTCs in general, outside of parents calling the police about getting drugs in the mail.

If anyone asks what the coins are for, say you are donating to the Electronic Frontier Foundation or something boring like that. Plenty of 'legit' sites are accepting BTCs now.

You will be fine. But use PGP seriously.

Title: Re: Am I safe?
Post by: wsd23 on April 11, 2013, 04:41 am
Quote from: jjds101 on April 11, 2013, 04:22 am
Once you've bought the bitcoins, the trail pretty much ends there.  That's part of the point -- anonymous transactions.  Sending MTGox -> personal wallet -> SR is more than enough.

MG asks for ID sometimes so there really isn't any way around that.  Buying bitcoins is a completely legal and legitimate transaction.

Well not entirely true, all bitcoin transactions are 'tracked' through the blockchain, which is fundamental to the existence of the coins. Without a clear record of where the coins came from, it would be impossible to tell if someone had a legit coin, or just one they had created. Fortunately all of the data is rather obfuscated and isnt very interesting, just a time, amount and IP address, but there IS a record that exists. You can find them at blockchain.info and find your own payments just by searching for the transaction ID. Thankfully however its fairly easy to game the system, simply passing the coins to different wallets works well enough, and SR I believe 'washes' the coins for you when they are in escrow bouncing them from wallet to wallet to further hide the real transaction.

So while ya the info is MOSTLY anonymous, that doesn't mean it is theoretically impossible to trace.

Be safe out there :)
