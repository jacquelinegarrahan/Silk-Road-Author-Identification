Title: Re: Do vendors prices change with Bitcoin exchange rate?
Post by: captainjojo on April 24, 2013, 12:10 am
The vendors price is in fact in US$.  The system converts that amount to BTC based on the current weighted average value from MTGOX.

What this means is that as the BTC price climbs, the amount of bitcoins you need to purchase the vendors listing will in fact drop as the value of your BTC increases.  But the amount you pay is always equal to the price in US$ that the vendor listed the product at.

If the price of BTC drops then the amount of bitcoins you will need to purchase the listing goes up, but the price of the item is still the same in US$.

I don't know how often SR updates the BTC rate on the site, but from past experience it seems to be about every 30-60 minutes.

Several times I have placed an order for something only to find that I was short a small amount of BTC (between .05 and 1 BTC).  I just left the order sitting until the price of BTC went high enough that the BTC price of the order fell below what I had in my SR account, then immediately submitted the order, before the BTC could go back down.  It takes patience but it is certainly easier than going out and buying more BTC and transferring them to SR.

Title: Re: Do vendors prices change with Bitcoin exchange rate?
Post by: captainjojo on April 24, 2013, 02:17 am
Hedging is only active after you have placed the order.  Up until the point at which you actually place the order, the BTC cost of the item is rising and falling with the fluctuations in the BTC rate.

Once you submit your order, if it is hedged, you and the seller are insured against any rise or fall in the BTC.  If it drops the vendor will still get BTC equal to the value of the original order in US$.  If the order is cancelled you will receive back BTC equal to the US$ of the original order. In each instance there is a 4% fee for the service.

If the order isn't hedged and the price of BTC drops, when the order is finalized the vendor will receive BTC in the exact amount you placed in escrow, meaning they will lose money as the value of those BTC has dropped.  If however the BTC price rose, they will in fact make more money than they were originally expecting.

If you cancel and the order isn't hedged and the price dropped, you will take the loss as the BTC you get back will no longer be worth what they were when you placed the order, if the price rose you will actually be making money as the BTC will be worth more.
