What most people fail to mention is the come-up. You may feel sick and like you want to puke for an hour or so. During this stage, relax, lie down, close your eyes, and stay perfectly still. Title: Re: I just ate 1/8th of shrooms. Advice?
Post by: soviet on June 02, 2013, 10:22 am
As I type this it's been about 8 hours after I came down. I'll write a full trip report sooner or later but here's most of it:

I came up with minimal nausea, which was pleasant. I sat back down at the computer to check your suggestions and then I looked down at the computer desk and i'm all like "holy shit when did this table become so cool?". The lines and growth rings on the desk became rivers of flowing, pulsating, breathing brownness. I began smiling because I knew the trip had started, and then I started laughing because I pictured how funny I must have looked sitting there fuckin' smiling at a wooden table. This went on for a little while. As a matter of fact I'm sitting at the same table right now, looking at the magical lines trying to get them to flow again but it won't work :(

I saw breathtaking patterns and impossible colors in many things, but there were two noteworthy ones. The first of these was my own face. Shortly after the table became magical I went over to my mirror. I noticed that my pupils were dilated as fuck and started cracking up again. Then I focused more on my face and began seeing scaly-like reptilian patterns there. This got me laughing more. The other noteworthy visuals I got were on my cat. I just laid down next to him on the carpet and pet him for like an hour or two. His fur was so mind-bogglingly soft that it was like running my hands through a fluffy fog. The fur also produced ice-crystal like patterns that pulsated beautifully along with the cat's breathing, and turned to a light hue of purple occasionally.

After laughing and happily tripping for I don't know how long things got weird. The laughing went away. I sort of went from actively enjoying the experience to just riding the high as the trip became more intense on my mind. After a little bit of this I really wanted to go back to the euphoria and the laughter, but changing the music or setting didn't work. As I realized that nothing was working I asked myself whether I was starting to have a bad trip, but all sense of pleasant and unpleasant escaped my perceptive ability, if that makes sense. The only "unpleasant" thing I felt were a kind of urge to go back to the laughing stage and some confusion, but these were pretty minor. I remember going back to this post and reading a few replies and the post of "dirtybiscuitzz718" probably ended up saving me from some really scary shit.

I rode the laughless trip for who knows how long, and then BAM my fucking my mom comes home early. I begin panicking a little and hastily turn off the music, shut down tor and go to my room. I laid there pretending to sleep, all the while worrying that I had left something awry in the house and that it would give away that I was tripping. Luckily nothing was wrong and nobody came in my room, but I was still buggin' the fuck out. When I closed my eyes to pretend to sleep I got a brand-new set of visuals. These weren't open-eyed visuals or closed-eye visuals, but rather they felt like I was seeing them from my frontal lobe or the inside of my forehead. These were very intense. They were pretty much fractals, but fractals of really weird shit (i.e. hands). So imagine a spiraling column of hands and/or equally strange and random things that would twist and turn and dive and turn inside out and REPLICATE in impossible directions. This is where I began telling myself repeatedly to "embrace the madness" as biscuitzz said. Coincidentally, I had my "revelation", if you will, in this panic maddened state. When I was watching the fractals I noticed that they lived, they reproduced, and they evolved. I saw them as a representation of the nature of life. Live. Reproduce. Evolve. I concluded that humans were no different than things like DNA or cells, in that we're just extremely complex chemical systems that are meant to replicate and survive. Being a very rational person I was pretty aware of this before the trip, but this really drove it in. The moment of clarity only lasted a moment though. The rest of the time I was buggin out in my bed.

After about an hour and a half of silently freaking out I began coming down. My friend called me to check on me, and I had a pretty solid grasp on reality but was still high (pupils still very dilated), so I asked him to give me a ride to my other friend's house. I was completely down about an hour later. The whole thing lasted about 6.5 hours.

Notes: Music/sound contributed greatly to the visuals and to my state of mind. Shortly after my laughing stage stopped I accidentally changed the music to something heavier and that added a bit of harshness to the trip. During some point when I was pretending to sleep I opened my eyes and, seeing as to how there was no music anymore, absorbed all of the other sounds. For about 10 minutes all of the sounds combined and formed a kaleidoscope of interesting nonsensical noise that actually sounded pretty cool. I was pretty surprised to find that trippy videos/pictures/lava lamps were really uninteresting. They seemed to cheapen the experience. I'm pretty sure I dosed about 1 gram too much, but I don't regret it. Next time i'll take a little bit less and go watch stars and hug trees and shit =D

Thanks to everybody who offered suggestions, and thanks especially to dirtybiscuit for probably saving my mind from a thrashing. If my ventures on the road go well i'll post a few more psychedelic first-times in the future. Thanks for reading comrades, and remember to embrace the madness!

Quote from: soviet on June 03, 2013, 05:50 am
For those of you that have had at least moderate experience with psychedelics, which drug should I try next: LSD or DMT? Would it be unwise of me to jump straight into DMT given my inexperience? Also, what are the similarities and differences between an LSD vs Shroom trip? I've read that on LSD the trip is less mental and you have more control over yourself. Thoughts?

There is, in my opinion, way too much hype over DMT.  Its effects are the most commonly "out there", but I've rarely met anyone who actually grew from the experience.  Not in a healthy way, at least.  LSD takes time and it takes effort sometimes and it, almost lovingly, shows you which paths to take on your journey.  DMT slaps you in the face with it. 

If you're afraid of your own mind, DMT is a bad place to start.  LSD will work with you, most times, so long as you can somewhat keep your wits about you.  Another suggestion is a 2C, which is fun but not all that deep, and would also give you a rolling feeling.  I've had some deep experiences with 2C's, but they were during times of great stress (being homeless and cold and needing something to keep me up so I didn't freeze).  Other times, the most it gets is visually beautiful and euphoric.

These are just my experiences, and I can't say for sure they'll mean a good goddamn to your trips, but my recommendation is neither if you want just a fun, psychedelic, what the fuck time.  That I'd have to give to Ketamine and MXE.  Great "in public" drugs, too, seeing as you mostly just look drunk while you're out.  At home has more potential for the deep, introspective, and "out there" experiences.  I've actually had more "break-through" type experiences with MXE than I ever have with DMT.

Cheers and good luck!  I'd like to know what you go with next ^.^
