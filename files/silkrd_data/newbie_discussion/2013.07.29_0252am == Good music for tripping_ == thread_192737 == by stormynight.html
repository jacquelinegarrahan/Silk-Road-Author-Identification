Title: Re: Good music for tripping?
Post by: mightyboosh77 on July 29, 2013, 06:12 pm
as an avid psychonaut i would recommend SHPONGLE for anything psychedelic and i do mean anything.  Younger Brother is also spectacular.

for lsd you cannot go wrong with The Disco Biscuits, but there is some disagreement as to which era of the biscuits are best, early bisco (pre-2006) is more of a combo of trance/rock-jam music while later era bisco is more focused on electronica and has less rock-jamminess, although the electronica is always part-improvised aka jammed.  it's all about what you prefer.

Sound Tribe Sector 9 and/or Lotus are less improvised and more composed electronica but still fab and awesome for rave psychedelics.

for mushies, i happen to be particularly partial to Primus, something about their sludgey, leaden tone just makes sense when the world is all gooey. 

but honestly, some of the classics are also awesome.  Pink Floyd (any album), the Beatles (Revolver onward), The Grateful Dead (pre 1980s), Hendrix, The Mahavishnu Orchestra, The Allman Brothers (when Dwayne was alive, post Dwayne just doesn't have the right feel), the list is virtually endless.

trip happy my friend.

oh and if you like weird and esoteric music, try Mr. Bungle, Sleepytime Gorilla Museum, or Idiot Flesh - these groups are truly out there and astounding.
