Yes the current price for bitcoin match those on SR. Meaning if something costs 1btc on SR it costs the same as 1btc across the network.

This can be helpful or harmful. If you purchase 1btc for $20 and the price jumps to $40 for 1btc then you will have twice as much btc to make purchases. However if the price goes down to $10 for 1btc then you have half as many btc to make purchases since the value of the btc has decreased.

Bitcoin is a very volatile currency, the value has rapidly inflated over the past two months more than doubling, the main reason for this is the mined Bitcoin blocks have halved from fifty to twenty five meaning the 'miners' have to work 'twice' as hard for the same amount of coins, add increased demand, and you've got a Bitcoin boom! If you're worried about Bitcoin devaluing, only buy the amount of Bicoins you need and spend them the on the same day, or on the day you acquire them, as some exchange methods will take longer than a day to process.
