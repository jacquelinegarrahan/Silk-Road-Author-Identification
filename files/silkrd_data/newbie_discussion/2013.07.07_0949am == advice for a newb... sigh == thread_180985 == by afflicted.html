Title: Re: advice for a newb... sigh
Post by: tankhere on July 07, 2013, 10:20 am
I'll tell you what I've found out so far, and if I'm all wrong, some Sr. Member is going to correct me.

One of the easiest places to buy if you're in the UK (like me) is https://localbitcoins.com/buy_bitcoins , registration and using the site is as simple as it can be.

You can go for the cash options which are usually cheaper than buying online. I did not fool around, simply ordered the coins via National bank transfer, just had to make a transfer to e.g. a Barclays account, then 5 minutes later I got my coins.

Then you transfer out of your localbitcoins wallet, e.g. to blockchain.info . From there, you should transfer them to Bitcoinfog ( http://fogcore5n3ov3tui.onion/ ) to anonymize your coins, they charge a 1%-3% fee when you deposit (withdrawing is free).

And from Bitcoinfog, you can finally transfer to SR. If you're really paranoid, you can divide the transaction into multiple parts and use multiple SR wallets for each, and of course use more than the initial 6 hours of timespan.

So yes, while buying bitcoins via bank transfer leaves a paper trail, it's absolutely legal and once it goes through the fog, you are good to go.
