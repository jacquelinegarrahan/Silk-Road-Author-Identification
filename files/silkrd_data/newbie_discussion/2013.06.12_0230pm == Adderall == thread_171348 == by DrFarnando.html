Title: Re: Adderall
Post by: SmokesHisBroccoli on June 12, 2013, 04:47 pm
Glad to see another adderall vendor coming aboard.  There isn't as much adderall and dex listed as there was in Jan and Feb last time I bought.  I bet a lot of vendors just ran out during finals week for exams.

But to answer your questions yes I think at the right price point it would be incredibly easy for you to sell these.  I know the person I buy dex off of restocks about once a month and lets their customers know ahead of time when they'll be posted because if you aren't on within about a 24 hour period to put your order in they're gone.  So they sell out quick for the right price. 

The prices since Jan and Feb have gone up though for some reason.  Let's face it, street value for a 30 mg might be $10.  You can get more from college kids during finals week because they're desperate and rich and grades me a lot when you're paying thousands for college credits, but for the everyday Joe I don't think most people want to pay more than $15 for one.  I think that's a very competitive and fair price point.  You'd make a nice profit and you'd be making customers happy.  There are vendors right now selling for $25 a shot.  That's fucking nuts.  I'll never pay that much personally.  It's time we have some vendors step up and price lower so that others vendors will follow suit because it's out of hand. 
Title: Re: Adderall
Post by: SmokesHisBroccoli on June 12, 2013, 04:51 pm
Another thing I was thinking you could try that would get you a little more profit and really help ensure sales is give away a free adderall with every order of say 5 pills.  If you charged $20 per pill for 5 for a total of $100, but gave away 1 free adderall with each order then you'd actually be taking in $16.66 per pill ($100 / 6 pills).  I can almost guarantee you'll do better this way than selling for $16.66 per pill alone without any free ones.  Your profits would be the same either way, but if someone sees free pill in the product advertisement they'll jump all over it.  It's a shame not all vendors think this way.  This is how big box retailers so it sometimes.
