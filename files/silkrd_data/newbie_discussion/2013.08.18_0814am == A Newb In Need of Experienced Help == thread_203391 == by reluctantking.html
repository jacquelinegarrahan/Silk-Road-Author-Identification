Post by: deluxedelivery on August 18, 2013, 08:44 am
You would need to find out your bitcoin address (a long string of seemingly random characters & numbers) here on SR and send to that address from the account where your coins are. BTW, I would never ever send coins directly from a site/exchange/wallet that has yoru real information directly to SR. Ideally, you would start up a wallet on a service that allows you to create a log in using tor and send yoru coins to t hat address first. Then, transfer those coins to another wallet that uses a "pooled wallet". This will help break the chain of ownership and ensure your sending different coins to your final destination, SR.

Ask here in the forums which wallet services people recommend to you and take your pick.
Title: Re: A Newb In Need of Experienced Help
Post by: fr33man2 on August 18, 2013, 09:23 am
I have not used the exchange you prefer. There should be a way to withdraw or send BTC to another BTC address. The best bet is to use a locally install bitcoin wallet. Configure it to communicate over the Tor network by selecting socks proxy and setting the ip to 127.0.0.1 and the port to 9050 socks version 5. Move the funds there. Once they have been verified move them into the SR by clicking on account at the top of every page then "click here to make a deposit". You will see a deposit address. This is where you send your bitcoins to. Start by depositing a small amount into your SR account. Something like 0.0001. This will allow you to see that the transaction completed successfully. Once you're comfortable with that move move BTC's in. At least enough to cover any purchase you intend to make.

For added security use something like Bitcoinfog (http://fogcore5n3ov3tui.onion/) as an intermediary step between your local wallet and SR. The benefits of which are described here (https://bitcointalk.org/index.php?topic=50037.0). Note that SR does it's own mixing and randomization.
