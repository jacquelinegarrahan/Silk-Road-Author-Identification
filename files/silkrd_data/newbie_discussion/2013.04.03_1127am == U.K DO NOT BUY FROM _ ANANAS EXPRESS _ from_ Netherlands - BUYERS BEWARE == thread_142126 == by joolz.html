Yes, I am a moderator - a forum moderator, not a Silk Road administrator. I do not know why the administrator that was overseeing the resolution decided to award 50%; that is something that you would have to take up with them. All I can tell you is that they take both the buyer's and vendor's account histories into account, and make a decision based on all of the evidence that is presented and available to them.

Regarding CharasBros, if you did not FE then he did not have an opportunity to take your money nor 'refund it' as he did not have it in the first place. It was being held in escrow and the vendor cancelled the order. As a result, you received a refund. Please see the following SR Wiki entries which may help you understand why you received the amount of Bitcoin that you did when the order was cancelled:

http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/wiki/index.php?title=Frequently_Asked_Questions#Why_is_my_refund_less.2Fmore_than_I_paid.3F

http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/wiki/index.php?title=Buyer%27s_Guide#Escrow_hedge

You have presumably read the buyer's guide so you presumably knew what would happen in the event of a cancellation. Neither Silk Road nor the vendors that trade on it are responsible for the volatility of Bitcoin. Silk Road already does its best to limit this volatility by utilising a moving 24 hour average rather than a moving 'last' price.

Regarding the cancellation itself, this is a free market and both vendors and buyers are free to elect to do business with whomever they wish. The commission that a vendor generates is absolutely NOT taken into account if they break the rules governing vending privileges. The reason that your report resulted in "FUK ALL" happening is because there was no actual issue to begin with. It seems it was a misunderstanding on your part as to how the escrow system works.
