Title: Re: Mailing address, how many to use?
Post by: chriscalderon on June 22, 2013, 12:42 pm
I started out with a PO box just to see how many of the ideas about them were personally true for my particular PO Box. The only truth I could find was that when you use a PO Box, you must make certain that the name or names associated with the Box are one of the names you have your mail addressed to. In other words, no fake names and no fake companies unless you have got those names on the sheet of paper that the Post Office collects when you first sign up for a PO Box. You can always amend this info later but until you do, make sure you're using a name associated with that box.

Other than that, you don't even have to worry about standing out. I recently left the country for a month and a half. Up until that point I was receiving, in some cases, 3 to 5 packages a day. On non-heavy volume weeks I was averaging 3-5 a week so it was a fair amount of exclusively drug mail coming in. For a month and a half though, no mail. I would have someone check on it once a week just to make sure nothing was getting into the po box. Those bastards started putting trash in my box!

So when I came back to the states, I immediately started ordering again, this time with the express intent to order odd amounts to see if any suspicious activity occurred.

So far so good man. No one has profiled me. No one harasses me. They simply deliver my mail and I in turn have tracked them down and tipped a few of them that I know handle my stuff directly. They are always super-appreciative of it and marvel that anyone thinks about or regards the people that make the magic happen.

There is a gent named PostalWorker (not sure of exact user name but he has posted in the NEWB section and he's very informative) where he brings up the point that unless your stuff obviously smells, postal employees are not too terribly interested in your mail beyond getting it delivered. We all tend to think of ourselves as the center of the universe and this includes our packages. We wonder or are certain that our package is receiving special scrutiny. We know for certain that this is the time that our package will be handled one too many times and our special contents will spill out all over the poor postal worker's delicate, soft hands.

But fear not fellow traveler and mind-lord, you are safe in the comfort of your God. Rest in his nook and cranny and let your package be. Certainly I am not trying to dismiss your concerns but alas you need not worry about the PO Box side of the equation. Xanadu, peace, solostro-needum, needum, Solari.
