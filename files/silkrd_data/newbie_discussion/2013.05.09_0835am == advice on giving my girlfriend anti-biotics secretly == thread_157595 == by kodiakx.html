Title: Re: advice on giving my girlfriend anti-biotics secretly
Post by: onufri on May 09, 2013, 08:57 am
I am also a person with bipolar disorder.

I am not saying this in judgment, but rather taking you at your word that you are concerned for her well-being:

1) There is always an interaction potential between antipsychotics and other medications, which is why us psychos need to regularly see psychiatrists and have our medications memorized for any unforeseen hospital visits. Do you know what antibiotics you're using? Do you know what antipsychotics she is using? Are you medical literature-literate enough to be able to confirm that there aren't adverse interactions between these medications?

2) By your message, it sounds like you want to give her just one big dose (perhaps I'm misreading). That isn't how antibiotics work. All that would produce is a drug-resistant version of whatever STD she may have contracted from you, which would leave her worse off. If you were going to do this, you would need to be able to give her the FULL course of the antibiotic, regularly, no skipping days, no ending the treatment early. So, whatever it is that you were doing, it would have to be routinized as a daily thing that would not arouse her suspicion.

3) Crushing or grinding the medications may compromise their effectiveness.

I sympathize with your situation, but I don't think there is a way to square the circle between your desire for her to receive STD treatment and your desire for her to be unaware of it. And doing an antibiotic treatment half-way or, God forbid, for only one day, would be worse than not giving her antibiotics at all.

That said, I'm not a physician.

But, as a bipolar person who has suffered from suicidality, I would rather have someone just tell me what happened and give me the chance to get proper treatment.
