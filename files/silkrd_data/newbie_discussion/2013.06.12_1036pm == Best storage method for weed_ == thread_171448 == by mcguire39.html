Title: Re: Best storage method for weed?
Post by: sameoldsameold on June 17, 2013, 11:10 am
Some considerations about your storage.
It should be:

1.) airtight - not just for prevention of smell leaking out, but also because it somewhat reduces the chance of your weed getting wet (by accident or through humidity).
2.) lightproof - light falling on your weed will degrade the THC to produce other cannabinoids with less "high" (though useful for some medicinal purposes).
3.) cool - if you want to cure, you might want it at room temperature, but generally, storing should be done in a cool environment. The freezer is too cold (and ice forming will make your weed soggy after you thaw). A basement or windowless room is the ideal temp. A fridge might be good too, but even here you run the chance of moisture building up when you remove the container. When in doubt, just keep it in a cupboard at room temperature, because the most important thing is -
4.) moisture free! - Moisture will make your weed soggy and help bacteria and fungus to grow, which will ruin your stash! This means you should never put an orange peel or other moist crap with your weed like some people here say. you can use these little silica packets that are shipped with some PC hardware or food to soak up air humidity inside your jar, but a bottom filling of uncooked, dry RICE will work just as well. use a few centimeters of rice in the bottom and put a layer of kitchen towel/paper tissue between it and your weed to catch small parts falling off when handling.

I have been using either old food tins with a screw cap, or glass mason jars with proper rubber lip for sealing, with a generous bottom layer of rice. Always choose a dark place to put your container.

Doing this, I have successfully stored my own produce for over 2 years at room temperature without any difficulties or degradation
