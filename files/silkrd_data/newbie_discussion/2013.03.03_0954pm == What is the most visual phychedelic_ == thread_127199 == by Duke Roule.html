Quote
I think people expect some experience of pink elephants dancing around a fire or something.
Those are not hallucinations. Hallucinations have a basis in reality and are a warping or bending of perception. So fractals, objects appearing the wrong size and/or color and other misinterpretations of your surroundings.

Delusion on the other hand have no basis in reality and tend to be more stark, drugs that produce these are called dilliriants. They act in a different way than psychedelics though most are fairly safe. However, they tend to make a user incredibly tired and are usually quite terrifying. High doses of Benedryl, Dramamine, Angels Trumpet, scopalamine, or most other over-the-counter anti-cholinergics, tend to produce these results.

As a side note there was a famous military developed dileriant called BZ, active in the microgram range, with a dose lasting around 72 hours. The stuff was insanely powerful too, it would fuck a person up and often resulted in PTSD. They developed bombs for the stuff and aerosol sprays. It was tested on soldiers and prisoners and most of this data is now public knowledge, go look it up if your interested.
