If you can, try to get a baby sitter. A perfect baby sitter is someone who has tripped before and will remain (relatively) sober. The babysitter will urge you on when you start going and will bring you up if you trip gets bad. If you smoke pot, smoke a bowl or joint after you take the acid.  That way, the transition from rational to tripping will be easier.

As other posts have mentioned, you might have a strong urging to be in nature. The great outdoors is truly majestic once your brain chemistry is altered. But, you might be in a mood to play video games or watch trippy shit on the computer or being social around people.

Whatever you do man, be safe! Don't go into public alone or into any places that might give you a heart attack (roller coaster, laser light show, concert etc). Right now, you might not be ready for such a trip.

Title: Re: First LSD trip tonight, need advise!
Post by: twodix on April 02, 2013, 07:10 am
   Be sure you're in a place where you feel safe, comfortable and cosy, where you're not going to be bothered by unexpected events for a few hours, that's really important.
  Make that happy space really extra cosy with blankets, cushions,  heaters, candles and incense if you feel like that stuff makes you feel good.
Your mindset will determine a lot of what you experience.
    If you feel like crap and the toilet's busted and the landlord's a practicing Satanist Hell's Angel gang member Ninja who wants the last three weeks rent or else and he's coming round tonight it might not be the right time, you know?
   Make sure before you start the trip that you have some kind of plan for what you're gonna do, like have your favorite music  handy, some of your favorite foods ready to enjoy,  some musical instruments or paints and pens and sketch books, if that's what you're into.
   Most importantly, if you have a trusted friend who's tripped before and is a kindly soul (who you know bears within themselves a certain amount of responsibility) see if they might be persuaded to spend some time with you while you're coming up and to help guide you at times when things might get as little rough (as they often do).
   Dr. Timothy Leary stated in ,'The Politics of Ecstasy' that certain, experienced people should actually be licensed by a governing body to ensure that they are capable of guiding people who are tripping for the first time so that things don't turn into a psychological train wreck for them. That was in a society Dr. Leary envisioned that sadly has never occurred in officialdom (and I wonder how many 'acid casualties' could have been saved the trouble had they listened to this man's intelligent guidance?)
  Contrary to some of the advice given here, Dr. Leary also warned against indulging in other psychedelics such as marijuana during the initial stages of an LSD trip.It really is pretty potent stuff that you might want to enter into the effects of in a sober manner, oddly enough!
  It's been scientifically proven that any task one can perform when unintoxicated one can also undertake if required to when tripping, and that even on a very high dose of LSD, so it's not like you'll be totally out of control.
You control the trip, don't let it control you, but that being said, flow with it too.
Have a nice trip.

I would suggest gradually working your way up to the stronger psychedelics. Start with a few Salvia experiences, move to a few small psilocybin experiences, even some edible cannabis. I don't know what your past history with psychedelics are, but I probably wouldn't start with LSD. Psychedelics are not party drugs. Take them seriously. Set an intent for why your doing it. Prepare your mind beforehand. Don't take it for the first time as your walking into a concert. I believe the psychedelic experience an important part of the human experience and one that should be taken seriously. If your just looking to "get crunked" there are a lot of substances out there for that. LSD and psychedelics are not meant for that.

Title: Re: First LSD trip tonight, need advise!
Post by: CRWHITLOCK on April 02, 2013, 11:24 pm
I would spent a little time beforehand doing some self reflection and try to set an intent for your trip. Something you'd like to work on. Continually remind yourself not to fight the experience, just allow it to happen. If things do start to turn negative than just calmly remind yourself that you are here to better yourself, and that you want a positive experience. Don't panic, that's the worst thing to do. Just accept wherever you go. Remember the less food you have in your stomach the stronger the experience is going to be. You could take identical doses, of identical product and have two totally different strengths based on how empty your stomach is. For the strongest experience you probably shouldn't eat for at least 10 hours beforehand. If you want to start slow I wouldn't do it on a totally empty stomach though.
