Open eye visuals were apparent, and I enjoyed watching my hand constantly transform into a gnarled old claw and back. I dropped another half tab at around 12:30am and continued recording my observations in handwriting, although my hand and body was trembling and the paper appeared to breath slightly and it was becoming difficult to write legibly. So I decided to switch to art and started drawing in coloured pencil.

The delightful thing I noticed about tripping on acid was that instead of leaving me sluggish and lethargic like shrooms, I felt light, energetic and restless. Unlike the heavy body load of shrooms, which gives you the palpable sensation of experiencing your own mortal body decaying, every breath a conscious effort, your heart beating hard, my body felt slightly sedated and pain free. In contrast to shrooms, my mind was lucid, the closed-eye visuals were clearer, and I felt in control of myself at all times - no chance of a segue into an unexpected mindfuck.

Around 2pm, I went outside into the backyard and lay on the grass. The sky was a brilliant, faultless blue and the weather was comfortably warm, althought it was windy. I closed my eyes and shut out the environment, deciding to pretend that I was dead. The wind constantly rustling the leaves of the trees under which I was lying sounded like the waves of the ocean lapping at the shores of the beach. The wind chimes were tinkling spasmodically. I could hear the paws and soft huffs of my dogs as they padded around nearby on the grass.

In my mind, I visualised dark rotating matrices and posterised tendrils and vivid kaleidoscoped images that were incredibly lucid. I started to perceive the nature of intuition and thought, the systems 1 and 2 that were running my mind.

As I lay there competely motionless on my stomach, trying to die, it I felt and visualised that the gusts of wind were disassembling my molecules into the universe, and insects and microbes had started to crawl into my nostrils and up my legs and over my body trying to start the process of decomposition. Pain, from the hard knobbly surface of the grassy incline digging into my check, was rendered as visuals, but it was relegated to a lesser and controllable sensation that could be brushed aside. I felt completely and utterly at peace at the notion that I would die then and there.

This was all a dream, a dream of a person before me. His last dying dream was my life, a spectacular one, all things considered. And then when I would die, I would have one last dream, and that dream would give birth to a new consciousness. And so on. Grasping life's infinite regress was a marvellous relevation. Man's curiosity, the efforts of science and religion, is directed towards finding that first person who died, the proto-dreamer.

Having done with that thought experiment, I tried the experiment of trying to eject my ego. Not in the way that the shocking soul-shattering blast of DMT does, but in a more conscious, step-by-step way. I could zoom out from my body, further and further, trying to encompass the nature of the entire planet and universe inside my mind and my small part of it, but no matter how hard I tried I couldn't get rid of the 'my' and the 'I', I couldn't quite depart from the tether of my mortal body. Tendrils slithered up and grasped the stalk of my escalating thought and soul before it could completely project itself into the unknown.

I realised the futility of constantly trying to record each and every observation during a trip. It is like trying to grasp what is ungraspable. My mind was automatically imposing a sense of order on the disorder of the night sky, the stars lighting up in their constellations and rearranging themselves into more pleasing arrangements.
