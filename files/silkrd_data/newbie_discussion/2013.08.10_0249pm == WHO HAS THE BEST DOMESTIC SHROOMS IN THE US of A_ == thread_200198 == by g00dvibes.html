Quote from: cyberscour on August 11, 2013, 08:55 pm
Strain doesn't really matter actually. Some strains of mushrooms are SLIGHTLY more potent than others, but it is a very slim difference. What really matters is how well the vendor takes care of his mushrooms, how they are packaged, how old they are, how well they are dried, and other handling variables. Poorly handled mushrooms will give you less psychedelic effects and food poisoning.
^ THIS!
It's all true. 'Shrooms need to be completely dried to a 99.9%+ dehumidified cracker-crunch state using a method that does not employ excessive heat, as heat destroys psilocybin. How much heat is apparently still debated (I can't find a clear answer), but using a heat-variable food dehydrater on the lowest setting is reported to provide good results. I use first a fan, and then a small dessication chamber instead to achieve this state at room temperature. For larger amounts I use a cheap box\floor fan, a few cellulose furnace filters, and a couple of bungie cords. That setup can render ounces of dried 'shrooms to a 95% state in just 24 hours. Makes the whole place smell like fresh 'shrooms for the first couple of hours... nice.

Here, the difference between psilocybin and psilocin should be noted:
* Psilocin is the active compound in Magic Mushrooms. It's what makes you trip.
* Psilocin is a rather volatile compound and degrades quickly. Even if carefully dried, this stuff will be gone with weeks or months if stored at room temperature.

* Psilocybin rapidly metabolizes into psilocin in the liver, at a rate of about 72%.
* Psilocybin is found in P. Cubensis at about 4x the concentration of psilocin (around 0.4% dry weight).
* Psilocybin is a tryptamine (like DMT). Much like Phenethylamines (MDMA; Mescaline) these are considered highly stable compounds and should last for years if stored in a cool, dark, dry place.

This is why fresh 'shrooms are more potent than dried 'shrooms (by maybe 20-30%). You'd really have to get into freeze-drying if you want to preserve the psilocin content.
Oh, and about food poisoning, that's no joke. If your 'shrooms aren't snap-cracker dry and stored in an air-tight container, the moisture will harbor the growth of who-knows-what. Dry 'em and store 'em carefully.

Now, to the question of which is the strongest. That would be one of the wood-loving non-cubensis varieties of Psilocybe, such as Cyanescens or Azurescens. they clock in at around 2-3x more potent than Cubensis. See here: https://www.erowid.org/plants/mushrooms/mushrooms_info9.shtml

And then there's the sclerotia producing species like P. Galandoi. they're even easier to grow because you never have to fruit it. Can some grain, inoculate with a syringe, wait 2-3 months and presto, Magic Mushrooms! The sclerotia are about 200% as potent fresh, and about 66% as potent dried as P. Cubensis. that's because the carpophores (normal fruiting bodies; ie. mushrooms) are about 90% water by weight, and the sclerotia are about 70%.

For more info I invite you to visit the Shroomery (.org). It's really an interesting, inexpensive and rather rewarding hobby to acquire.
