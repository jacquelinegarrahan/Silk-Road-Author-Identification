Title: Re: Why is heroin from Australia so fucking expensive
Post by: hamahed on June 07, 2013, 02:00 pm
@kushfan as a long time h user from the east coast of aus i kno a lil about the local market.
while its tru that being geographically isolated plays a part in the high prices i dont believe this is the cause of the inflated costs. because of our proximity to south east asian countries we are spoilt for quality and we seldom see the brown middle eastern alkaline heroin. when i started using in the early 90s the vietnamese/cambodian community almost had a complete monopoly on the east coast heroin trade. the quality was excellent and the prices were good. about aus $200 a gram but remember back then the USD-AUD conversion was 50% so USD $100 gram. on the street the typical 'cap' was $30 and you could easy go 2 ways.
in the summer 99-2000, in the lead up to the olympics LE went ballistic securing entry ports and attempting to 'clean up' the streets. in a 3 mth period Sydney went from having two 24hr open air heroin/cocaine markets and literally 100s of on call suppliers to a handful of dealers who themselves had lost their steady supply chains. prices doubled and quadrupled during this period. it took about 6-12 mths after the olympics had finished for new supply routes to be established and old ones re-established but the prices remained elevated. dealers now knew, thanx to LE, that users would pay top $$$ if they had to. fast forward 15-20yrs and AUD USD has reached parity and we pay 4-6 times the $$$ our US brethren pay for similar product. more often than not street quality is better here than in most european cities(i personally know that to be true of london and paris) and on par with what i know of US cities but a few rungs up the ladder and its of similar quality to most cities. i think the smarter aus suppliers realise they can make the same profit margins in aus as their international counterparts with less dilution of product so the quality at street level in aus is usually excellent(exceptions to every rule) but in turn we pay a premium.
SR could go a long way to correct this LE manufactured anomaly and i sincerely hope it does... and thats my essay on modern junky history
