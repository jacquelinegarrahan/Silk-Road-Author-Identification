Title: Re: Onion browser for iphone
Post by: Laissez-Faire on May 12, 2013, 06:42 am
I don't believe it's been peer-reviewed thoroughly enough to be certain of an answer.

If I had to guess, I'd say that the app itself is probably safe, however the main concern would be what happens to the data you enter (URLs, passwords etc) while you use it within the iOS environment. There's a chance all this information could be stored on your iProduct in an insecure manner.

As others mention, one of the safest ways would be to use a bootable, memory-based OS such as Liberte or Tails.

Whonix is more secure but trickier to use and probably unnecessary for your purposes and using the tor browser bundle within windows would be a less secure option.

If you must use onion browser, you could add an extra level of safety by using an anonymous VPN on your iPhone before connecting to TOR with the onion browser. This however, won't offer any extra protection to the data you input into your phone.


I hope this helps.

Laissez-faire!
