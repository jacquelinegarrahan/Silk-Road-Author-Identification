Post by: snapplecaps on June 17, 2013, 06:44 am
I dont know what part of the world you live in, but in NA the best time to pick psylocybic mushrooms is in the fall (sept-november).

be fucking carefull.  if you can, sign up for an identification class with a local guide.  remember, the only sure way to tell what type of mushroom you have is to to a spore sample under a microscope.  people say dumb shit like "if it bruises purple its a magic mushroom," while true that magic mushrooms often bruise purple, many other species do as well. 

only a few species of mushroom will kill you, but a HUGE number of mushrooms will cause extreme gastrointestinal distress and illness.

a good basic guide book is "everything the rain promises and more" so check it out.

there are lots of easily identifiable edible mushrooms that are awesome too.  morels this time of year, and chantarelles in the fall are delicious and very easy to identify with few look alike species

What Snapplecaps says is a good fail safe,

Ive picked for along time in many locations both tropical and cold climate, to the best of my knowledge and some other mycologists Ive spoken to there is only one deadly blue bruising mushroom 'known' and it lives quite close to the equator. As a general rule either Boletes or Psilo's are bruising blue but you never know as fungi can lay dormant for along time and only fruit at certain times in rare occasions and you cannot rule out that there 'may' be an unknown species thats blues and is deadly but generally speaking....A x400 microscope and a field guide is the best way, or an experienced hunter.
