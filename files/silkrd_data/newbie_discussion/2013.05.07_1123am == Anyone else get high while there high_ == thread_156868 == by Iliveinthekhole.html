In order to jump solo you need to take one of a number of courses that if you pass will qualify you for an A license.
The courses I'm familiar with are AFF (Accelerated Free Fall) You get around 12 hours of classroom work with tests. Then you'll make 25 pass/fail jumps with an instructor close by to get your A.
There's Tandem Progression. Were you make 2-5 tandem jumps then 20 pass/fail solo jumps with an instructor near by.
Lastly there's Static Line Progression. You get a couple hours of classroom then they shove you out of a plane (usually a shitty one) 15 (or so) times with a lanyard that deploys your chute upon line stretch. After each jump they give you a little more slack . But you get the least amount of freefall time with static line.
If you're just going for the experience (or want to see if you like it) I'd recommend doing a tandem jump.  That's the way most people experience skydiving.
You get 30 minutes of classroom and then you jump with an instructor strapped to your back.
I've never done a tandem, but I imagine you still get the 'rush' and in hindsight I'd probably do one or two just to get over the sensory overload you get on the first couple of jumps. You literally can't move for a couple of seconds, then when you can the first thought that comes to mind is to curl up in a ball and kiss your ass goodbye.
It's an amazing feeling. To know that you're going to be dead in a matter of seconds... unless you do something about it.
