Title: Re: Requesting info on making a STRONG password
Post by: cerealbox on April 29, 2013, 09:35 pm
1. don't use words that can be found in the dictionary. foreign words and unusual proper names are fine. don't use dates or names under any circumstances.

2. take the first letter of a sentence to get a good random chunk of letters that you can remember. Something like "that damned junky faggot stole my erect penis and sold it on the black market" becomes "tdjfsmepasiotbm" - very hard to guess.

3. Salt your password. That means, throw some shit in there in the middle. So if your password is konkrete it you can make it konkshittingdicknippleskrete.

4. Use unusual characters and substitute letters for weird characters. konkrete becomes kön|<r3t3.

5. make it long. each letter makes it exponentially more difficult to hack.

6. take advantage of caps if the password is caps sensitive, but do it in an unusual way that would be difficult to guess.

Title: Re: Requesting info on making a STRONG password
Post by: The Bubble Dreamer on May 01, 2013, 11:15 pm
I use the following trick to generate strong password but that I can easily recover: (on mac osx or linux)

- On the command line, I generate a md5 checksum for the name of the site + some salt (a random string, but always the same so I can remember).

For example (where Yo is the salt)

$ md5<enter>
silkroadYo<enter>
29b3adf5145e3d0b726a1800a50db0ec<enter>
<ctrl-d>

- To make this even stronger, I replace some characters (which are all in the 0-9a-f range) with other characters.

$ md5 | tr abc XYZ<enter>
silkroadYo<enter>
<ctrl-d>
29Y3Xdf5145e3d0Y726X1800X50dY0eZ

- The length might be exaggerated, so I shorten it:

$ md5 | tr abc XYZ | head -c 12<enter>
silkroadYo<enter>
29Y3Xdf5145e<enter>
<ctrl-d>

- This I can remember or just regenerate whenever I need it, since I know the sitename, the salt and the replacement strings.
