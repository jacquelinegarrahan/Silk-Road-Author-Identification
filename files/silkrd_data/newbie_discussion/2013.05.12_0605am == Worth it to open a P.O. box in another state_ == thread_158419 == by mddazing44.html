I would think you were a minor due to this post if you didn't imply you have the freedom to travel out of state at will (likely a college student on summer break?). If you are a minor, though, I would make the futile recommendation that you log off this site and don't return until you are of majority age. That said, and assuming that you are, in fact, of age to be a consenting adult, here is the answer to your question:

Sending and receiving drugs in the mail is a federal offense - a felony. You will be charged by the postal service with trafficking if they find the amount worthwhile enough to do a controlled delivery. It doesn't matter what state you're in; on the federal level (which is what we're tampering with here, folks, the UNITED STATES POSTAL SERVICE), marijuana is illegal. Most adults know this, which is why I went on that initial tangent about you possibly being a minor. Another reason I thought you were a minor is that, based on your post (and I might be wrong on this, but this is what your post indicates), you don't know what "decriminalization" means. It does not mean "legalization" by ANY means. If you are found in possession of marijuana in a decriminalized state, and you do not have a perscription from a licensed physician, you are likely going to your local jail where you will await charges, which will likely be that of a felony or perhaps multiple felonies (if you were distributing). So no, it would likely not make any difference if you get a PO Box in another state, since it would be the federal government that would charge you with any crimes you would be found committing through use of the US postal service.

I hope that puts things in perspective. And, again, I would recommend you log off this site if you are a minor. If you are not, please do what you can to educate yourself - pretend like you don't think you're invincible and won't get beaten to a pulp in a jail/prison setting. It is not fun to be there. You don't want to go there. Be safe.
