Just for those wondering in the united states:

I am a lawyer, even though I dont practice locally :) (passed bar just to prove to myself)

Your blood, your hair, your sweat, your urine and your spit is YOUR property. They cannot take this from you without YOUR permission, as long as you are ABLE To tell them NO, that you are NOT under the influence (and it doesnt sound extremely slurred).

The LSD guy had his urine and his blood taken because he made them have probable cause to think he was incapacitated to uphold his rights. He didn't tell them otherwise and he didnt have a viable excuse for why he was lethargic, for instance "I take pain medications for my knee". If you can say that sentence and that you are not under the influence of any NON PRESCRIPTION drugs, you have just built your defense if they decide to break the law and take your personal effects (blood, urine, spit, etc).
The reason you must be able to say something abide to keep your personal effects to yourself is:

Say you are in a car accident and unconscious; there are very few injuries and none severe (as they find out earlier or later in hospital) BUT your breathing stops anyway. They have to know the cause for your breathing to stop to be able to counteract it, for example, when you overdosed and caused the car crash. The personal property law is for your rights but the exemptions for officers/EMT's is there for your safety, to allow them to get around this law to save your life.

Yes, alot of cops are using this law unwarranted, as an excuse to incriminate you. Therefore, if you follow the advice I've given above and they break it to get your biological property, to then charge you for DUI, you have a defense when you go to court as to WHY they had no probable cause to take your biological property - because you were able to COHERENTLY say "No, Im not under influence; I am (sleepy or eyes constricted or etc) because I take medications for pain or muscle relaxers" .  By you being able to REMEMBER exactly what you said when the officer arrested you is proof right there, aiding that you were not lethargic.

If you are under 18 in the US, you have NONE Of these rights. if you are driving and a police officer pulls you over at age of 16 and he thinks you are under influence of narcotics, he can make you give your personal property to him (blood, urine, whatever); If you are emancipated, however, this doesnt apply to you, as you have FULL rights of an eighteen year old.

Of course, if you are drunk and you refuse simple breathalyzer (in Virginia) that is automatic admission of guilt for DUI, by Virginia law.

So, dont drive drunk and dont drive more than your limit of narcotics. Ask a friend if you are too lethargic to drive; ask them to watch as you run toward an obstacle and try to dodge it at the last second. If you run into it, you're too lethargic. :)
