There are two important things you'll have to learn before you make your purchase.

1) How to buy bitcoins so do a search for this in the forums (if you'reAmerican this should be fairly easy to do there seems to be a lot of options for you guys). Then click on your SR account to get the wallet number to send them to and sent them. It can take a few hours for them to transfer.

2) Google Kleopatra and then download and install it. This is for using pgp to encrypt your address and other personal details. It's pretty easy to use. I'm a complete technophobe and I learn to use it in about an hour.

Why not get started on those two and keep coming back here with questions as they arise. There are a lot of really helpful people here.

The best of luck.

Post by: vI on May 23, 2013, 06:59 pm
Oracle13,

one thing to remember is to write down your SR username, password and the pin, do the same thing for your wallet.

when you do a transaction, you'll be asked for a pin or a password, and if you forgot it, you'll end up locked out of your bitcoins.

as goldenrod mentioned, use the wallet address to buy or transfer bitcoins, you'll have in your SR account page bitcoin address for SR - use that one to receive the bitcoins from your wallet.

once you transfer from the wallet to your SR address, it'll take a while, maybe an hour or more.

after you'll see your balance on SR, and then you buy what you need, remember to make sure you've got all passwords and pin working properly.

there are some basic "how to" bitcoin tutorials on youtube.

In order to get actual funds into my Silk Road account, I made a different bitcoin wallet on coinbase.com, to use as an intermediary. I then used bitinstant (google it) to buy bitcoins for the coinbase wallet, then changed the coinbase wallet address. I then sent the bitcoins to my current Silk Road address (you can add another coinbase account to the chain here if you want), which I then change (I usually change the coinebase address here yet again).

As far as finalizing early, as far as I'm concerned I'll never do it, even with a trusted vendor (this seems to be the conventional wisdom). You might not have that luxury based on the vendor availability for what you want (I just smoke weed). Essentially that allows the vendor complete immunity to any issues that may happen (delay, didn't show up, bad quality, etc). You've already finalized and given them your money; transaction complete.
