Post by: rustykoin on July 12, 2013, 10:30 pm
Cheap Coke

What's up? Hey if you're using Keychain Access on the Mac first thing I would do is open up the Keychain program and in the upper left corner click on the GPG Keychain dropdown menu. Go down to Services and click on Services Preferences.

From here scroll down on the right side to where it has the Files and Folders menu, and under this category you'll see several options like OpenPGP Encrypt, Open PGP Decrypt, Open PGP Import, etc. Make sure all these check boxes pertaining to OpenPGP are checked.

Now open up your text editor, on Mac I believe it is TextEdit or something. From here you can do a number of things. First copy that message ISleepy1 sent you and past it in there. Now select the whole text again and I think it's like ctrl + click will give you the menu (like a right-click, sorry I'm a Windows guy) . Now choose Decrypt from that menu under Services.

Like ISleepy1 mentioned, it will prompt you for your passphrase and after you enter it the message should become clear.

Use this same method of pasting public keys in the TextEdit to import keys to your KeyChain. Whenever you type out a response message you choose to Encrypt instead and select from the options who the recipient of the message is.

Hope this helps in some way.
