Ive been scammed before where sender sends to different address.  If that is the case, chances are it was just a box filled with newspapers and no product.  Unless he put the wrong address there is literally NO way it would say delivered unless it came to your door or you went to pick it up at the PO.  Although in the USA USPS fucks things up royally all the time, and employees get lazy and say they delivered things, or left notes, and no notes were actually left, nor packages delivered.   You can always call the post office and provide the tracking number and see if they can locate where it went.  Ive had to do that before.  Make a dummy email address for them to contact you at if they respond via email to follow up on things.
