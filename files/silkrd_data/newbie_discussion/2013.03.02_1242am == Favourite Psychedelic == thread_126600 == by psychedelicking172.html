Post by: jägermeister on March 03, 2013, 06:39 pm
hi, shrooms can make you nauseous - but depends on your stomach i guess;
until now i didn't have too many problems in that regard, i just try not to eat anything or only little breakfast, when taking them the same evening
also, i would recommend dried sclerotia (so called "truffels"), for they always gave me a very lucid experience and i never had any nausea problems;
there are quite a variety of different strains and species of magic mushrooms (the active compound being psilocin/psilocybin),
and some of them, like the more commonly used/sold "cubensis" species (because they are easy to cultivate) don't provide me with the same mental clarity that the truffels do
in small doses the experience is very similar to lsd (they are both in the same family of psychoactive chemicals and dock to the same neuroreceptor sites), but only lasting about half as long (3-4 hours)

lsd on the other hand never provided me with any nausea, and for introduction into the world of psychedelics might also be more convenient,
as with magic mushrooms it might be difficult in the beginning to differentiate all that is all being sold under the label "magic mushrooms",
at least it was for me
so some background knowledge might not be worst idea, then you could even cultivate them yourself

i could definitely understand your reservation towards psychedelics when coming from salvia - it's a completely unrelated experience though, so no worries.
to me it is one of the most fulfilling experiences i can think of, and it comes without any serious risks involved

Post by: bokchoy on March 03, 2013, 07:40 pm
Quote from: druff1138 on March 03, 2013, 05:24 pm
I only have experience with LSD and Shrooms. I just trust those because of the history. A lot of this other stuff like DMT, salvia, Bath salts, seem kind of little studies and trials have been done.
Bath salts and the drugs in them are definitely unknown in terms of safety, but DMT and Salvia have most likely been around longer than the humans. Seriously both have been used in shamanic ceremonies in many primitive cultures since the beginning of recorded human history. Neither of them are for everybody, especially salvia, but both are pretty damn safe.

Personally I hate Salvia, but one time I smoked Salvia while on the peak of my first ever LSD experience (6 tabs, but it was known to be weak acid), and the nature of the salvia was completely different than any other time I tried it. It was honestly a really great trip, and I recommend everyone try Salvia on the peak of another psychedelic at least once.

About DMT, the only dangerous part of doing DMT is if you make an Ayahuasca brew that involves MAOI's and you don't know what you're doing and what/ when you can and cannot eat. Pure DMT, on the other hand, is one of the safest drugs known to man. If you smoke too much, you simply black out and don't remember the experience. It is possible that DMT is naturally bio-synthesized  in most if not all known life forms, and is even thought to be released in humans immediately before death and when we sleep (which would explain why we have dreams). There have also been many scientific studies of DMT done with humans and other animals (watch the documentary DMT: The Spirit Molecule if you want to learn more, last time I checked it's still on Netflix). And by the way, DMT is by far my favorite psychedelic  8)

Anyways, I have a lot of experience with many different psychedelics (I've lost count, but it's probably over 20 or so). Here's a list of my top 5:

1. DMT (smoked, but Ayahuasca would probably be amazing)
2. Psilocybin/ Psilocin
3. LSD
4. 2C-B
5. This one changes a lot, but right now I am really liking 5-MEO-DIPT

Realistically I have a top 10 list, but top 5 is easier to place and more consistent over time. There's a couple psychedelics that I haven't tried that almost certainly would knock on or two of these out of their top 5 spot, such as Mescaline, DOM, or 2C-T-7, but for now it is what it is.
