Sorry for the stupid thread.

First can we stop using the word safe. There is no "safe" drug out there, this is because of the drug and the fact that the market is illegal with no quality control.

ALL drugs cause harm. You need to understand where the harm comes from in your drug of choice. And then carry out actions to mitigate or reduce that harm, to use your drug in the safest way possible.

Everybody is different, and will have different reactions to drugs. What you consider safe isn't true for someone else. First understand what harm your drug has, and then decide if you want to take it.

How do we measure the harm done by drugs?

Measuring harm is a complicated process. There are lots of different harms to be considered - deaths, mental health, illness, social problems like crime and violence. Some drugs are particularly harmful in one area but not others. And this balance can change over time as use and trends develop.

A study in the UK, grouped harms into, harms against the person and harms against society. For example, Drug specific mortality and impairment of mental health or crime and economic cost. After this a technique called multi criteria decision analysis was used (Google it). In the end drugs where rated on a liner scale (0-100) with a higher number indicating more harm.

The list starting with most harmful are

1. Alcohol 72
2. Heroin 55
3. Crack 54
4. Meth 33
5. Cocaine 27
6. Tobacco 26
7. Amphetamines 23
8. Cannabis 20
9. GHB 19
10. Benzos 15
11. Ketamine 15
12. Methadrone 14
13. Mephedrone 13
14. butune 11
15. Anabolic Steroids 10
16. MDMA 9
17. Khat 9
18. LSD 7
19. Buprenorphine 7
20. Mushrooms 6 (include DMT, Mescaline, here)

Reducing your harm

As you can see everything on your list is rated as causing minimal harm. Only 2C-B is not on the list since little research has been done. You should practice harm reduction on the drug you are taking for example, make sure you follow simple guidelines on using psychedelics. A good place to start is http://howtousepsychedelics.org/ [clearnet]

MDMA has recently been confirmed to cause cell damage. But again if you follow harm reduction, and use one to two times a month. Follow correct hydration guidelines and know that it is MDMA you are taking. You should be ok.

Harms from psychedelics come from bad prep and too much being taken. Leading to overwhelming experiences in an unsafe environment.

Read up on your drug science at:

http://www.drugscience.org.uk/ [clearnet]

Read this book

http://www.amazon.co.uk/Drugs-Without-Minimising-Harms-Illegal/dp/1906860165 [clearnet]

Take some time to understand the basic science around drugs, and you can take drugs safely, taking no more risks than crossing the road in some cases.

Off your list - nothing would give great concern, MDMA is neurotoxic but less so than alcohol. On the psych front all are OK in low doses and good settings. An overdose doesn't mean death, you can overdose on LSD, this means you take too much and can no longer function. The higher the dose on psychedelics the more likely you are to have a negative, fearful experience that could cause lasting emotional damage. But none of this is a problem if you understand the harms and the risk.

My personal opinion is that psychedelics are pretty safe, and much better when you are older. Psychedelics work better with greater life experiences to apply some context.

Stay safe kids  ;)

Can someone briefly explain what 2CB is and what effects it has?

2C-B  is a psychedelic drug of the 2C family, others being 2C-E, I, P... It was first synthesized by Alexander Shulgin in 1974.

A 2C-B experience is said to be a cross between MDMA and LSD. But in practice it has its own signature.  It is quite a different drug.

To simplify, you get the social connection to people like MDMA, without being "pushed" into a loved up state. It feels like you have mental connection, but it can be more intellectual than emotional. But if you have an strong emotional connection with someone it will intensify it.

You get the visuals of LSD, but without the head space. The head space can be clear, but logical thinking suffers a little. Large doses can be pretty confusing. You can get really interested and in to things, like reading, writing, watching telly. It can be very euphoric.

The visuals can be really good, the world just sparkles and shines. Looking at stars on a clear night is great.

Dosing correctly is very important on 2C-B. The dose response isn't linear. That is to say, a difference of 1mg can take you from a nice experience to one that is pretty overwhelming.  A typical starting oral dose is around 15-20mg, then you work your way up to a level you are comfortable with. This is probably why it is not a "street" drug, you have to dose it well. Too much and you can end up in hospital.

You can snort or plug (rectal administration) when you do this you should halve your dose. Because you avoid digestion and more of the drug is metabolised.

The body load, i.e. the feelings and sensations, can be strong. More so if you take it orally, it can cause gas and sickness. But this is reduced if you snort or plug it. The tactile feedback is good, doing yoga or having a massage feels very nice, as is any sexual contact.

Orally it comes on in 45-75 minutes and lasts 4-8 hours. Peak can last for 2-4 hours, and you feel like it is coming on in waves. Smoking weed can intensify the waves.

Come down is pretty mellow, you return to a baseline with little hangover. Some people can even sleep on or after the peak.

2C-B is a good drug for different settings. Sitting at home on your own, at a rave or party, out in nature, it can all work. Watch your dose on it, and in social settings you can feel a little anxious on larger doses. And larger doses cause more body load, increased heart rate, sickness etc.

Mix it with MDMA and you will be in heaven. Take the MDMA first and then 1-2 hours later when you are just starting to peak. Take the 2C-B, they work really well together, and smooth out the MDMA come down.
