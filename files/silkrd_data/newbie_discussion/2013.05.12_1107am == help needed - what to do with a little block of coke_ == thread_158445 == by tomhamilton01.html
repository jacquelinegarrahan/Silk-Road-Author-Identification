Title: Re: help needed - what to do with a little block of coke?
Post by: 1conoclast on May 12, 2013, 03:05 pm
Ha ha, ya you must be pretty newb.  That's cool though, we've all been there. 

When coke is shipped from it's country of manufacture like Peru or Columbia it is wrapped into Kilogram sized bricks.  This is when the coke is most pure that is why when someone says "it's straight off the brick" it is suppose to mean it is uncut.

As the kilogram gets broken down and sold in smaller portions to other dealers, those dealers may choose to cut the pure coke in order to increase their profits.

Now, as far as being in a clump or powder, it doesn't really matter because a lot of dealers "re-rock" the coke after they cut it so it looks like it's straight off the brick.  If you get powder from the bottom of the kilo it could still be good.  Some of the worst coke I've had was in a brick form and I've also had good coke that was mostly powder.

A better judge of quality is just by looking at it.   Slightly yellowish, somewhat oily, flakey or a shiney sheen, in my experience, is usually better.  Dry and gritty like chalk and pure white is usually more cut.  A strong odor is usually a good sign too.  Also, if the rock breaks down into powder very easily it has probably been re-rocked.  If the chunk just keep breaking into smaller chunks then it might be better.

Either way, just break it down into a fine powder and sniff that shit.
