98% of the Priority Flat rate packages that I receive come with postage stamps. You can slap $5,6,7 (whatever it takes) worth of stamps on it and drop it in a blue box. They sell $1 stamps.

That's the easiest way. You also need to pick up some of those white DCN slips too. But that's it.

And I don't think that you need to go to a staffed post office to get any of that at all if you have an APC in your area. But otherwise, just get them at a regular post office. You can also order stamps and other postage supplies online.

But stamps are the way to go if you don't want to print online or go to the postal counter. Some vendors do pay at the counter, but they have to use a million different post offices. They don't go to the same on every time.
