Welcome to SR! My first advice to you would be to persevere and read through the forums. Naturally a lot of people come through here who want to score some weed but it's very important that you do this in a way that's safe for you.

My GPG software of choice is GPG4USB and there is an excellent, simple tutorial on how to use it here : http://32yehzkk7jflf6r2.onion/gpg4usb/

Post by: mercedesbenz on March 27, 2013, 01:42 am
Hi,

Not all vendors on the road require PGP for communication or giving your adress

This is in the SR wiki:

 Receiving address

From the moment you submit your order, to the moment it is displayed to your vendor, the information is fully encrypted and totally unreadable. Then, as soon as your vendor marks your package with the address and confirms shipment, the address is deleted forever and is irretrievable. For the extra cautious, you can encrypt your information yourself with your vendor's public key so that even we at Silk Road would be unable to view it, even if we wanted to.

I've already made  successful transactions from about 10 vendors and only one of them required pgp for communication and shipping details

Just my humble opinion though



Title: Re: Hi.. I could use some advice and help. Please!
Post by: ladyjane on March 27, 2013, 01:53 am
PGP is highly recommended and since it really is pretty easy, there is no real reason not to do it and give yourself the best protection possible.

The tutorial Fugitive linked you to is a really good one, I used it and found it easy to follow and use.

I suggest you try and take things in steps rather than trying to understand everything at once because it can be a lot to take in. So maybe first decide how you want to run Tor and what OS you will use - tails, liberte etc. Reading through the Security subforum will give you lots of good information.
Once you have your computer ready to go and secure, you can then learn pgp.
Once you've done that, learn how and where to get bitcoins.
Then you'll be ready to go. It takes some time, but it is worth it to be safe and secure.
