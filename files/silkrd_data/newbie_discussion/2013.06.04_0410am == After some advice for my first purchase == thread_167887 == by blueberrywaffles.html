Title: Re: After some advice for my first purchase
Post by: alchemetric on June 04, 2013, 05:41 am
If you feel comfortable FE'ing and the vendor has plenty of positive feedback it can't hurt. I know I talked it down before, but sometimes it's just a courtesy. The seller is taking a risk selling to a new buyer, but you're also taking a risk giving away your money without having a product. It's really up to you. That's why I suggest starting with small purchases, if you lose $20 here and there it's not a big deal. When I first started on SR I'm pretty sure I FE'd early a few times to get the ball rolling. I'm now up to 30 transactions.

One more tip to stay in good standing with vendors: Finalise and leave feedback as soon as your product arrives. a) That releases the money to the vendor. If you get your product then wait a week to finalise you are basically not giving the seller their money, which is rude. b) If you forget to finalise within 14 days and SR finalises automatically for you it gives you an "auto-finalise" score on your profile. Sellers can see this and some sellers won't sell to people unless they have a ZERO auto-finalise score.

Enjoy your travels on the Silk Road and beyond :D
