Post by: dmtrip on February 06, 2013, 06:05 pm
Yea sounds like you missed when you tried to hit on your wrist. When you shot it, did it burn as you were injecting? Thats one tell tale sign that you are missing the vein. It will burn slightly. Then you know to stop and retry hitting your vein. My best advice would be to stop injecting right away, before you get the hang of it. But that is coming from a recovering addict so I am a little biased. If you are going to continue IV'ing, this is the 2nd best advice I can offer you. Tie off. Use a belt or a string or something to tie off above the elbow. Pump your arm a few times until your veins start to pop. Now take the needle and enter the vein at the closest angle to perpendicular to your arm as you can. This prevents you from pushing the needle into your vein and then THROUGH the other side. This is a common problem especially if you're using a longer point. Now once you think you're in, pull back the plunger to verify. If you see blood, then bingo. You're in. Push the plunger in slowly and make sure you don't feel any burn. If you do then you came out of the vein and you need to readjust. If you feel no burn then continue pushing the plunger until about 1/2 of the shot has been injected. Now stop and pull back on the plunger again just to double check that you're still in. If you are, then go ahead and inject the rest. A small little bump (sort of like a mosquito bite) is normal after a shot, as this is just from the needle poking you. Hope this helps.
