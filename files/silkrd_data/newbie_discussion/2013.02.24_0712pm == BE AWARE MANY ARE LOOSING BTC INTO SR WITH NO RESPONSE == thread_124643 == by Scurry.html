Anabri, remember how bitcoin is like a currency, such as the dollar, yen, and euro.  They have a value against your currency, for example the dollar, and that changes over time.  You can check the latest, high, and low dollar to bitcoin prices on an exchange, most notably mtgox dot com.  Clearnet warning - https://mtgox.com

So what happened was that you went to bed and the price of bitcoin dropped slightly.  SR seems to adjust to the weighted average, so sudden spikes and drops don't move the value drastically and I assume that there must be a time by which SR updates its bitcoin to dollar/euro values.  You can see that weighted average on the mtgox dot com page.

Some vendors price their product pegged to the dollar, so the cost of the items in bitcoins will change.  I think that other times, vendors may charge a fixed bitcoin fee, regardless of the dollar amount.  So, if you deposit a bunch of bitcoin in your account and the price of bitcoins goes up while they are in your account, guess what?  You have more money to play with!  Conversely if the price of bitcoin drops, you may have less buying power.  So if you don't want to risk it, only put in what you are going to spend right away.

Also, Blue Sky Traders uses different metrics to calculate their price for bitcoin.  If you look on their webpage it talks about the minimum ask price being what you will probably get for a rate.  Bitcoin trading prices go up and down alot, it was probably just the price difference between when you asked and when they were able to buy...

It seems like what is happening is that deposits and withdrawals are getting stuck in SR's system.  What I mean is, withdrawals are registering within SR so that the bitcoins are taken out of accounts as usual but then they get stuck somewhere before registering on the network, and never appear on blockchain.

Similarly, with deposits, they make it to SR but then get stuck somewhere before being credited to the user's account.

This reaffirms to me that maybe all that needs to happen is for SR Support to push the "stuck" transactions manually in both directions.
