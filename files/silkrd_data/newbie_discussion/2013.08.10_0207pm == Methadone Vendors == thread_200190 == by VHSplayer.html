Methadone is free if you go through a lot of paperwork for the government, and they make you perform a physical and test you to confirm how much opiates are in you, and what else is in you. You basically get marked for life as a drug abuser in a number of places.  It's really not a desirable option unless you are really running out of options.

This is why methadone purchased by end-users who are trying to turn their lives around is something that SR should at the very least not be complicit in allowing a group of vendors to hinder by cornering the market and setting exorbitant prices.  I have posted a few times about how the price for 10mg methadone tablets shipped domestically in the USA seems to be moving in lock step, like the price movements were planned almost.

I remember at the beginning of this year tablets were as low as $5-8 for 1x10mg tablet commonly, and the prices crept up until they got to $10 for 1x10mg tablet, and then a group of vendors that have the most methadone and always have stock seemed to increase all of their methadone listings overnight to $20 for 1x10mg tablet, regardless of quantity (so they sell 20x10mg methadone for $200 USD!).

This should be investigated by SR staff in my opinion because it is depriving SR members of a tool that might be critical in their recovery from opiate addiction.  Seeing vendors profiteer by cornering the market for domestic methadone is absolutely disgusting.

I know a vendor who sells methadone every month but every time they list methadone for sale it is all bought up by one person within a matter of minutes.  This is on a different sales board, which is how I can see who bought what.  I can also see that this methadone buyer literally bought out every single domestic supply of methadone on that site as it came along.  That is all they seem to do, buy every dose of methadone that comes along that is less than $20 so they can keep their set price of $20 for 1x10mg tablet.  This kind of collusion only works with methadone. Oxycodone, hydrocodone, and other opiates can be substituted for each other relatively well.  But there is nothing out there with the long duration and strength methadone possesses that helps opiate addicts to stay on the path to recovery.

Whoever is doing this, shame on you!
