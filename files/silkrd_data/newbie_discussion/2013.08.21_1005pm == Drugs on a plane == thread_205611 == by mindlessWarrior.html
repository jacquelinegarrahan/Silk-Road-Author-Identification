Title: Re: Drugs on a plane
Post by: CMK2914 on August 21, 2013, 11:58 pm
Ok so first of all, taking any amount of drugs on an airplane is risky.

Now that that's over with...

A few things:

1) The TSA's job is to look for weapons/bombs.  They've said outright before that it's not their job to find drugs, but if they do, they will take it seriously.

2) Most of the dogs you'll see in an airport are bomb-sniffing dogs, but there may be some drug dogs.  You just never know.

3) How much do you know about your departure airport?  If you know that they only use metal detectors, and not the backscatter machines, it is probably safer to keep it on your person, and make ABSOLUTELY sure that there is nothing on you that will set off the metal detector and lead to an "enhanced" pat-down.  If you make it through the metal detector, 99% of the time you are home free, but you may get selected for a more intensive search.  Make sure it is REALLY well hidden just in case this happens.

4) That said, I once got to my destination, opened my bag, and found a bag of molly in plain sight, that must have been in that bag from another time.  I made it through the x-ray machine in my carry-on, and it was even in a suspicious-looking drug bag.  I got really, really lucky.

5) You could always plug it and then go to the bathroom and retrieve before you get on the plane...
