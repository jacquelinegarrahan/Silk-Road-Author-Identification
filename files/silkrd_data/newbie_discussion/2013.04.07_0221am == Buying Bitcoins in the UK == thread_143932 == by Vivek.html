Title: Re: Buying Bitcoins in the UK
Post by: Sophie on April 08, 2013, 05:43 pm
I researched this a few days ago, and decided on Bitstamp+Transferwise.

Bitstamp (https://www.bitstamp.net/) is a pretty large EU-based bitcoin exchange (nearest EU equivalent of mtgox, AFAICT). They accept SEPA payments (EU-wide bank transfers). Banks often charge non-trivial fees on SEPA (~£9), which you can avoid if you want (at the cost of it taking a couple of days) by wiring the money with a service like Transferwise (https://transferwise.com), which will charge ~0.5%. The transfer's done in €, but Bitstamp accounts are denominated in USD (they convert for free on receipt).

That's is the safe/legit route - much less chance of losing your money than with posting cash to a random SR vendor. Obviously it's not anonymous in the same way, but so what - I don't care to hide that I'm buying BTC. (And BTC can always be anonymised by paying to SR via a coinmixing service).
