Title: Re: Storing ground up mushrooms
Post by: cyanspore on March 01, 2013, 03:16 pm
storing dried mushrooms in a freezer isn't a good idea because that can introduce moisture back into it wich can case bacterial growth

oxygen and water and light are the three factors that contribute to the deterioration of potency

the best way to store dried mushrooms is to keep them dried in a vacuum in a dark place. So keep them in the vacuum pack until ready to use. I store my dried mushrooms whole in ball mason jars under tight vacuum until ready to ship. They stay fresh and potent for 2 years at least that way
Title: Re: Storing ground up mushrooms
Post by: niner on March 01, 2013, 03:49 pm
What I did was buy some desiccant packets (silica/silicon dioxide) and a large enough mason jar. I taped several packets but near the top of the jar just in case the mushies shouldn't touch them. Or you can buy one of those foodsaver mason jar  vacuum sealers, although I can't attest to the quality of it.
