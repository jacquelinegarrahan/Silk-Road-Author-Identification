You know, some vendors have policies like, FE for newbs or FE for outside USA or EU. You'll see it all the time and here's how it works.

The vendor is taking the majority of the risks in this transaction. You can deny a package sent to you—you could also send anyone anything, right?

So much of this is built on trust and respect. This is the BM remember. That's how it is. Like "Street 'cred" or whatever. It's all the same.

FYI: you can always CHANGE your feedback. For example, you are told to FE for an order before it's sent 'cause you're a newbie. You do and are told to leave a 5/5. If your package never arrives, message the vendor. If you hear nothing, you can change your feedback and their rating falls and falls. Eventually, vendors that aren't credible do get dropped you know…

Just so you know! I've FEd a couple times now. And now there's also a mutual trust between vendor and buyer. Dig?
