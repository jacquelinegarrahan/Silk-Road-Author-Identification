Title: Re: Digital Scales
Post by: FugNuggetz on February 03, 2013, 08:39 pm
AWS GPR-20 is a good entry level milligram resolution scale. It has a SD of about +/- 3 mg which is OK for my uses. IMO if you are going to buy one of these scales then you need to just go ahead and get a calibration weight kit. The one I got was only like 10-15 dollars and comes with tweezers as well as every type of weight under the sun (1mg, 10mg, 20mg, 200mg, 500mg) all the way up to 50g. Getting a good scale is useless if you don't have a reference to test its accuracy. Remember that changes in environmental conditions (pressure, temperature, air flow) can cause huge changes in a scale's reading. If you moved your scale, you most likely need to recalibrate it.
