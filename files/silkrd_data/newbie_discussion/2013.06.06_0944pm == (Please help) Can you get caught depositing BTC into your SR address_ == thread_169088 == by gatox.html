Title: Re: Can you get caught depositing BTC into your SR address?
Post by: ☼LightOfPi☼ on June 06, 2013, 10:15 pm
Yes, there is a chance to get caught like that: in case SR is compromised and if you access blockchain.info through your regular IP address.
 
I'd first route them through another wallet not 100% linked to your identity and/or IP address. That gives you atleast some plausible deniability. I suggest the regular bitcoin-QT client routed through TOR.

You can use additional wallets (while switching TOR identities when sending between those), but that's probably overkill for a small time buyer.

Title: Re: (Please help) Can you get caught depositing BTC into your SR address?
Post by: ☼LightOfPi☼ on June 06, 2013, 10:34 pm
Well if you already logged in the blockchain.info with your regular IP I would just do it on the clearweb. No need to 'incriminate' yourself by logging in with a TOR IP that is already linked to your real account.

If you want to do it again in the future I'd advise a bitcoin-QT client (in an encrypted volume) routed through TOR.

It's all very logical when you think of it. Best to keep the two completely separated (clear vs dark).
