Title: Re: When i need FE, what does that mean?
Post by: MrVidalia on February 03, 2013, 09:35 pm
FE = Finalize Early.

This bypasses the protections offered by the Escrow system. If your packakge never arrives, there's really nothing you can do if the seller themselves isn't willing to help. Make sure to do your part to ensure the package does arrive.

Hon, it means look elsewhere....

it also means, even though you can't post in the other forums, that you need to READ them - hell, just read the buyer's guide... they are there for YOUR benefit.

DON'T EVER FE on an amount you wouldn't throw away. kk, FE means "Finalize Early" and you'll have to do this for "x" numbers of purchases before "x" vendor will sell to you if you are a "new" buyer. So, the $180 gram of coke you want is NOT one you want to FE on.... buy some "somethings" else - digital goods, etc. that can be delivered and "FINALized" immediately. This will start you on your way to building buyer stats that will make the over-priced coke vendor comfortable in dealing with you.
