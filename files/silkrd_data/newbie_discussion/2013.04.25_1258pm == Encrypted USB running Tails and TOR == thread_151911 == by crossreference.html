How about I just answer it here.  Unless you already have TAILS on a flash drive then you need to burn it to a dvd first.  Boot up from the dvd and in the options on the top left click administrator I think it is and click clone to flash drive.  That simple.  TAILS has TOR built in already.  It's a hardened kernel and everything goes through TOR except the "unsafe browser".  Liberty is way less bloated (one third the size), you don't need to burn a dvd, and it's slightly more anonymous.  They are both good though.
Title: Re: Encrypted USB running Tails & TOR
Post by: Vegeta on April 26, 2013, 10:33 am
You don't need to burn the iso to DVD, you could instead run the iso in a virtual machine, and plug the USB drive in, route the USB drive to the virtual machine , and then use the TAILS USB installer to install TAILS onto the USB drive. Works just the same and saves you a DVD. Though having a DVD is nice because it can't be tampered with after the fact.

Now if you wanted to also configure a persistent volume from a virtual machine, you'd need to do a bit more work. This kind of stuff is handy when your old laptop's DVD drive doesn't work anymore or simply don't have a DVD drive or burner
