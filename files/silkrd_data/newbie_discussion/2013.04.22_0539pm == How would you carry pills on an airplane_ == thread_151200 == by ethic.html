I think the wrapped-up Baggie is a good idea for weed, but for 30 pills you may be creating more potential issues than you're solving. I think you're far worse off having the x-ray machine show a Baggie rolled up in a shampoo bottle than simply a prescription bottle full of pills. Agree 100% with your other advice about lots of toiletries and OTC medication (if you don't have Rx bottles).

My wife travels with a few hundred pills of about 10 different medications at least a dozen times a year. She has never had her pills checked. I fly about 40 times a year (really) in the US with 20+ Vicodin 10/325's, a few flexiril, and some Valium randomly mixed together in a starbuck's mint tin and have never been checked - not once. I've also taken 10 E pills in an Advil bottle on about 6 different international flights. Never had a problem.

The things to remember are -

1. If at all possible, have a prescription bottle in your name and put them in that. The more obscure the medication, the better off you are.
2.  If you don't have a prescription bottle in your name, get some completely unknown over-the-counter medication and replace all the pills with your pills. Problem with Advil is that everyone knows exactly what they're supposed to look like, so if they do open the foil and it doesn't look like Advil, you're screwed.
3. If possible, put as many random, small, normal odds and ends in the bag as you can - chargers, flash drives, little toothpaste tubes, etc. so it's not just your clothes and one bottle of drugs. If you have other medications, all the better.
4. Airport security is not looking for <50 pills of anything UNLESS you stand out or pose a potential threat. Dress nicely, act professional, don't look shifty, and don't go through security with a bunch of people looking like you're hitching a ride to Burning Man. I do go to Burning Man, but I don't wear my playa gear on flights :) When you arrive at your destination, you can wear whatever the hell you want, but don't make a counterculture statement as you try to bring drugs through airport security.
5. Best attitude to take is slightly bored, checking your phone to pass the time, barely noticing you're going through security at all. If you have a significant other or friend, go together. Larger group, go through with just one person, not a crowd of 3-6 party people.
6. Worst-case scenario and they do find them and ask, just tell them they're the medication listed on the bottle (I hope for your sake they don't have little smiley-face imprints). If they say they need to confiscate them, say fine - you'll just get another bottle when you get there. Do not complain or act like it's any big deal. While possible that they'll go full Monty and escalate it, most of the security people don't want to be caught looking like fucking idiots by freaking out over a bottle of Ex-Lax. If the pills aren't labeled, it's unlikely they'll take it further unless they feel confident they're illegal.

If you don't feel that you can keep your cool under pressure, then the above may not help. Airport security definitely does pay close attention to nervous people - maybe a couple of Valium/Xanax and a beer might help :)

Hope this helps!
