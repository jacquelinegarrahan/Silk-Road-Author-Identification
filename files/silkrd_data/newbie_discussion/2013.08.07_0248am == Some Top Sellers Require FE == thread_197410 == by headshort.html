Title: Re: Some Top Sellers Require FE
Post by: Praetorian on August 07, 2013, 04:41 am
NorCal420HookUp has cheaper listings for FE, but usually also offers Escrow for a bit more. 

The simplest answer is never FE... and you'll never get scammed.  But, some buyers know that there are just two kinds of vendors.  Professionals, and Opportunists.  The Professionals stick around year after year, some require FE, some don't.  The Opportunists, without saying, wait for the right opportunity, and take you for a ride.

If you're going to FE for someone, you better research them up and down before doing so.  If you do not see something in these forums by a simple search of the vendor's name, then you know something is up.  Next, if you're not seeing that others have also FE'd and got their goods, then don't jump right in with that vendor.  As stated, some vendors are opportunists.  Be that a FE'd package getting snagged by USPS, and them being like "oh fuck" and running off with your money... never to be heard from again; or by them maybe getting overwhelmed, or feeling some kind of 'heat' and wanting to plan an early retirement.  You just don't know.

It's been a touchy subject since the dawn of these discussion boards from what I've gathered.  I know a handful of trusted FE vendors, and if you look them up you will see nothing but rave reviews on these boards for them.  Again, if you don't or can't find much on a particular vendor requesting or requiring FE... don't buy from them, period.

Another thing to mention is that the "Report Vendor" button is far less effective at bringing something to the attention of the Silk Road Administrators.  If you have an issue, or see something that clearly breaks the 'rules' set forth in the SR Vendor Wiki, then by all means contact username: "SR Support". 

There's a link at the bottom of the SR page(s) that says "Support"; this is the same thing as contacting "SR Support" directly.  It is also set as a Vendor account, so you can 'fan/favorite' them as well if that's better for you.

I recommend reporting ALL vendors "Requiring FE" on any listing, big or small, if they have less than 35 transactions.  That's how 75% of scams here start.  The other 25% are Selective, or Opportunistic 'retirement' or 'fuck it' scams.  ???
