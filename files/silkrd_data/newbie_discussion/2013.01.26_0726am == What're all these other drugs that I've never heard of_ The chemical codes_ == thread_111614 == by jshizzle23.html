Post by: 2cnme2 on April 21, 2013, 01:50 am
You need to read PiKHAL and TiKHAL to be up on all of the different "RC"'s or research chemicals, which is a horrible misnomer that implies some sort of extra risk which isn't really there.  PiKHAL deals with phenethylamines, mostly mescaline analogues with a 2,4,5-trisubstituted phenethyl (2C-X) or alpha-methylphenethyl (DOX) moiety.

Just like any new drug (and most of those are by no means new anymore as they're ~30-40yr old), it is difficult to predict long-term/rare adverse events from said drug.
