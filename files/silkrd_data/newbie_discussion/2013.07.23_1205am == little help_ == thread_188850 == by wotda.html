Quote from: The Missus on July 23, 2013, 12:34 am
Never EVER never Finalize early. That is, release money to vendors before you receive your product.

Agreed.

Do NOT do business with any vendor who demands finalizing early (FE), which is releasing your money from escrow BEFORE your product arrives, and BEFORE you checked to make sure the product is all there as you ordered it.

Many vendors will package slightly more, just to please their customers that much more, since it is uncommon.

For example, you order 10 grams of meth, and they pack 10.5 grams (500mg extra crystal meth in weight) just to ensure the customer is satisfied.

However, many vendors behave unprofessionally and irresponsibly, and run out of funds before they can fulfill their obligations to their customers.

They ask to FE all to often out of incompetence, lacking the funds to follow through with their responsibilities.

Post by: AirshipAdmiral on July 23, 2013, 01:25 am
Bitcoin source: http://localbitcoins.com/ is my personal preference.

Best standards: Learn how to use GPG4Win (or whatever platform you are using. If TAILS, which is highly recommended, it comes with GPG and Kleopatra, a front-end GUIfied interface for it).

Learn enough about how OpenPGP works (and how public-key cryptography works in general) to understand how to encrypt and decrypt messages in more than just email, in clipboard (copypasta) text and TXT files.

Learn enough about Bitcoin and Tor to understand the limitations and flaws of the software and security models to avoid serious compromises of security, including anonymity.

Learn enough about network and system security so you understand how to manage risk.

Since you are not new to the scene, I probably don't have to explain how to protect yourself offline, with burn phones, disguises, circuitous indirection to throw off any pursuers, etc.
