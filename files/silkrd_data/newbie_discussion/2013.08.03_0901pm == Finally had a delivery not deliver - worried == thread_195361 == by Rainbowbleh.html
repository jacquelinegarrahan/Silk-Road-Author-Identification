The vendor should have the tracking number, unless they're an idiot.

If they check the tracking label from a remote location, and it was seized, the Track will have stopped, with either "forwarded" or a simple "Processed through sort" being the last updates in the system.

... some times things DO get lost in the mail, but it's more likely that the vendor is yanking your chain if they can't tell you what's up.

Good luck with that my friend, and definitely do NOT Finalize.

Typically it takes 4-6 months to receive a Love Letter.

Controlled Deliveries can happen very shortly after, all the way up to 30 days later or more.  Depends on which agency is taking charge.  DEA typically chases bigger cases, but your local PD might not be so open minded.  They could come after you for literally nothing on hopes of finding something bigger within your home.  Never underestimate the measures in which LEO will take to ensure they have a case.
