Title: Re: Cannabis and depression
Post by: postrex on August 08, 2013, 08:15 pm
Cannabis will absolutely affect the way you think and feel.  If you are per-disposed mentally, depression can certainly result from ongoing usage.  It's not a direct result of the chemicals, but a side effect of the other physiological and mental effects.

As with all psychoactive drugs, your environment and mental state will have significant effects on your experience.  With cannabis in particular, the particular plant (more so then strain) matters a lot.  What is was fed, how it was grown, and the energy that it experienced as it aged significantly effects the high.  The same strain can cause severe paranoia, or none at all, depending on how it was grown.  That said, for many people continued use of any cannabis will usually see a decrease in those side effects.  If paranoia and anxiety is a major issue, try a few different types, and specifically look for something non-kush that burns a very very light grey or white ash.

Repeated use over short periods of time will absolutely alter the delicate balance of chemistry in a users brain. Everyone is different, but especially in teenagers there can be some symptoms of depression or anxiety caused by cannabis use.
