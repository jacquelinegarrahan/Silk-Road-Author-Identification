Title: Mac users struggling with PGP look no further
Post by: kesser on January 29, 2013, 01:32 am
It took me a while to figure out the whole PGP encryption and i had to do a fair amount of lurking around and researching in able to find a good and effective way of using PGP through OSX. But here is a quick guide i wrote up for another user with the same problems as i had.

GPGTools, it is very easy to use and nicely built.
Go to: https://www.gpgtools.org/installer/index.html

Install the client, once it is installed open it up and you'll be greeted with the main page of the client.

Before you start generating a key you want to go to; System Preferences > Keyboard > Keyboard Shortcuts > Services (on the little side menu to the left)
You'll see a long list of different short cuts you can turn on and off, scroll right down to the bottom then go couple of shortcuts up you'll see a list of OpenPGP shortcut options, turn them all on from the very top one being 'Decrypt Selection' to the last one at the bottom being 'Verify Signature Of Selection'.

Now you have done this go back to the GPG Keychain and create a new key.
Click on the 'New Key' button in the top left of the window, it'll bring up a menu enter in a name and fake email, obviously nothing related to your actual TSR account or real name.
Select 'Advanced options' and change the length of the key from '2048' to '4096' as this gives better encryption.
Once you have done that click the generate key button, it'll bring up another window asking you to enter in a pass phrase for that select key, so enter what ever you like, longer the better just make sure you don't forget it.

Now that you have your key, open up Text Edit and right click you should see 'OpenPGP:Insert My Key' click that then update the PGP thread with your new key and link the post url to your signature.
Once you have your PGP key, send me the link to it (or just the actual key if you want to test it quickly)
You then need to get my key, mine is located in my signature so just go to that link and copy and paste it into a new or blank Text Edit, highlight the entire key you copied then right click > Services > Import Key From Selection.
When you go back to GPG Keychain client you should see a new PGP added with my name and email on it.

If anyone wants to try it out, post your public key here and send me a message, i will get back to you as soon as possible.
Also you do not have to be using a mac to want to test out your PGP key here, just post your key and i'll help out.
My key is in my sig.
