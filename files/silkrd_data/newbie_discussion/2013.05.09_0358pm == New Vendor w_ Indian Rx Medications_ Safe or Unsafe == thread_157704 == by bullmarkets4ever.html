Title: Re: New Vendor w/ Indian Rx Medications: Safe or Unsafe
Post by: onufri on May 09, 2013, 06:15 pm
India has a law that allows pharmaceutical companies there to ignore patents filed before 1995, so a number of drugs that are still under patent in the US are already generic there. But generic doesn't mean fake — it's usually the exact same chemical formula, or perhaps an enantiomer (a mirror-image configuration) with the same bioactivity.

It's plausible that someone in India could obtain a number of these genuine generics without going to too much trouble. Many generics that are controlled substances in the US are available over the counter in India. Modafinil (Provigil) is a good example.

I don't know about the rate of counterfeiting medication in India, though. I've never heard of it, but absence of evidence is not evidence of absence and your n is 1 here, so.

I would request that he post a picture of the packaging and I'd confirm that India does indeed have a generic version of whatever drug you're interested in for sale in pharmacies. Wikipedia's pretty good on that front for most of the fun Rx drugs.

Title: Re: New Vendor w/ Indian Rx Medications: Safe or Unsafe
Post by: SourGrapes on May 10, 2013, 02:31 am
i've ordered prescriptions from indian pharmacies, and had both good and bad experiences. make sure your pills are in a blister package or have some kind of evidence of being genuine - at least do a search on the color, shape, and imprints on the pills, make sure theyre not sugarpills or anything. don't go for meds that dont seem right -- something that should be a tablet, for instance, being sold in a generic capsule.

keep in mind the standards for factory hygiene, product purity, exact mg doseage, etc are nowhere near as stringent in india as they might be in, say, the US.
