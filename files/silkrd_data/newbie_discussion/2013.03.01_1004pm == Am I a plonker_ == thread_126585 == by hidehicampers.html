Title: Re: Am I a plonker?
Post by: millhorse on March 02, 2013, 12:27 am
I can't comment on the specifics as I'm neither in the UK nor use the coin exchanges you mention but I'll offer my 2c anyway.

I know that my Btc exchange does not answer or acknowledge mail from Tor clients. As a matter of fact I think they reserve the right to cancel if an account is accessed via a Tor client.

Bitcoins, in and of themselves, are perfectly legal so you have no need to try and maintain any anonymity when converting your cash to BTC. I'm fairly new to this but my understanding is that the exchanges need to maintain an air of "Bankishness" so they are not seen as an easy anonymous way for criminals to launder their proceeds. On the other hand, there is nothing even slightly illegal about you or I buying BTC with funds from our personal bank accounts. As far as our banks are concerned, we are just transferring our money to somebody else, they don't care who. The exchange don't care what you want the BTC for either. Once your cash is converted to BTC you can transfer them anywhere you like. Your Bitcoin exchange has no idea whether the "wallet" you transferred your coins to was your sister, an anti-establishment artist in San-Francisco, or Silk Road.

Your account number with whichever exchange you use should stay the same. The number of your bitcoin "wallet" is randomly generated and may change every time you use it.
