Title: *UK* buying guide. Ten step simple process.
Post by: android465764E on January 28, 2013, 01:56 am
I thought I'd make a simple guide for new UK people. Questions I had when I joined. I'm sharing it so that anyone who is as confused as I was, can get a leg up. It's so simple you don't even need a PC if you're a smart phone junkie. (adequate firewall and protection software is advised however, personally I use AVG Pro Mobile, and Droidwall). 

 

1. Get a ฿itcoin wallet from blockchain.info

2. Download the Barclays pingit app and register your VISA details.

3. Top-up your pingit account using your VISA card.

4. Open your blockchain wallet, and click the little Union Jack flag, then select "pingit dsposit", enter the amount you're gonna put in, follow instructions and make a note of the mobile number and reference number.

5. Open the pingit app, and select "send money". Choose the amount, and enter the phone number and reference number when prompted. Follow on screen instruction.

6. Go into SR and copy your SR wallet number to clipboard (again, long string of letters and numbers).

7. Back to blockchain wallet, and select "send money". Paste your SR wallet number and send.

8. Buy drugs and go through the checkout process.

9. When prompted, enter your address BUT encrypt it using the seller's PGP key.
SR have extensive help files on setting up PGP on your PC.
I also have a guide for using PGP on Android handsets..  dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?topic=116666

10. Wait for drugs to arrive. 



Simplified process :

VISA > Pingit > Blockchain > SR. 



Some things to keep in mind:

There's a lot of people out there who will try and catch you out with btc scams.
At present, there is no safe way of buying btc with paypal. In the UK at least, so don't even bother looking.

Also concerning bitcoins, you can check the rates at mtgox, or download "Bitcoin Paranoia" app. 

Barclay's pingit charge %5.97 for sending out money, transfers between wallets is free, and (from my own experience) VISA do not charge anything extra to send money to Pingit.



__________

That's my contribution to potentially confused UK people. Hope someone finds it useful.

@OP: I'd recommend transferring your coins through a couple of temporary wallets for extra security or preferably use a tumbling service (at a cost!) - this will help your coins stay anonymous. It may take a little while longer, but in my opinion it's worth it. This would change your process to:

VISA > Pingit > Blockchain > Instawallet > Instawallet > SR. 

or

VISA > Pingit > Blockchain > Bitcoin Fog > SR. 

I've always gone for the temporary wallet route and it's worked out, and makes me feel a bit more secure :)
