The short and simple answer is Supply and Demand. For a more complex view on it:

First one has to consider at what stage is the product processed? For example Cocaine might be processed in Colombia while Opium in Afghanistan might have to be exported a certain distance for them to be able to process it into Heroin. Now that gets us to the next stage which would be in what Region does the product come from? Cannabis grown in Colombia versus Cannabis grown in Australia who have been grown the same way will obviously not have the same prices due to a lot of factors.

Next up we have the wholesalers who all have different prices for the most part, the markup from wholesalers to local retailers is obviously because of the risk involved - the price is set by the amount of other people who have been sent by jail more or less. The stages after this are highly customizable depending on the Region and now with the addition of Silk Road there's even more alternatives which has the potential to reduce prices even more. It has however not been able to do this in the slightest yet so far, most likely due to the fact the majority of the population aren't capable of using Silk Road for various reasons. Beyond these basics it will obviously be up to the product itself in terms of legality, popularity and ease of concealment. Now add to this mix the human factor where people are ripping people off, mixing product with crap to increase profitability and so on and it gets a mess - impossible to quantity those factors. To summarize the points that matters:

* Does the product need processing and if so, in what Regions?
* Where does the product get produced and how what risks are involved in transporting it?
* What is the penalty of using the product in Region X?
* How popular is the product in Region X?
* How cheap is the delivery from Vendor X?
----------
