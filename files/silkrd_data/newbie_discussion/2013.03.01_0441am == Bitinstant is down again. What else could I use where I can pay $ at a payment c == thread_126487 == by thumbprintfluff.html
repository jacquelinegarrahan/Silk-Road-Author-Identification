Post by: Dickens018 on March 01, 2013, 06:45 am
Bitinstant permitted  anonymous transactions until recently, but it appears they are now requiring email, name and dob
and "validating" your ID.  I have seen several people mention getting ID errors for small ($250) orders,  and requiring
photos of government issued ID before they can  buy Bitcoin.  Their Terms of Use say they record transactions,   and
may generate statistical reports looking for 'suspicious or illegal activity' to comply money laundering laws. 
They also reserve the right to sell or distribute this info them to third parties.  I imagine they have received pressure
from government,  but that just makes them semi-government agents to me since these agents were made without
informing customers of the change from anonymous to tracked transactions.  I wouldn't trust them.

The odd thing is that person buying $200 of Bitcoins per month for 6 months might look suspicious,  but the guy who
makes a $5000 bank transfer for Bitcoin investments would not raise a second glance.  Indeed, Forbes magazine mentioned
them as a hot investment.
