Post by: unbeknownst on August 22, 2013, 01:01 am
Hey, first post! I've been on SR for awhile, pored through the forums and had quite a few transactions though, so I'd like to share a few thoughts on this and try to put this to bed completely. Sorry if it's long and didactic but since this is for newbs it could be very useful information for those who are paranoid. Also, I'm talking here about the US specifically and many countries have much more rigorous standards so keep that in mind.

I'm going to say that receiving product in the mail carries so little risk, at least domestically and in smaller quantities that as long as you take a few precautions you shouldn't ever lose sleep over it. Not to mention it is orders of magnitude safer than buying on the street.

First, regarding what others have said regarding the amounts you are dealing with, I agree. Even the very rare packages that are detected and intercepted are usually just seized and at most they will send the "love letter" to the receiving address. Doing a "controlled delivery" (i.e. staking out your residence and nabbing you when you pick up the mail) uses a lot of resources and few local LE agencies, to say nothing of the Feds, are going to bother for anything less than keys of product.

The main issue here is illicit substances being detected in the first place. By buying on SR you are placing a lot of trust in the stealth of your vendors, so doing research on sellers (as always) is one of the best ways to protect yourself. I have seen stealth from SR vendors that was so good I'd practically walk in to a police station and sign for the thing without sweating.

Another thing to consider is that domestic orders, provided the stealth isn't egregiously bad, virtually never get inspected (they would need to obtain a warrant to do so) International orders, especially from enthusiastic vending countries/known vendors (esp. from The Netherlands) get scrutinized much more closely, but still quite few of them get seized. For anyone new on SR, ordering domestically at first may be less anxiety inducing, and there are good domestic sellers for most products in most countries where the users of this site are concentrated.

Many people have this image of postal facilities that includes DEA agents wandering around with drug dogs, inspecting and smelling piles of mail. The truth is these plants handle tens of millions of pieces of mail a day, all but the biggest stuff sorted mechanically and at very high speed, most of it barely being touched by human hands. Even if someone were to smell your weed, they most likely wouldn't care or they'd be too busy to do anything about it (or they might take it home, of course!) The postal service (in the US) is also way beyond broke. They can barely keep the lights on, much less worry about coordinating closely with LE and opening your mail (again, they would need to obtain a warrant to open it) And there's also the fact that there aren't anywhere near enough LE personnel to search a fraction of this mail regardless.

BTC. In order to convict you of a crime, I'm pretty sure they have to prove you actually ordered and paid for these things. You have plausible deniability if they can't, especially if you have stuff shipped under an alias. Otherwise, anyone with a grudge could send anyone a big bag of heroin, tell the feds, and have their enemies sent to prison. They can arrest you all they want, which understandably could be embarrassing and inconvenient for you, but that's actually the simplest part for LE. Not having evidence of a transaction effectively means no dice for a conviction. Buy BTC for cash at localbitcoins.com, feed through multiple wallets and you should be fine on that end.

Use PGP encryption for all transactions. There are a bunch of forum threads on how to do this. Of course, don't give out any possibly identifying info in the forums or in feedback. Remember to keep your TOR browser up to date. Block all java script and other add-ons. Using a good VPN in addition to TOR is also good policy. In reality, you are likely much more vulnerable to LE attention through a browser exploit or something similar which could expose your IP address and possibly more, than through having a piece of mail searched.

TL;DR - The risk is almost negligible, with a few caveats. Take a few precautions and you won't need to sweat it, ever.
