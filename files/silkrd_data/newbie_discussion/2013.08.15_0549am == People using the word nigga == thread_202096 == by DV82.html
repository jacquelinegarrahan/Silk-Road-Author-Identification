Bullshit. It is in no way a "racist slur". It is a misnomer of the term "negro", which has been the universal, most commonly used term to refer to people of sub-Saharan African descent throughout history. "Black people" have been referred to as Negros since time immemorial. In fact, in the 1960s and previously the only respectful way to refer to blacks was "negro".  If you called them "african"-anything they were ready to fight over it, it was highly offensive.  "Nigger" is a misnomer of "negro", and is not a "racist slur".  Furthermore, no one is "oppressed by the term".  No one is "oppressed" by any term at all. It's simply a word, a combination of syllables. If anyone at all is "oppressed", it's those people who suffer consequences for uttering the wrong syllables at the hands of other people who believe it should be determined which syllable combinations can and can not be used, or believe that they have a right not to have certain words said in their presence. But a word is itself incapable of "oppressing" anyone.  If you believe in freedom of speech, blacks don't get any special rights that other groups don't get, and everyone is free to say "nigger" or "nigga" whenever they please, and in any context they please.
