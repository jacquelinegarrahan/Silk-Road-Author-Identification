Title: Re: What is Karma all about???
Post by: cerealbox on May 03, 2013, 11:03 am
Karma, Reputation, up and down votes, any number of variants on this exist and it's pretty common these days. The idea is if you like a post you click "thank" (or some equivalent). If you don't like a post you click "discourage." The idea being that humans, who thrive on attention and acceptance, will always attempt to make posts which increase, rather than decrease, Karma, resulting in better posting.

I'm not a huge fan of this system. Everywhere I've seen a similar system implemented it tends to reduce rather than increase the quality of posts. Good comments get downvoted because people to lazy to look up the facts or argue with the poster downvote quality (if counter-intuitive) posts. Users are generally discouraged from having original thoughts and it tends to become a giant circle jerk of weird political correctness. It's not so bad here, but check out yahoo answers, reddit, youtube and other like sites that have completely destroyed any intelligent culture they might have because people are just trying to accumulate crowd-pleasing but vacuous posts instead of having honest and open communication.
