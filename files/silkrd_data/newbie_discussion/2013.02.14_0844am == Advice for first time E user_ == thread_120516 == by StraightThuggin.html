Title: Re: Advice for first time E user?
Post by: MilesStandoffish on February 21, 2013, 01:39 am
I can't recall the actual mechanism of action for magnesium vs. clenching or chattering, but it definitely helps (take a supplement shortly before you take the molly). Some also recommend calcium, and/or a multivitamin just to cover all your bases.

If you're going to be in a place where it's hot, and/or you're engaged in vigorous physical activity (dancing, or, shall we say, other more horizontal things), staying cool is important. MDMA slows down your body's renal system, so you retain water and don't sweat or pee much (if at all... although paradoxically you'll feel dehydrated later). To avoid any risk of water toxicity (if you drink nothing but water, you'll make yourself very ill. Think of the chick who died a few years back drinking gallons of water in a radio contest for a Wii.), drink water with added supplements, or gatorade, or other fluids that add more than just H2O (you'll need sodium and potassium, mostly, to maintain your body's fluid density).

Don't get too overstimulated, but on the same topic, don't just roll on your own... from my limited experience, it can be a rather boring thing to sit there, unless you're very engaged with something fun.

Since MDMA does deplete your serotonin, the next day you'll probably feel very mellow or a little hungover. Take supplements like 5-HTP (Tetra-Hydroxy-Tryptophan), which you can get at places like GNC, to help restore your serotonin and tryptophan levels, and to help you rest. Some individuals who've rolled end up feeling both listless AND restless afterward because of the various neurochemicals being depleted.

From what I remember, it takes about 10+ days for your body to restore its Serotonin levels fully if you're in good health. I'd say 2 weeks between rolls is a good time period.

Hope this helps :)

I looked it up after I left. Apparently Magnesium is used in the inhibition of action potentials along motor neurons and many people are often not getting the recommended daily amount. In addition an slightly increased dose over the RDI can have a muscle relaxing effect due to that mechanism of action. This would mean that this should work across substances.

Also, on the note of serotonin. I noted that no one mentioned this yet: if you're taking any anti-depressants (specifically MAOIs or SSRI/SNRI's), I would recommend not taking them for a while (the waiting period varies based on half life and metabolism) to avoid getting serotonin syndrome (or worse: serotonin toxicity). Interestingly enough they don't really need to take that SSRI much anymore, but that's just more speculation on the matter (although I guess there are therapies now using MDMA+therapy to cure certain depressions and PTSD).
