Title: Why I like mushrooms
Post by: HenryC0833 on July 12, 2013, 12:13 am
"We willed the goddess into being," the man said.  I was almost out of my mind on mushrooms and it was hard to focus. And it was noisy. Bar in a little ski town.  Longest day of summer, dark now.  Lots of almost-sex going on in there. Gorgeous people. The man had a bushy beard and a tie die shirt.  A hippie, and judging from the grey in his beard, probably not a neo-hippie.  A real one.

"It was our desire for her to manifest herself that brought her into being," he said.

I turned and looked at his wife.  Long stringy blonde hair, fringy suede jacket, kind eyes, drinking a beer.  "He always does this.  Ignore him if you like."

"So..." I said.  "We created her?"

"No.  We allowed her to manifest."

"So...."  I looked in the mirror. Even in this town, there is evidence of authentic life.  Moms take their little boys in the summer to play soccer in the park late into the twilight, and I remember what that was like, when I was a kid.     "So... she needed us?"

He smiled.

I looked around, gathering my thoughts.  I was a little overwhelmed.  "OK, let me get this straight..."  But he was gone.  Just that quick; wife and all.  I walked outside.  No hippie. 

It had been a long day.  A long grind though the prairie.  The wind was strong, and it blew heat from under the fairing onto my legs, and I needed  a gallon of Gatorade at one dusty Kwikee-mart.  There was the thrill of running up the backside of the Sangre de Cristo range, and the road past the lakes that reflected the mountains and the sky, the freezing overcast pass, and the highly technical part down into town. 

It was cool now, and if you walked two blocks, away from the bars and the gorgeous rich people laughing in the light, you could see the stars, and the Milky Way.  A meteor shot across the sky, leaving a trail like a sparkler.  I've seen nights where geometric patterns shimmered across the heavens. 

The sky.  Hippies.  The Goddess.  That's why I like mushrooms. 

And that, my chirren, is 50, I believe.

Well....

For me there are always four phases.
1.  The come-up.  This can be a little anxiety provoking.  When things first start to slide, it's natural to want to hang on.  To the extent that shrooms make you queasy, this is why.  First time out, might want to keep a little cannabis or some nice wine to take the edge off.
2.  Euphoria.  The come-up starts at :45 and is over precisely at :60.  Check your watch, it really really only lasts about fifteen minutes.  At that point, you're dumped into this beautiful, sparkling world. It's really like landing on Pandora.  Move through the world with wonder in your heart.  I love this part.
3.  The lesson.  There is always a lesson, and it's not always easy.  You might feel emotions of shame, sorrow, regret.  This is a part of the thing and you have to learn to cherish it, regardless.  Look.  The mushroom is a thing.  It may sound crazy, but once you've been there a couple of times, it will seem quite natural to look at the mushroom as a very old, very wise, and in the end very kind entity.  To be clear, we have a symbiotic relationship.  You learn this when you grow mushrooms, and realize over the years that psychedelic mushrooms couldn't live absent human habitation and agriculture.  So to be fair, you give the mushroom life on this planet, he gives you wisdom.  Just realize that's what this is, it's not all ha-ha look at the crazy lights.
4.  Resolution.   There is always the sense of peace at the end.  The sense of at-one-ment.   

Shrooms aren't easy.  But they are deep.  Which do you want?

Title: How to have a non-crappy mushroom trip
Post by: HenryC0833 on July 20, 2013, 05:44 pm
Using the five "W's"

Who:   Your first time, bring someone who is calm and experienced.  Be around people who are curious, thoughtful, and love to laugh.
What:  Your very first time out, I would start with a quarter of an eighth.   That's like two capsules if you use them that way.  Be very cautious with mushroom tea, it can hit you really fast. 
When:  Don't take shrooms on an impulse.  Make sure you have plenty of time set aside (it's like a six hour experience) and give yourself time to prepare mentally.  Make sure you have plenty of time to relax, visualize, and fill your heart with love.
Where:  Shrooms tend not to like busy environments.  It is possible to go to a party or club on shrooms, provided you've taken a low dose.  If you take just enough to where you are just starting to see visuals out of the corner of your eye, you can be around people.  If you're taking an "effective dose,"  you're a lot better off in a calmer environment.   Personally I think shrooms love the sky and do well outdoors in nature. 
Why:  you are here to learn.  You are taking a journey to what will probably seem like an alternate reality.  This is the real thing, and if you're not ready for the real thing, think twice.  I'll tell you a little story using Christian symbology.  What if you knew for a fact that Jesus Christ himself regularly attended your church, and there was  a possibility he might plop down on the pew right next to you?  How would that make you feel about going to church?  If your answer is, "That would make me nervous, I'm much more comfortable going to church with symbolic representations of the divine rather than the holy Dude himself,"  then please do NOT take mushrooms. 

Look, nothing can beget something not of it's own nature.  An apple tree can't bear peaches.  (Holy dude said that.)  That spark of intelligence you perceive between your ears, that didn't come from nowhere.  You live in an intelligent universe.  You are well insulated from this universal intelligence, being all wrapped up in a bag of skin and bones.  The purpose of the mushroom, no more and no less, is to remove that insulation, to get you right there in it.  I think that's why the mushroom likes nature so much, why it likes the sky, especially the summer sunset over the longest day of the year.   Because you're in it!  Because you get the insight that, for your entire life, you've been watching MOVIES of the sunset, and this is the first time in your life you've ever been IN the sunset.

You sit down in church, and the guy next to you nudges you, and points across the room, and says, "See that?  That's Jesus Christ himself.  How bout that!"  And like when you see a celeb in your club, you feel like you're a part of something.  And then one day, you show up at church, and the big man just plops down next to you.  Turns and smiles.  "What's up, little brother?" 

I hope you're not so ashamed, or embarrassed, or insecure, or afraid that you can't chat the dude up.  That you run out of the room and realize, later, staring at the ceiling in your bedroom, you probably could have asked him anything.  Anything. 

That, little brother, is why people love shrooms, or hate them.
