Welcome. Yes of course ordering smaller amounts is safer, however the best thing for you to do is read vendor feedback, look for good consistent stealth/packaging ratings and then contact the vendor directly if you have any other questions about stealth. (Just don't ask them exactly what it looks like because Vendors don't go into detail for security reasons).

Whatever you use, make sure it's a real address, that you have access to, and the owner of said address/mailbox is someone you are very cool with. Also never use a fake name or different name to different address.  The goal isn't to use methods to hide yourself, it's to blend in. Consider the sheer volume USPS deals in. Anything out of the ordinary is pretty much the only way you will probably have a package scrutinized.

PGP? ABSOLUTELY. It's not hard AT ALL, if you just follow directions and practice it a couple of times. If you can install and run TOR successfully you can use any good PGP program.  It's too easy to use with benefits far too high not to. PGP not only encrypts your messages, you can use it do verify PGP signed messages so you know the sender has A) the private key to sign it in that fashion and B) the passphrase to sign it.  You don't want messages floating around in plain text that has information about you personally.  Security is a group effort.

From the silk road wiki, information on pgp. http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/wiki/index.php?title=PGP/GPG_Encryption

Anyway, that's my opinion as a newish user of SR.  Except the PGP isn't opinion, it's absolutely necessary if you ask any rational person here.

The main points for a buyer to focus on are:

1. Getting bitcoins into your SR account without leaving an obvious trail.
2. Picking the correct vendor, paying close attention to their shipping and stealth reviews.
3. Learning pgp so that, when you talk to the vendor, your details are encrypted.
4. Making sure that, if it all goes to hell, your house and computer are clean and that you look as innocent as possible.

That's an oversimplification but focus your research on those areas and you'll do fine.
