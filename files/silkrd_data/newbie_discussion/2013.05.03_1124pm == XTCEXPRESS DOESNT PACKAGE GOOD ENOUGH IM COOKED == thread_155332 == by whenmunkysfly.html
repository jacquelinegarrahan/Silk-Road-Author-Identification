Post by: Baraka on May 04, 2013, 08:42 am
Exactly. The bottom line is that if LE can't prove you either paid for a particular product shipped to you, or requested it, then they can't make a case beyond a reasonable doubt that you imported anything illegally. That's because people are free to send you things and you're free to accept them- if you don't know (by the legal standard of reasonable doubt) they contain anything illegal. That would include pretty much any package that is properly packaged and wrapped, while being improperly or inaccurately labeled.

And if LE started banging on your door shortly after you got back home, if they don't have a warrant then you MUST exercise your rights and refuse entry by saying that you don't consent to any searches of your premises. Even if they do have a warrant and find your package's contents, that would almost certainly get thrown out of court if they didn't properly establish your wrongdoing in the importation of the product.

Go with the flow, keep your mouth SHUT (everything can only be used AGAINST you, not for you), ALWAYS exercise your rights, and retain a lawyer if LE ever tramples upon them. You can sue for damages if they do. This involves any kind of activity, whether it's fully legal or legally questionable in any way.
