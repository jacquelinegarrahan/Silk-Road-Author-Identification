Title: Everything you need. Secure, Quick, Easy and Portable... with TAILS.
Post by: GrimWaldo on June 23, 2013, 02:49 am
You should choose TAILS for your Tor browsing, as it's the easiest and safest way to get all this stuff working. Here's how:

Hit DuckDuckGo and search ClearNet for TAILS on the Tor Project Website and download the ISO.
Burn it to a DVD-ROM and boot from it.
Click the "Login" button and insert a USB Drive (minimum 2GB).
Select "Applications/Tails/Tails USB Installer"
Select "Clone & Install". Once complete, remove the DVD-ROM and ReBoot from the USB Drive.
Click "Login" and select "Applications/Tails/Configure Persistence Volume"
Choose your password (make it a good one). When complete, select to save all of the settings and ReBoot.
*Note* I also add "/home/amnesia/Desktop" to the save list, otherwise no new icons on the Desktop.
This time, select "Use Persistence Volume" and enter your password, then select "Login".
Select "System/Preferences/Passwords and Encryption Keys".
Select "File/New" and choose "PGP Key", then click "Continue"
Enter a Name and e-mail address. Under "Advanced Key Options" set Encryption Type to "RSA" & Key Strength to "4096". Click "Create".
Your new key appears under "My Personal Keys". Right-click, choose "Export" and save the .ASC file in the "Persistence" folder. This is a text file containing your Public Key.
To import other's Public Keys so you can encrypt text message to them, select "File/Import" and browse to a text file containing their Public Key Block. It will appear under "Other Keys".
To encrypt a text message, select "Applications/Accessories/gedit Text Editor" and compose your message. Then select "Edit/Encrypt", choose your recipient and click "OK" ("sign message as: UserName", if you've posted your Public PGP Key).
To decrypt a message, Copy&Paste it to gedit then select "Edit/Decrypt" and enter your password.
If you need to enable WiFi, select it from the Network Icon on the right-side of the top Task Bar.

To practice your PGP skillz, send a plain-text e-mail containing your Public Key Block (the .ASC file you saved earlier) to adele-en@gnupp.de. She will respond with an encrypted message containing her Public Key. Decrypt it in gedit, save it, and import it into "System/Preferences/Passwords and Encryption Keys" and send her another e-mail encrypted with her Public Key. She'll exchange messages with you until you're comfortable that you're doing it right.

Now you're all set!

Be smart people, use TAILS for your Tor browsing. Everything stays in an encrypted Persistence Volume instead of on your HardDrive. Pop it out, put in in your pocket, and anonymous connectivity from any computer is always with you... instead of somewhere else.

Would you mind explaining to me what "persistent volume" is?
Wow, sorry for the belatted reply...

A Persistence Volume is an additional partition on the USB TAILS drive used to store system settings, additional software, other files, etc. It is fully encrypted and can be activated via PassWord just before TAILS loads the Desktop. It not only allows you to keep Bookmarks, Desktop Icons, PGP Keys, Electrum Wallet, and the like, but without your password, no one can access it.
