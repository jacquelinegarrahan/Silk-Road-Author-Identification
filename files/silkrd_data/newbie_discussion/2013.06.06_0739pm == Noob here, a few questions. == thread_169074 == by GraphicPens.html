Post by: soviet on June 06, 2013, 08:14 pm
CD means controlled delivery. A controlled delivery is when your package is discovered and LE (law enforcement) delivers it to your door under the disguise of a postal worker. Once you sign and accept the package they bust in your house the same day and search the place. For 3 grams of weed this would be EXTREMELY unlikely. If you order internationally and your package gets seized by customs you get what is called a "love letter", which is just a letter from customs telling you that they took your drugs.

In the unlikely scenario that your package is discovered, remember to DENY DENY DENY. Anybody can send anybody anything. I could send a box of dead babies to somebody I don't like. Even if you sign for it you could say that you were expecting a package from Ebay. If the mailman looks suspicious that day just sign for it, put the package on your table and write "return to sender" in black marker, so that if LE busts in your house later you can say that you didn't order it and were planning to return it.

But again 3 grams of weed is nothing. Take a look around the forums, there's lots of other useful info. Good luck on the road!

Also, revealing your country is perfectly okay.

Escrow is the system through which you make payments. For example: You order a platypus from vendor A. Your bitcoins then go into the escrow system where they are held until your package arrives, which ensures that the vendor won't just take your money and not send your package. When your platypus arrives you click "finalize" on your order, which releases the bitcoins and vendor A gets paid. The vendor may request that you FE (finalize early), which means that you give the vendor the money before you receive the package, but this is an easy way to get scammed. NEVER finalize early.

It's possible, which is why many vendors don't sell to new accounts or require FE. After 20ish days have passed since you placed your order the escrow system auto-finalizes your payment, so the vendor gets your money anyway. The obvious question then is "why don't vendors just let all of their orders auto-finalize?"...the answer is that after about 10 days since you placed your order you're given the option to go the resolution center. This is where the buyer and seller, with the help of SR staff, come to an agreement if the package hasn't arrived or is unsatisfactory.

Of course the system has flaws, which is why you should start out by buying small quantities at first to see which vendors you can trust. It would be wise to only buy from vendors who have many transactions and good feedback. Be sure to read each vendor's refund policy as well.

If you FE and the vendor scams you then nothing can be done. If this happens then you should post about it in the forums to lower the vendor's reputation. Reputation is everything around here.
