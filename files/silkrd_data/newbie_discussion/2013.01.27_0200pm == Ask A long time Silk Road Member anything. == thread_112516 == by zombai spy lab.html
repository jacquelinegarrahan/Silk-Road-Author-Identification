Hey Entity,
I found out about the 50 posts thing by messaging the SR help center on the main site, and they informed me of the situation.

As to your question; I personally have never had to order over an ounce of weed in one go off the road, I buy it in real life most of the time. However, the same principles for ordering any product apply; choose a reliable vendor who has good feedback both on their SR seller's page and on the forums. If you choose a vendor who is experienced then you will almost definitely receive your order, and don't be afraid to PM them and ask about their shipping success rates to your country if you're unsure. I'm pretty sure that many vendors send larger amounts of bud in multiple, smaller packages, so that if one is intercepted it's not devastating and most of an order will probably get through...unless they flag your address :p

Signing for a package is undesirable as it suggests that the signer was aware of said package being sent to them, so in court this could be taken as evidence suggesting the signer in fact ordered the illegal item. If one receives a random letter in the mail that just happened to contain a narcotic, then it isn't that person's fault and it is down to the pigs (*cough*, sorry, I mean fine law enforcement officers) to prove that they did in fact order said item. With all the security surrounding SR, that can prove to be incredibly difficult, if not impossible for them ;)

Most packages going through customs aren't checked too carefully and get through without any issues. Plus, most post-handling departments are much more concerned with letter bombs than relatively harmless drugs. That being said, a letter that stinks of bud and is coming from the Netherlands will no doubt arise suspicion, so a vendor shipping his product discreetly is also very important.

If there's anything else I can try and help you with just holla'

Hey Entity,
Over here, in Spain and other European countries I've ordered to, a package that is sent with tracking/signing-on-receipt is delivered to the receivers address and if they are not home a note is left by the delivery company (not the same one as the national postal service though) with a phone number or another delivery attempt date. The only way to have an idea as to what is inside the package is if it is accurately labeled on the customs form, providing it is coming from another country. If it has been sent domestically then there won't be a way to identify the contents, however I'm pretty sure most packages we send over SR aren't accurately labeled anyway ;)

If you pick up a package from the post office, and have to sign for it, then the principle is the same as if it was received by you directly at your residence. It may be an indication that you are aware of the contents / expecting the package, and so a claim that someone sent you illegal narcotics as a set-up may not be accepted. I still think that signing for packages containing small amounts is relatively safe, as if the package's contents had been discovered by customs they would have probably just sent you a letter informing you that they have seized the package. Only if you were ordering large amounts would they bother carrying out a controlled delivery; delivering the package, waiting for you to sign for it and then arresting you and carrying out a search (providing they have a warrant) of the residence. Of course they will arrest you for possession of a 'suspected' illegal narcotic and possibly with intent to supply or some other bull-shit.

All in all, if you're ordering small amounts, or from a vendor who is experienced with stealthily packaging larger amounts, then you will probably be safe. That being said, I only order with tracked shipping/signing when for some reason I need to know exactly where the package is along its journey, all the rest of the time I just order via regular mail. If you're planning on a bigger order, check the vendor's feedback for larger orders and see if you can find out whether people normally order with tracking/signing from them, and if they've been successful. I understand it's a debate; you want to have tracking because you don't want to loose a big order, but you don't want it because of the possible legal repercussions. At the end of the day it's your call.

Hope this helped,
