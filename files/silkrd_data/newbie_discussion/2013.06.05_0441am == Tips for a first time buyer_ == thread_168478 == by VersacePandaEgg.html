Title: Re: Tips for a first time buyer?
Post by: deepinblu on June 05, 2013, 01:39 pm
-Check the buyers' feedback on the forum. High rating doesn't always mean high-quality service.
-Don't rely on the feedback on the vendor's profile. Always check the forums for reliable reviews.
-Don't order big quantities or many orders simultaneously.
-Use your real name and address, fakes are a big red flag
-Order express only if you REALLY need to. Express packages are triple-checked and are looked at much more to ensure they get to the right place in the right condition. Regular or priority is the way to go.
-Don't sign for packages unless there is no other shipping option
-Do not use TOR to check tracking.
-Use PGP if possible to send your address details
-Don't send BTC directly from an account that states your name or other credentials
-If you encounter the LE, keep your cool, clean your house of any drugs, paraphernalia, clean your PC of any evidence and most importantly, YOU DID NOT ORDER ANYTHING, YOU HAVE NO IDEA WHAT IS IN THE PACKAGE OR WHERE IT COMES FROM

That's everything I can think about. As long as you follow these steps, you will be fine :) Good luck.

Title: Re: Tips for a first time buyer?
Post by: Bottletop on June 06, 2013, 01:10 am
My suggestions as a newbie (a lot has been mentioned already, and feel free to challenge me on the stuff that hasn't)

Before buying

- Read read and read the forums, get a feel for how it works obviously, things to watch out for, how the resolution process works etc. I found the wiki's buyers guide pretty light and resorted to searching for guides on the forum. Even read the sellers discussions to understand their side and DPR's philosophy posts to understand what it is you're signing up to
- Get PGP and use the test threads / facilities, there's website with an automatic service listed somewhere where you can practice encrypting and decrypting all day long
- Read all around the security stuff. I completely missed that accessing Mtgox from Tor will be flagged by them, and if the layer upon layer of protection (truecrypt partion, running off a USB etc) sound like paranoia to casual browsers at first, you'll wish you'd followed those set up rules properly the more you get into it
- Similarly keep usernames / passwords separate across everything. You'll end up signing up to a lot of different services (building your Darknet persona I guess). You'll have trouble remembering them all if you're keeping them unique and lengthy enough not to crack, so find a creative way to record them if needed. (not in one handy to read text file on your desktop!)
- Read the product-specific testing / opinion threads. I was ready to jump straight in and order from the best that was on sale at the time, until I found the names of recommended sellers who didn't have active listings at the time and so decided to wait
- Narrow your seller selection down to a few, and read their review threads. Look for the recent posts (I was about to hit purchase on an item until I noticed others commenting that the seller gone AWOL for over 2 weeks). Also try to make sure you're reading reviews about the particular product you want to buy from them. 5/5 seller feedback on the main site might be about something else.
- Give it some time from sign up before ordering, I might be wrong but sellers will look at buyer stats and might be unsure of someone ordering on the first day
- Feel free to send a message to the seller, but don't take issue with it if it's not read. At least you'll get a feel for the customer service level (maybe a hangover from days of buying face-to-face, but while buying goods here can be like shopping on faceless Amazon, given the nature of them I think it's preferable to build a relationship)
- Don't try to buy 5 small safe items to get past the newbie perception, it doesn't make a seller trust you more (a misconception I had at first)
- Slightly contentious, but I wouldn't even worry about ordering something relatively expensive the first time. You'll get scammed less often on smaller items, but positive first buys don't equal any more security when you do come to bigger purchases, it completely depends on the seller

Buying bitcoins

- A few of the main guides recommend the same process (I initially opened up a Mtgox account until I saw the latest threads on it). Also it might not be the best option for your country. In the end I found one that was quicker, gave a better exchange rate and looked less suspicious as an outgoing payment
- I'd recommend (though this could be purely speculation) to wash your bitcoin extremely thoroughly the first time. A first time outgoing sum to an new recipient may trigger more red flags with the bank than if it's been done before

Ordering

- I'd say order from a big and recognized seller the first time (98+ rating or so). They've got the process down and can easily answer questions (even the big sellers aren't too busy to respond).
- See if you can find one that is willing not to FE on first orders. There are big sellers that don't.
- Order domestic. The added challenges of international customs means that if it does get lost in transit, you haven't written off the idea of travelling the road entirely.
- Try a few different suppliers if you're curious, but then compare your feedback to others on the forum so you'll get a feel for who's opinion you can trust
- Always use PGP, request the seller's key if it's not publicly listed. Any decent seller doesn't really have a reason not to use it, even if it's only for your satisfaction

After ordering

- You will worry about it the minute you hit order. Did I pick the right seller? Will it be the best product? Did I pay too much? Did I do everything correctly? You start analyzing all the possible ways in which it could fail given the risks involved. It is weird the first time, but have faith and you will be rewarded
- Post a review on the forums, they're always useful even there's little detail, it just shows the seller is running their business ok. Be mindful you're not giving away stealth techniques / any other incriminating evidence
