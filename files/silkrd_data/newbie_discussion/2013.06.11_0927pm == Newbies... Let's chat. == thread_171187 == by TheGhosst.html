http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?topic=8962.0 <<--- guide to PGP!!!

A basic explanation, when you create a PGP message, there are 2 sets of 2 keys. A public and private key for the encryption end, and a public and private key for the decryption end.
Each user has a private key, and only that private key can decrypt a message that has been encrpyted to that private key's respective public key, and vice versa.

You ALWAYS want to keep your private key private(duh) and your PUBLIC key is the key you give to other PGP users, to encrypt messages to you.

For those of you who have PGP Desktop, it's as simple as:

Selecting the entire PGP Message Text from a vendors Public Key, Ctrl+C(Copy), go to PGP tray, Clipboard, Decrypt & Verify: Import their key/email info.

Write a message. When finished, select your message text, Ctrl+C(copy), go to PGP tray, clipboard, Encrypt: then select the email/key you imported.

Quote from: EZLuvzXTC2 on June 12, 2013, 02:41 am
Interesting. Is there a difference in quality/experience between the two types of products?

Yes. MDMA that comes from MDPV a lot of times has impurities in it that need to be washed away. As stated, a lot of "bath salt" derivatives come directly from MDPV, so if the synthesis isn't impeccable(and even if it is) it's very hard to yield the maximum purity of MDMA. For instance, if you ran a Mass-spectrometry on the grey-white MDMA that comes from MDPV, you will find that it's usually 82-83% MDMA, 16% Hydrochloride, and 1-2% "other" MDxx salt(s). It typically yields a Grey-white "moonrock"

Of course, versus Shulgin's method II, MDMA from PMK yields the maximum purity under the proper conditions, of 84% MDMA by mass. However, Shulgin's method I is what everyone knows and 'loves' as "brown sand" MDMA, and also contains a lot of impurities. Usually needs to be washed, producing white-purple "moon rocks", sometimes pure.

It is my understanding that the only way of yielding pure MDMA Hcl with no other impurities is through PMK. Of course, anyone is welcome to correct me if I'm wrong! This is just what I've been told by a chemical engineer who used to make the stuff.
