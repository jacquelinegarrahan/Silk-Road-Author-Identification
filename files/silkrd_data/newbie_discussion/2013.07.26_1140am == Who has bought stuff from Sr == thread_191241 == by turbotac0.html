One great tip I could offer is: 'never get anything sent to your house or your name'

Over the last couple of years Ive had almost 100% success rate this way from hundreds of packages, and have only had a few packages go missing, which I suspect was more to do with greed from the person receiving the mail than it was from it not actually showing up.

The one main argument against this is that the package will get through easier and arouse less suspicion if the shipping details are legit and under your name (which is only true - and only to a certain point - for a few aspects of standard shipping practices). Its SO EASY to circumnavigate this issue, just takes a little planning.

A few things to consider when doing it this way but def much safer, especially for me with long international flights and tight customs.

One of the core rules I have lived by for years now is 'once you're on their radar, you're on their radar'. This principle helps govern my every move and has assisted in keeping me safe for a very long time now.
