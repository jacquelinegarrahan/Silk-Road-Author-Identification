TIPS & TRICKS

Go into your account settings and have it display amounts in US dollars instead of BTC (bitcoins); the prices become much more obvious.

Use vendors that use the escrow ( 3rd party money holder) system. Escrow is there to protect you from getting scammed (by handing the BTCs temporarily to silkroad, and waiting until you get the package before finalization, after which the BTCs are now irreversibly handed to the seller, but in event of not receiving the package you can claim your money back or settle a partial refund with the seller), and early finalization means you're putting up yourself to be scammed (not always). THAT being said, if you're a new user with zero purchase history, chances are most vendors will ask you to finalize early as it is somewhat easy for buyers to abuse the escrow system and scam the sellers (i.e. receive the drugs, claim they didn't get it, and claim a refund), so you may want to only go with vendors with >97% feedback so you can at least be reasonably confident they won't scam you even if you finalize early and are just doing it for their own protection.

Don't bother buying a bunch of small/cheap things to get your transaction count up. Vendors can see how much you've spent and scammers do this as well. It will do nothing but waste your and there time and money.

When browsing drugs or other products, there is a checkbox at the top for domestic only, (imho)It's better to buy things from within your country for obvious reasons - they don't have to go through customs (so are less likely to be seized) and they arrive quicker) Don't rule out international sellers, though. Some of the best stuff can only be bought elsewhere, just understand that customs is involved.
you can also sort by relevance, vendor feedback, new vendors, as well

Security considerations

As safe as Tor and SR is, you need to realize that the DEA and other agencies have been well-aware of SR for quite some time now. There is no telling what resources they may put into cracking down on it and contrary to popular belief, it is not impossible to be busted on SR. Please take all necessary security precautions to stay safe.

 • Addresses you send get deleted but your information is only as safe as your vendor is! And if SR were to ever be discovered and raided, or hacked by a 3rd-party, any information could become publicly available and exist on the Internet forever. This is very unlikely to occur but it's at least worth mentioning. ALWAYS use PGP or Privnote!!!

 • Do not be worried about buying bitcoins. It is not illegal and plenty of legitimate online stores take bitcoin payments. If you are already under investigation, it could raise further suspicion but at that point, it wouldn't be the bitcoins you should be worried about!

 • Use your real name and address on orders and do not order to a vacant house and I would personally recommend against a PO box. You can never be 100% sure how well the vendor will package your drugs and if it's something like weed then having the cops there waiting for you to pick up your mail so they can bust you at the Post office would probably cause a few problems in your life and make you look like a fool.

In my opinion, the name that the drugs are ordered to is irrelevant in most situations. I think you're much more likely to get busted OUTSIDE of a mail delivery setting (like getting stopped with drugs on you, while high, etc), than to actually get a "controlled delivery." Use your real name, mainly to draw less attention to the envelope in the first place, and also because when the cops knock on your door, they don't give a fuck who's name is on the envelope if it's in your hand. If it was addressed to John Doe, but you opened it and did the drugs inside, all that proves is that you open other people's mail.

Don't put someone else's name on it (who actually exists) without their permission, or you are a fucking dirtbag. I've read several different people talking about how they used their parents names, or their grandmas name (because obviously she wouldn't order drugs and they'd never arrest her! Bullshit.) Don't do it, it will not help you, and it could GREATLY fuck up someone's life.

Note; The PO Box. Technique : The argument behind it is that you've essentially gone out of your way to have a delivery spot ( The PO Box) exclusively for drugs, if you're opening one just for SR purposes. Whereas if you send to your home address, you just get the packages/envelopes/whatever along with the rest of your mail. It's much less conspicuous, and there's really no way for any sort of LE ( Law Enforcement) to prove that it was you that ordered the drugs.
Also, it's simply unnecessary. Either way, you're getting drugs sent to an address that is under your name or in some way associated with you. So why go out of your way to secure a PO box when you could just have them sent to your

No matter what you use, the seller is going to have to have your address at some point. Even if you encrypt it with PGP, it needs to be decrypted. Your address is only as safe as the seller makes it. If they keep your address stored somewhere, encrypted or not, it is always possible for law enforcement to get it. Obviously, it's safer if it's encrypted but it might be possible for law enforcement to get the decryption key from the seller or make them give it up if they're busted.Anyway, chances of that are slim. Most sellers don't keep addresses on file. ( I mean their drug dealers right )

Encrypting Your Address
 
[This is important so that ONLY your vendor has access to your personal information.]

 • We need software that will handle the PGP encryption protocol. In your regular browser on the clearnet, go to 'http://www.gpg4win.org/download.html or gpg4win.org/index.html .The latest stable version is what you want and it usually has a green box around it. (Full version) Download and install it but when you get to the Choose Components page, only select GnuPG, Kleopatra, and Gpg4win Compendium. Install those components.

How to use PGP Video:
 youtube.com/watch?v=SywCI91kfq0

 • Next, open Kleopatra. If it doesn't ask you already, you need to create a new OpenPGP key pair. (File --> New Certificate)
 For your name, enter your Silk Road user name.
 For the email address, simply write '<user name>@silkroad.onion'. [The email address does not have to be valid.]
 Click Next, Create Key. Kleopatra will ask you for a passphrase for this key. Choose one that you've never used before and Write It Down. Kleopatra will then make your key pair.

 
Next step is to import your vendor's public key.

 •Go back to Tor Browser and back to Silk Road. Find your product (check your cart) and click the vendor's name. On the vendor's profile page he or she should list their GPG public key. It will start off titled:
 -----BEGIN PGP PUBLIC KEY BLOCK----- and the key will look like gibberish. Copy everything from:
 -----BEGIN PGP PUBLIC KEY BLOCK----- to
 -----END PGP PUBLIC KEY BLOCK-----, INCLUDING the begin and end lines. You want and need it all for it to work. Copy the key to your clipboard. (Ctrl+C or right click --> copy)


 •In your system tray, Kleopatra will have an icon. Right click the icon --> Clipboard --> Certificate Import. That will import the vendor's public key.

Now you are ready to encrypt your address.

 • Open up Notepad (Win+R --> notepad) Type your address as it would appear on a letter. This is what your vendor will see. Double check that your address IS correct. (Seriously, do it.) Copy your address to the clipboard.
 Right click Kleopatra's tray icon, Clipboard, Encrypt. Here you choose who you would like to send the info to. [Remember ONLY the recipient can decrypt your message]
 Click Add Recipient and select your vendor's key.
 Click Next and Kleopatra will encrypt your address.
 Click Ok.

 • Now go back to Notepad. Paste what is on your clipboard. (Ctrl+V, Right click --> Paste) Your address is now encrypted in a way that ONLY your vendor can decrypt. To anyone else, your address is gibberish

Completing The Transaction
 
Now that you have your encrypted address, go back to your cart. In the box asking for your address, paste your encrypted address. Enter your PIN (that you wrote down) and click 'place order'. That's it!

• Purchases
 
So you placed your order! Now the order will be 'Processing'. The vendor will get the order fill it and ship it. Once that happens, it will be marked as 'In Transit', which is self-explanatory. Once your order arrives, mark the transaction as 'finalized' and leave feedback to the vendor. Do not forget to do this because SR tracks how frequently you let transactions auto-finalize. (Vendors see this info when you order [citation needed] and may reject your order if your rate is high.

While purchasing IS illegal it is unlikely to be monitored or traced back to you if you have taken all the security considerations and done your vendor homework, look for stealth, the best shipping methods  you can find,  reviews, warnings and alternatives. seriously the message boards will be your biggest time + money saver and will put you on the right path.

• Messaging / Finalizing
 
Yes, things take ages on the silk road- but if your experience is like mine, drugs are unfortunately just as slow in real life. Vendors can and will take 24 hours to process orders or message you, don't start worrying until it's been more than a few days.

Don't bother vendors with small questions or ask for constant updates. they're busy people. message them when they ask you to message back (for finalizing or confirming you're alive

Finalizing early means you immediately release bitcoins to the vendor before receiving the product (a sign of trust on your end or distrust on theirs). Finalizing early is the most likely way to make sure your product doesn't get to your doorstep. please, please look at review threads, forum posts and ratings before choosing a vendor who will make you FE. some vendors are worth FE'ing for, but they are few and far between.

• Shipping
 
Use your real name and real address. as torr points out, go to your post office/online and ask how to officially write your address (nothing suspicious about this) to accelerate sorting. This will maximize machine sorting, good routing, and minimize the number of people that have to intervene. I notice that what vendors say about how to write an address isn't the case in all countries.

Always use PGP. if they don't have PGP you shouldn't be ordering from them

Order domestic to expedite shipping- many offer next day delivery because there are no customs.

Give it 3-4 days domestically before asking questions and AT LEAST a week/two weeks if internationally. stuff really does get lost or takes time to process.

For international, 2-3 weeks is a minimum to wait. Sometimes it will be surprisingly fast, especially for letters, but sometimes things can take a while.

I'd recommend for a first purchase ordering something that is actually legal. Find something cheap that you're interested in  or might one day need like Books! This will help tp Find out if your package will require a signature or not. I wouldn't order from a vendor that sent packages requiring a signature. If your package arrives and a signature is requested,  RED FLAG ! REJECT AND DENY, unless its not around the time you have ordered off the road. But during the wait for any SR mail, its not a bad idea, id rather loose some cookies from mom, than my freedom.
Keep a clean house. Nothing gets a home cleaner than an upcoming SR delivery. Keep a clean computer as well.

Don't talk about SR or mail-order to others. What they don't know can't hurt them.

Keep your postal workers happy and not suspicious. You don't want them feeling like they smoked up whenever you open your door. Getting to your mailbox shouldn't require going through an obstacle course.
