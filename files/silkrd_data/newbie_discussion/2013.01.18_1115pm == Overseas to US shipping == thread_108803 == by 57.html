There isn't much more but anecdotal evidence to support this but lets say your local postal carrier is your last chance to have your package seized assuming it doesn't get stolen also. For every layer you put on top of that you have an extra percent chance of that package getting found because there are more hands on it.

In the end its sorted just like you imagine. All gathered and shipped to where ever on a plane most likely. All the packages removed then carted to their respective state by, truck?

Unloaded into the sorting facility and carted to their local office. Brought to your door.

For a "legitimate" package it won't matter how many people touch it unless it gets broken, you can use your insurance at that point.

For other things? They have a printing press full of paper to send you a seething and vile passive aggressive letter about some contraband with your address on it that your crazy ex-girlfriend probably had sent to you because she hates you ;).
