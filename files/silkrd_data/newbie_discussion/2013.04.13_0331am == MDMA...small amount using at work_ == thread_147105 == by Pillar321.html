Title: Re: MDMA...small amount using at work?
Post by: Canadork on April 14, 2013, 03:05 am
I've done MDMA well over 50 times, and I'd never even THINK of doing it at work. And I'm the sort who can rail back an entire gram of pure in a night. (NEVER do that by the way. In some people, that amount IS lethal.)

Don't do it at work. Just don't. There isn't a good reason to. And if you've ever experienced mdma before, you wouldn't even be asking the question as to whether or not you should be doing it at work.

Find a quiet spot you can be alone in for at least 6 hours. Maybe on one of your days off, you can go sit in a park somewhere and be out in nature. Or heck, you can even just wander around a mall for awhile. I'd say that you should find a good friend to do it with, but unless they are on it too, they might not appreciate your hyperactive company. *winks*

My best experiences with mdma have been when I have an entire day to myself, and I do it with my absolute best friend in the entire world. The drug can be a very useful tool. And that is how you should view all drugs: as a tool. Treat them with respect. If you've never operated a band-saw before, you need to first do some research into how to operate one safely without losing a finger on an eye. If you're prepared for using the tool, and you have an idea of what you want to build, you will have a safe and fantastic experience. But if you use a tool without knowing how it works, or use it haphazardly, you increase your risk of injury.

Go to erowid.org, look up mdma, and read. Read read read. Read what the chemical is, and what it does. Read experiences from people who have done it before, both good, and bad. Don't let the bad frighten you, but neither let the good make you over confident. Take the information available to you, know what to expect, and be excited when you finally take it in a safe, controlled environment, knowing that you're about to have a wonderful experience.

If you have never driven a car, should you get in one and pull out onto the freeway? No. However, driving around slowly in a parking lot for awhile is a safe way to get used to the experience. Always treat drugs with that very same respect. Always.
