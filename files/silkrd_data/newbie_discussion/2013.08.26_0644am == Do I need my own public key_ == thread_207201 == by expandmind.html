Title: Re: Do I need my own public key?
Post by: burntblackwalls on August 26, 2013, 10:34 am
Keep it handy.

No SilkRoad does not have a feature similar to Atlantis that lets you enter a public PGP key into a profile setting to encrypt your commuinications to and from the vendor.

All through this feature to some seems like a good idea it was pointed out by the PGP Club founder Pine that it has multiple flaws.

You never know though that some time in the future SilkRoad may have a feature like this that is a lot more secure than the one Atlantis has setup.

What you should do is keep your public PGP key always handy to give to vendors that you want to keep important information out of prying eyes.

But PGP should not just be used only with SilkRoad it is a very good tool to always have handy to talk in a private manner to other people around the world.
