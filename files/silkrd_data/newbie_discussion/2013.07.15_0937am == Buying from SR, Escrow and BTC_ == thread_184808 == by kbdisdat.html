Quote from: kbdisdat on July 15, 2013, 12:34 pm
Okay so I buy BTC, say from MtGox, transfer them to my SR account (with the wallet ID thing) then SR handles the rest through escrow?

Yes, when you make a purchase, for say $100, or the BTC equivalent, the Road holds those funds in escrow. It comes out of your wallet but it isn't released to the seller, it's just held in escrow by SR. When your product arrives and you check it out, make sure you have all your supposed to have, then YOU finalize the transaction which releases the funds to the seller.
Title: Re: Buying from SR, Escrow and BTC?
Post by: TrippingSquares on July 16, 2013, 04:41 am
From what I understand (have done weeks of research before making an account/ordering), you could compare the financial system to Amazon. When you buy a product, Silkroad will hold on to the Cryptocurrency and once you've received, left feedback and finalize the order, the money will be released to the vendor (after a small fee SR takes).
To get money to the SilkRoad wallet in the first place, you first have to buy the BTC, and send them from your wallet, to your SR account :)
