Hey Qthello, I have seen it take several hours for the bitcoins to get to SR.  The issue is that the bitcoin "network" needs to confirm the transaction and it may take up to 6 confirms for the transaction to be considered legit and non reversible.  This is all technical junk that the bitcoin system is made up of that requires this.  I also did see some other threads on here about delays, perhaps there is an issue going on.  Don't freak out though.  Since bitcoins are run by computers, everything is trackable and if SR has issues, they can fix them.

Also, I believe you may have misunderstood the guide earlier.  I am not sure why you go from Mt Gox to offline wallet to blockchain wallet to SR wallet with your funds.  All you are doing is making the trail longer of where the funds are going.  If you aren't using the tumbler on blockchain dot info, you are still linking your Mt Gox account with the SR wallet at the other end.

Anyhow, if you still don't see your funds on the SR wallet (account), go back to blockchain dot info main page and there is a Search Box.  Paste in your blockchain dot info wallet address there and click search.  You should see your transaction, the amount of bitcoins you sent, as well as how many confirms it has.  It will say Confirmed or Unconfirmed (or something like that).  If it shows confirmed and you still don't have the $ in your SR account, put in a ticket with SR Support.

Nobody is spending any money to come after you for a gram of whatever.  But, now that you have your first purchase down and the pressure is off, maybe go back and read up on what I was saying.  When you are sober!  Bitcoins transactions are the most traceable transactions possible.  Computers are used to transfer them from address to address.  Here are some things you need to do to keep yourself sane.

1.  Go into the "account" page on SR and your wallet address will be there.  There is a button to get a new address to send to.  Do this before sending yourself any coin again.
2.  Use a bitcoin anonymizing service such as the https://blockchain.info/wallet/send-anonymously page.  It's easy.  Go onto this page, paste your new SR wallet address into the  "Enter A Bitcoin Address" box.  Click the "Create Forwarding Address" box.  The web page should print out a new address below that you copy.
3.  Send your bitcoin to this new address that you copied from the webpage, from your offline wallet.  Skip the blockchain wallet altogether.
