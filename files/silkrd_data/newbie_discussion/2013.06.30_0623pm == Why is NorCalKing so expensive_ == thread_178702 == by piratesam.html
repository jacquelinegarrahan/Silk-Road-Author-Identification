Title: Re: Why is NorCalKing so expensive?
Post by: tbart on July 10, 2013, 03:50 am
I don't know the vendor you're asking about .....but I've been in national marketing sales for 32 years

i'm a merchant in another area - i sell high end products, most are unique to my company with no competitors carrying those products,  but some of my inventory (probably 40-50%) are items common to other companies that sell the same product for 10 to 25% less. My customers still buy from me knowing they are paying more than they would at another company. Reasons: 1) i'm a known quantity and if there's an issue, they know i'll be there tomorrow or next week to stand behind the product 2) i'm a known quantity - same reason as last but known as in that they can trust me, if i describe something as x,y,z , what will show up is exactly that

someone in my market popped up in one of the collector forums, not too long ago, offering a product that i charged $700, offering it for $399 - i knew what i was paying for it and couldn't understand how he could sell for that price, he had to be taking a loss. 2nd reason i couldn't understand his move, was that it was stupid - you want to steal some of my sales, come in at $649, dropping as low as he did was insanely stupid - 2 months later, after 3-4 weeks of internet cheerleaders / groupies (more on this topic down below) describing how great this new vendor was, a few guys popped up asking if anyone had heard from him, as they'd sent $2500 and never received what they'd paid for and couldn't get an email response or return phone call. All of a sudden about 20-25 more guys chime in, reporting the same experience - some had fallen "in love" with this guy, singing his praises before they'd even received the product and hadn't said anything, figuring their product was somewhere enroute, etc. When they saw others with the same complaint, they popped up with their stories. In short he was a scammer. Even if he hadn't been a scammer, he was selling at a loss - how long could he stay in business to support his products he'd already sold?  Guy ended up being caught, as he'd scammed too many people out of too much money (the authorities won't chase a scammer unless there's a threshold amount of theft involved.

point is - when you're buying something, you might go for the  lowest price vendor thinking you are saving whatever amount, but if you don't receive it or if the product was not as described, then you haven't saved a dime and you'll never forget the experience or the frustration of having been screwed. Buying from someone you know you can trust, you might be paying more than you think you should be, but if the product shows up and performs as described and as expected, you'll forget what you paid

so ask yourself, what's the value of running a risk with an unknown seller and the value of the frustration (and hole in your wallet) if the product doesn't show up or doesn't deliver the satisfaction you were expecting. ADD TO THAT THE VALUE OF NEVER FORGETTING WHAT YOU "SAVED".

FWIW

I've been on the internet in sales, and in forums since the mid 1990s - and have seen a phenomena that i view as some people just needing someone to cheer for or be a groupie for - never understood it, but they'll have one good deal with a company or a vendor and praise that vendor like he's baby jesus come back from heaven, with no real basis for their support. I'd love to know the psychology behind their actions
