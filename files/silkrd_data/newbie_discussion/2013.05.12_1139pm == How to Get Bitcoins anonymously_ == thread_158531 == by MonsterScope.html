Title: Re: How to Get Bitcoins anonymously?
Post by: JohnMaddox on May 13, 2013, 02:30 am
@quixotist if you sign up for an account with blockchain.info you can make deposits using cash.  Here is a quick guide on how to do it without revealing any personal info.

1. sign up for the account at blockchain.info by clicking wallet in the upper right hand corner then click "Start A New Wallet" on the next page (located to the upper left)
2. once you have created a new wallet using a fake email that you can access go to Wallet again then click "Login Now" then "Open Wallet" on the next page.
3. after opening your wallet click cash deposit on the right of the screen.  It will redirect you to zipzap for a cash deposit.  Follow the steps through using fake information but keep track of all the info you use and make sure you use the same email you signed up with for blockchain if it asks as you will need to access your email.  Proceed to the bottom of the page and after you submit it will ask you to launch something and the tor browser will warn you that it is possibly unsafe.  To get around this CANCEL it.  Then wait 5 minutes and you'll receive an email with all your information you need.

4. at this point head on down to your local walmart and pickup a small stack of Blue MoneyGram Express Payment Service forms to fill out at home.  Fill in your fake information along with the details for zipzap (obtained from your email) like the receive code account number, company name and city/state.  Take the form back to walmart with cash or have a friend do it for you to remain even further anonymous.  Walmart won't ask for your ID, if they do I read you are supposed to just tell them that the last person didn't ask you and that you forgot it.  I can attest that when I did my first deposit they did not ask me for my ID. 

At  this point your deposit should show up within a few minutes, now you can access your bitcoins in your blockchain.info account.  Send them around to some free wallets if you want to tumble your money up a bit.  I won't cover that here as it is different for each provider you want to tumble with, otherwise you can just send them straight to SR and you're done. 

Please note that deposits to your SR account generally take an hour or so to verify funds and show up.  I hope this helped a bit, I learned a lot of this from browsing other posts.  About the only original content here is the CANCEL suggestion for running the external app.  I figured that out by accident because I didn't want to compromise my identity with external software and then saw the email come through minutes later.
