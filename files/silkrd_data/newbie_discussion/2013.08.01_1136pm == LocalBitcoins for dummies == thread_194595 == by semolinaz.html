Title: Re: LocalBitcoins for dummies
Post by: Zapdosss on August 01, 2013, 11:45 pm
You are correct. Just make sure right before you make the deposit, that the transaction is still up in escrow and has not been canceled. I've never had a problem with it but I could see some complications depositing the money in the account right after(for whatever reason) the seller cancels the transaction. You should receive your coins after you deposit and you provide the seller with the transaction ID on the receipt you're given during the deposit at the bank.

The escrow system works wonders on localbitcoins.

Another note is increasing the anonymity using localbitcoins. Cash deposit is on the best ways to remain anonymous, but if you want to take it a step further, make sure to use a non personal email or account named used in any other personal matter in your life online. This will create more space between you identity and the bitcoins.
NOTE: Buying bitcoins is NOT illegal, but why not be a little more cautious with some effort?  :)

Wish you the best of luck.

Title: Re: LocalBitcoins for dummies
Post by: Treasurer on August 02, 2013, 11:49 pm
You absolutely can use Tor for personally identifiable action, such as Facebook.

But! You shouldn't do it during the same session as something else. If you check your Facebook, and at the same time check the SR, you risk having your IP connected to both.

Bottom line: Using Tor for personal stuff is fine, but don't mix personal use and anonymous use at the same time (make sure you select "New Identity" each time).
