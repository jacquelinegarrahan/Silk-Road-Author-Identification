Title: Re: 0.01g scale from amazon. anyone with experience?
Post by: mcguire39 on July 10, 2013, 05:41 pm
It's the high precision lab type scales that can benefit from a cover. A relatively small air flow such directly down from an air conditioning duct could affect a high precision scale as the force distributes over the surface area of the weighing area. I would say one test would be take that scale, and after taring, blow maybe 6 inches away from the surface and see if it registers anything. Not sure if a .01g scale would see anything, maybe it would, but a .0001g almost certainly would.
