Wrong.

The Tor Project never created a discussion forum. They have mailing lists which most newbies will never use. They have a blog which contains unorganized posts on different subjects all on one page.

TAILS had a forum - which was nothing more than a poorly contained wiki platform - which the TAILS devs shuttered. The TAILS devs posting there were rather grumpy when you mentioned Tor, which is the heart of TAILS, insisting users take their Tor questions to the Tor Mailing List. With the TAILS forum closed, they now offer you the ability to (wait for it) sign up for a mailing list! Just don't mention Tor if you decide to join. (snort)

There are thousands of message boards dealing with complex (or more) discussions where newbies are welcomed. It's time The Tor Project did the same. There are also large forums with a varying degree of topic complexity which receive thousands of posts per day. Those moderators seem to do fair well in light of the traffic. I'm certain a tiny amount of the money sent to The Tor Project can go towards official discussion forums and there's never a shortage in volunteers.

The only insanity I see is pushing users to a stupid Q&A site instead of designing a friendly discussion forum.
