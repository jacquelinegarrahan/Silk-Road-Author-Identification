Post by: johndoee on April 08, 2013, 06:30 am
If you are sure you have a secure host OS,

the best way is Whonix (using virtual machines)

You can find lots of information and faq on its homepage

I would add, learn how to use VirtualBox's snapshot feature and use it effectively

Also,

Connecting to a VPN before connecting to Tor (You -> VPN -> Tor) is recommended.

This is valid as long as you trust your VPN provider more than your ISP, choose a non-logging VPN service.

This way, your ISP, your government cannot see what you are doing except your encrypted connection to VPN.

Also, the entry nodes that your PC connects will not see your original IP, instead the VPN's IP. This is nice

For extra use cases, e.g. when you want to connect to a website which blocks access for Tor IPs, you can add an extra VPN to your chain:

you -> VPN 1 -> Tor -> VPN 2

This way, your ISP will only see your VPN connection, Tor entry nodes see VPN 1's IP, Tor exit nodes see VPN 2, VPN 2's admins will see Tor IPs, and the website you are connecting will see just a VPN IP.

If you pay anonymously (bitcoin) or find a free service especially for the second VPN then it is very secure.

Additionally, use Whonix with Portable VirtualBox inside an encrypted disk, for extra safety.
