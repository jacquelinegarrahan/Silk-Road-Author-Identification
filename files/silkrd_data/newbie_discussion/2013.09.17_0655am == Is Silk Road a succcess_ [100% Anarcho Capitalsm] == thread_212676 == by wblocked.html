Since Silk Road is one of the very first experiments in radical and unadulterated Anarcho Capitalism (maybe even a harder form due to the anonymity), the qustion arises:

Does it work?

I think yes, but the whole site covers such a broad range of products -- none of which I'm terribly familiar with to be honest -- and regions that it is too hard for me to tell if it _really_ works.

So what about the big fears the usual statist brings up when he heras about the free market (especially in food and drugs):

1) Are the "big companies" exploiting the little man? Do they have more power than new competition entering the market?
2) Do vendors lie and scam? Do they deliver shitty products at high prices, touting 100% purity?
3) Do vendors even cut their product with harmful substances (think of rusty nails in cereals analogy) becuase there is no FDA controls?
4) Is there a tendency towards all-controlling monopolies?

I guess many of these points can already be laughingly be put to rest, but I'm wondering about the general honesty of the vendors, as well as the quality of the product, as well as how quickly liars and supplyers of faulty product are called out and shunned.

Responses and discussion very much welcome.
