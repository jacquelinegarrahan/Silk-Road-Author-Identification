Title: Re: Ketamine Tolerance
Post by: frank-butcher24 on September 06, 2013, 09:04 pm
You can reach a stage with ketamine use where you're able to take pretty large amounts and still function - to a degree. You're wasted, but you can get out of bed, shower, go to the shops, cook, communicate with others etc. You shouldn't be driving a car, and don't expect to hold down a job, but you can be on a gram a day or more and still live a reasonable enough life.

It's a very odd drug in that respect. I spent 6 months on it in my 20's, then one day whilst on it, I just decided to stop. It was a full on trip in which I re-evaluated myself, my position in life, my goals and so on.

I have taken it maybe 10-15 times since then, and haven't ever felt the urge to get back on it.
