Title: Re: moving my bitcoins to SR
Post by: rosewoodangela on October 02, 2013, 02:51 am
Do blockchain.

But here's what you need to do:
Make a new blockchain, inside your tor browser, and make sure the blockchain is linked to an UNTRACEABLE email. I always use a hma email (hide my ass email).
This destroys your evidence, and makes it untraceable to you.
Then send the bitcoins from your blockchain to your SR account.

This way, if anything bad happens to SR, your bitcoins won't get you in trouble

I'm pretty sure that's a good way to raise some red flags for your blockchain account. Everything in Bitcoin world is completely traceable. So if you buy coins from someone, he'll be able to trace where they've gone.

I wouldn't worry about it too much though. Transferring Bitcoins to your Silkroad account moves them through the Silkroad tumbler which mimics the Bitcoin economy but jumbles them around quite a bit. If you're only a buyer. I don't see why you'd need much more protection than that.

You can also use a new address each time you transfer them into your account.
