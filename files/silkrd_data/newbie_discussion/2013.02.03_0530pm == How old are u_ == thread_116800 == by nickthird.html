I didn't say any of them are rejuvenating, just that they don't naturally wear out / get weaker with age.
These animals do die, but it seems their mechanisms of 'natural' death are different.

The causes of death as to my current understanding are primarily:
1. Limit on the division of cells
2. Damage to the cell caused by cell activity.
3. Damage to the cell caused by activity of other cells.
4. Damage to the cell caused by the exchange with the environment.

In most species 'natural' death is caused by 1-2 before 3-4 come into effect,
however this does not apply to these animals:

Wiki (alligator): "There is no measured average lifespan for an alligator"
Some other site: "...there are no scientific methods of analyzing an alligators age while it is alive"

Wiki (Tortoise): "The record for the longest-lived vertebrate... a 226-year life span"
Some other site: "...accurately determining the age of a wild or captive tortoise is not possible"

Look it up, its strange I know.
