Title: Re: USPS Priority
Post by: jshizzle23 on February 18, 2013, 05:08 am
What a bunch of bullshit responses...  USPS works like this, its gotta get from one major mail center to another (by plane) and then to your address...  So, it starts with how far away your vendor is from a major metropolitan area...  I've had "Express" shipments from the same state take 2 days because it takes a day for it to get to the nearest metropolitan area and then another day to me since I'm in a major metro area... So, I always ask my vendor how close they are to a major metropolitan area - if they're in one and you're in one, Priority takes 2 days... If one of you are in one and the other isn't, look at 3 days - maybe 4 depending on how far the person who isn't in a metro area is from the metro area... If you're both in the sticks, look at 5 days, maybe more...

Best of luck!

Actually, some delivery companies use the same system that the Post Office uses - called Next Flight Out -- and mail gets priority placement on these flights.  They have contracts with all of the airlines and know when flights are direct to cities.  The only thing that can bump mail is human remains.  (Private shipping companies are called Quik, Sterling, Midnite Express, etc...)  So, I think they don't actually make 'weird' connections that delay the packages - almost all of them are non-stop, maybe a few have connections, but they're few and far between (and they aren't way out of the way)...
