I am also a very fond of DMT. As far as your question, my first handful or so of dmt experiences were with a basic spoon pipe. I measured out my desired dosage and nestled in in between two layers of cannabis ash. Regardless of what instrument you use to administer DMT, you always want it to vaporize. That being said, the ash layers help shield the DMT crystals from burning in the event that a flame touches the contents of the bowl. My best experiences with the spoon pipe resulted from when I had someone there to help vaporize the DMT after the first two inhalations...very important to instruct them to let the heat of the flame be pulled through by your inhalation instead of torching the whole bowl.

I now use "The Machine." Its a homemade vaporizing device made from a small glass bottle and heavy-duty metal scouring pads. One of the best features about this vaporizer, other than being very efficient with DMT, is that it has enabled me to feel more comfortable smoking DMT by myself, not having to worry about setting an ash-filled pipe down. For me, the solo psychedelic journeys that I have had have been tenfold better than having someone else with me, but that's another story.  I'm currently looking into making another vaporizer such as "The Key." Both can be found via a Google search or on DMT Nexus.

From age 17 until around 4 years ago, I indulged myself (on and off) in frequent bouts of serious substance abuse.

- Cannabis - Smoked everyday since the age of 15 (and still do).
- Clubbing Scene Ecstasy / MDMA - x 3 Re-visited for up to a year or two at a time.
- Amphetamine abuse including "Speedbombs", Base, Paste, etc
- Cocaine abuse including Crack
- LSD / Magic Mushrooms - Not too often over the years, but always good experiences.
- Valium & various other pills.
- Heroin - Never did this thank goodness!
- Legal Highs - Mephedrone and many others (THIS SHIT NEARLY KILLED ME)

Basically to add some background to the above drug taking, I was always able to abuse but kept things on track with the job and family responsibilities. There were two exceptions during the last 22yrs:

1. Clubbing Scene (late 20’s) - on the 3rd time around, one time I hadn't slept for 2 - 3 day and went to work and more or less passed out. I was signed off work for two weeks to recover. To be fair I felt fine after a couple of days rest / sleep but still a shock to the system.

2. Legal Highs (37-39) - This was something that completely crushed me (Methadrone). I had previously developed an addiction on speed/coke and this carried over to the legal highs. The buzz on this stuff was incredibly dirty and I went wild on the stuff.

I found myself not trusting my own family / friends and had suicidal thoughts, as I felt manically depressed and could see no way to get myself back to who I once was.

I thought I had given myself brain damage. My parents were going to get me sectioned and it was my brother and friends who stopped that from happening.

After being signed off my work by my doctor, I nervously returned but continued battling the demons.

Legal Highs had literately turned me into a zombie and I hated myself.

Things HAD to change.

Things did improve slightly over the course of that last year, I got a semblance of “normality and routine” but my confidence still lacked and I felt I was definitely a shadow of my former self. I felt I had lost brain power, my “mojo” and lust for life.

It was at this point around April 2009, that I made my wish. I took the time to ask with all of my heart for something to happen to me, that might give me back what I knew I had lost to Legal Highs and the massive breakdown I endured (was still enduring).

It was from that day that I started searching the internet for self-help / self-therapy / meditation / confidence tapes, etc

I bounced around from one idea to another, trying this and that, with absolutely no success……although I would highly recommend Dale Carnegies “How to win friends and influence people” - a great read for anyone.

I had noticed once or twice during my reading online, mentions of DMT and eventually one VERY fateful night - I got around to finding out what it was all about.

After googling DMT, the first thing I ended up watching was Terrence McKenna’s You Tube - “Rap Dancing into the Third Millenium”.

I remember vividly it was a weeknight and I started to listen to his speech around 11pm. To say I was gobsmacked by what this man was saying is a massive understatement; I could barely sleep and was thinking about it all the next day. When I got home and the kids got off to bed, I immediately started to read more and more about this unbelievable substance. At this point I had no thoughts on trying the stuff (I was COMPLETELY OFF DRUGS, apart from cannabis anyway); But I was just totally getting off on reading and watching as much as I could.

I just couldn’t believe it was in us naturally; the birth, sleep and death releases…..Hyperspace, the Hive, dimensional travel, Entities!

I mean what the F!!!

UNBELIEVABLE – I read and read and read and read from April 2009 until August 2009. Thanks DMT Nexus & Erowid!

I convinced myself that DMT was NOT a drug in the regular sense and that it might be useful for me to try it.

DMT had found me and for a reason….

So, that’s when I extracted my own Spice from MHRB and took my first launch in to the DMT Dimension :-)

THIS was what I had wished for – THIS was the therapy I had needed.

My setting was all about my own personal mindset of “Wishing to get back to my regular self” - Setting is an internal thing, not external (although being somewhere you are comfortable and with someone you trust does help).
