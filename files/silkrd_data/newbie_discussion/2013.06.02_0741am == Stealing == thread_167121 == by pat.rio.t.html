Interesting first post, But Google can get you halfway to being a successful thief as well as supplying the tools without the SR markup( This whole post can only be accurately applied within the US). Pickpocketing is an art and skill, which only under a hands on apprenticeship could you honestly learn quickly and well. I own lock picks, a lock pick gun for the easy ones, and general knowledge of locks and alarm systems. Common sense and lots of low risk or at home practice can get you pretty darn far on that second half, but only experience will prepare you for true heists or large attempts. Might sound a little dumb but try setting up a mock situation at your household or a friends. Have an item, maybe in a safe or not easily reached area. Create multiple ways to retrieve it. Even better if a second person creates the course so you dont have intimate knowledge of it, just like IRL. Set up trip wires or similar simple alarms and add a real human guard if wanted. This will allow you to develop what you like and what really works or helps. Once you've done that, maybe pull a few pranks that require silent entry through locked areas or areas under surveillance. I have done all this and am currently preparing a job on a local convenience store thanks to some inside information as to where the alcohol pallets are currently stored ;) Be careful, Plan, re plan, then plan again, And never ever take a short cut. There is no such thing as too paranoid, and always have an escape plan and alibi.

Stay safe, If you have more specific questions i would be glad to give you what i got, Pm or reply post.
