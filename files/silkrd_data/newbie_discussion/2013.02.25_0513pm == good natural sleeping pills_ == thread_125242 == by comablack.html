Post by: MugwumpSucker on February 26, 2013, 12:08 pm
Ambien can put you to sleep, yes, but it often tends to not *keep* you asleep. It's also not natural, and at higher doses (10mg and above) it can do funky things to your short term memory, induce odd behaviors, etc. Another unnatural sleep aid is dyphenhydramine (yes, Benadryl, available anywhere, cheapest by far at CostCo). This is the exact same chemical that's used in over the counter sleep aids like Sominex and in night-time cold medicines. Typical dose is 25mg (one pill). 50mg in a pinch, but it can give you a bit of the groggies in the morning. More than that generally isn't a good idea. In fact, it can actually keep you awake with something that feels like restless leg syndrome.

But! You were asking for natural sleep aids. I can recommend valerian root (no, it is not related to valium). Nature's Way makes a decent standardized valerian in capsule form. Start with one capsule to see how it affects you, because (like diphenhydramine) and can make you groggy the next day. I found that two was all right, more than that was counterproductive. It's also available as a tea. Be warned: it's stinky, weird-smelling stuff.

If you get the herb or the tea, stick to single ingredient (that is, no "valerian plus other stuff").

Other than the aforementioned and valid exercise/diet/sleep schedule lifestyle changes, the other important thing to do is to avoid caffeine within about six hours of hitting the sack. Caffeine is metabolized in several stages, if you have coffee at say 5:00PM you'll still be on the tail end of that if you're getting to bed at 11:00PM That includes soda as well.

Good luck!

Unless you're malnourished or old, melatonin supplements will do you no good... Instead, try to establish a regular sleeping pattern. Go to bed the same time every day, get up the same time every day. If you can't sleep, get up, do something for 30-45 minutes, and go to bed again. Title: Re: good natural sleeping pills?
Post by: MugwumpSucker on February 26, 2013, 11:58 pm
Good god! How could I forget 5-HTP? I actually take 100mg every night for general brain maintenance, but when I first started taking it it was as effective as (and gentler than) Ambien. I think of valerian as an "acute" (i.e., I need to go to sleep right frickin' now) solution, and 5-HTP is a long-term solution.

Agreed about the opiates: bad idea for sleep. It seems counterintuitive, but the nods are not the same for your brain as gen-yu-wine sleep, they don't refresh...and for me, a good dose is something I'll actually stay awake all night with, drifting and feeling good (like last night). Similarly, over the long haul benzodiazepines like Klonopin and Xanax actually work to prevent your brain from entering the full-on delta wave state that characterizes the deepest and most restorative portion of the sleep cycle. So although benzos will knock you out, the sleep is actually lighter and of worse quality.

All of that said: your mileage may vary, everybody's body's different, so on and so forth. In truth any chemical/herbal solution should be temporary; lifestyle changes in support of proper sleep habits are the best (and cheapest) solutions.
