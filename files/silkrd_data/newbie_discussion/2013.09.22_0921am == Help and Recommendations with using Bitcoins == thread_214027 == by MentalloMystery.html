Title: Re: Help and Recommendations with using Bitcoins
Post by: doodoo79 on September 22, 2013, 10:23 pm
redisyellow is right. Tumbling BTC is a good idea. I use CampBX (unverified account registered with a burner email) and Localbitcoins.com (also unverified account with a burner email) to purchase BTC. I send money orders to CampBX every payday and use Localbitcoins.com when I'm in a bind. Everyone's got their own method of tumbling, but what I do is just send BTC back and forth from those two wallets, getting a new wallet addy with each transaction. I also get a new SR BTC addy every time I send coin to my SR acct. I'm sure (I know) there are better, more secure, less time consuming methods for tumbling. But if nothing else this practice makes me "feel" more secure. And don't forget that on top of whatever you do, SR tumbles the coins when you make a purchase. In none of these BTC buys is my real name or bank acct number ever used.

By the way, CampBX has about the best exchange rate you can find, in my experience anyway.
