Hey falerin.  I don't know if I have all the answers for you but I'll try to give you my 2 cents based on experiences. 

First off it's a real bummer that you don't have tracking on DCN so you can track your package.  I don't vend so I'm not entirely sure how that works but I believe you have to walk into the post office to get the tracking and a lot of vendors don't want to do that and instead just drop it off in a blue box on a street corner. 

From what I've read on these forums the Express mail is supposedly handled by different people than who normally handles the regular first class and priority mail and I've even read comments where people say the Express mail employees are much worse at their jobs than the normal employees (go figure right). 

The good news is all is not lost with your package.  It could be and probably still is out there somewhere trying to be delivered to you.  If you've never had a package take forever before the first time it happens you're just thinking the worst and that all is lost.  I had a priority package take 8 days about a month ago and I was getting pretty anxious.  Only when I had given up hope that it was coming did it show up.  So your package may still turn up.  But in the future, you do what you want of course, but Express packages should be avoided if at all possible.  They typically only get your package to you a day or two earlier and cost that much more but the biggest factor is how law enforcement looks at Express packages more closely for contraband.  It's possible your package was discovered and destroyed.  Typically you'd get a letter regarding that though. 

To take care of your depression, this may not be your thing, but exercise is a great ally.  It really gets your good chemicals flowing and makes you feel good and sleep good.  Keep doing it on the daily and eat some good food and you won't feel depressed.  If you're in the Northern Hemisphere, get out there and enjoy summer while it's still going on.  Take a walk or a bike or just people watch at the park. 

Finally in regards to the vendor, if you can't prove it was his fault the package didn't show I'd give him the benefit of the doubt.  Wait until the resolve button is available and then message the vendor saying perhaps you'd like to give it a few more days and if the package doesn't show perhaps they'd do a full refund or a reship.  A lot of times the vendor puts their policy for refunds or reships in their profile.  Considering you're living paycheck to paycheck I'd be firm and push for a full refund or reship so you aren't out of pocket.  Hope this helps.
