Does a drug need to be recreational in order to be liked?  I like drugs that serve specific purposes, even if they may not be recreational.  I like having a few Benadryl(sp?) handy for allergies or as a sleeping aid, or some Dramamine for a long drive or flight.  Neither of those are recreational at the doses I take them in, but I still very much enjoy the effect the drugs have on my body. 

To answer your question about benzos, I enjoy them because they can serve many different purposes depending upon the dosage and particular benzo ingested.  A Klonopin is good for a long work day, it's never really a bad time for Valium, and Xanax is probably the most effective way to instantly stop an oncoming panic attack that I know of.  Benzos also make me hungry which is good because I am too underweight right now, and are great for getting to sleep if you don't mind the distinct benzo hangover the next morning. 

I like benzos because they are my Swiss-Army knife of drugs.  If I am looking for recreational I could get reckless and go with higher doses of benzos, but to be frank that is too expensive compared to just smoking weed and far more dangerous.  I respect the power of benzos and the terrible potential of withdrawal too much to ever let them become too recreational. 

If you want some recreation, drop a few hits of LSD and take a walk through the muddy woods in the rain.  For added "Oh......fuck......" you can play Alice in Chains and Soundgarden on your iPod or such and watch the World get a whole lot weirder faster than you'd have expected.  I've done both parts of that recreational activity, and it is definitely something you have to do once in your lifetime, if only to know what insanity feels like.  As an added little bonus, that benzo you brought with you in the woods can abort the trip a lot faster than normal if it goes in a direction you can't handle.  See, Swiss-Army knife, I tell you.
