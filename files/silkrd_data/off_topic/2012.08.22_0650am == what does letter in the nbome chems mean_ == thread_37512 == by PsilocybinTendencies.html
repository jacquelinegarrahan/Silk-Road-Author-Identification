Title: Re: what does letter in the nbome chems mean?
Post by: k1k1 on August 22, 2012, 07:44 am
Relating to erowid it means:

2C-C-NBOMe →
   4-chloro-2,5-dimethoxy-N-(2-methoxybenzyl)
   phenethylamine
2C-I-NBOMe →
   4-iodo-2,5-dimethoxy-N-(2-methoxybenzyl)
   phenethylamine
Mescaline-NBOMEe →
   3,4,5-trimethoxy-N-(2-methoxybenzyl)phenethylamine

Source: http://www.erowid.org/chemicals/nbome/nbome.shtml

the number just means the relation of the chemicals in the molecule. I'm not an professional chemist, but a 50i seems to be pretty unstable and unrealistic to me.

Post by: TheAbsurd on August 22, 2012, 07:48 am
They're related to phenethylamine cousins.

"i" is for iodo, "c" is for chloro. You'd have to look at the chemical structure to understand it more.

Title: Re: what does letter in the nbome chems mean?
Post by: Tryptamine on August 22, 2012, 08:30 am
The 25 refers to the two methoxy groups at the 2 and 5 positions on the ring, and the letter (F, C, B, or I) refers to the substituent at the 4 position.
