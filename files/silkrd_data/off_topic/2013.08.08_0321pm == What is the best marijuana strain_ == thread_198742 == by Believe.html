Title: Re: What is the best marijuana strain?
Post by: MichaelWesten on August 08, 2013, 05:17 pm
Ok, this is easy.


The best strain boils down to two options, in the sense that they are the two most wildly popular strains out there. 
True OG Kush completely locked the whole nation for a long time, from rappers (wiz khalifa has a batch specially grown for him, so did snoop dogg for the longest time) to dispensaries, even today some of the highest prices are for a top notch version of OG Kush.   Vendor OGBryan offers a good version of OG but not the best I've ever smoked.  I have not bought OG from another vendor

Sour Diesel is my vote for best strain, uplifting (not tiring) high with little to no comedown and one of the richest tastes I've ever had in weed.  Vendor 4me2know occasionally offers it and he has a very good product, again not the best version I've had but it was excellent.


As far as any other strain: I had a great experience with some Afghan Gooey at a festival a couple of years ago, it was some of the strongest I had ever smoked at the time.  There was also a batch of Romulan at that same festival that had a wonderful cure and honeycomb structure and tasted EXACTLY like a bowl of Fruity Pebbles.  I would literally pay $100 for an 8th if someone could provide me with that exact bud again.

Truthfully; however, what really makes a good weed only partially has to do with genetics, if the plants do not have ideal growing conditions, are not flushed properly, or are not allowed to cure at a slow rate and then stored properly, you will not receive a product that is quality in taste, aroma, or length and quality of buzz.
My Qualifications:  I have grown and consumed 100's of different varieties of cannabis over the last 10+ years.  I have been smoking dispensary grade dank since 2003, and before that was growing my own.  I have smoked from blunts, joints, pipes, non-percolated bongs, percolated bongs, hash skillets, vaporizers, vape pens, and once when I was very young out of the side of a powerade bottle covered in tin foil.  I know my dank.

Another good question might be "what strain makes the best quality oil/wax". I wonder if that answer would be the same?

The technique used to create the oil/wax is what really gets you the quality.  You can obviously look at cannibinoid percentages after that they try and achieve the highest concentration of sources, but I don't think any particular strains are easier or turn out better product when converting to wax or oil (or cannabutter).  Maybe a little more potent because you chose a more potent strain to start but that's it.

Usually for people making these sorts of things they are growing it personally though since it usually takes more product to get a good oil return compared to flowers one would buy for smoking..  In this case, harvesting at the precise time can significantly effect the type of cannibinoids in the plant and impact the type of high it provides.
