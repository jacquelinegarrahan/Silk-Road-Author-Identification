Weigh the dry sheet.  Figure out the retention volume of the blotter.  Saturate the sheet for a few minutes.  Hold it for a couple minutes to allow the extra to drip off.  Weigh it again.  The difference is the solvent retained in the sheet.  One gram of water is about 1ml at 72 degrees.

Say it's a 100 tab sheet and it retained 15 grams.  Divide the retained solvent by the number of hits to get the maximum amount of solvent that can be absorbed per tab, aka 0.15ml or 150ul.  The dispensed volume should be about 30% of the max saturation limit, aka 45ul per tab.

Hi!
I take it you mean you already have the dilution at a drop, like the eyedropper bottles ready for taking. In which case I wouldn´t suggest bathing the sheet, you will lose a fair bit of acid. Bathing the sheets and weighing the retained liquid is for industrial production when you have many sheets going through the one bath. If you only have enough for one sheet you wont be able to get the liquid level high enough to immerse the entire sheet and you will get an uneven distribution. In this case if you only have enough for one or two sheets I would use the spray or drop method. The latter being most meticulate and effective. It consists of starting at the central point and moving out in a square pattern. ensuring you leave a good 4 seconds between drops. Just drop the center of each perforated square preciceley in the middle and move out in a square pattern till  your last 4 are the 4 corner ones. leave the sheet to dry flat on an upturned clean pyrex dish. do not touch/move/expose to light etc the sheet for 48 hrs, if you want to double dose them flip it over after 48 hrs and repeat exactly
