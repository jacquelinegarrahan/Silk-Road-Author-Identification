Title: Re: Why I hate psychedelics
Post by: zipstyle on December 18, 2012, 06:06 pm
This is just my personal opinion but...

I think that when used at the right times, psychedelic drugs are some of the most powerful, beautiful, healing and potentially enlightening experiences anyone can have. However, the states of mind produced by these drugs *can* be misleading. I have seen many people chase the psychedelic high because at first it facilitates higher order contemplation and understanding but then later on, these repeat users are unable to do those things on their own without the drugs.

Psychedelic drugs have made a major impact on my life, both in the positive and negative. I have no regrets from using them though. Some of the insights I had during my experiences I still am understanding deeper to this day (several years after my last trip, by choice). They are not for everyone, and I am very much against their casual use. Though I took them at raves or concerts, the most productive trips were the ones taken in the privacy of my own home alone or with a friend. Psychedelics have an addictive side on their own. Not an addiction to euphoria or "feeling good" per se, but there is a draw to "figure out" what the experience is all about. And the answer is that the experience will probably never be fully understood on the individual level.

(In my opinion) These substances are best used once or twice a year *at most* as tools to facilitate a deeper spiritual understanding of the paths that we walk in this great journey of life.

Wishing everyone the best holidays.
