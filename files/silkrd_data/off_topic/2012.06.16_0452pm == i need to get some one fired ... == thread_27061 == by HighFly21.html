Title: Re: i need to get some one fired ...
Post by: Sname on June 16, 2012, 07:31 pm
If he really did mess with you and your family, he sounds like a bad dude. But consider HIS family, and the implications getting him fired would have on them. They are innocent in all this, and him losing his job would affect those around him permanently. If he did something illegal to you and your family, consider legal avenues to punish him such as criminal complaints or a good old civil filing. A lawsuit may indeed be the best thing to do to him because it will drain his bank account and time defending it.

That being said, if you really want him fired, we should know at least what country you are in so that we may identify applicable laws and use them against him. In the US, complaints against an employee for sexual harassment, racism and discrimination are the easiest way to get someone fired. Even sending an anonymous letter to his boss, or even addressed to all employees, about his involvement in a child sexual abuse or female sexual harassment incident will get him into hot water. If nobody comes forward to substantiate it he may well keep his job, but his reputation at the office will be permanently ruined.

My advice is to take a couple weeks to cool off and let your temper subside. Think rationally about the damage he did to your family. Instead of focusing on revenge, perhaps you should focus on healing your families wounds and building a stronger bond with them through this crisis.
