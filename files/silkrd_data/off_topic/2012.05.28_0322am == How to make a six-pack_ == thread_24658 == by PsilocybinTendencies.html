Post by: Freeman on May 28, 2012, 05:50 am
Getting a six pack has very little to do with exercising your abdominal muscles with crunches etc and a LOT to do with changing your body composition.  That means eating well.  Diet will be about 90% of your effort.  In order to have visible abs you need to have your body fat % under 15% and more likely around 12% but this depends on your body and personal genetics.  You need to check your BF% to know where you are at.  Then just look online for information on lowering BF.  Since having a low bodyfat % is a priority for body builders then some of your best information will come from "competition prep" diets and workout plans.  But just a heads up a lot of those plans are designed for people that are taking steroids and other drugs.

That's about all I can fit here.  Do some research but what you're trying to do is lower your body fat.  If you try to look up how to get a six pack you're going to get a lot of bullshit marketing.

Title: Re: How to make a six-pack?
Post by: PiggyPie on May 28, 2012, 08:58 pm
Well people seem to have summed it up here but il throw in my .02 BTC.

The key to a six pack is low body fat. Thats why really skinny people have six packs, if there is no fat to go over the muscle then your gonna be able to see the muscle. I think it would be a little lower than 12% body fat to get a good definition on your abs, maybe 8%.

As for exercise then crunches are the best for abs, i would combine them with some planks, side planks, leg raises, bicycle crunches to get a good abs and obliques. However in terms of crunches i think i would aim to be able to do 4 (maybe) sets of 20 to 25 a day with about 90 seconds rest between them. Once you are able to do that i would think about holding books behind your hear or to your chest (depending on method) or just anything to weigh you down. Building muscle size is achieved best not through 1000's of reps but through heavy reps, i think 25 is plenty to be managing in a set and 4/5 sets is plenty to be doing.

As far as the diet goes low fat and low carbs are probably the best way to go as far as weight loss goes. I would suggest that you probably increase you protein intake, whey supplements are the easiest way to do this, if you can afford it go for the impact whey as it has less kcal etc. As was said previously its about eating a few small meals rather than 3 big ones. The reason is that every time you eat you bodys metabolism kicks in which means it starts burning energy, so by eating more times a day you keep it going for a longer period of time. 6 meals a day would be fine and a protein shake can be counted as a meal (pretty sure thats right). Try to avoid carbs from say 6pm onwards, the reason being is they are the bodys immediate store of energy, if you eat them late and then don't use them they get converted into fat for storage.

30-60mins cardio as many times as possible (up to 7) a week would be a great start to loosing the body fat. Try doing interval training as well which works on the principle you use 90% effort for 4 mins, 50% effort for 3 mins, 90% for 4 mins, 50% for 2mins or something like that. The point is you keep switching between moderate and intense. People seem to report better losses this way.

As for working out your abs i think the regularity depends on how much you are doing. If you did every exercise i listed for 3 sets, i don't think you could do them daily, they would probably take too long and im not sure they would have time to recover properly (if you over work them then you will just pull/strain/tear something and this will stop you doing most exercise, you wont get a six pack that way!). So il suggest two programs;

Daily;
3-4 sets of 20-25 crunches (key to a good crunch is not to bend your neck)
x3 1-2min planks
3 sets of 20-25 leg raises
Now this is only really working on your abs , once you get a decent show you mite want to start putting in exercises to work your obliques to give you some side definition.

Now this routine i think would work 3 (maybe 4) times a week, say Monday, Wednesday, Friday;
4-5 sets of 20-25 crunches
4 sets of 20-25 bicycle crunches
x3 1-2min planks
x3 per side 1-2min side planks
4 sets 20-25 leg raises
This doesn't seem like much more but i think if your are doing them to failure (when trying to build muscle always push to failure) you will find that pretty exhausting, and should have a noticeable DOMS (delayed onset muscle soreness) the next day. Just to clarify in my opinion you would be far better off only managing to do 16 crunches on your last set because you had some weights on you can couldn't push any more, than doing 25 on your last set and feeling fine. If you are hitting 25, you need to add some weight.

As for protein consumption, bodybuilders work on 1g per pound of weight i think. I think that is probably a little excessive but if you can hit 1g maybe 1.5g per kg that should be fine, you are not a competition body builder after all!

Just to add this work out is based solely around abs. If it was part of a balanced work out i would either stick to just doing the daily routine, or doing the other routine as twice a week in combination with one of the major muscle groups (probably make most sense to do with Back as both constitute part of your 'core' muscle group).

A great great website for free, no bullshit, home work out advice for both weight loss and muscle building is scoobysworkshop.com i admit the guy is a little weird, and his videos may seem a little silly. But the guy knows his stuff, and baring in mind he is 50 he is in amazing shape. I have always found his website informative and pretty damn inspirational =)

Hope this helps!
