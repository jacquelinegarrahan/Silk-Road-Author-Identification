Title: Re: Growing Kaya Gold outdoors NEED ADVICE
Post by: redalloverthelandguyhere on April 25, 2012, 01:00 pm
Buy some good quality organic soil. Water it well. Poke a hole in the soil with a toothpick to a depth of half an inch or so. Pop the seed into the hold and gently firm down the soil to bury the seed.

Put clingfilm over the pots. Remove as soon as each seed pops its head up.

Germinating is best done under fluorescent lights but you can hang up 3-4 household energy saving florescent lights. Best germinate in around 78 degrees heat - buy a small spray bottle and use the thin jet setting to oxygenate the soil around the emerging seeds.

Let the pots dry but never go bone dry! Never use feed on seedlings. You can give them a feed to condition the soil they will be placed in. You may need to contend with rabbits and deer in some parts. Humans will steal plants so go off the beaten track. Plant the plants away from sight of each other.

It might be an idea to just use clones so take clones off the plants before the flower. That way if anything special grows you have the DNA copy. for next year. It is easy to plant a lot of clones outdoors and its possible to plant up in April / May and come back in the Autumn!

Now the summer is here I would suggest hardening off the plants in your garden or a greenhouse. If you only have a few then go to the woods and find a sunny spot. Dig the hole and bring some good soil or worm  castings to condition the soil. Plant near a water source if you can otherwise you may be hiking with 5 liters of water. Put plenty of vermiculite or other water holding materials in the hole. You can get water crystals which will retain any water. Cannabis will spread the roots out and take up any available moisture.

If you see nettles growing well its likely cannabis will perform well also.

Tie down tall plants as nothing gives a plant away like being 8 foot high! It might look ok in the summer but remember in autumn everything brows but cannabis stays green till the end!

To tie down - peg up and tie rope to bend over the main stem.

Hide any materials or tools you use.

Taking a dog with you helps. If not a dog then take a camera, look the part as if your just another woodsman. Don't dress like Tupac. Don't wear some ganga leaf tshirt.

You may need to buy some 'neem oil' to deter any bugs which can do real damage on younger plants. For the first couple of months its best to visit twice weekly. After that once the plant is rooted it will generally do very well if the sun is shining and we get some summer rain.

During flowering you need to visit the plants often as its a race against mold if the weather is damp.

If we get rain in the last few weeks of flowering, shake the plants of excess rain. If you have a monster some even go to the trouble of putting up plastic sheeting for the home run.

Some plant up in big buckets which they can reuse each year.

Some even plant in trees but potheads climbing trees is a recipe for broken legs.

Harvest is something we can deal with later on. That is fun outdoors. Make a camp for the harvest. Trim it all outdoors and bury the waste. Then its prayer time. The drive home with your buds. Most dry at home. You can always make hash or oil from the moldy bits. If your on the ball you will harvest early if it looks like rain.

Frost is a killer also.

Best to grow indicas as they will flower in 8 weeks or so. Sativas might never flower in the UK but the USA is fine.

Good luck.
