With the recent demise of my most cherished and valued mentor, "The Norseman", I have elected to take a sabbatical. Most knew him as "Captain". He reached 97 and was agile as a ghost even unto his last day. A living legend, his greatness was unsurpassed even within the world's of fiction, perhaps with the exception of Jack London, who he claimed to have met. I never heard him lie. Embellish, yes, but always based in truth.  ;) In his honor, I now begin my own final great undertaking as was his wish.
I like to be bold. Thinking that's why I'm here. Truthfully, I have local FRIENDS that score me tree that's as good or better than what I've had here...so far. Example: It's blue and purple and after chopping up three pinners worth, I had a nice tiny piece of blue hash to scrap off my finger and thumb. I know right; but that description does not come anywhere near the real thing. Upped the order to Super Jumbo Size and, of course, one for the Wifey. :P
There's a vibe here, an atmosphere I feel. Can only describe it now as sticky. The ugly's try to get in. Everywhere exists a state of war. I have a different war to fight now. I'll explain further by quoting Liz Angerford. "In times of peace, be at war. In times of war, be at peace." Let us say a retreat. I like to be sticky free as possible. We're not huge tokers and with this product, one only needs a hit or two to be fine. My little secret gym bag stash should last us at least a year. Numbers crunched= not worth the risks, however small they may be.
This computer has seen better days. It's loud! Think I'll have some sledgehammer/furnace fun soon. I do promise I will return, strictly as a scutinizing forum contributor only though.Hearing the clamour already. ;D
I have not and do NOT mention the Captain to any in my private life. Just the way it had to be. Private relationship. Therefore, I am thankful for this anonymousness and to those of you here that have listened and sympathize, if any. My grief  goes undetected.
"Never love anything so much that you cannot watch it die." The Norseman
