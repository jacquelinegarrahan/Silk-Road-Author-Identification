Title: Re: Breaking Bad (Thread for Crystal Cookers)
Post by: madamebradley on December 02, 2011, 05:58 am
Safety equipment while doing any chemical reactions is very important. Even when being very careful accidents can happen. A beaker can be toppled over and a caustic solution is sprayed into your eyes or your lab coat catches fire (you can easily rip it off) just to name a couple reasons why safety is important.

If you don't have a proper fume hood for ventilation then it's best to wear a respirator that filters harmful organic substances.

Quote from: gumby on December 02, 2011, 05:52 am
When cooking MDMA is it possible for everything to go up in flames like meth manufacturers that are in the news sometimes dopeboy?
I'm guessing most clandestine lab fires are caused by solvents like toluene and acetone. Actually I remember once a friend was recrystallizing some meth at the westin hotel by boiling it in acetone. He was using a jerry-rigged distillation setup with a coffeepot heater and a fishtank water pump and we ended up falling asleep in separate rooms. By some miracle he woke up because he heard a "pop" and low and behold the entire coffeemaker was in flames and melted, right next to the window curtains. A few more minutes and the entire room would've been on fire.

Making MDMA with the Aluminum amalgamation 1)gives off hydrogen which is explosive, 2)requires methanol at boiling temperature which is flammable and 3)requires methylamine gas which is toxic and explosive. So to answer your question, yes it is possible to end up in the news like meth manufacturers. But I think for the most part making MDMA takes a lot more preparation is a multi-step process that requires more time to learn, it's not just "toss these 3 ingredients in the flask and cook for 3 hours" like some meth synths.
