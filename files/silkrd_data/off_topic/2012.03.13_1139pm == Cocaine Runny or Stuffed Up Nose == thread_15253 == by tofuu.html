please use distilled water or a saline solution. you can buy saline nasal spray for like a couple bucks which is sterile and the best option. this is definitely a great idea to keep your membranes rinsed which help reduce any damage from the powder and help absorb any of that pesky residue hiding

the reason is there is a nasty eukaryotic microorganism known as acanthamoeba which can live in municipal water systems. when this microbe is exposed to the nasal membranes,  it can actually cross this barrier and make its way into the brain. Once in the CNS, acanthamoeba causes encephalopathy caused by the inflammation and cytokine damage from the infection and is difficult to treat because of how similar the cells are to human cells compared to bacteria. Frighteningly, these eukaryotes literally eat your brain which is unfortunately frequently lethal.

I guess infections are not that common, but in the name of safety I avoid tap water and use a saline spray myself.
