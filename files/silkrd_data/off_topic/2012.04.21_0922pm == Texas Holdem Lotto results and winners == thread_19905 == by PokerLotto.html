Post by: PokerLotto on April 22, 2012, 12:17 am
The best hands are determined in this order:

Royal Flush (4 consecutive face cards and a 10 of all the same suit, eg. A K Q J 10 suited) [tie splits]
Straight Flush (5 consective cards of all the same suit, eg. 5 7 8 9 10 suited) [tie splits]
4 of a Kind (eg. 9d 9s 9h 9c) [highest kicker wins]
Full House (3 of one rank, 2 of another, eg. 4d 4c 4s Jc Jd) [highest 3 of a kind wins]
Flush (all of one suit, eg. As 4s 6s 9s 10s) [highest card wins]
Straight (5 consecutive cards of various suits, eg. 2 3 4 5 6) [highest card wins, if same then split]
3 of a Kind (3 of one rank, eg. 9s 9c 9h) [highest kicker wins]
2 Pair (2 of one rank, 2 of another rank, eg. 8s 8h 2c 2c) [highest kicker wins]
1 Pair (2 of one rank, eg. 7c 7s) [highest kicker wins]
High Card (eg. Ace)

How are you drawing the cards dude?

I virtually draw a text list of 52 cards from 1 deck using random.org, which uses atmospheric noise to generate random numbers.
https://www.random.org/playing-cards/

The order of the cards is out of my control.

I take a screenshot of the card list, generate an MD5 for the screenshot, upload the screenshot to Onion Image Uploader so people can verify the card order for themselves, then I use the last digit in the MD5 plus the last digit in the filename at Onion Image Uploader to determine which player is "dealt" first, I copy the text from random.org and each card is assigned a label and the assigned cards are posted in the forum.

The player dealt first is out of my control. And so the winner is out of my control.

The 5 cards that represent the board determine the winner, the player with the best hand.

In Texas Holdem Lotto each player gets 2 hole cards and everyone shares the 5 cards on the board, and the best 5-card hand that can be made is the winner (some games might have more winners, from best to worst hand). Right now, the cost of a ticket (the "ante"), is all that goes into the pot. (Although I might do freerolls at some point).

Since I'm a new vendor, I don't think betting rounds is a good idea at this time, because I'd be asking people to trust me (and every other player) that card information is unknown. I've tried to create a process of doing a drawing where people don't have to trust me. The order of the cards and who gets dealt first is out of my control. If people wanted to bet, they would probably have to bet blind before any cards were dealt. Texas Holdem usually has a small blind and big blind, but I haven't considered how to do that yet.

Maybe when I get more established I could consider betting rounds, maybe by posting an encrypted message of how the cards were shuffled and dealt, but hiding the cards and betting rounds would increase the likelihood of cheating and collusion, since we're all anonymous people on the deep web. A dishonest operator could tell a player someone else's cards. If each player knew their own cards, they could tell their friends, and work together, and so on. In a real life game, a dealer might be able to tell if players were sharing information with each other. Not online though.

If you would prefer a bitcoin poker game that has bets and is not on the Silk Road, I have heard of https://sealswithclubs.eu which supposedly uses the software Poker Mavens by Briggs Softworks, but even that site says opponents might be cheating, and says the only way to ensure it's a fair game is if two people play heads up. I've never tried it and I can't vouch for it, but you might like something like that.
