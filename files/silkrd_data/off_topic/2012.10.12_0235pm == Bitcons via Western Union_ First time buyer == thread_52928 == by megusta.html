I am also a noob to the site. I have only purchased bitcoins twice, both times have been through moneygram at CVS. I deposit using the service 'Bitinstant' which takes 4% of the amount (in USD) of BC you deposit. You also have to pay a small fee to ZipZap (the company that actually gives you the BC). They do ask for your information, but I don't think it serves any major purposes, as I have used fake information both times, with no problem. Yes, there are a couple of fees tacked on for doing it this way, but it's worth the convenience of being able to walk into your local CVS, 7-11, or Wal-Mart and have the bitcoins in your wallet by the time you get home. It doesn't get any easier than that. I'm also one of the most paranoid individuals in the world and after getting the first time jitters out, it's now a cake walk in and out.
