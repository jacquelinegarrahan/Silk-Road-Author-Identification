* - I was hammering away on my papers when I got inspired to write this little poem for all you Road roamers. - *
* - If anybody else has a poem for the Road, or feels inspired to write one, Lets hear it! -*

So I noticed that at this moment, as I was smoking my sidecar,
That there was something amiss among the silkiness.
I know a noob shall not expect to be respected,
But I want you to see, a perspective that grows inside me, and it on the rise in society.
The generation that are the teens of today, are those that have the most to say.
There are more than ever, but the opinions they have are like a feather.
They are easily convinced of social trends and base their life around their friends.

The web and its social networking is dangerously increasing the level of consumerism that we ingest.
At the same time, more people are using the web to do research before they invest.
Have I lost you off the far end yet?
Humans know from research that the web changes our brain but we aren't sure exactly.
Most studies seem to show it makes people think abstractly.
The Government as we see it today is a pretty linear system.
But it looks to me like the educated adolescents of today may resist them.
As they begin to take office positions, the new views will cause suspicions.
As those with abstract minds, grow to join the ballot lines,
The change will begin to be defined.

The web is teaching people to critically think.
More people are learning to question their views and come to the brink.
They have a view that if you believe it, say it.
The great majority use the web to relay it.
Every person strives to be unique.
Yet our teenagers motivation is especially weak.
So they continue to use their screens instead of their feet.
They will come together in a way that no generation could before.
Corporations will reach for support, but fall through the floor.
And its starting with something we all abhor,
The drug war.
Thank you Tor.

This is not a business that is influenced by advertising or how much someone spends on capturing an individual's focus.
There are no pop-up or side-banner ads that flash with "smoke this!"
This free market is bigger than any of us
Its a representation of what the new generation is made of.
We know that we want the government to protect our homes,
But we donâ€™t expect to be scrutinized for what paths we choose to roam
Let us each walk along this road
We are going somewhere fast, but where?
No one knows.
