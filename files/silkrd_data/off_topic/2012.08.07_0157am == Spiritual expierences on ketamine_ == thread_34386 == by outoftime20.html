I find it can give you a spiritual experience without k holing. I think a lot of it depends on your state of mind. I mentioned in the "what do you watch while you trip" thread that I like to read sci fi or fantasy books while I use dissociatives. I find that familiar books can take you out of the world entirely and let you live in the book's world for a while. Depending on the book and author, this can have profound effects. Try the short story "Call Me Joe", read it once sober, then reread it as you're using Ketamine.

Provided you enjoy reading, this can be a very rewarding experience.
Title: Re: Spiritual expierences on ketamine?
Post by: blackend646 on August 07, 2012, 07:34 am
I bought a small amount of K to experiment with recently and I really don't know what to make of it. Low doses seem pointless to me, it's like being sober except you can't move without feeling nauseous. I just did a large amount last night and listened to some music, I spent about an hour having vivid recollections of events in my life that I totally forgot about. It was really interesting.

But a few weeks ago, the first time I took a stab at a large dose, during a shroom comedown. That was the most what-the-fuck hour of my life.

I split it into two big lines, railed the first one and waited a while. Once I started to feel it for some reason I started thinking about vampires. I started wondering if vampires exist, and eventually convinced myself that they do, and the government is covering it up. Then I did the second line, and in the most intense moment of clarity I have ever felt in my existence, I realized that I AM A VAMPIRE.

I spent the next hour twitching around in my bed saying "Vampire... I am a vampire", whilst drawing and retracted my fangs that I totally believed I had, and I was completely oblivious to the fact that I was under the influence of a drug. Tasting immortality felt damn good, and made for a hilarious story when it was over.
