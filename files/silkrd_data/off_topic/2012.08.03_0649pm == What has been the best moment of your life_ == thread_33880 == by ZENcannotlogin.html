Title: What has been the best moment of your life?
Post by: ZENcannotlogin on August 03, 2012, 06:49 pm
What was the best night (or day) of your life so far? I mean the best you have ever felt in a single moment!
And was it a drug related experience or not?
And lastly, is it sad if someones happiest moment of their life is a drug experience?

I say this because when I was 15 I took my first E with some friends at our local bonfire night (UK). Hundreds of people in a local field with fairground rides, fireworks and a bonfire. Me and my mates got so fucked off one pill each (those were the days) we just went around finding people we knew from school, telling them how much we loved them and asking for hugs. Embaressing really, especially when we went back to school the next monday! The venue wasn't important, I've never felt such sense of pure love and happyness with no sexual side to it. It was the best E I ever did, never got that same feeling again. At the time I remember thinking "this feels better than winning the lottery, better than anything".

I've been in love in the past, but I've never had kids or got married, these things would probably trump my first E on a long term basis.
But, just thinking of that single night, that intense few hours, I'm not sure I could feel much happier. There have been a few nights that come close, again they're all times I was on drugs. Don't get me wrong, I have lots of interests outside of taking drugs!

Which leads me to my final point, because these are moments of induced artificial happyness, is that not a little sad? I'm living my life for the experiences and I'd hope that when I look back from my deathbed, the best moments of my life weren't just times I was on drugs. I'd like to think that my first child being born or getting married would be the highlights. Moments involving other human beings, genuine accomplishments and things I can be proud of. Or does it not really matter? Thoughts anyone? And post your happiest moments, on drugs or not.

Drugs are no more "artificial" happiness than anything else. Emotions are all based off of chemical reactions in the brain anyway.
