Professor John McWhorter, Stanford University, has recorded two lecture series through The Teaching Company:
"Understanding Linguistics: The Science of Language" and
"The Story of Human Language"

Many interesting ideas are discussed, including (from TTC description):
Quote
- Comprehend that changes in language (including new words, constructions, or usages) are a normal and inevitable part of the language's evolution.
- How does a language change, and when it does, is that change indicative of decay or growth?

He doesn't distinguish between the map and the territory, indication and implication, denotation and connotation, etc. And he certainly does not go into interpretation of metaphor, hermeneutics, analysis and critique of poetry and art, etc. - all of which seem to be a fundamental part of human communication. He does however have interesting things to say about language prejudice and the common misconception that there is such a thing as "correct" grammar or "correct" pronunciation.
