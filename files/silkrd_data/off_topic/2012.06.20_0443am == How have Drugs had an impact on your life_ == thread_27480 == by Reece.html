   I went off to college and smoked a lot more than I should have. I ended up in a psych ward for a week and a half due to a psychotic break from smoking too much cannabis. I now know that you can smoke too much cannabis and that I probably have some underlying mental issues.

I still love pot but don't see how it has benefited my life. Now strong sativas make me anxious and seeing as how that's popular around my area I can't find any Indicas and when I do I can never get a hold of the "vendor" again. So I've stopped using cannabis for a bit.

• Nutmeg made me laugh at the mundane and find humor in church as well as pretty much started me down the road of drugs.

• Sativa makes me a certified crack head around certain friends who bring that out in me.

• Indica/Sativa hybrids are my favorite and increase introspective analysis, the enjoyability of music as well as take the edge off my newly developed anxiety.

• LSD sent me on a psychedelic mission to get blasted into space and further my experience in the realm of spirituality. I love L and hope to find more. Having only done it 3-4 times at low (~70ug - 200ug) dosages I can't really say much about the hallmark effects.

• Alcohol makes me everyone's best friend. Seriously.

• DXM made me realize how wasteful I can be... with my money. I may never buy OTC DXM products ever again after only ~4 experiences.

• MDMA is great! I tried it twice and loved it both times. Want to try it at a club setting (Hopefully with me as the DJ). People seem to think I am a smug jerk. AT heart I am not but I guess it can seem that way, I often have no expressive response for things I should feel emotional about. I try to be friendly but at times I find it futile. If I could have MDMA pumped into me every day maybe that would go away. Awesome experience. Not to be missed with good friends and even better tunes.

Lately I've been doing some thinking about drug use and see no reason to delve into it. If life is good why change it? I tried Cannabis at 16 and I liked it but there was no reason to keep going. I was searching for a high. A high that I found many times after my first few experiences but I haven't really gained anything from my use.

Some may say drugs are like food. You are willing to pay good money for some good food that tastes great and makes you feel good. But food is necessary for life. Some drugs or most drugs aren't necessary but they can easily become a necessary part of your day if abused. I'll undoubtedly raise my kids to understand, and respect drugs and their users but wouldn't care either way about their position on the matter. As long as they have the common sense and education I think they'll make responsible decisions when it comes to drugs. I didn't act responsibly even with the education and respect. That may just be a character flaw. But then again why am I talking about kids when I'm only 18.

Cheers!

-Liquid Eater

But something else was happening...conversations were deeper... I had known my friends for years, but that night the discussions we had we truly opened our eyes to the world and things would never be the same.

My first LSD trip was also the first time i dabbled in cocaine. Just did a couple of small bumps from one of the older dudes at a party i was at. I slowly started doing cocaine and incorporated it into my daily drug routine. Mostly sniffing, but sometimes adding a 100 mgs or so to a joint or blunt with my MJ and smoking it. Woolies we called em....

Then ecstasy hit the scene and once i took a pill it instantly became my new drug of choice. It was everywhere and i had unlimited access to it. I was just a dumb kid who had no access to harm reduction sites that exist now and over  a few month binge i depleted my serotonin levels and began suffering from panic attacks and anxiety. I had no self control and ended up fucked up in the head still to this day....

Along the way i came across some mushrooms....I think it was due to my lack of serotonin, but i had a horrible trip where i suffered ego death. I thought i was going to die that night and although i survived the trip, i was left with hppd and i havent been the same since. There is something about ego death that causes you to realize how insignificant you really are in this world and how life is so short and meaningless. To this day i have social anxiety and always put everyone else above myself. Self confidence is zero, and im a good looking, smart, and well educated semi successful individual. Ive accomplished so much in my life, but cant accept that im worth 2 goddamn shits.....Its hard to explain but my life is daily struggle....

Then i discovered opiates....Ahhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh finally some relief. I feel like a million bucks when im high, and i even have to power to shut of my brain from its crazy ramblings if i ingest enough to just nod out and say fuck the world. Its a selfish thing to do, and i often fuck people i know over to enjoy the peaceful serenity of a good old fashioned nod session, but i feel i earned it. Why cant i be selfish sometimes??????

Anyway i love drugs. Yes they have caused me some problems in my life, but they have also always been there for me unlike many people in my life who have fucked me over.

There is so much more to this story, but ive said more than enough. Im very grateful for silkroad and the community here on the forums. Sites like erowid are really great too and help with harm reduction and information to avoid misuse of the thousands of different mind altering substances out there. I wish i knew growing up what i know now about responsible drug use. Drugs have been such a major part of my life...honestly i spend more time, energy, and money on drugs than anything else which is kind of sad but at the same time its so deeply instilled inside of me i honestly dont feel like its that bad.
