Regarding the varied opinions on weed as an anti-anxiety -

I've had the full range of experiences on different strains. The first thing one needs to do before trying to use weed to cure social anxiety is figure out what strains they're smoking and whether the strains are more indica, afghanica, or sativa. I personally have a strong preference for sativa leaning to pure sativa strains, as the highs I get are more clear-headed. Indica's tend to stone my ass and lock me to the couch and in my head, raising my anxiety levels as I feel like I may have gotten myself too high to contribute to group discussion (rising anxiety due to fear of having committed social faux pas). Sativas, though, leave me clear headed and creative so that I can contribute more effectively than when sober, alleviating anxiety entirely.

I've always experienced the two ends of the spectrum from a single bag. First, I was treating the plant and the high with no respect. Not paying any attention to it. It slapped me and made me its bitch - I smoked and became useless. Then I meditated on what the high can do for me and what it can teach me, and the next day instead of clouding my mind further it evaporated them as I focused on appreciation and respect for the tool I'd been given.

It depends so much on how you do it, it's silly to say yes or no to weed as a whole for social anxiety.
