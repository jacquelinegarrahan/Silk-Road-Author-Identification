Facial Recognition buddy, that's what.  When I got my 6 fake ID's for opening up various 24 hr access postal boxes around town, for each image (since it's my face), I slightly modified it in an attempt to defeat facial recognition software, but not so much that it wouldn't pass as my ID.

For Example, for Fake ID No. 1, I would slightly change the distance between my pupils.  Fake ID No. 2, I lengthened my forehead, slightly, and so on and so forth.  Many jurisdictions are using facial recognition for all newly issued ID's.  Therefore, my real life image is or will be in a database, however should something go awry at one of my boxes, and the postal service scanned my ID upon renting the box and that image is submitted to authorities for facial recognition purposes, well, it may come up as a match, but a weaker one than had I got the id with simply my un-modified face photo.

Little things like this can help you from being the low hanging LE fruit.
