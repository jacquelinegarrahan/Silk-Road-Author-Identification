Pharmahuasca in general has some subtle differences from a "pure" ayahuasca brew (and even between individual brews there are differences btw) because it is a blend of two extracted chemicals (harmala + N-N-DMT) vs. a blend of many of them (as in the chemicals contained in a plant in total). While the so-called "active alkaloids" of ayahuasca are indeed harmala + DMT, there are however many more alkaloids (plus lipids, oils, sugars etc. etc.)  in the plants used in ayahuasca themselves that also if not "active" in the psychedelic sense (at last for what science knows today) anyway can subtly change the experience.
However the type of voyage in its general aspect has the same connotation, the differences will be, as I said, much more of a subtle nature (and these changes depend also on the types of plants used to begin with).

The great advantage of a pharmahuasca is that it is very easy to use. No need to drink awful tasting tea or anything of the sort. For a first time it is indicated since for the first time it will anyway very difficult to understand those differences. Still I personally think that if then you are interested you should then try a pure ayahuasca brew since the type of "energy" you will encounter has some definitive difference (it's difficult to explain what it is meant by this but when you are there you will surely understand it).
