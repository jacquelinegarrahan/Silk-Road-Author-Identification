It's actually true in a lot of places. But it's usually an almost-catch-22 type of situation, where consumption is legal but buying, selling, growing/making your own, stocking it anywhere or just carrying it on your person are all illegal. So, if you happen to find some laying around and use it immediately, you're not committing a felony... unless it can be argued that it was evidence that someone else was planning or trying to commit or had already committed a felony, in which case you're obstructing the flow of justice.

I know for a fact that this is true of most illegal drugs in Brazil, Chile, Peru and Argentina (though marijuana is legal in Peru and has questionable legality in Argentina, and cocaine is also legal for personal use in Peru... sort of. You can grow a whole forest of coca if you want, so long as you don't refine the product. When I lived there, a day didn't go by when I didn't either drink coca tea or chew coca leaves, so I was technically a regular cocaine user).

Title: Re: When do I become a criminal?
Post by: Horizons on February 03, 2012, 07:29 pm
The most legally sound answer to your question, at least from the point-of-view of a criminal theorist (if you ask police you'll get a different answer, of course) is this:

The moment it becomes proven in a court of law that you committed a conduct which constitutes a crime, you retroactively become a criminal from the moment when you committed that crime forward. You cease being a criminal when you finish serving your sentence, be that jail time, community service, paying a fine or anything else.
