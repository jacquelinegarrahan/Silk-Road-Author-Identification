Post by: captainjojo on May 05, 2012, 05:16 am
Yes, for accounts created since the PIN came into existence you create the PIN when you create your account. 

Forgotten PIN's seem to be an almost daily occurrence on SR.  Either they forgot to set it when they created the account (or forgot what they set it to) or they set it so long ago they have forgotten what it is.

If you have forgotten your PIN try not to lock yourself out of the PIN entry routine.  Stop before the maximum tries.

Then send a POLITE pm to SR support.  Keep it short and simple:

"Hello, Sorry to be bothering you but I was trying to create an order only to realize that it's been so long since I setup my PIN that I have completely forgotten it.  Would it be possible to have my PIN reset so that I may make a new one?

Thanks"

If you keep it simple and ask politely hopefully you will have your PIN reset in a few hours.

It worked for me.  I was on SR before they had PIN's and they installed a routine that would kick in when you logged on asking you to create a PIN.  I set the PIN without really paying much attention to it and when it came time for me to place a new order I couldn't for the life of me remember what the PIN number was.  I sent a short, polite, PM and they reset it for me that day.

Post by: cacoethes on May 06, 2012, 08:43 pm
Here's the way it goes regarding PIN resets, at least as far as I understand it, and what my experience tells me...

If you lock yourself out by using up all your attempts, you'll receive a PIN reset message from SR.  You're supposed to wait seven days to respond to it, then SR will reset your PIN.  Supposed to be another step to protect against people trying to gain unauthorized access to your coins.  That way, if your password is compromised, you still have the protection of your secondary PIN to prevent the same person from draining your account.

However, if you already know that you've forgotten your PIN, don't use up all your attempts trying to remember it.  Message SR preemptively, and let them know what's going on.  Assuming everything seems legit on their end, they'll send you the PIN reset message, and will let you reset it without waiting seven days.  Once you've reset your PIN, it may take roughly 24 hours for you to be able to use it.  This has been my experience the one time I forgot it.  I was off only by capitalization or punctuation, and I suspect that this was probably evident to whoever fielded my request for help.

Keep in mind that if something DOES NOT seem legit, no amount of pleading or badgering is going to convince them to let you reset it immediately, and you'll have to wait the obligatory seven days.

Remember that there is a human being receiving your desperate private messages for help.  That human being is going to have to make a judgment call based on the information available to them.  It doesn't seem to be a set-in-stone, cut and dried process, which is why some people are able to get it resolved immediately, and why others can't even get it resolved in seven days.
