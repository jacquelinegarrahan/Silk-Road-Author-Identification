Most (well all) pharmacies, even bitter rivals have an interconnected network of open information on suspicious scripts.  They fill so many that it's pretty easy to spot a questionable script.

Having just said all that i'm going to be extremely hypocritical in telling you some of the more common latin acronyms/phrases used.  This is for educational purposes, like I said in my first sentence, enjoy your stay in jail if you plan on attempting anything like this.

Mitte and Sig are the two ... well primary part's of a script, Mitte means quantity, sig means instructions, typically the script will just say M: 60
S: blah blah.  OD means once daily, BID means take twice daily, TID (3 times), QID (4 times), PRN means when needed for pain, CF means with food.  I'm sure there are comprehensive lists of latin prescription abbreviations on the internet.
