I'm not sure for other countries but in the U.S. it is determined by a State's bylaws for the carry and conceal permit.

Sporting guns like a Rifle, Shotgun, Black-powder don't need a CCW (Carry Concealed Weapon) Permit but smaller fire arms such as a pistol do. Some states require the gun to be visible (Open Carry) while other State's you can cover and hide a weapon.

Some State's will recognize a CCW Permit that has been issued in a different State while others will not. In order to find the information that specifically pertains to you where you are located, local Sheriff or a simple web search will suffice.

I got my CCW to carry a Taurus .380 ACP in my vehicle or on my person. It was an 8 hour class @ 150$ and then a 100$ fee to the County Police Dept. - I try to target shoot once a month but with the shortage of ammo now, its been more like once every 3 months.
