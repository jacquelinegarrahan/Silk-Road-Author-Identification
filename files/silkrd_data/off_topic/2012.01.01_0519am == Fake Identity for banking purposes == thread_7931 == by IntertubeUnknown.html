Panama, Cayman Islands, Bermuda, Turks & Caicos, plenty of small ex-British colonies can be helpful.

My advice would be to split your funds over at least two jurisdictions, don't keep all your eggs in one basket to help reduce the risk from getting ripped off.

Panama has been known to take control of funds from certain individuals.  There is always a risk with any financial arrangement and past performance is no indication of future performance, do not rely on a country having been decent in the past to stop them from stealing from you in the future.

If your funds come from an illegal venture you are at more risk, most tax efficiency regimes are interested in helping companies and high net worth individuals to legally increase the efficiency of their tax arrangements.
