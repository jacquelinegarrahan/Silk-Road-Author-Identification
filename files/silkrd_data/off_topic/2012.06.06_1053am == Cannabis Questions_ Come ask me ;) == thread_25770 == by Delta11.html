I have experimented with isomerization but in my line of work (edibles) it's not worth it because most users will get headaches . While it does make your THC more potent you really need to have the chemistry down to do it right, if you have the skills and lab equipment than by all means go for it. I personally do not go for it because of the simple fact that it gives the THC a more psychedelic feel and most customers would rather have the good ol' mellow high of THC rather than a potent psychedelic high, but maybe in the future I will offer a more potent product that has gone through isomerization  :)
