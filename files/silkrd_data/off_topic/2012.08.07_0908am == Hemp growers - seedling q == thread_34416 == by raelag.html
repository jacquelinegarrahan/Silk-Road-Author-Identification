Title: Re: Hemp growers - seedling q
Post by: UKMJ on August 07, 2012, 09:23 am
This is usually due to the weight of the top of the plant. This can be prevented by pointing a small fan at the seedlings from as soon as you see the seedling come tyrough the soil, this has the effect of strengthening the stalk of the plant, causing it to thicken up and become more suppirtive. Very important if growing sativa strains due to their tendency to stretch and bend due to the weight of mature buds.

Title: Re: Hemp growers - seedling q
Post by: dkmonk on August 07, 2012, 11:21 am
I am confused what you have your seedlings in is it something like an aero cloner?

Whatever it is you want plain water in there without any nutrients. Reverse osmosis water, spring water, or anything like that is fine. There should be zero salt in your water.

I would use peat pucks, or a special seedling tray until they have some roots then place them in your growing system, but that is just my personal preference.

You also do not want the water on the leaves of your seedlings just the roots with plenty of oxygen too. When water is on the leaves it amplifies the light and hurts the seedlings really you never want water on your cannabis leaves unless you are doing a foliar feeding and that would be in vegetative stage once they are past seedling.

Title: Re: Hemp growers - seedling q
Post by: jmatts on August 07, 2012, 01:47 pm
Seedlings will grow tall and lanky if you don't give them enough light and if the temperature is too high.  They will not grow as high and won't fall over if you keep the temperature around 68-70 degrees Farinheight for the air, [the soil temperature can be higher around 75 degrees Farinheight to help roots grow faster, by putting a heating pad or something underneath the cups they're in], but make sure the air temperature is still around 68-70 degrees Farinheight after the heating pad is on and watch it to make sure it doesn't get the seedlings soil too hot. The only other thing to not having tall lanky seedlings that are week and fall over is to make sure they get intense enough light, a 65 watt CFL, [Compact Fluoresant Light] or two,[depending how many seedlings you have], should solve your light problems. They need to be placed kind of close to the seedlings, [about 6"-12" above the seedlings], because they don't put off that many lumens, plus they're not that hot at all so they don't burn the seedlings.
 That should solve your tall lanky week seedling problem, hope this helps.
