UK here so no DARE, but we had similar things including a workshop named 'Cannabis to Chaos' which essentially involved parading a couple of ex-junkies in front of us and having them talk about how they started with weed.
The most nonsensical thing I remember were some of the 'drug education' books I read...this was in the early 2000s and they were written in a kind of schizophrenic way, torn between 'don't do it' and trying to be cool, like it was all our choice and they knew where we were coming from.
One book divided drugs into 'uppers, downers and weeeird' and the 'weeeird' was all written in green swirly writing. The page on LSD had psychedelic imagery in the background, while the text starkly warned us that we were all going to die of strychnine poisoning.
I was always interested in psychedelics and the teachers never told us shit about them so I read this garbage. One leaflet warned that 'on LSD, you may see things which frighten you!' The first time I tripped I got hard into the headspace and laughed for about an hour about the fact that the leaflet really thought it would be what I SAW that messed me up. They never mentioned the mindfuck, no they did not...
Not as bad as DARE, but clueless all the same. I was lucky that my parents at least were far more open minded and would answer my questions honestly.
