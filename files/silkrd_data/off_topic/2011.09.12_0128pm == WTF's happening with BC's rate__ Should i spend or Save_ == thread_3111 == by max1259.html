Here's my view on spending/saving btc. Thus far, I have lost money on most of my transactions due to the fluctuation. I cash out half and keep half in the hopes that the value will go back up. If it doesn't, well I will be screwed. If it does (which I believe it will at some point in the future) I will have a nice chunk of change to play with. If bitcoins get to the point where they are 1% of e-currency transactions, each of the 21,000,000 that will eventually be mined will be worth $50 or more. As evidenced by the success of paypal, liberty reserve, pecunix etc, digital currency isn't going anywhere. Bitcoin will spread beyond the hacker/drugs by mail scene eventually and when it does the value will go back up. As the popularity increases, the value will also increase. The one benefit the bitcoin has over the other currencies (besides being anonymous) is that there is a finite number of them that will be created. Not like other e currencies that are based on USD or EUR or something similar. Once there are 21 million bitcoins, that's it there are no more. When this happens, value will increase. It may take a long time but from an investment standpoint it is worthwhile to keep them. IF you need money right away from selling your product, sell them. IF you can manage to keep at least some of them, they will be worth more in the long run.
