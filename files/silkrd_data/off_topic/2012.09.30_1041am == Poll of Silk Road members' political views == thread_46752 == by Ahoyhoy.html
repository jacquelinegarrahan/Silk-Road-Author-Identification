Post by: anonymarse on October 09, 2012, 03:38 am
My political philosophy can be summed up with the Golden Rule: "Do unto others as you would have done unto you." That means thinking about what others want and respecting their wishes, as you would want your wishes respected. I can't think of anyone who really, deep down, wants to be told what to do under the threat of fines, prison and death. Nobody likes having their choices cancelled by those stronger than them. They only like having OTHER people's choices, which they do not like, canceled. But the Golden Rule would say, if you want to be free to do as you wish, you must give others that same freedom - even if you don't like what they will do with it.

So basically, I'm against coercion in general. Give the person doing the coercing a badge, a costume, a crown, or an elected office and it makes not a damn bit of difference. Give the coercion special names like "eminent domain", "welfare", "public morality", or "national defense" and it still makes no difference.
