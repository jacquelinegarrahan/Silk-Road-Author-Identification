In theory it could be done.  In practice, it's a really, really, really bad idea.  Think about it.  Even if you jailbreak your phone, you still don't know what other kinds of hidden features are still hidden in the ROM or which could be remotely activated over the air.  And let's not forget that you're still giving away your location more or less constantly.  It's most likely true that your network traffic won't be decrypted, but wouldn't be a real pisser if there's some as-of-yet-undetected spyware that runs on iPhones to "improve the user experience" that keeps track of what apps you run or what's on your screen or something?  Remember the whole CarrierIQ scandal from several months back?  It's not just tinfoil hat conspiracy shit.

If you're in a place where getting caught with your laptop will lead to very bad things happening to you, maybe the best thing to do is just take a vacation from selling for a little while.
