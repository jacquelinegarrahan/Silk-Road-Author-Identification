Not iboga, but i have smoked dmt and tried VERY high (probably too high) doses of acid. Not so much shrooms, I tend to have a bad trip on them, regardless of how much i ingest...they also tear my stomach up.

I'm under the impression, that while psychedelics do have the ability to change a person's perception, some personality traits of a person is more or less just who they are.

I'm not gonna lie, I had whole worlds opened up to me thanks to those drugs. I was able to see things that I wasn't able to see before. I believe that once you try one of those drugs, your perception of life really does change forever.

That being said, everyone also handles those drugs differently, and, due to the fact that it's simply the way I am and probably always will be, I tend to just always be overly analytical and hold  beliefs more grounded in what can be proven by science.

For example, a person that's somewhat spiritual pre first trip, is more likely to be more of a spiritual person after they experience a trip.

It also means that person that thinks the way I do, is more likely to continue to think that way afterwards. the only difference between pre-trip and post trip is that a person like me is more open to other people's veiws and or thinks of ideas, ideas still suited to that person's pretrip mentality, that he or she would have never thought of before tripping.

To be more clear on what a person like me is like post trip is, I became more philosophical. I didn't suddenly have new spiritual type ideas, though, because that simply is not how my brain works. I just simply became more aware of the different ways of thinking....a lot like any other person tripping would do, except that I treated my new ideas like a researcher would treat any discovery in the science field.

Before I ever tripped, I would have never said this:

to each his own.

I truly believe in that more than anything.
