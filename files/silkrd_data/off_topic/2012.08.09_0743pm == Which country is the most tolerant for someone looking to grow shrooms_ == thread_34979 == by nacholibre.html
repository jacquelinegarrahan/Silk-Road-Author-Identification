Title: Re: Which country is the most tolerant for someone looking to grow shrooms?
Post by: Rawkingfish on August 10, 2012, 09:26 pm
I grew shrooms for about 6 months, They are very easy to grow, all of the materials required are easy to get and not watched (with the exception of a spore syringe or print) As long as you are very thorough, and follow strict sterilizing rituals(disinfect work area, sterilize surfaces, turn off all fans, etc.) You wont lose any jars to mold, i never lost a jar during colonizing  There is no smell(maybe slight when they are fruiting, but you only smell that when you get right up on your terrarium to harvest or humidify) They dont require a lot of space, and they dont need crazy hot HID lights....If your climate is right for it, Mashmans suggestion to move your colonized cakes or medium outdoors to a discrete plot, youll end up with a nice little almost never ending forest of mushys...if you are lucky they will come back next year when the temp is right. Id suggest The Shroomery forums, everything is there.

I started with the intention of personal use....they were my first psychedelic and I had an amazing experience....but after a few months of weekend visits to happyfunland it just became less special....and I found that the more i thought about it, the more that would invade my subconscious and color my trips. I ended my run with mason jars full of dried p. cubensis in my pantry and a huge tupperware container full of shroom chocolate squares. So when you talk of moving to another country to farm, im not sure what you mean.....are you taking acres upon acres of medium? giant buildings filled with row after row of grow boxes like the legit gourmet mushroom farms use?
