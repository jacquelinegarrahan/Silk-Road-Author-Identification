This is absolutely true. Thay are called vitreous floaters, and I have had them, much to my annoyance, since I was a little kid, maybe since birth. Thay're awfully irritating, more so at some times than others. The secret is to try to accept them and at the same time to ignore them, get past them, because I am afraid there is no remedy.

But as to being caused by mushroom use? BS. It has nothing to do with that. They are torn pieces of the retina that just float around in the vitreous humour.

The only long-term effect (and not even close to permanent) is a trippy mentality, a surge of psychedelic thinking and associated emotional state, that you can get even months after ingesting, especially if you've been doing them for a good long time at frequent intervals. Some refer to this as "flashbacks", a terribly derogatory term in my opinion.

Yeah, like I said, they get torn off the surface of the retina. A hit in the head could do it, or some other strong movement; MAYBE even emotional turmoil, like the kind that a strong psychedelic trip may produce.

The fact that you didn't notice them before the trip may indicate that you didn't have them, or it may show that you just didn't notice them period. But there's no direct physical cause and effect between a trip shroom or otherwise and the sudden appearance of floaters.
