How do I love thee?  Let me count the ways:

1.  Silk Road mitigates the inherent violence of government prohibition.  Yes, I still have to worry about violence from the gang of people with badges and guns (cops) in some regard but the threat of getting robbed by a dealer is nearly non-existent.  The threat of being caught "red-handed" in the act of a drug deal is nearly non-existent.

2.  The community is wonderful.  We will never eliminate irresponsible drug users and crooked dealers, but through communication and sharing of knowledge through trip reports/experiences, safety & best practices, and vendor feedback we raise the overall responsibility level.  Very few people want to get brain damage or kill themselves through drug abuse.  Because of drug prohibition, we don't have (many) professional doctors, chemists, psychologists and scientific testing to study the benefits and detriments of many of these substances.  However, by cooperating, communicating, and supporting the community we can negate this harmful side-effect of prohibition.
