pbody88, I'm only going off the manufacturer's figures regarding the 30 days for cannabis. It does seem like an aweful long time, but 30 days would be a one off for someone in this world. Apparently, THC takes a long time for the body to break down compared with other illicit substances and remains in your system for longer periods of time. The initial swab test is only an indicator. If it shows a positive result, a second more thorough test is carried out in the bus with a professional, calibrated Drug testing machine capable of detecting a lot more. To top it off, your sample is then sent away for further lab testing to confirm the result. It's probably more like 15 days for your average cannabis user as apposed to 30 days IMO. Just to clarify, there's no level of intoxication you need to reach to be charged. All the test has to show is an indication of drugs in your system, unlike alcohol where there is a safe limit to stay below.  :)

Title: Re: Any TIPS on beating road side Drug Tests conducted by Police for most drugs.
Post by: Wadozo on January 12, 2013, 06:20 am
In my part of the world, Police use a saliva swab test where the driver slides the test along their tongue. A 5 minute wait is all it takes for the results to show. If that's a positive result, a second test with a much more sophisticated machine is done where the driver has a test "strip" which they place under their tongue, protruding out of their mouth while leaning their head forward (eyes looking down) as this helps with saliva production. Then for confirmation, the test strip is sent to a lab for further testing. A 3 stage process in total.
