Title: Re: Mushroom hunting!
Post by: Foxy on January 24, 2013, 09:20 am
Northern hemisphere? You are looking for semilancenata (spelling?) also often known as liberty caps. The mushies grow on the grass that has been fertilized by the dung, not on the dung itself... Dung growing magic mushrooms are tropical ones. To test if the mushroom is magic, pick it and bruise it (squish the end of the stem) and wait less than a minute and it should show signs of blueing. If it doesn't blue it isn't magic. You only need 1 dry gram (thats 10 grams wet) of this species of mushroom to trip hard and see visuals. Although they do grow best on fertilized ground, depending on the area, spores may have never arrived there to start growing. Magic mushrooms are most commonly found on university campus ground, or parks where lots of people's shoes have walked and trailed spores around. I also recommend you look for psilocibe cyanescens which grows off wood chips/mulch often found along pathways and gardens.
oh and as for the animals.. er.. cows. Most animals are afraid of humans and will leave you well enough alone. It doesn't take much to scare them away if they get too close for comfort. Try some loud noises/yelling/clapping and waving your arms around or holding your jacket up over your head to look bigger. As long as there is room in their fenced area to get away from you, they have will have no reason to charge you. As far as I know, cows aren't territorial and don't actually care about the color red >_>;
