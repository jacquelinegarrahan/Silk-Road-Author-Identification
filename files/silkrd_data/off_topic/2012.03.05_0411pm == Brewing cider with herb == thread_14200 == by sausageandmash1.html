Title: Re: Brewing cider with herb
Post by: wowzers on March 05, 2012, 05:30 pm
Focus on making good cider first, then look at ways of enhancing it. Would be a shame to waste good plant material in bad cider.
Having pressed cider apples, I can assure you you'd be wasting your buds if you tried co-pressing them. Unless your doing it on at least a 50kg scale you're better off juicing the apples in a kitchen juicer and fermenting out the juice.
From a taste point of view oil would be pretty horrible, soaking some tasty citrus smelling buds in neutral alcohol would make your cider taste delicious.

Sure there's no skill in stuffing herbs into vodka but the real skill comes in making something drinkable from your apples. It's at least as hard as making beer- when you make cider from apples, the fermentation comes from yeasts on the apple's skins/ the hay in the press. This also incorporates a lot of bacteria and fungi into the juice, if the fermentation doesn't start quick enough you end up with horrible off-notes in the taste or those little snotty bogies of bacteria which you find in rough farmhouse cider.  :-\
