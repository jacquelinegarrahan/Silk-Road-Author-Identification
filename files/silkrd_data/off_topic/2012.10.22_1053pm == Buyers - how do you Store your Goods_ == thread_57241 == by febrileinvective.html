Post by: Motherfucker.Jones on October 23, 2012, 05:42 pm
Cannabis is legal to grow and possess in my area, so I just grow my own outside in my backyard. I don't usually think much about stealth (i.e. hiding from parents, etc) since I'm an adult and can leave my shit out in the open. However, I do know a lot about long term storage.

Mylar bags with oxygen absorbers are the best for long-term storage because they block moisture and light. Second best would be glass canning jars or mason jars. They don't block light, but they do block moisture and are fairly durable (e.g. rodents can't eat through the bags and can't be poked with a needle).

I use jars myself mainly because their cheaper and I don't need to store my weed for 20+ years. Although thinking about it, having a few pounds stashed away in case SHTF wouldn't be a bad idea. You can always barter with weed or just smoke it when your down. You could probably even eat it if you really needed food.

Baby food jars work great in a pinch. Just make sure the lid's rubber is still good and it's able to hold a tight seal. Then store it in a cool dark place like you would with a good bottle of wine.  Plastic bottles work okay for short term storage, but they absorb odors and moisture so your weed won't last as long in them, but they're fine for short term storage.

IDK about using an RX bottle. I'm sure it's a great option for some, but I'm just thinking if I was in my son's room and I stumbled across an Rx bottle I'd immediately be suspicious. Their usually transparent enough to see through and if I happened to pick it up I'd know instantly it wasn't pills that was in it.

Now if my son was smart enough to get a baby food jar and have his girlfriend paint & decorate it and  just sat it on the shelf,  I'd never think to check that. Use some pipe cleaners, googlie eyes, glue and colored sand and you got one gay looking jar that's mighty stealthy. I know they also sell those soda can stash jars. My girlfriend's dad has one and its pretty realistic. Even the weight feels right.
