Post by: flicky42 on February 08, 2013, 04:51 am
Please don't go for the diet pill shit all that does is decrease your appetite which will hurt your metabolism if you aren't eating the right foods. The best rule I can give you is "if the diet isn't something you can do forever, its a shitty diet"

The truth to weight loss is simple (but complicated to do).

Calories in < Calories out. Running and sweating is your best friends and ensuring that you don't reward yourself or over eat.

To live a productive life you need to find a balance of vitamins and minerals that you take in without storing to much energy. I always recommend people to take multi vitamins everyday and fish oil. Also understand this:

Fat   9  kcal/gram
Ethanol (drinking alcohol)   7 kcal/gram
Proteins   4 kcal/gram
Carbohydrates     4  kcal/gram
Polyols (sugar alcohols, sweeteners)   10   2.4
Fiber   2 kcal/gram

As you can see fiber and proteins are your best friends. carbohydrates are ok as well as long as you aren't consuming carbs that contain high amounts of sugars.  If you monitor your caloric intake everyday while ensuring you get the right vitamins and minerals in your diet you WILL lose weight when combined with exercise. The only caution is to NOT starve yourself (like diet pills and meth do) because then you are signaling to your body that food is no longer abundant and we need to secrete more glycogen and store as much energy as we can. Then once you start going back to your normal eating habits you will maintain the state of energy conservation.

Post by: astor on February 08, 2013, 05:30 am
Low carb is very effective. There are a few dozen causes of hunger, but I'm convinced that a major cause is the drop in blood sugar. You eat a meal, your blood sugar spikes, a few hours later it drops and you get hungry. If your blood sugar never spikes, then it can't crash and you don't get (as) hungry. I can consume 1000 - 1500 calories a day without getting hungry on a low carb diet and quickly lose weight. I've found it to be very effective for short term weight loss, although hard to sustain for more than a few months.

So if  you need to lose 5 to 20 kilograms (10 to 50 pounds), try low carb. If you need to lose more, you will need a more sustainable long term option.
