Greetings fellow grower.  :)

I'm curious as to why you went with LEDs?  Other than in a very few (expensive) systems, led panels are mainly marketing bullshit, and will not perform as well as HPS or even CFLs.

You would be better served at this point by using cfls.  Even standard 20 watt household ones are fine for seedlings, provided you get them nice and close.  6500k (blue spec) are preferable, but even red spec CFLs still put out plenty of blue light.  A flowering spectrum led is completely lacking in blue, which is necessary for good veg growth, and will lead to stretchy weak plants when used in veg/seedling stage.

I usually meet a lot of resistance from LED users on forums when pointing out their shortcomings, but I only do so in the interests of helping people get the most out of their plants.  I have seen the grows that get passed around as "proof" that leds work, but I remain unconvinced.  The few  grows that aren't sponsored by panel manufacturers are generally done by expert growers who know how to get the most out of their plants, but when it comes to the average grower, these results are rarely, if ever replicated.

CFLs for veg (Or MH, if you are feeling brave) and HPS for flower is the way to go, if you want the best bang for your buck.

Post by: UKGrower on August 13, 2012, 01:49 am
Sorry if my last reply came across as rude, I didn't mean it as such. Your concerns about heat and flir are not rational though.  For a start, a watt is a watt, and it is all turned to heat eventually.  Leds don't generate any less heat, per watt, than any other form of lighting.  I recently made a post addressing this, check my post history if you are interested.

Flir is not something to be concerned about unless you are doing a huge grow, or doing something stupid like hanging bare bulbs on the ridge of an uninsulated roof.  Much is made of the dreaded eye in the sky on grow sites, but it is 99% propaganda, and the cops love to use it as a fear device when talking to journos etc.  In reality, most people get informed on, or fail to take basic precautions regarding odors.  Flir devices can only see surface temps of walls, they can't see through anything, so unless you are doing a full room grow, with enough wattage to heat up the walls, you are safe.

As for amperage draw, again it is a non-issue, unless you are drawing many KWs you will not be "flagged", as long as you pay your bills.
