Title: Re: Buyers holding vendors hostage!!
Post by: Rook on January 25, 2012, 10:23 pm
Feedback extortion is a very real problem here on the Road.  It happens to me probably 3 or 4 times a month.  Any vender who's had this happen to them can attest it is very unpleasant experience, far beyond the monetary consequences it may entail.  The feeling is akin to that of being violated.

However, I think sharetheroad has a point in that we venders carry a lot of responsibility being given buyer's addresses.  I also think that if venders were allowed retribution feedback it would change the dynamic of the Road for the worse.  I want buyers to feel free to leave honest feedback with out fear of receiving a vindictive blemish on their own record.

My proposed solution is to allow venders to be able to see the feedback left by a buyer for other venders in previous transactions in the same way that a vender is given access to a buyer's stats once a buyer places an order.

This would lend a little accountability to the feedback system and reign in asshole buyers.  If I see a buyer has a history of poor feedback for otherwise well-ranked venders, then I am given the opportunity to avoid a potential trouble-maker.  That is really all any vender should need:  the opportunity to avoid the asshole here on SR.

Once buyers realize that their feedbacks can effect their future access, they are less likely to be dicks.   This way a buyer can only pull a feedback extortion attack once or twice before other venders catch on.
