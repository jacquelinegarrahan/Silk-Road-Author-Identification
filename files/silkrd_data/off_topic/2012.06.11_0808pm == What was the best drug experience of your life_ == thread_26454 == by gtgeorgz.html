A light trip that made me feel so in love with the world, so unconditionally blissful, mindful- that I wanted to stay that way forever. I wanted to go out and ease people sufferings, to paint, make music, explore the mysteries of the universe - with the most childlike curiosity. Out of more than a thousand drug experiences, if I had to keep only one, that would be it.

Under the influence, one can honestly believe things to be true, have a set of goals, ideas, priorities... but when the drug is no longer in effect, they can drastically revert back to normal. A life of being perpetually high on methamphetamine would likely not be a long and fulfilling one. So even though the labrat has tried every (relatively) popular hard drug, some of which produced euphoria beyond his wildest dreams, they were not the "best" experiences. Heroin comes second because it was a "lower" type of experience. It's definitely a giant, but after the experience, there was no desire to do much except sleep and get more heroin -  and I judge an experience on its effect on me as human being.       

I believe that this LSD trip fundamentally changed me. Despite all that I've gone through since that time, I always remember that one piece of sunshine I was gifted - allowing for a more mature, positive perspective on life's shittiest moments.
