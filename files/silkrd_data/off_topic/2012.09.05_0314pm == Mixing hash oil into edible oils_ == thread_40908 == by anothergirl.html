Title: Re: Mixing hash oil into edible oils?
Post by: Ballzinator on September 05, 2012, 04:02 pm
It won't work. Most of the THC in the plant matter isn't actually THC but Tetrahydrocannabinolic acid (THCA), which is active but not psychoactive. It does have antiinflammatory and neuroprotective effects but won't get you high.
However, THCA is unstable will decarboxylate into THC over time. The decarboxylation process takes place rapidly at temperatures of 106°C or higher. If you don't have the equipment to heat your oil to just above 106°C reliably and carefully (some cannabinoids begin to evaporate at about 180°C IIRC), you can heat the oil by mixing it with butter or any kind of oil, putting the mixture into a container made of metal or heat resistant glass and putting the container into boiling water. The temperature will only reach close to 100°C but that's enough to speed up the decarboxylation process. Use a thermometer to check the temperature of your oil mixture and once it has reached close to 100°C, maintain the temperature for 20-30 minutes and the decarboxylation should be complete. You can use the mixture to make brownies or muffins or almost any kind of food. Just don't heat it over 180°C or you'll lose cannabinoids.
