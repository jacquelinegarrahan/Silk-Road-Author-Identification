Post by: microRNA on April 07, 2012, 11:52 pm
Your question is not that clear, are you looking for a drug that will get you high but not stress your brain out too much?

I think your concern is with all the excessive dopamine and serotonin release you will be effecting the levels of the receptors and other down regulation... which has been shown and much of the discussion of neurotoxicity is related to these lasting cellular changes

All drugs work by affecting on of these systems, and anything that increases the level of a neurotransmitter can be subject to mechanisms countering the effect... so the key is just to use them in moderation. if you take a potent drug, but then give your body time to process and return to normal before consuming more, then you will not quickly cause these changes in significant amounts. Raising the levels of , norep, or dopamine and receptor changes are not inherently bad (this is what anti-depressants do) but meth can deplete the stores which causes actual damage to the cell...

Any and all drugs that make you feel good are going to target these serotonergic or dopaminergic systems, even if only downstream... but they will basically all lead to tolerance, but this does not indicate necessarily "toxicity." For a recommendation for drug, it really depends on what effects you want... if you want a stimulant, ritalin which is a dopamine re-uptake transporter inhibitor will cause similar effects as cocaine, which is also a DAT inhibitor. Amphetamine could be used sparingly without too much impact. I think meth has more of an effect on the SERT transport, as does MDMA which cause the increase in serotonin, than amphetamine but I am not completely sure. If you want to use a psychedelic, these actually generally work on the receptors directly, instead of dumping vesicular contents into the cleft through reversal of the transporters... and and some of the least toxic drugs you can take. They will dramatically interfere with the normal firing of the neurons, which patterns can become more likely and produce HPPD after heavy use.

Even benzos will raise levels of dopamine, by impacting the gabanergic sites on inhibitory neurons, causing disinhibition and the release of dopamine and serotonin downstream producing the pleasurable effects some experience, but these certainly are not directly as stressful or cause as large of a change.
