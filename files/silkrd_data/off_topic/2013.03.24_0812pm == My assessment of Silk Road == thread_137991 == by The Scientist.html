I've suspected ever since ''Silk Road'' emerged that a lot of this product is originating in hostile and semi-hostile territories, or at least states where the authorities have an interest in not cooperating with the United States - the PRC, North Korea, Iran, Syria, Turkey, Russia, etc. This system doesn't really make sense otherwise.

In the early (mid-1990s) days of the internet, government would handle the purchase of anabolic steroids and other banned substances as follows: they'd aggressively prosecute domestic sources but largely ignore overseas providers of contraband; so long as the purchaser was not importing comparatively large amounts of the product in question. The reasoning being, its a waste of man-hours to simply prosecute people for buying Schedule III drugs when there is no way of indicting the source.

That doesn't mean government isn't indicting people who purchase drugs on Silk Road - but it seems like something of a losing proposition if the governments in the states where the contraband originates are unwilling to prosecute in concert with US authorities.
