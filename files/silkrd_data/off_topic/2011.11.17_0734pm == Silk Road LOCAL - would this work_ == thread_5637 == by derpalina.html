Title: Silk Road LOCAL - would this work?
Post by: derpalina on November 17, 2011, 07:34 pm
Last night I was hanging out with a friend and was like "derp, yeah I just ordered some drugs online" -- obviously he wanted to know HOW, but trying to explain TOR/Bitcoin/MtGox/Silk Road/GPG to a stoner is... difficult.

Introducing Silk Road LOCAL:

1) Customer sends email to chicagoweed@tormail.net - "I need an eighth"
2) Customer gets response "Deposit $50 at Wall Fargo into account GetBitcoin LLC / 123456789 then email me the receipt"
3) A couple hours after customer emails the deposit receipt they get a reply "Your dank has been delivered under the bench at the corner of 5th & Acorn"

What are problems with this? Obviously you would need a local distributor, but I think the two main innovations:
1) not requiring customers to use TOR - it seems like a fake yahoo email sent from the local coffee shop is a sufficient substitute for TOR-- the paranoid could always use tormail as well.
2) dead-drop delivery. the consumer would have to put a certain level of trust in the supplier since they have to make a pickup.

Thoughts?
Title: Re: Silk Road LOCAL - would this work?
Post by: Sobchak on November 17, 2011, 08:16 pm
Seems like it'd be a bad idea. How easy would it be for LE to setup a sting with a fake bank account or wait to pick you up at the drop off? At least with mail shipments, you can deny ever ordering anything. But, if you're at a bank caught on camera making a deposit into that fake account, I think it'll be hard to refute your intentions. SR is meant to keep anonymous and making local deals kind of defeats the purpose IMO.

EDIT: I probably misunderstood about the bank account thing, assuming that it was the seller's. But, even if it wasn't, it's still not a great idea emailing a timestamped deposit receipt to LE.
