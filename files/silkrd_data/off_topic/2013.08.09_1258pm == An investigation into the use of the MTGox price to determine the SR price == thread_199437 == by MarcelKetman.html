Post by: DrMDA on August 12, 2013, 07:05 pm
Thank god! Finally someone said something. It's stupid to list an item for $70 when in reality it means $60. You can say just raise the price to make up the difference but then it looks like us vendors are charging a lot. I wish the price would reflect the reality of the situation and not use a completely irrelevant exchange such as MtGox for pricing. Perhaps there are technical reasons why our prices are pegged to MtGox but if it can be done I think it would be much more fair to peg to BitStamp.

Lets take an example. If I charge $1,000 for an ounce of MDA I'm paying a %4 hedging fee, like %5 in commissions, a %2 mixing fee, %1 fee in transaction costs sending it to wallets, a %6 cashout fee, and I also lose %10 for it being listed in MtGox price when BitStamp is the true value of a BTC. So all in all thats freakin %28!!!!!! Then my free shipping costs $10. So in the end out of a $1,000 ounce I see $700 and that does not even start to take into account my costs. This internet thing is great for everyone's safety but people need to know they are paying a %30 premium for it and I think SR should be honest about it at least by pegging the BTC to a relevant exchange. The alternative is to raise prices and look greedier than we really are.


EDIT: I guess SR did address this issue a few days ago and I did not notice it. Now instead of MtGox they peg it to a variety of weighted exchanges. I think this is great.
