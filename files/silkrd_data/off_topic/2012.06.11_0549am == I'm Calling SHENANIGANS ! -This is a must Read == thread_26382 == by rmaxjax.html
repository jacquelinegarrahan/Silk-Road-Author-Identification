Post by: UKMJ on June 11, 2012, 09:12 am
SR is a free market, that is sellers are free to sell their goods at a price determined by them. Buyers are free to buy from any seller at their listed price, they may choose to do business with any number of sellers based on their own choices. The street is not a free market, you do not have chouce between sellers without significant travel between these sellers, you do not have information on the price, quantity or quality of their product before you get there, if you decide not to do business then you ate out your travel tome and costs.

Prices on SR will fall only when new sellers come to the market to compete with the established vendors. However the new vendors are attracted by the high profit margins available at current prices so will not be in a rush to lower them significantly and usuall only do so as introductory offers.

SR works just fine as it is, the premium prices reflect product quality and ease of access.
