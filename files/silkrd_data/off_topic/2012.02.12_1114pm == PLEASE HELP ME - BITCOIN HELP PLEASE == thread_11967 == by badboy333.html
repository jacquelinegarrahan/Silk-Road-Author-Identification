Getting Bitcoins is indeed the biggest pain in the ass of this whole process.  The crazy hoops you have to jump through were driving me nuts until I thought about the bigger picture. The whole point of SR using Bitcoins is that they're anonymous and virtually untraceable, so buying them means you're laundering your money.

If you think about it that way, as a form of money laundering, _of course_ it's a pain in the ass with lots of hoops.  If it wasn't, it'd be easier to connect you with the money you're spending.  It also makes sense from that perspective why PayPal and the credit card companies would want nothing to do with it.  That said...  It's still too confusing.

Here are the steps that I went through to get ahold of Bitcoins when I started buying things in earnest a few weeks ago.  If you're in the US I think this is likely either the simplest mainstream course or close to it.  People in other countries may have other options.

* If you're not in a hurry, sign up for a login at http://dwolla.com -- they're a little bitty PayPal competitor, with few features but a nice clean interface.  Add and verify your bank account (this might take a day or two) and then transfer money to it from your bank (this will take several business days).

* If you ARE in a hurry, sign up at http://paxum.com instead.  They're a lot more confusing, and you have to submit crazy amounts of documentation (which may take overnight to verify), but once you get all their crap in order you can add funds directly from your credit or debit card, and not wait several days for the bank transfer every time.

(NOTE: I went the Paxum route, and the first time I did the credit card thing I got a call from the bank about a "suspicious charge."  I told them I'd authorized it and haven't had a problem since then.)

* Go to http://mtgox.com and sign up for an account.  This is the big daddy of Bitcoin markets. (Make sure you're using your regular Web browser! Mt. Gox will shut people down for using Tor, due to some past problems with hacking.)

* Once you're set up on Mt. Gox, click "Funding Options" and transfer dollars straight into it from your Dwolla or Paxum account. READ THE INSTRUCTIONS about what to put in the message text!  It may take an hour or so for them to notice the transfer, but eventually you'll see a positive balance in US dollars.

* Still on Mt. Gox, make a trade for however many dollars' worth of Bitcoins you can buy at the current market rate.  Just go with the default price per coin, and it should email you in a few minutes that the trade is complete.

* Now we finally get to Silk Road.  Sign up for a Silk Road login (if you didn't already) and click on the "account" link at the top. There'll be a line for "To deposit Bitcoins, send them to this address:" followed by a huge string of gibberish.  Copy that gibberish to your clipboard.

* Back on Mt. Gox: click "Funding Options" again, then the "Withdraw Funds" tab.  Put in the number of Bitcoins you just bought, and then paste in the long Bitcoin address from Silk Road. Click "Confirm" and then go have lunch.

(NOTE: If you want to be more cautious-slash-paranoid, you can download the Bitcoin software client at http://bitcoin.org and send the Bitcoins to yourself first, *then* send them to Silk Road.  This is more complicated and not strictly necessary, but it does add more misdirection if any Powers That Be try to figure out how much money is going from Mt. Gox to Silk Road.)

* Some period of time later (maybe a few minutes, maybe a few hours), your Silk Road account will get the Bitcoins you sent to it.  You win!  Buy drugs, get high.

Whew.  Questions?

Oh no, you wasted a couple of hours?!? Poor thing!  :P. I found this place 3 months ago, and I wasted probably dozens of hours over the course of two weeks learning all this crap. Here's something I wrote in an earlier thread that I believe will help you.

The biggest hurdle to buying drugs on here is getting bitcoins. Here's the easiest, most straightforward way. Make an mtgox.com account. Mtgox is the biggest trader of bitcoins. After your account there is made, go to bitinstant.com, which is an official partner of mtgox. Use the payment method "Cash Bank Deposit." There will be a 5% fee, but it's worth it. You'll go through the steps, and it'll have you pick a bank near you and print out a bank deposit slip. Take that deposit slip to that bank, with the money, and after that transaction is over the money will instantly go into your mtgox account.

After the money is in your mtgox account, then you'll go to the "trade" section of mtgox, and convert your USD into bitcoins. Then after you have bitcoins into your mtgox account, go to "funding options" on the mtgox page, and select withdraw bitcoins. Then send your bitcoins to www.instawallet.orgâ€‹. The coins will show up immediately on instawallet, but you'll have to wait about 30 mins before you can send them. Then you'll go to the Silkroad page, click on the "account" button on the top right, and get the address that you need to receive bitcoins. You'll then send the coins from instawallet to your Silkroad address.

There it is. It seems complicated, but I swear it's easy as hell. For your first few items, just buy cheap things, like a $50 items. This is just so that you get the hang of everything and build feedback and reputation for yourself. Just take and learn everything one step at a time. Here's the steps to buying something off of Silkroad,

1. Get bitcoins to your Silkroad account.

2. Find an item you'll want to buy. Make sure it's from a good, reliable vendor. If the vendor is ranked within the top 20, expect that package to take a long time since they get a huge influx of orders. In my experience, it's best to buy from someone with above 100 successful transactions, but not someone who gets 25 orders a day.

3. After finding the item you want, read the vendor's profile page. Does he require you to use PGP encryption to send your address to him? If so, the next step will be learning how to encrypt a message with PGP. That can be tricky, but feel free to post a thread here if you're having any trouble learning how to do that. Also, make sure to use your REAL NAME and a real address. A P.O Box is fine. If the seller sends you a tracking number, DO NOT check that tracking number on TOR. Most sellers don't send tracking numbers.

4. Since you're rather new, the seller may send you a message asking you to "finalize early." The "finalize" button will but found under the "orders" tab on Silkroad's main page. It's normal for a seller to ask a brand new buyer to finalize early, but you are at a slight risk. That's why you don't want to jump in balls deep with $500 dollar orders. Keep it small at first while you get a hang of things and build a rep.

5. Here's the hardest part of the whole thing.. waiting for your package to arrive. It will consume your mind, your soul, your very essence. You'll be anxious and excited 24/7 until you get the package. When it arrives, it's the hugest rush ever, it's probably a better high than the drug you're even getting. I think I'm addicted to just receiving orders at this point.
