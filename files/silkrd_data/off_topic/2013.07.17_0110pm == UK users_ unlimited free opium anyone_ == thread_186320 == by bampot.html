 Not sure about planting on verges etc... would be very difficult to tap them in public.  OTOH good if you are just doing it for tea (which is about 1000x less hassle generally, and pretty much just as good, given that the opium you do make will be poor quality, raw, and you probably don't have access to proper smoking equipment...)

Precise location... in the link, it is the field with the rectangular brown patch in it, Mongewell Wood to the North, Bixmoor to the South.  The latter is a good discreet way in.

They are gone now.  But the same field will no doubt be used next year (it is the same one as in one of the Daily Mail articles I linked to above...the one with Didcot power station in the background).

Best time of year... well, poppies' cycle is the reverse of weed, in that they start flowering once the hours of light per day exceeds a certain level... usually beginning of June.  Ready for scoring around mid-July, and can continue to be scored till about mid August.  After that they can still be picked and dried for tea till they get harvested at the end of August.

I went back after they'd been dug up (still plenty stray pods lying around :-).  Surprised to see there were still helicopters.  Then realised there is an army/airforce base nearby.  So actually I was just being paranoid... they aren't being watched at all!  Crazy really: some entrepreneurial spirit could rent a tractor with front-loader and trailer, drive in there and dig up enough poppies to make many thousands of pounds worth of heroin....

By the way, I actually spoke to the land-owner... he drove past when I first came across them and was taking photos.  He was quite friendly....said he just rents the land to the government and they do everything.  So if you do decide to take a trip there next year, don't worry, you aren't taking anyone's livelihood (also there is a public footpath going across the land, so no need to worry about trespassing).

It's a very odd grey area, the whole opium poppy thing.  The DEA has a hard time treading the line between keeping it quiet that you can easily get hard drugs out of a legal garden plant, and stopping people actually doing so..,,good article about this here:

http://michaelpollan.com/articles-archive/opium-made-easy/
