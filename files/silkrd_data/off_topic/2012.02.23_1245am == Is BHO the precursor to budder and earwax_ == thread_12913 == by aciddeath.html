Title: Re: Is BHO the precursor to "budder" and "earwax"?
Post by: aciddeath on February 23, 2012, 03:40 am
grandaddyturtle!
You are correct isohash and BHO are completely different -- the primary different being the solvents used to extract the THC.
Again, 'isolater' hash refers to hash made using a "cold water extraction" method whereby all the non-soluable oils (THC!!) fall through what equates to different micron size silk screen.

I will have to disagree with you that BHO is liquid.
BHO is only liquified when the  temperature is raised. At room temperature BHO takes on a 'hard candy' texture that (when using high quality starting material) can be translucent/amber. If you squish a piece of good BHO in your fingers to warm it up you can turn it into a translucent flat piece

All of the budder videos/pictorials I have seen show the same process you describe, heating and whipping the concentrate until it turns into a more waxy consistency. I believe that is where the term earwax is derived from, looks like earwax

I read some article on hightimes about 'budderman' and his 'extremely secret highly researched recipe' but it mostly sounded like nonsense after reading through the pictorials/videos

Title: Re: Is BHO the precursor to "budder" and "earwax"?
Post by: madhash99 on February 23, 2012, 03:05 pm
Budder is whipped BHO. They make the oil, purge it accordingly, then whip it with a small tool under some heat so it remains a liquid. Basically just creating pockets of air in the oil to make it easier to handle and dab, plus it looks 10000000x tastier. Not more potent, but usually of higher quality.
