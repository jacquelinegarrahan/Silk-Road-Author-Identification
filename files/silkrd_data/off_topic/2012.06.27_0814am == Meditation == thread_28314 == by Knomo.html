Hi Knomo,

I meditate a lot of the time, but not every day. I try to do it most days, but wouldn't beat myself up if I missed it for a few days.

When you're starting out, you should get a guided meditation on youtube and just do what the person tells you to do. That is how I started and it worked well for me.
After a while doing that, I did a course on meditation, specifically mindfulness meditation. Mindfulness is all about being in the present moment. This is where I learned that it is OK if the mind drifts off for a while during meditation.

As for meditating on psychedelics; I did it for the first time on acid the other night at a festival. It would never have occurred to me to do it until I read about people doing it on here. All I can say is that it was unbelievable. I was sitting a bit away from a music stage, but could still hear the music. The beats of the music sounded so organic and every beat, I was going further into my mind; it was simply amazing. I'd highly recommend it.

Best of luck with the meditation,

Post by: afflictedlife on June 28, 2012, 04:54 am
I have been known to do this:
When a thought enters your mind, don't fight it. Instead acknowledge that your brain is thinking and let it pass. Focus your attention on the air traveling in and out of your body especially between the two front sinuses just above the eyes. If you can feel the air that is outside your body move as you inhale and exhale you are pretty focused. Do that till you no longer have to force yourself to do it. Do it till you are as aware of your entire body as you are of your lungs and airways. Once you are aware of yourself, let your consciousness drift. Let go of the focus and you will become similarly aware of with whatever is around you. Be mindful of everything but don't try and focus on it or filter it. Just like thoughts acknowledge what you are aware and let it pass naturally. If you find you can't help focusing intently on some detail or thought just resume focus on your being.
also,
Have you ever reached the point where you have stopped thinking but your brain still spits out meaningless strings of disconnected sounds and fragments like a sort of mental white noise? It honestly makes me feel something has corrupted my natural way of being somehow and is preventing a state of just being.
