Sorry for the late response..

Let's see... Well, League of Legends is a Multiplayer Online Battle Arena. There are a few different maps and 2 game styles to choose from, but basically you log in, click "Play" and you can either invite your friends onto your team or wait to be put into a team of players whose skill levels are close to yours. Once your team & your enemy team is put together, you begin Champion Selection. You then choose from your list of available Champions(There are always 10 Champions that you can play for free, 10 different Champions are free every week, but you can unlock more Champions by buying them with real money or using Influence Points which you earn by playing games.). You can play 3 vs 3 or 5 vs 5, but 5 vs 5 is the only one that really matters lol. On the 5 vs 5 map, there are 3 lanes where you have vision on your mini-map, and between the lanes is the Jungle, where neither team has vision(with the exception of allied champions, you can always see where your allies are) and several neutral monsters hang out who grant different enhancements when killed. In each lane are 3 Turrets(basically big, immobile objects that deal damage to enemies that come too close) that you have to defend. Each team has an equal amount of Turrets and Minions, your minions are just little guys that descend in small waves down each lane and toward the enemy team's base.You get experience from killing enemy minions(and some for just being near when minions are killed) and your level increases as your experience increases. It's a really fun strategy game, you earn gold per second, per minion kill & per Champion kill so you can buy items to enhance your Champion's abilities and natural statistics. It's not technically a roleplaying game, but a good team is usually made up of:

 1 Bruiser -
A Champion, melee or ranged, who deals a good amount of physical damage, but is also fairly hard to kill

 1 Ability Powered  Mage -
A ranged Champion who deals magic damage

1 Attack Damage Carry -
A ranged Champion who deals massive amounts of physical damage but lacks in defenses and it usually easier to kill than your Bruiser. It's called a "Carry" because your team should be helping you get more kills, thus making you more powerful and allowing you to then "carry" your team to victory.

1 Jungler -
A Champion who farms the neutral monsters from the Jungle and helps lanes when he can by ambushing enemy Champions.

1 Support -
There are different ways to play Support, most people choose either a champion with the ability to heal/restore mana or a champion with alot of health/armor/magic resist who can effectively defend your AD Carry. Champions with the ability to stun enemies are sometimes picked for support as well. Your main job is to keep your AD Carry from dying and place Sight Wards around the map which grant an aura of vision for a short amount of time so you can see where your enemies are and plan your strategy accordingly.

The goal is to attack and destroy the enemy team's turrets to get to their base, where the Nexus(just a large power source, deals no damage) is located. The team that destroys the enemy's Nexus wins the match.


What I PERSONALLY like about League of Legends.. I like the simplicity of the objective, not bashing games with detailed plots, quests, ect., but when I play games like that I want to keep playing and playing, while with League I can play a match, I know about how long it's going to last, then I'm done lol.. I dunno, kind of dumb probably but you know. I also like the amount of strategy and skill required to play, it's like even though you've got such a simple objective, you get better and better and you learn something new all the time. I'm really not a hardcore gamer, or even a gamer at all compared to some of you guys LOL but many hours of my life have been spent playing League..
