   Buyers are fickle creatures, but they're much more driven by the herd instinct then most are willing to admit, this being a radical libertarian environment and all. A lot of customers will want to buy off you if you're cheap and selling quality product, but would rather go with what they know than risk getting ripped off. Fair enough.
   Yes, jokes aside, CannabisConsumer is absolutely correct. Even though the majority of customers don't use pgp, they will be alarmed at a vendor who doesn't provide the option. You need to be a stealth & customer service oriented professional.
   A tried and true marketing tactic is dramatically dropping the price for a period, even to the extent of making a (small) loss. This can help you reach critical mass, a tipping point where enough customers now see you as there go to guy/girl that their repeated confidence in you reassures and attracts more and more customers.
   CannabisConsumer's appeal for patience and perseverance is fundamental. Most vendors peak, plateau, dip constantly. Establishing a loyal base should be an immediate aim. Treat good customers especially well, give them more attention, prioritise them. It doesn't need to be extra gear or discounts, just make them feel like you appreciate their business and the fact that they hand over their hard earned coin too you!
