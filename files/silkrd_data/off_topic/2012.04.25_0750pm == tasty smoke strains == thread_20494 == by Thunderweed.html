Like all weeds, when it is badly grown or badly cured, you can have a really bad weed from an incredible strain. And this work in the other direction.

The growing method and curing is what it makes a good or bad weed, finally not really the strain, you can have a very good skunk1 classic to a bad sour diesel.

I agree that even the best strains can be mediocre if grown by a bad grower, but I wouldn't say the same is necessarily true in reverse.  Some strains are just bad, weak, tasteless, or otherwise undesirable, and there is little the grower can do to make them better.  You can't take a plant beyond it's genetic potential. 

Even individual phenos of otherwise "good" strains can be somewhat shit, which is why it is generally a good idea to keep clones of everything you grow from seed, so the best phenos can be kept as mother plants for future grows.  Seed grows can always be a bit hit-and-miss in terms of quality of the end product.
