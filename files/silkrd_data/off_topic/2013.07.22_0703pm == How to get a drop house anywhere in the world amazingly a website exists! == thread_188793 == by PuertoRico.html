Title: Re: How to get a drop house anywhere in the world amazingly a website exists!
Post by: OzFreelancer on July 25, 2013, 03:22 am
Having been a couchsurfer for several years, I can guarantee you'll be just setting yourself up for failure with this plan.

For one thing, it's not generally that simple to get a host.  Most hosts are inundated with requests and only take on a tiny percentage of those.  Many hosts will only want people with a string of positive reviews from other hosts, as well as lots of photographs showing their potential guest's travels.  You'll usually need to do some emailing back and forth, and perhaps even a Skype hookup, to ensure you'll be a match.  Nearly all hosts have a finely-honed bullshit detector when accepting guests into their homes. A lot will ask for a passport or other ID when you arrive and many won't accept travellers from their home country.  And the usual deal is 2-3 days in one host's home, though this will often be extended if you get along.

And there's no way of knowing what the mail collection deal is at a particular house.  For example, my letterbox has a lock on it and I work from home, so will always be there to collect the mail.  My surfers go out and do traveller stuff during the day - it would be pretty weird for one to be hanging around waiting for the mail to be delivered.  And it would be highly unusual to receive mail for a surfer, who by their very nature are transient folk.

All in all, it seems like a lot of work for a VERY uncertain outcome ::)
