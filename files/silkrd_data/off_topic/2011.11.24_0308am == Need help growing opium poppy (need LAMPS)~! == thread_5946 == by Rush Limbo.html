Title: Re: Need help growing opium poppy (need LAMPS)~!
Post by: towelie on November 30, 2011, 04:48 am
  Lrp72, poppies are really really hardy suckers. 
If you live in an area where it gets dead cold in the winter just find yourself a spot with decent soil: nothing too rich or dead, rough the soil up a little, wait until last frost and then cast your seeds around your chosen area.  Water thoroughly to set the seeds in as there is no need to bury them. water occasionally but not too often as they are prone to root rot when they are babies.
    Once they start to pop up, thin to about 12" between plants.  Water occasionally and watch those beauties grow!
One thing to remember.  If you live in an area where it gets very hot in the summertime you will want to plant in the fall so they have a chance to mature in the warm spring.
Happy growing.
P.S. Danish flag and the giganteums are really good producers.  Stay away from any "peony" types and most that are a vivid purple as those seem to have very low alkaloid content.
