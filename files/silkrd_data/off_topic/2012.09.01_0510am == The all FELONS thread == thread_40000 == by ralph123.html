if it makes you feel any better, most "background checks" that take place when you apply for a job only go back 4 to 7 years.  your record will still be there if someone looks for it deeply enough, but consumer reporting agencies that are bound by the fcra (fair credit reporting act) don't usually have information older than that. 

also, typically if you have a full felony conviction, you probably won't have as easy of a time (if it's even possible) getting your record sealed, and you almost certainly won't be able to get it expunged.  however, if your situation resulted in a pre-trial intervention/diversion program, or if the state where you had your situation has something called "adjudication withheld" (it's known by different things in different states, and not all states have it - but the idea is that you end up with some sort of punishment, typically probation, but they don't *convict* you - which means that technically, you are not a convicted felon if you're ever asked that on a job or apartment application), then you have a much better chance at it.
