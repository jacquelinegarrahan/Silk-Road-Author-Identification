Quote
if i ordered a package and it never shows up, am i able to get the $ back/ will the escrow system really do anything other than make the faulty vendor wait an extra 10-15 days?
If it is still in escrow, then you can take it to resolution with SR and they will facilitate you and the vendor working something out.  If you agreed to any refund policy before ordering, you'll probably be subject to that.  I haven't had anything go to resolution, so I don't know more.

Quote
Obviously i'd never sign for a package, but what would happen if my shipment gets caught in the mail? I would obviously deny it but whats a good defense, i'd probably say i didnt order it, plied the 5th and find a lawyer, but does anyone have any legal advice, or could present me with the most likely scenario of what might happen?
If your package gets caught in the mail, there are three major possibilities.  Nothing might happen, which means it either got lost or stolen, or it wasn't important enough and they threw it out.  You might get a love letter, which is a note from the PO Inspectors/customs saying they confiscated some contraband and basically warning you that Big Brother is watching.

If you're ordering larger amounts, or they feel it is otherwise worth it to them, they can do a controlled delivery.  This is where a cop dressed as a PO employee tries to deliver the package (with contraband removed) with a signature for confirmation.  When you sign it, they pretend that this is an intentional acceptance of contraband, and execute the warrant they brought with them against your property.  So they'll come inside the house and search the place for more contraband.  If they find nothing, you're probably fine for the time being, but knowing people like us, they'll probably find a bunch of drugs and you'll go to jail.  Note that a controlled delivery is the least likely scenario.  From what I understand, it is usually reserved for those ordering large amounts, or those who have been caught a couple times already.

As far as what to say and do if you DID get a controlled delivery, I don't advise doing quite what you said above.  Just be a normal calm person who isn't expecting a package.  Don't shout, "Oh no you don't!  I know your tricks, muthafucka copper!" DON'T plead the fifth or request a lawyer at this point, it's just a "mailman" asking you to sign for a package, that would be super suspicious.  They can't do anything if you don't sign it, so just smile politely, say something like, "Thanks, but I'm not expecting any packages," then go inside and shut the door.  They have some resources invested in this already, so they will probably be more persistent than a real mailman, possibly trying to get you to not leave.  Remember, don't freak out!  You're just a normal person who didn't order any mail, and this mailman is being a real weirdo.  Get inside, away from the weirdo mailman, and don't talk anymore or listen to what he's saying to you.

Quote
other than that all i want to know is approximately how long it would generally take to receive a package in New York from California/ the west coast, from Canada to New york, and from The Netherlands/ UK to NY?

Shipping times vary.  I'd expect a week or less for domestic mail, 5-14 days for international.

Domestic is inherently safer since it avoids customs, but a shitty domestic package could easily get caught before a perfect international one.

Always stay anonymous when obtaining coins.  I use get-bitcoin.com often, I've also heard good things about bitinstant.com, which is a similar service.  I like GetBitcoin because I can anonymously deposit cash into a bank account (like BitInstant) and send it directly to a BTC wallet address of my choice (unlike BitInstant, where you have to use Mt. Gox, CryptoXChange, or some other similar service to actually buy the coins).  I understand that BitInstant is "faster," but GetBitcoin always takes <24 hours for me.  I think both places charge 5% fees.
