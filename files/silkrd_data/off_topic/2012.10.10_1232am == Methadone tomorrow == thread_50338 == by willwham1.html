its hard to say because everyone reacts differently, but i recommend you do a little reading on bup cause its a funny drug. not many others switch from agonist and increase their half-life in the same disproportional way

the thing is some people have really bad withdrawals from subs, but one reason is because they are taking ridiculous doses and with the half-life it lasts a long time. if you reduce the dosage, the half life is reduced significantly so the withdrawal will be shorter

some docs maintain on like 16 or even 32 mg which is crazy... if it was me i would try to cut back. since youve stabilized it should be relatively easy to get down to 4 mg a few days, then to 2 mg.  2 mg is around the best dose before the partial agonist properties kick in. below 2 it gets more difficult because it acts as a full agonist, so take your time and reduce down slowly, maybe to 1.5, then 1 mg... eventually to 1 mg every other day if you can. at this point some jump without issues but others need to reduce down to .5 and then even .25 mg every other day or so. the slower the taper the more painless. a few lucky people will even start to get mild enjoyable opiate effects at these doses

another thing about getting the dose down below 2 mg is the reduce half life means one can take subs and later that day or definitely the next theyre able to take a full agonist without the blocking effect. doses around 8 mg can require 2-3 days for some people before they are able to get results from their DOC. the reduced half life means you have to dose the sub more often though too
