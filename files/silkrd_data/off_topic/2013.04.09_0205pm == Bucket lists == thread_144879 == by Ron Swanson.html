Post by: surmer on April 09, 2013, 09:58 pm
Bucket Lists are tools for lazy people to get up and do something.

I live everyday like it will be my last. I look both ways at an intersection when driving through a green light. Yesterday, a full sized pickup truck ran a red light, in the middle of the light sequence and in between a pack of a dozen cars. It was travelling in excess of 45 mph into cross-traffic, of which I was a member, that was traveling 40 mph. I'll let you do the math on that.

I don't have a "bucket list". Instead, I think about "legacy". What will I leave behind? What have I accomplished in my life. I live this way, on edge, ready for the cops to take me out of society. (I'm a nonviolent offender, and always will be; I don't carry a gun, unless I'm going hunting or to the shooting range). I race to get everything done, so I have time to make sure what I do is right. I double check my work--most of the time. If it is important, I triple check it. Sometimes I don't leave the house because it's not clean enough. I don't make plans with friends or family, because work and perfection comes first. I don't use lazy type-speak on forums, because I believe that following rules doesn't end when we  become "anonymous". Everything I do has purpose, and if it doesn't, then I make it purposeful.

No, my friends, I don't have a bucket list. I may die tomorrow. Our society may go extinct form climate change, meteors, volcanoes, earthquakes, gamma ray bursts, viruses, superbugs, or some such event that we have yet to discover, experience, or understand.

With that said, I have been to all 7 continents. I speak more than one language. Ich verstehe und liebe Deutschland! Und Oestreich! Und Frankreich! Yo estudio y amo todos cosas. Genki desu ka? Se va? I have a love for all men and women, because we share a common ancestry, and if we stick together, we can share a common future. I am not a full-blown libertarian, because I still have too much love for unfortunate people. That is why I am concerned about my legacy, and not a trivial bucket list. It's about what I leave behind, not what I selfishly experience myself. Helping others is the greatest thing anyone can ever do in their life. I know, because much of my life was spent in my mind, by myself. I didn't start talking until I was five....

So let's ditch the bucket list, and think about how short life is, how fragile it is, and how quickly it ends if we don't leave things behind for the next generation... Or the next 7 generations.
