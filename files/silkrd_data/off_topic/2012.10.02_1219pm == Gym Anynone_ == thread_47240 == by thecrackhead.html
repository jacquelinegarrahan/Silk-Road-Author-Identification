Post by: Barbijuana on October 02, 2012, 06:30 pm
Diet and proper technique.

Don't waste your money on any pre-workout supplements and for fuck sake don't use any AAS (Steroids) until you are 1 year consistent and with proper form. They're only dangerous if you treat them with disrespect.

If you want to get big you gotta lift big fucking weights (Good form!). Don't be a pussy and do "toning" "high rep" shit. Compound and Olympic-style lifts that incorporate as much muscle fiber per movement will force your body to get big and they act as a synergy to your smaller muscles. Biceps are 1/16th the size of your back muscles but will develop tremendously during your pullups and bent over rows. Anyone that does a curl is just a jersey shore douche bag. Get in that gym, do your work and GTFO.

*Heavy Squats (Monday)
Bench Press (Monday)
Weighted Dips (Monday)
Push Press (Wed)
Power Cleans/Snatch (Wed)
Heavy Shrugs (Wed)
Weighted Pullups (Fri)
*Deadlifts (Fri)
Heavy Rows (Fri)

*I like to alternate Week 1 (Heavy Squat) / (5 x 5 Deadlift) then Week 2 (5 x 5 Squat) / (Heavy Deadlift)

That's it. If you see someone doing cable kickbacks laugh in their fucking face while you pick up 225lbs of solid metal above your head grunting like an oversexed ogre. If you are interested in functional strength training and tone though you should check out a Kettlebell and cross-fit routine.

Eat 5 (500cal) small meals a day consisting of a lot of egg whites, chicken, fish, turkey, pork, lean steaks, vegetables, nuts -- On your workout days slam a caffeine pill and eat an apple/orange 30 minutes before you hit the gym. You can also add in some good carbohydrates on your workout days like Oats, Brown Rice and Sweet Potatoes.

Stay away from the machines
Work up SLOWLY with perfect fucking form on all your free weight lifts -- If you have to bench press 2 cheese wheels before your feet stop flailing off the ground and your shoulder blades can stay planted then do it. You're going to look like a pussy for awhile and that is something that will humble and help you progress but just STICK WITH IT.

It take 10 weeks for the average human to develop a good habit. Keeping your body in shape and strong as potentially possible could save your life and it will make you feel tremendously better health and confidence wise.
