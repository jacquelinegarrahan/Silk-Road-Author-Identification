The past few nights I had done methylone alone and was having a good time as I usually did, although not being around people forced me to facebook to talk with a bunch of people at once which made me happy. But as time went on I noticed the empathy was going down and all that was left was stimulation. By the time I took the gram I was feeling nothing but really anxious stimulation, almost like stimulation from ephedrine.

But anyway - I was sitting at my computer and (being high for about 4 days straight now, with very little sleep, maybe 1 - 1.5 hours a night ) I noticed the shadows on my walls making patterns and slowly morphing into people. The shadows of chairs and wardrobes formed into distinct silhouettes of people I didn't know and I could hear them whispering. I didn't know what they were whispering about but I assumed it had something to do with me. I recognized this as a symptom of a stimulant induced psychotic episode, as I consider myself quite familiar with drugs and their effects ( although that didn't stop me from nearly OD'ing on 'em ) and tried to calm myself down but eventually I just became a babbling mess, trying to talk with these shadows in my room at 3 A.M..

I decided that I needed to lay down. I laid down on my bed, and tried to fall asleep, but every time I closed my eyes I was haunted by horrible visions. People I know and love dying, and the like. At one point a giant flying millipede with razorblades and other assorted weapons instead of arms flew inside of me but didn't hurt me. It just sorta phased through me.

At that point I realized I was probably either dying or having an out of body experience, so I focused on trying to astral project myself to different places. But I couldn't control where I was going.

I went to my dorm room in college and the place was dilapidated and covered in blood and my roommate was lying dead so I opened my eyes. When I closed them again it was as if I could see everything in my bedroom, without having my eyes open. Like a third-eye opening sort of deal. I even got up to test if it was right and I was surprised to find that everything was the exact place it was in my minds eye.

All in all, it was a very fearful experience, lasting probably 8 or 10 hours for the residual stimulation to depart in which time I could go to sleep. I notice now that I see a lot more floaters in my eyes, I don't know if that's because of the drugs or because I didn't notice all of them before. However, I don't regret the experience. I learned a lot about myself by examining myself as I thought I was going to die and all that. It was rewarding as well as terrifying. I don't think I did any long term damage to my system, but I don't know. Soon after that experience I began noticing tingling feelings in my legs, and went to the neurologist and figured out that I had restless leg syndrome. I'm diabetic, so this sorta makes sense, but I don't know whether the strain on my system from all the methylone exacerbated already existing neuropathy or created new neuropathy altogether.

It seemed to me like the only escape. Thankfully my mom eventually found me, but if no one found me I might easily have killed myself.  When you have experienced a trip as bad as the ones I have had, it doesn't strike you as absurd when you hear "urban legends" about people having committed suicide while tripping. Such stories seem very plausible to me. It should be noted that I also have a mental illness, so I don't blame LSD itself for it, but in my case, I blame the interaction between LSD, schizophrenia, and a very bad setting.

I know of several other cases. One is the man who jumped out of his window in the 1950s while under the influence of LSD. He was a victim of MKULTRA. He didn't know he had ingested LSD. He thought he was going insane. And this isn't an urban legend. It's well documented. Another case I have read of is a girl who committed suicide in the 70s or 80s under the influence of LSD. I don't remember who she was, but I remember seeing her father argue with Timothy Leary about it on TV. (I know of one more case, but it won't help you because it is anecdotal. It's someone I know personally.)

While I would never blame LSD for it, it is obvious that people with a pre-existing mental condition, or people who have been given LSD without their knowledge, could easily kill themselves under the influence of the drug. LSD can be dangerous in the wrong setting and psychological state.
