Title: Over 100,000 ecstacy pills found in the woods
Post by: Kevin Rudd on May 11, 2013, 10:22 am
More than 100,000 ecstasy pills found buried in woods in Ibiza were hidden by an alleged drugs gang smashed in the summer, police believe.

The designer drugs were found by a walker who noticed part of a plastic bag sticking-out of the ground in woods on the edge of Sant Josep, just south of the resort of San Antonio. Police found a total of 113,000 ecstasy pills weighing 37 kilos inside waterproof plastic bags.

Officers from the Civil Guard said yesterday that it was believed that the drugs had been hidden by members of a gang smashed on the holiday island in August in a series of swoops codenamed Operation Arrow.
article_mpuAdvertisement

The paramilitary police force, working in conjunction with SOCA, Britain's criminal intelligence agency, arrested a total of 20 people and seized 38,000 ecstasy pills, small amounts of other designer drugs and four kilos of cocaine, worth 168,000 pounds at street level.

Eighteen of the Britons, who came from Liverpool, Manchester, Newcastle and London, were either sent to jail or placed on bail after appearing before an investigating judge.

They were accused of selling drugs to young holidaymakers in San Antonio on the island's west coast.

The outfit consisting mainly of teenagers were caught with tens of thousands of pounds of drugs, two guns and all terrain vehicles during the sting.
