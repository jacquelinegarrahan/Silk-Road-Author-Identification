Post by: zapatista36 on September 15, 2012, 01:20 am
Japan is a fucking cool, albeit extremely different place to go/live.  I have friends who I have visited twice there - Tokyo and Hiroshima - and I loved it.  Culture shock?  Kinda.  But once you get over yourself and realize how crass the massive commercial culture is wherever you are, and hang out with folk who live there, then you get a real sense of the place.

Get lost on the Tokyo train system for a few hours, even a day, that's an experience, especially when you really don't have a clue how to communicate properly, and are hoping you know the number of the platform you're looking for, and how to say it correctly if there's no sign about.  I spent 3 months learning Hiragana before I went, only to discover that's like learning about a third of our alphabet and knowing how to make vowel sounds.  Then you get hit with Katakana and Kanji and realise you are fucked, and ask politely if the person speaks English.  My friends have lived there for years and are fluent verbally, but struggle to read the newspaper.

I love seafood and if you do to you will be spoiled for choice - had dudes in bars and restaurants trying to make me puke with various types of raw and bizarre-looking fish, but they were great.  Proper sushi and sashimi, fucking delicious.  But you can get decent pasta and pizza outlets, and burger joints all over the place if you ain't so adventurous.  But they have this 'delicacy' - can't remember what it was called - but it tasted like bins smell - wasn't a big fan of that.
