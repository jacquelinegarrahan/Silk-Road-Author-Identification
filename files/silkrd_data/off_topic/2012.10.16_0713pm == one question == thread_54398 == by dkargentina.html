Title: Re: one question
Post by: Ahoyhoy on October 16, 2012, 09:05 pm
If the package is intercepted in transit, opened and found to contain drugs, most postal services simply destroy the item. In other cases you may receive a warning letter from your postal service. It is very unlikely that you will get a knock at your door, but not entirely inconceivable.

I would avoid having the item addressed to you. I get the items sent to my home address but with someone else's name. If the worse comes to the worse, I can deny ownership of the item and claim it was addressed incorrectly.

It sounds like your strategy is a bit high risk. I wouldn't be running the risk of your mail being opened by anyone else. Most vendors package items very discretely, such that, even if an item was opened, it wouldn't be immediately obvious that it contained drugs, but not all vendors are as careful. Better safe than sorry.
