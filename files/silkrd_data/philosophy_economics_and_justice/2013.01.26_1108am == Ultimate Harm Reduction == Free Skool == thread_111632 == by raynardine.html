Quote from: Scampony on January 26, 2013, 11:09 pm
Obviously one form of censorship is not viewed like another.

Well, the solution to that is obviously 100% Peer to Peer transition and decentralization, and eventually ceasing to rely on centralized servers.

Basically, the reason for this is that centralized servers require significant amounts of money to pay for, and this encourages server administrators to be more punitive and dictatorial, because, hey, they're paying for it, it's their private property, you have no natural right to be here at all, so why should the admins treat you like you do?

In a pure 100% decentralized peer-to-peer design, such control is impossible by design. All objects such as forums and chatrooms within a pure 100% peer-to-peer network are abstracted from the underlying servers, if any, with multiple layers of cryptography and decentralization.

I think that for something like a pure anarchic free skool to work in reality, and really succeed, we need to cease relying on centralized servers for anything whatsoever.

Although I'm willing to host servers in the interim, the longer term vision is to collectively write programs and software that would make servers irrelevant and obsolete.

Silk Road would still exist, but not in the form it takes today.

Tor and Bitcoin would be unrecognizable in 20 years. Bitcoins will probably cost 500-1000 USD each in 20 years.

Technology marches on, but someone has to engineer it.
