Title: Re: How do we or more specifically you achieve happiness?
Post by: zookaa on June 19, 2013, 01:20 pm
Drugs may give you short-term happiness, but as you all know, they don't last forever...

Is it possible to achieve REAL happiness, without the use of drugs? Oh yes, it is... but it's a very long and hard path.
A universal, non-religious way to achieve real happiness is meditation. And if you meditate long (many years) and hard enough, you might even achieve enlightenment, no kidding, but only very very few people achieve this stage.

But even if you don't achieve enlightenment, meditation has sooo many benefits for your life quality. For example, you will learn how to not react to strong emotions like hate, anger, instead you will be able to observe this emotion objectively, without reacting to it like you normally would. Once you observe all your emotions objectively, then they won't be that strong anymore and they will fade away quickly... which means that YOU, the I in your body, will keep full control. But otherwise you will get driven by your own emotions, do stupid things that you will forever regret, because you simply reacted instead of observing and thinking first. You will never have control of your own body, you get driven by simple biochemical reactions in your brain.... but the real YOU (not your brain) is much more powerful than you think, you just have to train and sharpen your mind.

Same applies to the positive, beatiful emotions and moments that happen your life. Humans always seek pleasure, which causes craving, but want to avoid bad experiences at all cost, which causes aversion. Craving and aversion are very powerful, it controls most humans.
Once your mind is sharp enough, you will see all things with equanimity, the good and the bad things. They won't control YOU anymore, YOU stay in control.

So one of the ultimate goals of meditation is equanimity. Does that mean that people that meditate are "careless", or "indifferent"? Hell no! People that meditate can enjoy the beatiful moments in life much much better than normal people, because they know exactly how beatiful and rare such moments are, but they still stay equanimous, without craving for more and more pleasure. Same for the bad moments in life. You know they happen, and will always happen, and you know there is nothing you can do about it. You just have to stay equanimous.

All this leads to another wonderful thing.... compassion and love! Trust me, life is all about love, compassion and the unity of everything and everybody. Life = love

If anyone is interested in meditation, then I must tell you the truth... you might think that sitting and meditating for hours must be boring as hell? Well, YES it is, at first at least. It is painful, boring, and very hard. But once you understand the benefits, and once you learn to quiet down your chatty mind, it is just wonderful. 1 hour passes by like 10 minutes and you will really enjoy the deep meditation.

I can recommend anyone to visit a meditation course. I have visited a 10 day vipassana meditation course in India this year. This was the fucking HARDEST thing I have done in my life, but at the same time the MOST rewarding experience I have had! Vipassana courses are the hardest and alot of people give up after the first few days.

And let me tell you I'm in no way that much equanimous yet, I'm still at the beginning and I have a long way to go. I don't meditate every day, 1 hour in the morning and 1 hour in the evening, like I should, and I still smoke weed every day. But that's only because I came back from a wonderful India trip to all the shit here at home... my mood went instantly down to shit the moment I came back home. But even just those 10 days in the meditation camp have helped me to change my mind. I strongly believe in the technique and I will visit at least once a year a 10 day course and also meditate at least 2 hours a day. But first I have to put all my energy into all this shit I have here...

Posting this on a drug forum is quite contradict because drugs are a shortcut to temporary happiness but true happiness that lasts for your whole life can be achieved with hard work through mediation but most people that take drugs unfortunately won't have the discipline to go such a hard path.
