Post by: abitpeckish on May 27, 2013, 01:40 pm
A world without fear would be a world without Seals. Fear is the root of many issues, but the root of all issues is not singular. The brain works in a much more complex and interdependent manner than we commonly understand. There is fear, desire, belief, suffering, uncertainty, reward/punishment, retribution, compassion, and so on and so on. All of these things are manifested biologically and expressed by interdependent operations of the systems and territories of the brain. There is inter-regional communication going on in our brains –chemically, electrically, informationally– and out of these communications arise thoughts, emotions, voluntary and involuntary decisions, involuntary/voluntary actions. Our personalities operate as the direction of thoughts and emotions, but not the creation of it. These will come to us whether we want them or not, and we ultimately have zero control over the exact shape of our next thought.

In summary, there are myriad issues at the root of suffering. I posit that we must "simply" orient our awareness toward truth through the scientific, reasonable direction of our attention. Not only must we learn how to point ourselves toward truth, we must continually learn how to reliably identify it.

I tend to equate pain and fear, and lump them all into suffering. I refer to psychic pain, and non-physical fear. I've always had a mantra that goes something like, "I'll be successful to the extent that I am willing to experience suffering." I recently became a nurse (as a third career, later in my life).

My success in this endeavor was in direct proportion to my ability to stand the suffering of the process--the 20 hour days of classes and studying, the traumatizing clinical rotations, the quick action needed in emergent situations, the smells, fluids, blood, invasive procedures, death, the heartbreak.

At any point in time I could have quit, just walked out. Many mornings I would be on my way to clinical and I say, "I can turn around at anytime and just quit." That permission allowed me the strength to continue for the next few minutes, which turned into hour, then days, then finally graduation!

In all of this, I was never free of fear or "pain" I just carried on in spite of it.
