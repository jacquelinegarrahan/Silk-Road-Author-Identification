Title: God, the Universe, and Ideas on Reality with Science welcome!
Post by: Snoopish on January 07, 2013, 02:33 am
Seems pretty general so I hope this hasn't been covered too much previously. This forum area appeared during a bit of a hiatus for me and I haven't had much chance to look into it until recently but I find it fascinating chance to share ideas. 

Two Quick Rules:

1) Please discuss your opinions as either "ideas" or "opinions". A friend of mind said when he stopped thinking of his belief system as more of a series of ideas that he thought might be right it made it easier for him to examine other perspectives.

2) Please do not attack a person for their ideas or opinions. From my research, I don't think this thread will come up with any reality-destroying ideas about how our existence works. However, with enough open-minded and light-hearted discussion perhaps some of us will come away from this thread with some new ideas (or at least a rather long list of recommended readings).

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In short, I know there are some psychonauts on here who are believers in a higher structure (god, energy, fate, whatever you wish) while many laugh at such ridiculous notions.

I'd like to withhold any of my ideas for the time and ask if others have thoughts on is there a god? Why do you believe that? How was the universe formed (no big-bang please: yes it started where we are at now but there was the presence of matter before the bang so please, if mentioning this, address your idea of what brought about such a genesis).

How do you feel we experience reality? Are our experiences real? Our are lives a fine 0-100 year trip in this world and then it's over? Did we live before this as a different collection of atoms with a core, a "soul" if you will? How does reality exist?

Perception varies between people so do we all actually live in the same world?  (Ex: is the world I'm in the same as yours if I'm on 5g of mushrooms and you are sober? Or, mundanely, if I am having a shitty day and you are having a great day are our worlds still the same?)

I only have light knowledge of physics, religion, philosophy, and any other topics that may be discussed which is why I'd like to start some discussion. Don't feel the need to address everything or anything particular, just discuss your ideas.

****************************

I'll start off with this assertion: All religious doctrine (from the Bible to the Words of Siddhartha Gautama) should be respected for the wisdom they have to offer but, in themselves, should be read as scholarly works and not infallible documents of Truth.

That said, although I'm no longer as sold as I am about this next idea as I was years ago when I read into it, I want to throw one more idea of mine out there: Rene Descartes discussed the possibility of a god by saying we could not imagine something that did not exist (i.e. man developed an idea of something that is omnipotent/infinite despite never experiencing such a thing--therefore, a thing, or god, that has omnipotence must exist). I've read strong criticisms against Descartes but these are two lines of thought I hope might give you rest an idea of the kinds of things I'd like to see discussed.

One more: in physics, it's been discovered that light can behave as a particle or as a wave depending on whether or not it is observed. If this is  how light works, does the universe need an omniscient observer to influence light?


As I said, I'm a scholar in none of these areas but a dabbler in all. If I have stated anything poorly or innacurately, please bring that to my attention and I will make the appropriate corrections (and if you have readily available literature to back up corrections, all the better!) but let's not get bogged down in making reference pages for our ideas and more focus on interacting and question why we think what we do and how it all ties in to this world we are apart of...or are we apart of different, overlapping worlds? :P

Cheers and I hope this sparks some interest as I enjoy some good philosophical discussion,

Snoopish
