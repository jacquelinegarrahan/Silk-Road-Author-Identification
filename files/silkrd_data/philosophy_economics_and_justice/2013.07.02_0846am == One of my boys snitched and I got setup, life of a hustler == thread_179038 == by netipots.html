Title: Re: One of my boys snitched and I got setup, life of a hustler
Post by: one2bcurious on July 02, 2013, 07:30 pm
I've never been in your situation but have known some people that have and have seen differing results and it mostly comes down to the attorney and how the judge/prosecutor will perceive you and your impact on the community in the past and going forward.


Do your due diligence when it comes to obtaining an attorney.  If you know anyone that works in the legal system, ask them for referrals.  I would search really hard to find a pitbull that does criminal cases in that court district.  Make sure the lawyer has a working relationship with he judges and prosecutor in whatever place your case will be.  That can go a long way in getting a better deal.  If your attorney doesnt know the judge and prosecutor, he isnt going to be able to pull any favors.  A friend of mine should have done time but got off with probation because his attorney played golf regularly with the prosecutor and judge.  If you can find an attorney that is also a judge, that would be ideal.  Another friend basically got off because his lawyer was a judge in a small town on the side.

If you have character witnesses that can help you out like a pastor, someone that works with a charity, teacher, or anyone else that holds a respectable position like that, talk to them and see if they will testify or submit a letter to the judge and/or prosecutor on your behalf.  If you have ever done any charity work, get proof of it.

Make sure your taxes are up-to-date, cars properly registered, and all that kind of stuff.  They check that kind of stuff and not having your shit in order will be held against you.

Lastly, always dress appropriately for court and be courteous and polite.  I have seen way too many people end up with a far more severe sentence because they wore jeans and a t-shirt to court or they were just very arrogant and rude to the judge.  You probably didnt need to be told his but I see it everytime I go to court and it never goes well for that person so apparently this isnt common sense.  It is so easy to do this and could be the difference between prison and probation.
