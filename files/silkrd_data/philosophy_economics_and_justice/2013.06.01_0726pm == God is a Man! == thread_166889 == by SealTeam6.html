Post by: BlackIris on June 12, 2013, 08:41 am
Quote from: SealTeam6 on June 01, 2013, 07:26 pm
"The mighty God is a living man!"

This is a Bob Marley line and it got me thinking.  What if Mr. Marley is right?  What if God is right here on Earth living anonymously?

This is the concept of Eastern religions, and the concept of Crowley "*there* is no God but man". Many have interpreted the sentence erratically, however, because the emphasis is in the "THERE", meaning that in the ego there is only man, but inside of it, when the ego is no more, *there* is God.

In even less words: God is inside yourself, not outside of you. Your soul is what men calls "God". BTW even Christian saints as St. Loyola or Theresa D'Avila reached the same exact conclusions by their mystic states. They said: "don't look outside to search God, that's the wrong place; God is inside you".

Even Christ said exactly the same. He called himself the "Son of Man", and he explicitly said "How can you say, 'Show us the Father'? Don't you believe that I am in the Father, and that the Father is in me? The words I say to you are not just my own. Rather, it is the Father, living in me, who is doing his work." it is just that as always the church interpreted things as they wanted to. As an aside note the Christ is a gnostic figure, it has nothing to do with YHWH (and so the external concept of a God seated in a throne outside). The Gnostics believed YHWH to be an Archont, an enslaver of humanity.
