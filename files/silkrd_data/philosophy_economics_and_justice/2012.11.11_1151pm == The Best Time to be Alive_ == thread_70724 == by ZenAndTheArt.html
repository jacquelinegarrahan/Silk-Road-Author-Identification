That will always be a matter of personal preference.

From a technological standpoint 'today' or any time in the future is probably preferable. Today, we have a device in our pocket that allows us to communicate with the majority of people on the planet, regardless of where they are at that specific moment in time. This is something that was almost unthinkable only 3 decades ago.

If i look back that the last few decades, i'd probably still say the best mix of it all was around the year 2000. Before the 9/11 attacks that turned the privacy vs security debate, just before smoking bans hit public places, and when technology was not as good as it is now, but when it was functional enough to communicate with the world with a little bit of effort. Factoring everything in, i think the optimum point must have been the summer of 2000 or 2001.

I suppose one downside is that we did not have smartphones or decent mobile data networks at that time, but at least we had cellphones and high speed internet connections at home, and few people were living in fear of terrorism, second hand smoke or avian flu.
