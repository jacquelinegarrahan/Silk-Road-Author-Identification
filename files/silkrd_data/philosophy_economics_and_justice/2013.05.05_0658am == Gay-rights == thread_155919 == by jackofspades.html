Title: Re: Gay-rights
Post by: Visionary on May 09, 2013, 08:46 pm
Gay marriage has two main negative externalities: (1) on children, and (2) on the cultural institution of marriage.

Both of these can be empirically evaluated; neither necessarily undercuts the State endorsement of same-sex marriage.

Ronald Bailey, both as a libertarian and as a columnist for the libertarian magazine Reason, recently wrote (http://reason.com/archives/2013/04/05/the-science-on-same-sex-marriage) that, "A 2004 study of registered partnerships in Sweden reported that gay male couples were 50 percent more likely to divorce than were heterosexual couples. Lesbian couples were nearly three times more likely to divorce than were heterosexual couples." Although I have not reproduced evidence, it is (I hope) common knowledge that children from divorced parents have dramatically poorer life outcomes (e.g., more likely to commit crime, not attend college, etc.). Accompanying the rise of same-sex marriage would be the rise of same-sex parenting, with the attendant consequence of predictably-disproportionate divorce rates (ceteris paribus). This is a negative externality, imposed upon children (and thereafter the community-at-large). Moreover, if we want a society that possesses the cultural habits needed to sustain peace and free markets  (e.g., SR), then such a commercial society likely necessitates strong, family-focused households.

Likewise, Dan Savage (a well-known homosexual and columnist) has advocated (https://www.nytimes.com/2011/07/03/magazine/infidelity-will-keep-us-together.html?pagewanted=all) a "more flexible attitude" about marriage (as have others, like Andrew Sullivan), loosening the constraints of monogamous fidelity. To legitimize same-sex marriage and to then begin legitimizing nonmonogamous marriages can be problematic. Such arrangements are more likely to fail yet more likely to tempt experimentation by onlookers.

Perhaps these considerations are insufficient or can be rebutted by countervailing evidence, but the debate is not a mirage: same-sex marriage can have real effects, both on the spouses and others.

There is no need to advocate for being gay, black, jew, women, witch etc. You don't need that, this is not the problem. We need to understand why there are groups of people (minorities) who are scapegoated.

In a modern society everything is in a constant change, what was right thing to do yesterday is wrong today and is going to be punishable tomorrow. Since the rapid urbanization began (around 16th century) there have always been some sort of witch trials going on, it is because people don't accept fast change as a part of the way life is evolving. There must be somebody somewhere who is responsible for the unwanted change, finding a common enemy helps to strengthen in-group relationships.

It's all driven by FEAR, if you want to know why certain groups are hated just ask what are the reasons/traits they are hated for. In case of homosexuality main things people attribute to them are - they are non-reproductive, hedonistic, gender role-defying, spreading diseases. People who hate homosexuals attribute themselves the opposite characteristics. So it's not really about the homosexuals or whomever, if we want to make the change we need to address the fears of the haters and let them become aware and help them construct their identities on positive programs.
