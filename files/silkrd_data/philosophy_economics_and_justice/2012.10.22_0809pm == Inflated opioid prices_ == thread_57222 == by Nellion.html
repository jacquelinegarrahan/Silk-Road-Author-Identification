However, $48 for 2 hours of the second shittiest opioid buzz available (codeine being first) is FAR from worth it. Hydrocodone is very weak and doesn't last nearly long enough to pay $48 for a buzz.

Perhaps I'm nitpicking, but this is what drives people to injecting. Obviously, since people think hydrocodone is a gold mine, they assume oxycodone is a diamond mine, and oxymorphone worth even more. With the amount of money people are asking for these pills nowadays, it is only feasible to buy them if you plan to inject them. And, personally, I think injecting is one of the baddest monkeys out there and absolutely ruins oral consumption for people and is a fast rack to a downward spiral. Once you feel that rush, it's over, Swallowing pills doesn't completely scratch the itch for your any longer.

So, why do you guys think the price of opioids have inflated so much? And, is it justified, in your opinions? I understand that you are expected to pay a premium to cover convenience and shipping and whatnot. That's fair, I guess... But, the prices on the streets I roam are pretty similar.

You guys think it's due to overall inflation and devaluing of the currency? Think it's from people who ventured into injecting who could reasonably offer a few dollars more here and a few more there to ensure they get there dope, effectively pressuring more and more people into injecting to get the best bang for their buck? Think it's due to unscrupulous dealers, the overall increase of popularity of opioids?

Personally, I think it's a combination of both. Opioids are the PERFECT work drug. They give you a generally positive attitude about working, and personally, allow me to volunteer for a lot of overtime hours. I think the devalued and inflated currency and crappy economy is causing more and more people to turn to opioids so they can work harder and earn more money, and unscrupulous dealers are taking advantage of the increased demand and asking for more money for their product so that they foot the bill of their increased grocery bills due to inflation and the shitty economy. Then, to get more bang for their buck they turn to injecting which will cause them to pay more and more as their habit progresses. Then, once a dealer gets $10 more than usual for a set amount of dope, it feeds his greed and so he eventually raises the prices for everyone and the cycle resets and starts over.

However, whatever it is, I don't like it. I have had to seriously scale back my opioid consumption and I LOVE my opioids. But, I refuse to pay this inlfated ass prices to these greedy bastards. I just wish consumers would quit paying these ridiculous prices so dealers would lower them. But, I doubt that's going to happen because opioids are known to cause irrational behavior that manifests from the dependence on them.

And that's the bottom line, IMO- dealers charge more because they CAN. They know a lot people are hooked on opioids and will continue to pay for them regardless of price. There is no incentive to lower price because demand just keeps on growing.

Luckily, though, I've forged a pretty solid network of opiophiles who have learned to treat each other fairly as it's cheaper for everyone in the long run when everyone isn't trying to get top dollar from everyone else.
