Title: Buyer on cancelled sales can now leave feedback?! WHY!
Post by: Colonel Sanders on September 11, 2011, 10:49 pm
So, I just noticed, unless it is a glitch, something extremely disturbing.

I had a buyer last month who claimed non-receipt of shipment even though the Delivery Confirmation indicated it had been delivered.  It was a relatively small order and I did not want to deal with the risk of bad feedback by disputing it, so I simply canceled the order so that the buyer would get his Bitcoins back in full.  I suspect I was simply getting ripped off, but it just wasn't worth the headache and canceling the order was the only real way to protect myself from a scamming buyer who might have to stick to his story to get a refund.

NOW...  Now I see that I have feedback from this buyer for that transaction.  Thankfully, the guy left 5/5 because I refunded him, but WHAT THE FUCK, MAN?

Why on EARTH are we going to have feedback on canceled sales?  How is that possibly a good idea?  Feedback is for speed of shipment, product, transaction quality--if the transaction is canceled, THERE'S NOTHING TO RATE.

Not to mention, that it removes the one big protection sellers had against getting smeared by an unscrupulous buyer.

Make a special statistic that shows the amount of orders canceled by sellers if you must, but allowing buyers feedback on canceled orders is pure madness AND only serves to make feedback less meaningful.  Please reconsider this change.
