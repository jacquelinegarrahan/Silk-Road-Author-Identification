Quote from: danconia on March 12, 2013, 06:23 pm
While we're at it, can someone give me a dumbed down explanation of the difference between BTC and Litecoins?  I suppose it's always good to have a plan b but doesn't it just work on the same technology as BTC?  So if BTC were hacked then Litecoins would also be compromised?
If I understand it well enough, the biggest difference is that Litecoins can be efficiently mined using a typical personal computer, whereas mining for BTC really requires using a multi-grand mining rig at this point. The "difficulty" of mining LTC is much lower. So the idea is that the computational backbone of the two currencies will be different, with the LTC propped up by a large base of PC's and gaming machines, and the BTC supported by a much smaller number of miners running heavy duty processing capability rigs.

The TLDR version of the litecoin vs bitcoin differences:

1) There will be 3-4x the amount of LTC as BTC (dont feel like looking up the actual number)
2) LTC uses an algorithm for the mining that makes thing like FGPA and ASIC mining not feasible--hence "everyone" will have access to LTC until it has all been mined.
3) LTC takes more confirmations, but is supposed to be faster getting those. 

People shy away of the Silver vs. Gold argument, but its pretty relevant.
