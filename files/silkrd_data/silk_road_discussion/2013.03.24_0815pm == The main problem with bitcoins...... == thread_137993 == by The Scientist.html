So over half the world's population is excluded from using this freedom-promoting technology.

Title: Re: The main problem with bitcoins......
Post by: AfternoonDelight on March 25, 2013, 09:29 pm
The reason the coins dropped a couple years back so much is MTGox was hacked and coins were stolen enmasse.
There was another high-profile wallet theft last year.
These depressed the price the most, as the bitcoin speculators largely gave up.
The rest was a lack of confidence.
There will only be so many bitcoins mined, and they are currently mining with half the rewards they got before - AND the difficulty is constantly increasing.
So they can only really go up in the long term.

The other thing, is that the exchange is VERY fluid, so if one of our major vendors or DPR were to cash out and set their bitcoin prices cheap, it would depress the price of bitcoins across the board.
The sellers of coins have the price in their hands, if they want them to be something, they have to cash them out knowing they need to set a firm price, not much lower than the average, and can't dump them all at once.

Small amounts won't make a huge difference, but the folks with big stashes can really mess with it all.
If you're a buyer of coins, buy and spend as fast as you can to minimize risks of it going down.
