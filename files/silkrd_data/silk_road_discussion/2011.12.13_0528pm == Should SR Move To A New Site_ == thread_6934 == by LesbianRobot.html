Title: Re: Should SR Move To A New Site?
Post by: DrBenway on December 13, 2011, 07:29 pm
No disrespect, but this is a terrible idea for a number of reasons, which I will list.

1) Changing the address or otherwise trying to get away from the media interest in SR would be utterly ineffective. LE will have the new address, or an invite,  or whatever system you could implement without basically shutting down the site, within a day of any change at the very most. The way this site must work, if it is to work at all (and so far it has), is for LE and everyone else to know about it, but have no way of stopping it. I suggest you read up on the Tor hidden service protocol. There isn't a whole lot LE can do to compromise the site directly, especially if everything is properly configured. The biggest risk is compromise by social engineering, and that risk would be made greater by what you propose. See the next point.

2) Changing the address or anything else would create an opportunity for LE to hijack the site. They have done it before with carder forums. We have a nice address now, and after a scary several hours the community was able to regain confidence that the site had not been compromised, but those hours were scary for a reason, and going through that sort of thing again or worse would be a serious mistake, especially with so little to be gained.

3) Less overt boards have existed before. What makes SR different is the novel synthesis of technologies that allow it to operate in the open without being easily shut down. To make the site less open would be to give up what makes the site so great. SR is, among other things, a form of civil disobedience against a set of unjust laws, and civil disobedience must be public to be effective.

Don't get me wrong. We must all be vigilant and guard our anonymity carefully. If set up improperly, the site could be exposed. I am nearly always one of the first to point out potential security vulnerabilities in any new feature proposal. Sometimes the desire to do something about security is the enemy of real security, and I believe this is one of those times.
