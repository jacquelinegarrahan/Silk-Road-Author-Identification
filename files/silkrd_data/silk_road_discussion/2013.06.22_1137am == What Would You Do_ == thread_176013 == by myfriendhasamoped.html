Title: Re: What Would You Do?
Post by: CaptainSnowFlakes on June 24, 2013, 06:53 pm
The rules are the rules. If the order is placed in transit by the vendor, it must mean that they dropped it in the mail. If not, said vendor can get his privileges revoked. So no, it is not right that you have to give a vendor anything close to a 5/5 unless they can prove that it was dropped in the mail the day it was marked in transit. If a vendor describes a packages shipping as express or priority or snail mail, it must reflect that in the receipt of the package. That is, unless there is a problem that occurred when in transit. A vendor cannot tell you to do anything you do not want to do and if they are instructing you to do so, I would report that vendor. It is against the rules. Also, a vendor cannot blackball you or whatever. Even if he/she does there will be 100's of other vendors you can choose from. That would be the vendors loss. Not yours.

My advice would be to tread delicately on the situation. You have rights and if you read through the sellers rules you will be able to decipher whether a vendor is breaking the rules or not when they are dealing with you.

Good luck with everything.
