Title: Re: Quick Question from Noob (I have read the First Time thread to no avail)
Post by: ForTehLulz on January 05, 2013, 08:29 am
Yes, it means finalize early in the sense that when you make your purchase, if you were to finalize early, you'd be releasing your funds from escrow and the vendor would then obtain them. This is essentially losing protection from getting scammed, or gettinghelp if your order doesn't arrive, etc. It is strongly suggested that you not FE even though some vendors might insist on this.

Post by: Jopular on January 06, 2013, 02:17 pm
Quote from: DiamondHeart on January 06, 2013, 02:10 pm
What if they have excellent feedback and have been around a while?

Then it is a risk you have to be prepared to take.

The best vendors do not require FE.
If you have a zero transaction history though then even these good vendors might ask you to FE to make sure that you are capable of answering PM's and to get a feel for how serious a buyer you are.

I had to FE on my first couple of transactions but after this most vendors don't need it.
The odd occasion I have still had to FE as per the vendors policy on their info page. But in this case I still have the option of leaving bad feedback if the package is not up to scratch or does not come. (This has never happened yet though.)

If you have to FE just make sure the vendor has excellent 100% feedback and has been around for a bare minimum of 3 months. Research their name on the Rumour mill and make sure everything feels right first before ordering.
Make small orders 1st.
