Title: Re: Finalizing early..
Post by: SelfSovereignty on December 16, 2012, 08:32 pm
That means it's waiting for the vendor to mark it as "shipped."  Then it will say "in transit," or whatever, and allow you to finalize.

There's an exception: if the vendor is new, it won't let you finalize for I think a full 36 hours.  Not sure.  But be careful with finalizing early.  It's usually a very bad idea unless you've done a lot of research about the vendor here on the forum.  The feedback system is shit, never trust it -- it's a useful guide to know whether or not researching a vendor on the forum is worth the time or not though (if there's a bad feedback score, don't bother researching, just pass)

Add us to the list of vendors who support the anti-FE movement. We buy our bricks, we put tens of thousands of dollars of our own money on the line.

We did our research and learned that part of being a successful business is maintaining a rainy day fund to cover eventualities like postal strikes, major consumer fraud, and auto-finalizers,

New businesses like us feel the most pressure from the immediate lack of cash flow.  The overhead costs of starting up in our field are huge, so our lesson learned is to have the funds on hand after product purchase to cover about 1.5:1 reserves to purchases.

If your vendor can't carry your $200 purchase for the two weeks it takes for  you to receive, the fundamental foundation of their business should be questioned.

We trust, until you give us a reason not to.
