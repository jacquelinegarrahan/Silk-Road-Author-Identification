Title: Re: why does it take so long to get btc to sr?
Post by: frank-butcher24 on August 05, 2013, 07:59 pm
Quote from: Spiral Eyes on August 05, 2013, 12:09 pm
I've wondered about this too, actually. I send my btc from one wallet to another before sending off to SR. The non-SR transfers are always pretty much instant, while SR is generally hours. I'm certainly not complaining, but I was curious as to the reason behind it.

Sending from one wallet to another will rarely take more than 10 minutes, because after the first wallet broadcasts its transaction out to the network, the other will pick it up as soon as it's mined into a block. A new block is mined approximately every 10 minutes. This counts as one 'confirmation'.

However, SR waits for (I think) 5 more blocks, so that your transaction is confirmed 6 times over as valid before it allows your BTC to be seen in your SR wallet.

It's basically a way of protecting SR against the dreaded 'double spend' attack, which means you deliberately spend the same coins twice. I don't know how feasible it is to do a double-spend, but by the time the transaction has been confirmed several times, it becomes pretty much impossible to do.
