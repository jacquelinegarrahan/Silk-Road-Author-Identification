Title: Re: Looking to become a vender!
Post by: Silpheed on January 30, 2012, 09:40 am
Yes, Silk Road has a seller's guide:
http://silkroadvb5piz3r.onion/index.php/silkroad/sellers_guide

You'll need $150 worth of bitcoins to buy a vendor/seller account. If you're selling physical goods you'll also need a vacuum sealer, gloves, a scale, and printed labels. Here's a thread about what postal inspectors look for:
http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?topic=346.0

This comment list things that set off red flags for a package:
http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?topic=1687.msg14234#msg14234

You may also want to read some of this page, the Art of Smuggling (but it's currently down):
http://g7pz322wcy6jnn4r.onion/opensource/II/smuggle.html

Also look in the shipping forum:
http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?board=6.0

You may even want to buy something from a top vendor to see how they package things. However, NEVER USE A SELLER ACCOUNT TO BUY ANYTHING, and never start selling from an account that has been used to buy before.

It's also more convenient for buyers when vendors include the cost of shipping and SR fees in the listed price. You can read about the latest fees here:
http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?topic=8397.0

List your prices in USD (US dollars).

I also suggest you become familiar with PGP/GPG, as many people will want to encrypt their addresses before they will buy from you. Read this:
http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?topic=9441.msg86924#msg86924

And post your public key on your user profile on SR.

You may also want to read this helpful guide written by v01d:
http://4eiruntyxxbgfv7o.onion/paste/show.php?id=fcc21f55e8225274

If SR ever goes down, you'll probably want an email address at tormail.net or elsewhere so people can contact you. Another option is TorPM:
http://4eiruntyxxbgfv7o.onion/pm/

There are also other ways to communicate offsite like SILC, TorChat, XMPP, etc. Research those on the forums if you want.

You can check if SR is down here: (or maybe on these forums)
http://warweedoeg6lfyoz.onion/updown/

And if you take a picture of your product to upload, make sure to strip all metadata from the image first. (AFAIK this can be done as simply as taking a screenshot of the image and then cropping it and resaving it, or saving it as a BMP and then again as a JPG).

You'll probably want to start by only shipping packages within your home country. Don't send packages from your residence or at a counter inside a post office. Start small. Regarding what to put as the return address, look around the forums for suggestions.

Introduce yourself on the forums and be active there. Sending samples to established forum members is one way to establish positive feedback.

As for cashing out (turning bitcoin back into USD), there are BTC vendors on SR who will pay money for bitcoin (I think they will give you a moneypak code). However, they probably take a fairly large cut. You can also withdraw BTC to an exchange, place a sell order, and then withdraw USD from that exchange, say, via Dwolla, or however else you want to withdraw.
