Post by: BlueGiraffe on January 16, 2013, 09:30 pm
1.

Many theories - and many levels at which it can be described (pharmacological, neurological, psychic, spiritual etc). I prefer to keep it at the level of natural experiential language from a psycho-spiritual perspective.

Huxley put it very well in Doors of Perception (required reading for psychonauts IMHO). Essentially his thesis was that the drug (mescaline in his case) effectively reduced the functioning (or efficiency) of the aspect of the mind that acted as a "reducing valve" on consciousness, thus allowing a lot more of the psychic-energetic dimension of experience "in". In his understanding, most of that psychic-energetic content was not needed for basic survival (and indeed might actually compromise it), so the mind habitually narrowed it down or "filtered" it to produce the content that we take to be the world as we know it - or normally experience it.

And I would agree with that essential description. My further understanding is that we essentially ARE consciousness altogether, i.e. we are the totality of the context of all experience. We are that in which the very universe arises. That is our true nature. But the functioning of the ego-mind, or basic identity, creates the illusion and sense that we are each a separate someone with limited being and perception. Taking a drug like LSD essentially impairs the habitual functioning of the ego-mind, or put another way releases the contracted sense of separated identity, and in doing so, the truth of who we really are is known more directly. And this gives the sense of expanded consciousness. But it's actually just an expanded (and truer) view on our own nature.


2.

We actually experience loop thinking all the time, it's just that our attention is not normally on it, or is distracted by "external" content - i.e. events in the world outside of the mind. On acid, attention can move into very acute sensitivity to the processes of the mind (including its unconscious and semi-conscious activity), and because the psyche is open and in full view we can choose to "lock on" to something that would not otherwise be noticed. And as the mind can readily function as a feedback mechanism and "repeater" one can loop. The way out of the loop is always to simply move attention elsewhere and allow it to dwell in the new place for a while - preferably "outside" the mind - in the body, or the physical world.


3.

Your body will definitely die. And you will continue to be you as conscious awareness and, in time, move onto other destinies that are in resonance with your deeper psychic self. But not a recommended way to leave the planet :)

Title: Re: Questions For LSD Gurus
Post by: awhiteknight on January 17, 2013, 12:52 am
I'm a skeptic when it comes to the magical properties of LSD.

The 50+ trips I've had have showed me that my mind is made of meat, visual perception is as organic as branches of a tree and that perception is fractal in structure. The brain is made of subsystems, each has distinct properties and when hit with LSD or similar psychoactive drugs, you see them differently and learn a bit about how they actually function. It feels like something to be brain meat and it feels like something else to be brain meat on acid, the difference is partly what you're made of; you might catch sight of Plato's cave amongst the twisted shadows now that you're looking from a different angle.

I like being brain meat on acid. I wouldn't say that my consciousness is expanded though, it's more like I've been shoved into a twisted fractal hall of mirrors made of self-reflective tree bark and emotional instability to laugh and cry at the shapes of my own mind. We know that even epiphanies are emotional and drug-induced ones are often as fake as their real-world counterparts... "the smell of petroleum pervades throughout", so taking anything else from such an experience would be foolish.
