The Silk road is home to the majority of the TOR network's users, and since its a marketplace, and not a singular entity, its content will be superior until another marketplace can acquire an equal or greater number of buyers and sellers and bring them together in a similar manner, which may require almost doubling the number of active TOR users.

Mathematically, another marketplace would be highly unlikely to thrive. The barrier for entry is $500 worth of bitcoin, and maybe a few other things depending on what someone would intend to sell, for the silk road. That is a fucking pittance to enough people who want to sell, that we have the variety of goods that we do currently have.


There are a variety of intricate and tinfoil hat worthy ways in which the silk road could be depleted of its users, but I find them all highly unlikely.


A note to add... The Silk Road is one of the few "old" institutions of the TOR network to still be around, and not exist as a laughingstock like hackBB. We have tryed and tested mechanisms to deal with attacks, which newer onions may not have.
