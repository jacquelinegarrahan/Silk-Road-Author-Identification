Title: Re: If you MUST FE, do us all a favor....
Post by: Wazup7 on September 06, 2012, 12:12 am
Personally, if I *have to* finalize early, I don't leave feedback until I get the product.  This way, a.) The vendor doesn't get an additional "FE will update" on their page, and b.) It forces me to remember to update the feedback once I get the item, since the feedback box is still on my Orders page.  If I leave the "FE will update", then the only way I can update is if I go to my account, then view the feedbacks, then find the one I need, and click edit...too much work! 

Plus, how can I possibly know what rating I should give for the order?  All I did was place the order, the vendor marked it In Transit, and I released my bitcoins--does that really entitle the vendor to an immediate 5/5?  Be smart--don't leave "feedback" until their is something worth commenting on...like receiving your order!

That being said, try not to FE ever, unless you like taking risks.  Even the most established and reputable vendors can turn and walk away with your money.  Thats the risk.  If you stay in escrow, there is almost no risk whatsoever.

Title: Re: If you MUST FE, do us all a favor....
Post by: Wazup7 on September 06, 2012, 03:29 am
Quote from: zero effect on September 06, 2012, 03:04 am
You can cancel out of the order without leaving feedback after FE'ing. I think there should just be an option to leave feedback later if you do FE. Making new threads weekly about the issue does nothing to help the situation.

Correct.  When you click finalize, the funds are released from escrow, and you are redirected to your orders page.  Your orders page shows any current orders that are still In Transit, as well as any orders that were Finalized, but have not yet had feedback left.  In theory, you could never leave any feedback for any order, and your orders page will always show you the feedback boxes for all of your finalized orders.  The point is--finalizing is one step, and leaving feedback is another seperate step, because you aren't forced to leave feedback when you might not have even tried the product yet (nor should you be).

As the forum slides, this issue gets harder and harder for noobs to find, which is why it seems to pop up so often.  I agree that it feels like beating a dead horse, but apparently there are a ton of people who seem to think that they are absolutely required to leave feedback as soon as they finalize, or they die. 

I think a good solution would be to show a message on the orders page:  "MAKE SURE YOUR FEEDBACK IS MEANINGFUL.  YOU HAVE "X" DAYS TO COMPLETE IT.  YOU DON'T NEED TO COMPLETE IT NOW"  Or make the feedbacks easier to manage, integrate the feedbacks more tightly into the orders page.  Perhaps keep a months worth of orders on the orders page, and keep the feedbacks right on that page with each order.  Again, I think part of the reason people don't update their "FE will update" feedbacks is that once they leave the first feedback, it essentially disappears.  It's not very intuitive how to get to the feedback history page, and it takes more clicks than it should, which sucks in Tor-land.
