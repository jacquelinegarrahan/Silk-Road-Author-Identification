Post by: Rook on October 23, 2011, 12:25 am
Quote from: amush on October 22, 2011, 02:38 pm
Its not wise to use your real name.

Please do not listen to this.  Ordering a package to an address with a name that is not registered there is the number 1 cause of non-deliveries.  It really doesn't give you any added plausible deniability either.

Think about it. What are you going to say if the cops show up at your door, "Not mine", right? "Someone must have ordered it to my house hoping to snatch it before I got to it", right?  It actually hurts your story to have it addressed to a fictional name because someone who is using your house as a drop wouldn't want to risk a non-delivery like that, they'd just use your name.

As long as you don't sign for anything and you keep your mouth shut in the very unlikely case that your package in intercepted and investigated, you should be just fine.

The post office has a policy of having people register their addresses.  If you have something delivered to a name not registered at that address, and your post office actually does their job, then you will get a slip in your box compelling the unknown person to register.  If you do not comply, eventually they are suppose to hold the package until the person named on the package comes to the post office and signs for it, or they just wont deliver it.

  Either way, ordering your package to a name not registered on your address raises the chances that your package will get unwanted attention (i.e. raises flags). It is much more important to avoid unwanted attention in the first place than it is to try and have a preemptive alibi that garners you very little, if any, additional plausible deniability.

Read the forums, there are far more stories of people regretting having used a fake name than there are stories of people who wish they had used a fake name.

Mostly, just put a little thought into it.  There are common sense ways around every problem.  Many of us who have found out good ways, can't reveal them on here, simply because if our identity is discovered--and it's not *that* hard--it gives out too much information if someone were tracking us...we can only give advice 'in a general way' as we operate on the theory that LE also reads here.

So think about it a bit.  Understand the rules, and then show a little common sense.  Is your name misspelled at times?  Did someone else use your address?  Is there a secure mailbox that is unused at this time?  Is there a business I know that goes to all the houses round here, and posts?
We can't hold people's hands, and anything revealed will be revealed to *everybody* and we don't want that...some of this stuff you're just going to have to figure out all by your lonesome...but the real truth is, you have no control over who sends you mail.  You do have control over what you say if someone starts asking questions...

And mostly, if shit *doesn't* show, and I've used some unorthodox delivery system, then I damn sure can't go back on the seller.  It's on me.
