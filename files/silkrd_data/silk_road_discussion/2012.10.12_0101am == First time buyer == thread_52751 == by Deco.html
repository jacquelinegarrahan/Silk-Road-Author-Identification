Title: Re: First time buyer
Post by: kitkat82 on October 12, 2012, 01:11 am
Off the top of my head Chronic Crew is an awesome vendor who sells both.

 Make a small purchase to start out, and maybe even buy a few small electronic purchases of something you are interested in first to show that you are a serious buyer.  They should only cost you a dollar or two.   Buy an e book or something and leave good feedback, this will help your stats.  It is also good practice to  buy the electronic items.  It helps you get acquainted with the checkout and finalize process.

To select a vendor be sure to check out the feedback on his or her page, and search the forums for any reviews or feedback that they may have here.  Sometimes a vendor will have good feedback on his or her page, but when you check the forums you will see some different things.  Read all the details in the vendor profile on the vendor's main page.  Some vendors require FE (finalize early) for any customers with less than 5 purchases, and some require that you use PGP to encrypt your address.  Be sure to carefully review the feedback, and check the ratings for the stealth (how well the vendor camouflages and disguises the packages contents).

In my opinion you really should read everything, even what you may consider irrelevant now, it won't be later. That was basically the first day after account set up.

I set up PGP and added the link in my profile, to my public keys. I sent the forum member, Pine, a message and joined the PGP club. Cool stuff!  In the meantime, I was also reading different stories and bloggers, on their opinion and security of SR. I did a lot of reading. I have a family, and I must be careful.

Day 4 was research about the postal system and about bit coins. I wanted coins but not a paper trail of any kind.  I decided to simply go to the bank and make a deposit. I have to admit, it was easier that what I had prepared for.
A plan was made to handle deliveries....nuff said.

Day 5 was all about researching vendors. I spent hours reading the rumor mill....I like reading...and rumors. I chose vendors that stuck to the fundamentals listed on Sr. Each of the vendors did not require Fe early, expected you to Fe on time, and had good reviews on the forums.

My first order is in transit, and I don't anticipate any issues with the delivery. I want to thank all of the members for taking the time to provide specific instruction or advise. All the information literally saved me time and money.

I selected an item that I was familiar with. Read and re-read the Vendor Page. Checked the Rumor Mill for any posts related to the Vendor. Then...when I was sure of what I was doing I messaged the Vendor (DirtR0ad) to make sure he'd take my order. He responded promptly and professionally letting me know he would accept my order.  I added the item to my Cart and checked out. I waited patiently, checked my Orders the next day and saw the item status had changed to: "In Transit". Waited patiently for the package to arrive. As soon as I received the package and inspected I Finalized the order and left 5/5 feedback.

I think research is the key. Go over the material (wiki, forum, etc.) until you understand it. I put the time and effort into research ahead of time and was sure of what I was doing. Doing that greatly increases your chances of success. I believe you are on the right track. Just keep reading until you have a firm grasp of how things are done. And messaging the Vendor you select ahead of time will give a good impression that you have done your research. A lot more so than buying a couple of eBooks.

BTW: I consider my first transaction to be a stellar success. I will definitely will be a repeat customer with D1rtR0ad.
