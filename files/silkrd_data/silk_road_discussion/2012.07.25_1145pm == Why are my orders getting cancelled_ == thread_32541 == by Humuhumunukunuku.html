Title: Re: Why are my orders getting cancelled?
Post by: AlphaLima on August 27, 2012, 03:42 am
Message the seller. It at lets them know you're interested, maybe explain why you want to buy a small amount. The may agree to sell to you if you buy a quantity large enough to make it worth shipping. Establishing some sort of rapport before ordering is always a good idea. It's not like Amazon or Ebay, where an order shows up in their box and off they ship....

I generally deal with opiates, but as you've read on the forums: Don't FE! There are more than enough vendors on the road, one will sell to you without requiring FE. I found several, and I'm a consistent, repeat customer. I've only FE'd once and, naturally, the transaction went south. So if you choose to FE, make sure it's money you're willing to lose.

And as the other poster said, don't let your order auto-finalize. It's just plain wrong. Since I usually use the same vendors, I know the quality and can finalize the minute it's in the mailbox. For a new vendor, I try it, wait an hour or so, make sure it works, and finalize.
