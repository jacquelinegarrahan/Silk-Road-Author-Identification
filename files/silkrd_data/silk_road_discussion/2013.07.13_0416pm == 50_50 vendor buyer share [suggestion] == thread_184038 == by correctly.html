Title: 50:50 vendor buyer share [suggestion]
Post by: correctly on July 13, 2013, 04:16 pm
I just thought of this. It is merely an idea.
Here it goes.

Once a vendor marks a product in-transit and actually sends it, 50% of the BTC (cost of product) goes to the vendor and the other 50% remains in escrow.
No vendor should mark it in-transit before sending the product.

The remaining half will be held by SR and released once the buyer finalizes the transaction after receiving and inspecting the goods.

In case of any problem or customs letter, the remaining 50% can be used as refund or resolution.

Hopefully this could get rid of the want of vendors for the buyers to finalize early.

Buyers on their side should take the 50% risk once they purchase from a vendor. They should research about the vendor first and make sure they are ready to receive the product.

What do you guys think of this idea?

OK, so what you're saying is buyers should just not buy off scammers, and vendors not sell to scam buyers? That would work pretty well.
         What you are proposing is pretty much how the escrow system ends up working (50/50 each way) in resolution, except with the current system there is the total ability to vary from 100%/0% to 0%/100% based on the evidence either way. Its far from perfect but I think its better than most alternatives,
        This is basically a market where both sides want to remain as anonymous as possible while sending a non reversible transaction to each other. Its not possible to completely remove scamming, but the escrow system is the best we are going to get.
        The main flaw with the current system is that there is incentive for both buyer and vendor to resolve amicably without going to resolution: much quicker and doesn't affect stats. This is going to mean a 50/50 split of the loss in most cases. Unfortunately this tendency can be used by both scam vendors and scam buyers to get 50% free. Buyers can do something against this by mentioning what happened in feedback, so that it will be noticed if a vendor is doing this more often than you would expect...... but wait, this could mean the vendor has just been victim of lots of scam buyers!!!!
