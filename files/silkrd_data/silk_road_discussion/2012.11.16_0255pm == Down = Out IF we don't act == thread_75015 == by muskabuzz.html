Title: Down = Out IF we don't act
Post by: muskabuzz on November 16, 2012, 02:55 pm
in terms of significance we have now experienced SRs greatest outage. going forward the Muskabuzz wish to pose a question:

what do you think we showed about ourselves during this period?

the forums were open during most of the downtime giving everyone a chance to express themselves - potentially for the last time. with initially sparse updates an unusually sober paranoia immediately formed.  there were frequent goodbyes - jaded as well as sincere and much whinging: lost orders, payments and opportunities for both buyer and seller. some immediately said their loss was foul play from those they trusted the day before. fortunately there were also plenty of old soldiers around, reminding us that the situation was not without precedent and that this was no time for fear.

they were right of course, this was not the big one and we are all here to live on and learn from it but had this been the real end of Silk Road did we act the way we should have?

if circumstance left us with a brief and final access to the forums would we know how to make proper use of them? it was striking: with limited time and so much critical information to exchange we were basically arguing amongst ourselves. even attempts at collecting contact information degraded into squabbles.

having the great good fortune of working with so many of you the Muskabuzz know just what this community is capable of - changing the world of everyone in it. and we know that we are all certainly capable of much more than this.  if one wants to argue or lament please save it for the refugee camp, not the field. identities and reputations are what will allow us to regroup. the communications tools of the forums are limited enough for exchanging this information safely; no one is aided by accusation nor complaint at this critical time.

if there's a lesson that comes from disaster its a universal one: be prepared. whatever your security standards are there are backup communications available if we simply know where to find you.

for our part the Muskabuzz is adding a tormail address to all correspondence

muskabuzz@tormail.org

for we intend to be here with you not just until the end but after, so that there never need be an end. this is neither our right nor privilege but rather our duty.

for those that feel the same please use the comments to add your own backup contact information.

thank you, we look forward to sharing a very long ride.
