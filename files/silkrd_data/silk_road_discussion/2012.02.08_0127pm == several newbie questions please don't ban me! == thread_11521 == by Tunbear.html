Post by: Horizons on February 08, 2012, 01:50 pm
Hey, calm down. There's no need to be nervous. The mods here aren't heavy on the banhammer.

I don't know the price of a seller account, since I'm not interested in selling. I don't think it'd be as high as 150 bitcoins, though. Maybe it was 150 dollars? Though all transactions in SR are made in bitcoins, prices are usually listed in US dollars since bitcoins fluctuate so much.

You won't get in trouble for listing things in the wrong category, but it'll make it harder for potential customers to find your products, so it's better to be careful when making your listings.

You can make custom listings for free products and advertise them as limited deals. This is common practice and won't get you negative feedback. Just be polite and send people a message explaining why you cancelled their orders - also be sure to remove the free listing quickly once you're out of free samples, to avoid confusion.

Finally, it is possible to specify multiple shipping options at different prices in your listings. You can have a free option for UK clients and a paid one for foreign clients, no problem.

I hope I was helpful. Welcome to SR!

Title: Re: several newbie questions please don't ban me!
Post by: Horizons on February 08, 2012, 06:14 pm
Haha, no problem. You can feel at home asking questions here, several users will be glad to help if they can.

The money is "lost" - you're buying a seller account, after all. This fee is in place partly to prevent what would otherwise be a huge influx of scammer sellers offering bunk or non-existant products to try and make a quick buck at the expense of the buyers.

There aren't any trusted middleman services here who could sell your product for you. One kid (and I do mean kid, as in "not yet out of high school") tried setting one up, and may try again under a different account name. I wouldn't trust anyone offering such a service, though. You can never be sure that they aren't out to scam both you and your customers, or that they aren't working with Law enforcement. From the buyer's point of view, the fact that both the middleman and the actual salesman get to see their address is also a huge risk. So all in all I think it isn't a very good idea.

The Tor network is very slow compared to the regular Internet (which we call the clearnet, to distinguish it from hidden services). If you find that you can't connect to a website, try clicking the "Use a New Identity" button in Vidalia. If it still doesn't work, the only thing you can do is wait. If you find yourself clicking that button more than once every ten minutes, chances are your problem won't be fixed that way.

Cheers,
Horizons.
