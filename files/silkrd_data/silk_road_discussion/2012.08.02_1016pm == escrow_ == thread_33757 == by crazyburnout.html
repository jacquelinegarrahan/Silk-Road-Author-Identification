Post by: bill417 on August 03, 2012, 01:04 am
Hello and welcome;

In short, what happens when you buy something on Silk Road (by placing the item in your cart, then paying for it using Bitcoins after having entered your pin number - don't send out any money if you don't recognise that) - the funds go to Silk Road themselves I believe. Here, they take a ~4% fee on the transaction(s) you made and paid for.
Once a seller has marked the item as "In transit", you (the buyer) will have two options - to finalize or resolve.

Finalizing is the act of releasing the funds to the seller. It's basically saying 'I've received the product safely so there's the money' sort of thing. Or more so, SR is saying that, I don't know!

But that's basically what escrow - the act of the funds going to somewhere third party, but only being released to the seller once you've received the item. They will still be removed from your account whether you've finalized or not.

Be wary of new sellers. If they've less than 35 transactions, they've NO-RIGHT WHATSOEVER to ask people to finalize early (this basically means releasing the funds early before the overall deal is complete - ie - product received). Make sure you report these offenders to Silk Road and they'll usually give them a message as one-chance-reminder about the Seller Contract, referencing the Seller's Guide, which must also be adhered to.

Hope this helps.
