Title: Re: What's the difference between 2C-I, 2C-E, 2C-B, 2C-X, etc?
Post by: Tryptamine on June 20, 2012, 12:15 am
The difference is the substituent on the 4 position of the 2,5-dimethoxy-phenylethylamine. C is chlorine, B is bromine, I is iodine, D is methyl, E is ethyl, P is propyl, T-x are thio-alkanes, N is nitro, H is hydrogen (no subsituent).
