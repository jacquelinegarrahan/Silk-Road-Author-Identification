Post by: jnemonic on January 16, 2013, 09:46 am
Quote from: Dalmata on January 16, 2013, 06:51 am
Quote from: danconia on January 16, 2013, 12:59 am
I feel like the problem is that they can have dogs and none of these small safes will really be able to keep a dog from smelling it.  I realize the dogs can be unreliable though, and maybe something submerged (like someone mentioned earlier) or too high up for the dog to smell would work well though.
Don't go up high, scent will trickle down; search on "scent cone."  Out in the woods in a sealed spike is great, but not accessible all year (depending on climate) and may not accommodate your volume. I suppose in an apartment that you really don't have the option of hiding anything in a sealed container you can haul out of a septic tank?
How about a water heater? (Fish tanks are not that uncommon a site, I think; water heaters might be common too.)
The spot behind a dishwasher is common enough that I wouldn't use it, and it is -very- easily scented by a dog. I have rented innumerable apartments/houses now where I have pulled out the dishwasher and found the old baggies of weed / empty booze bottles / porn stash / etc.  There are other appliance spots, etc., that are less obvious. I think it depends on how repair-minded you are.

A sealed glass jar is scent-resistant, and you can get really small sealed glass (or metal) containers.
A Pelican case is also handy, and they make foam-lined ones that will hold your glass jars and that are weather-resistant/easily stashed in weather-exposed locations.
You'll see the K9 officers using these for training and also scenthound people for practice scents.
Moisture barrier bags (sorry to keep beating that idea to death) are worth a look-see. Can't say yet just how long I'd rely on one to keep something away from a pup.
Make sure your hands are -clean- when putting things away, and, by clean, I mean "think sterile" and "gloves" and things like that.

Dogs are unreliable ... although not as unreliable as their handlers, with false alerts, etc.

Stay safe,
