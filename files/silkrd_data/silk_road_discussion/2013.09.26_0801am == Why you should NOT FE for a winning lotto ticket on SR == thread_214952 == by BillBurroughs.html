Title: Why you should NOT FE for a winning lotto ticket on SR
Post by: BillBurroughs on September 26, 2013, 08:01 am
Hey there roaders! I wanted to write this out because I got ripped off by FEing on a lotto I won, and because I FE'ed, couldn't do anything about it (i.e. take it to the resolution center.), not even leave less than a 5/5 (besides removing my rating).

There are various postings for lottos where you spend a small amount (usually $1-10) for a chance to win items/money if your ticket ends in a certain number or letter. When a vendor asks you to FE and leave a 5/5 no matter the outcome, watch out! This means that if the vendor decides they don't want to ship your item, or it gets 'lost in the mail,' they have no obligation to send you your stuff, and you can't give them less than a 5/5 because of it. You can, of course, leave them a bad review and remove you rating, but they will still keep your ticket price and you can't take it to resolution.

It also makes no sense, if the vendor is honest, to FE if your lotto ticket is a winner, before you get your items. It's not like they need you to release $1-10 for shipping, and it's not like they are shipping out expensive items that they won't be able to re-up other products because they are waiting for a huge amount of money stuck in escrow. If they can't afford to send out the items in their lottos (which I believe was what happened in my case), they should not have lotto listings up. If you think about it, you shouldn't even have to FE for a losing lotto ticket, the vendor is losing literally nothing (besides a delayed couple of bucks and a delayed 5/5), while if the buyer lets the ticket auto-finalize, that negatively effects the buyers purchasing stats for life.

So what I'm saying is, go head and play lottos if you want to, but be weary if the vendor asks you to FE on a winner. You may end up getting ripped off like I did.
