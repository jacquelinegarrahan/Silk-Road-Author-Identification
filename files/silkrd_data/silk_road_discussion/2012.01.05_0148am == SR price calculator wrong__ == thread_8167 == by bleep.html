Title: Re: SR price calculator wrong??
Post by: CaptainSensible on January 05, 2012, 05:52 pm
Silk Road has always had a delay of several hours when posting the Bitcoin value.  This means that the "1 BTC=X dollars" display at the bottom of the page was at least a little too high or too low.  It didn't matter too much if the Bitcoin value stayed pretty much the same, but lately this has been a real problem when the MtGox Bitcoin price is well over six dollars and the Bitcoin price used by SR is about a dollar less. 

What this means is that buyers who want to get the most bang for their Bitcoin have to wait... and wait... and wait until the SR Bitcoin value starts to catch up with the actual market value.  This is a frustrating and time wasting process.  When you've just purchased Bitcoins at the market value it just doesn't make sense to use them on SR if you're not going to get the full value of your Bitcoin.

I've never heard of SR using the weighted daily average from MtGox, and I hope that's not true. But I do wish SR would reinstate the "1 BTC=X dollars" display, and do something to keep the price up to date.
