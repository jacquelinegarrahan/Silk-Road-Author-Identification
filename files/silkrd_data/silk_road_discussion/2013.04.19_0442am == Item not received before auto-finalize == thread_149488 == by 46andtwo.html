Are you in contact with the seller? Do they have any kind of tracking info they can supply you with? If no to these, search the forums for the seller's name, or they might even have their own review thread. Have others gotten burned by him/her recently also or is it a fluke?

This just happened to me the other week, and the seller had a 100 rating with many positive comments by many customers both in his feedback as well as these forums. I didnt wanna be the first person to tarnish his record, but he wasn't replying to any of my messages. I came and made a post just like yours, and someone going through the same thing asked who the seller was. We found out it's the same one and he hadnt fulfilled any of his recent orders or communicated with anyone. This greatly relieved me that LE or the post office hadnt caught on.

But if your seller is communicating and there's no reason to suspect foul play on their part, ask them if theyre receptive to a partial refund. Then, if it were me, I would probably not have anything shipped to that address again, at least for a time. But that's me & my paranoia.

Post by: Duffman on April 23, 2013, 09:46 pm
Quote from: 46andtwo on April 19, 2013, 03:49 pm
Thanks for the replies all, sent out a message to the seller. Just was wondering if there's an automated process that I'd be able to do this through. If the seller doesn't respond in 24 hours, I'll do the resolve thing. Thanks again, love this community!
If the order is close to autofinalizing then I advise you to already place the order inside the resolution center.
You can still wait with submitting your claim for an extension until the vendor responds to your message.
However by already placing the order inside the resolution center,
it can't autofinalize anymore and the due date will automatically be extended for a few more days.
This way you don't have anything to worry about in case SR suddenly goes offline, rendering you unable to extend the due date.
Because if the order does autofinalize, it will show on your purchasing stats and it will be up to the vendor's discretion if he wants to offer you any compensation should the order fail to arrive.
