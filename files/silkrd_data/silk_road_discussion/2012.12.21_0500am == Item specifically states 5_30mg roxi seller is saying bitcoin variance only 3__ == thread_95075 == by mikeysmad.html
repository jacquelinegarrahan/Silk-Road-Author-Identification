you ordered 5 fot x btc. He have to send 5. When sending 3 ( lucky you have already proof from the message) he is wrong and res center will certainly agree with you.
He is right that the prices change, It can be in -sometimes extreme situations- that it is pretty much.
That is why you need to peg your prices to the dollar.
He made a listing for say 10 btc = 120 dollar
Now a little later he sells it for 10 btc to you but....now for him for the 10 btc is not 120 dollar but 110, 100 or even 90. Of course for him it also can be 10btc is 125, 130 etc.
So, again, the vendor should ( I think) peg the prices. That way you always pay the same dollar price although the btc is changing.
At the moment you change your $ for btc you get x but that does not matter because the price you pay will be at that time the same as what you got changing.
Of course there will always be a difference but in this case it will be acceptable.
I use this now for a long time and it is working very well.
Only problem...people looking at my listing see the price, say 10 btc., a week later they look again and now it is 11 btc. (usually not that extreme, its an example)
Sometimes they accuse me of raising the price to get more money, the opposite is true, there is no more honest system.
So, you can explain this system to that vendor
When now he is losing when changing to dollar he can decide to wait for better time or peg it...
