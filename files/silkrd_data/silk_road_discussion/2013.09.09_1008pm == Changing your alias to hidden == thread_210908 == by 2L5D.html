This isn't possible.  The reviews that say hidden are from feedback left under the old system.  I asked about this before because I didn't like a systemwide alias being available (since it makes purchases easier to track if you find out someone's alias).  DPR said that it needs some kind of name there so many purchases from 1 person can't be used to pad the feedback without it being obvious.  I suggested an alternative system already, hashing the alias/username + vendor name to create a system generated name that is the same for one user within a vendor's page (to prevent feedback padding) but different across vendors (to prevent user tracking), so I hope something like that is implemented eventually, but I don't know if there are any plans to actually do this or not.

Title: Re: Changing your alias to hidden
Post by: convergedlight on September 17, 2013, 04:23 am
Quote from: 7Kz2 on September 15, 2013, 11:28 pm
It's common sense to not let anyone know your alias on there, do not use an alias you use anywhere else, and do not use a real descriptive name.  If your last name is wilson and you were born in 1990, do not put Wilson1990.

The problem is if you are active on the forums comparing products and discussing them and writing reviews on the same products under an alias, you might inadvertently use a unique phrase to describe said product in both places, and it might even be at about the same time.  Even if you don't use the same phrase, just the timing could give it away if the vendor does not have a high volume of sales.  This could provide strong circumstantial evidence linking a forum name to a buying account review alias, and with this info someone could scrape the site to identify everything else you reviewed under the same alias.  Until this is fixed it's clearly an incentive to not review in some cases.

Nonsense. The alias links together all reviews from a particular buyer, across any number of vendors, and allows anyone to see exactly what that buyer has bought, and when and from whom.

Under the old system (until a month or so ago) that was not possible. And now, all of that information could be used as evidence in case of a busted buyer.

That is why I have not written any reviews since these changes were made, despite having completed a couple of orders with two different vendors. (If those vendors are reading this, now you know why. And I suppose you'll have to take my word for it that I left you a 5/5 rating).

Also I've heard it said that the main (or only) reason for the inclusion of a pseudonym in reviews is so that we can tell when a vendor is padding his own feedback. But as convergedlight and others have said, there are other ways of achieving that without linking all reviews to a buyer. Some have been suggested already, and I have at least one other suggestion which I'll be posting shortly in the relevant thread.

But for now, the only way to hide your alias is not to write any reviews.
