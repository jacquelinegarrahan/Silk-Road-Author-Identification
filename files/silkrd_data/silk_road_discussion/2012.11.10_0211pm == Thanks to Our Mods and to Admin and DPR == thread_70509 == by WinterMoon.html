Just my .02 cents: for one, you don't need to reboot the entire computer if you can't connect.  I don't think that'll help much.  Just get a new identity.  Two, most programs I've seen people use to "take care of their registry," or "keep their system clean," ended up causing more harm than they actually fixed.  Either the program itself, or the combination of it running with other bloatware (or even with an antivirus).  Windows 7 is pretty good at taking care of itself.  Just use an antivirus program, and don't go letting things fuck with your registry unless you're pretty damn sure that's actually one of your problems.  Windows XP gets messy after a few years, but reinstalling is the only thing that really solves that for long.  Windows 7 takes care of itself fine as far as I've seen.

Pardon me for flat out disagreeing, but there's no way SR will bog down the entire operating system until it doesn't connect anymore. Be careful what you install, especially when you're trying to stay anonymous...
