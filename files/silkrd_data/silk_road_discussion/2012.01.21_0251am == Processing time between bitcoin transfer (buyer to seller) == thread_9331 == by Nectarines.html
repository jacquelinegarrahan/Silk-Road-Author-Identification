If a vendor requests early finalization you have to confirm the item as received before he will send the item. Do not worry though because you have the option of going back and changing your feedback from a 5 out of 5 to a 1 out of 5 if he doesn't fulfill his part of the agreement/sale.

Can I ask does the buyer have to ask you to finalise early before you see the option? I only ask because I have just made my first transaction and on the orders page I don't see an option to finalise early but below my order it says "The finalize button is FINAL and cannot be reversed".

The seller won't necessarily personally ask you to finalise early, but if they expect you to they will say so either in the item description or in their profile page.

Until it is marked as 'in transit', it will say 'processing' and you will have the option only to cancel the order.

Once the seller has marked the item as 'in transit', you will have the option to finalise. Under normal circumstances 'in transit' should mean that it is in the mail, on its way to you and you should finalise when the item is in your hand. But as we know, many vendors want you to finalise before they will send it out, especially if you are a new or slightly risky user based on your history on the marketplace.
