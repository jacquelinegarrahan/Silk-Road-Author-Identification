Title: Re: Have u guys herd of his new drug market? Sheep marketplace>?
Post by: anonymart on February 02, 2013, 03:45 am
To say that automatically the name of the marketplace has one and only one interpretation, and that it's automatically a bad place just because you can interpret its name in a particular way because of the connotations of the use of the term "sheep" particularly in political contexts is a pretty thin argument.   Anyone engaging in this sort of market has reason for healthy skepticism, but I recall back when forums were dropping left and right, and so many were avoiding the silk road because of the same kind of talk:  that it MUST be a honeypot, that it MUST be fatally flawed in security, etc.   There are a ton of knee jerk reactions that I advise you avoid, but I am not saying you should jump in the water with sharks either.  The important thing to keep in mind, is if any of these dangers have any merit then they must have a mechanism.   

So the question is, is this site trying to execute javascript?
Is it trying to get people to engage with a less secure medium?
Is it fishing for passwords or pins? 

So far we don't have any reason to believe these things are true, but it would be wise to exercise caution.  Make sure your not running javascript, make sure your not installing ActiveX or other extensions that compromise security,   Certain files like PDF's can run exploits on the machine you download and run them on.  The farmers market went down not because of the security of tor, but because people decided to do a ton of completely insecure transactions using wire services and paypal, and weak email security.. so it had nothing to do with tor.   I take this as a good sign that government is at least being forced to attack indirectly rather than truly taking on Tor itself.

aside from all that,

Having multiple tor market places out there is probably a good idea.  I've programmed much of the same myself, but part of what makes it worth diversifying the market is not only the distributed power that if one goes down there will be another, but that one site can fork on the concept and attempt to improve it.  There isn't much of an improvement on
the concept of the site in this regard, which doesn't inspire me much, as when I start thinking about how to go about on improving the silk road model, I literally cannot imagine building all the possible variations on improvements.  They claim that they have "improved the security concerns" regarding silk road, but they don't really comment much on exactly what that is, and exactly how they imagine they can telepathically know silk roads code that they don't have access to. 

If your going to branch off from silk road with a new site I suggest this:

implement further anarcho-capitalistic methods for dispute resolution..
create better contractual models for sales, you can't resolve conflicts that are based on vague terms.
stop using the old forum model when you can create entirely new systems for dealing with the reporting problem.

I could go on and on, but I plan on telling you the rest in my own way.  It's clear to me that the model must be evolved.
