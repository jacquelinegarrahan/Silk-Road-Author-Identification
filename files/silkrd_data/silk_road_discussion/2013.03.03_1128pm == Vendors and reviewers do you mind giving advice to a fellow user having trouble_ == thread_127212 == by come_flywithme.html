Post by: chemdog on April 05, 2013, 12:15 pm
We took a *lot* of flack like this when we first started - freebie hunters, tyre kickers etc.

That's fine, we had stuff to give away, but it wasn't for free. We wanted feedback from established community members in exchange, but a certain minority seemed to ignore that bit and ask endlessly for giveaways.

I remember getting quite irate at a few of them. I was struggling for money at the time and had scraped and saved to get my vendors account, supplies, security rig etc. I let rip on a few of them...

And instantly started kicking the shit out of myself for it. "What the... All the I'm doing is pissing off my potential customers... Grrrr..."

It felt counter-intuitive because I want to do a good job, serve my clients well and generally be level in all my activities, but I held my own, even in the face of threats of bad reviews.

And I found that the minority were exactly that - a minority.

Play by the rules, follow your own code within it and treat everyone with respect unless they prove themselves unworthy of receiving it.

You may piss a couple of people off in the short run, but you will be ultimately in a better standing in the long run.
