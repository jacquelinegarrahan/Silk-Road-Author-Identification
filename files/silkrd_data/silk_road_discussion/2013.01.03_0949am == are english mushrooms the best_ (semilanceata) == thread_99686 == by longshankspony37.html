Title: Re: are english mushrooms the best? (semilanceata)
Post by: mcmurphy on January 03, 2013, 02:14 pm
The 2 main active ingredients in magics are the same for all species.  These may be different in different amounts and the proportions are different in fresh and dried shrooms as one doesn't survive in air very well.  It's possible that other compounds contained in the shrooms are responsible for things like dodgy stomach so side effects may differ between species / strains but generally you're getting a very similar hit from every species.  Some species taste better than others so that might be a consideration.

The best strains I have tried are also the strongest I have tried.  I think this is because you have to take less material meaning that both taste and side effects are reduced.  These are "Hawaiain" Panoleus cyanescens and "Philosopher's stone" Pscylocybe tampanensis (sclerota).  The buzz is pretty similar for all psylocybin containing shrooms though. (I find fresh more visual and dried more psycological but that may just be me or the dosage)
