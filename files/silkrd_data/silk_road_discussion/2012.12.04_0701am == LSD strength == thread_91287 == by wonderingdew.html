Title: LSD strength
Post by: wonderingdew on December 04, 2012, 07:01 am
I've been doing some reading on Erowid and other places lately, and every bit of independent research done over the last 10 years, including LE tests, points to the average hit of acid, globally, being around 50µg.
This page (http://www.erowid.org/chemicals/lsd/lsd_article3.shtml) says out of 11 samples in Spain in 2005, "38.81 µg was the average quantity detected."
From 6 samples in Switzerland the same year, "50.1 µg was the average..."
From 97 to 2003, the Spanish NIT sampled 2,182 blotters. The average: 69.22 µg.
Citing all available research, Erowid concludes: "Absolute average concentration, counting all the analyzed batches: 53.38µg."

So how does every seller on SR claim to have 200µg blotters, if nobody has ever tested blotters that high globally in the last 30 years?  ???

Title: Re: LSD strength
Post by: BlarghRawr on December 05, 2012, 06:57 am
Dear Thread: Average does not mean maximum. The AVERAGE of the tests is low. In NO WAY does that mean that high-strength blotter is impossible. It just means it is uncommon, thus the low average. And why is it less common? Because less blotter has to be laid, as well. More per hit means less sheets means less on the street. It's also harder to make a profit on because convincing someone to shell out $30 for one tab is harder than $30 for 3 tabs, even if they have the exact same amount of LSD on them.
