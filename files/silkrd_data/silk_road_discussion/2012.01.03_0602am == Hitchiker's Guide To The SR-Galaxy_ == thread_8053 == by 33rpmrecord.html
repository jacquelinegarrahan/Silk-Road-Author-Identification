Quote from: 33rpmrecord on January 03, 2012, 06:02 am
how communication and sending messages works out in regards to the public keys and encryption
It's really pretty simple. If you want to send somebody a message which you don't want anyone else to read (either because it contain private data or discussion of illegal activities, for example), you encrypt the message with that person's public key. That way, only they will be able to read it (by decrypting it with their own private key, which nobody else can access). You should include your own public key in the message (before encrypting it!) so that they can reply in a way that only you may read their message, too.

If you want your message's recipient to be absolutely positive that you're the person who sent the message, and they already have your public key, you can sign the message with your private key. That way, they can use your public key to verify that it was in fact you who sent it. Signing a message does not encrypt it, though. You need the recipient's public key for that.

Quote from: 33rpmrecord on January 03, 2012, 06:02 am
the best ways one can go about acquiring BitCoins and utilizing them to purchase whatever one may want to buy off of here.
That depends on a lot of things. Where are you, do you prefer anonymity over speed or security, et cetera. You can use legitimate services like MtGox and Virwox to acquire the bitcoins and find some way to launder them, you can buy them with cash from some vendors for total anonymity (but that usually requires sending cash via mail, which is not without its risks) or you can buy bitcoins from vendors right here on the Silk Road. Sugar Mama is one such vendor. She sells bitcoins for moneypaks.

There's a lot of good ways to get bitcoins, in the end it comes down to your own priorities and personal preference.
