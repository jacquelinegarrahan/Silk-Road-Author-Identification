Title: Re: How to tell how much stuff a vendor has left to sell?
Post by: QualityMeds on August 08, 2013, 04:31 am
ok it's time that me, a vendor, chimed in here:

"Can a vendor have, say, a hundred of something but make it possible for any individual buyer to only buy a maximum of, say, four of that item"

The answer is, absolutely NO! There are no per buyer limits as there is no way for me, as a vendor, to limit the number of items an individual buyer can purchase automatically. I can always cancel your order after the fact but when I make a listing, the only thing I can put, in reference to the quantity of the item, is the total quantity I have of the item.


"Is there anyway on the road to see how many lots a vendor has left for sale without having to contact & bother them? "

YES! If I list item X, I enter say, 50 in the quantity field. Then when someone makes a purchase, the quantity available goes down by the number of items he bought, and this is automatic. If the buyer purchases a quantity of three, the quantity available goes down by three and the total number available changes automatically to 47. So, if you want to see how many are now available, just put a quantity of one in your shopping cart. Now look at your shopping cart. The maximum quantity you are able to buy is the number left for sale. It's as simple as that.

By the way, buyers, don't feel like you're "bothering" me when you contact me to ask questions. Too often, buyers walk on eggshells with me when they message me with questions, scared that I'll get mad for them bothering me. I guess many vendors are like this? Not me, I'm thankful for my customers, feel free to message me with any kind of question or concern.

Hope the above info helped!

QM
