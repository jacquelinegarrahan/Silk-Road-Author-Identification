Title: Re: Refund stat screwed due to refund for an item that was never shipped
Post by: PowerToCharm on August 03, 2013, 12:58 pm
It's interesting that on SR, refund rates count against the buyer. On mainstream e-commerce sites like Amazon, refund rates count against the seller, the thought being, I suppose, that if a buyer needs a refund it's because the seller somehow screwed up. If your refund rates go above a certain amount on Amazon, your selling account will be put under review, suspended, or even permanently revoked. Amazon buyers can and do scam sellers left and right, knowing that Amazon will (almost) always side with the buyer and leave the seller to eat the loss. So it's kind of refreshing, or at least interesting, on SR to see that the admin is a little more on the side of sellers.
