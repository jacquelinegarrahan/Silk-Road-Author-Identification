So one week has passed since I announced the imminent closure of Atlantis and now here I am writing from the last remains of the sunken kingdom, I had high hopes they could go out with dignity but it's with a heavy heart I now have to write it's obvious Atlantis has sunk to the lowest depths of the darknet.....That of the scam artist.

They say the greatest scam is the one you don't see coming and after advising many Atlantis users on how to avoid being scammed I didn't want to believe I was a victim of the long scam myself.

My motives now and in past are still first and foremost to get involved wherever I can to help break prohibition and nullify the so called war on drugs while secondly carving out a path for myself in life that I truly believe in.
The last few months have been very hectic but I now know what it means when people say they found their calling, The sense of satisfaction I got from being on the front lines in the war on drugs was enough to hook me. I went from a freelance Tech/Social media gun for hire who's only darknet experience was reading the articles that came out in Gawker and other media about a year ago to absorbing every single thread of information on the Silk Road forums in as little time possible.
Of course I didn't learn everything fast enough as "Astor" and other forum members pointed out on a few occasions but in three months I went from just about knowing PGP and that it's a good idea to utilize a VPN over TOR to knowing real security means physical isolation and never saying what exact security measures you use besides "all of it".

Unfortunately I think it was similar case for Admin at Atlantis, While they knew what they were getting into and were quite adept, A lot of security holes have been found in TOR of late along with a few supercomputer purchases potentially capable of breaking the RSA 1024/2048 encryption used to run the TOR network and other "uncrackable" encryption methods.
The Atlantis admin were very level headed guys and I firmly believe closure was a case of the con's started outweighing the pros at a time where it wasn't too late to get out, destroy any evidence and move on with a semi-clean slate. My guess is they went from having 100% faith in the technology to recently realizing it's not foolproof nor future-proof and like DPR said in his post on Atlantis closing "the fear" is something everybody has to deal with and make peace with by themselves and for that 99.9% assurance in your security is never enough unless you are willing to face the heat if that 0.01% comes up some day.

I think claims the site was unprofitable are far from true and on the contrary if the plan was always to close shop when the piggy bank was full then now was the time, While I don't have sales figures the traffic to all the social media pages was at an all time high and I believe money was rolling in faster than ever and I was hearing reports of support response times being slower than ever.

Perhaps naively I told myself should I ever be arrested my stance is that throughout history freedom fighters of one sort of another have always come with a voice or political representation attached, I viewed Atlantis and by extension Silk Road as freedom fighters in the war on drugs and while I support the cause I have no involvement in the so called crimes they commit and only speak on their behalf.
I mean if Al Capone was still fighting his was on prohibition in this day and age I'm sure he'd have a social media presence too and I'd be glad to represent him.

To those who have lost funds, I truly apologize if it was the result of my work that brought you to Atlantis, If it means anything they had me going too. With regard to my future I considered going public for a short period but in this day and age where you no longer need to commit a crime to be considered a threat I thought against talking too much, As the kind of person who hates to waste I've consumed too much of these forums over the last months to disappear without trace so with that in mind I will continue to be a passive part of the Silk Road community and blog about the changing darknet scene under this pseudonym. For now I'm going to re-use the ill fated Atlantis blog which was I was working on when the news broke, Perhaps it can stand as a reminder that trust needs to be earned in this world.
