Title: Re: New and not sure what to do about this problem.
Post by: jameslink2 on August 01, 2012, 02:30 am
Turkey for Breakfast,

When you enter the resolve process you can enter a message and what you want, an extension on the finalization time or a % return of money.

There are 4 days on the clock then, the vendor can respond and enter a message as well as counter offer. This goes back and forth tell you come to an agreement or in 4 days or so the SR admins step in. It should be noted that if you put it into resolution 1 hour before auto-finalization it will extend it by 4 days and will more than likely tick the vendor off. If you have ordered locally (For me US to US) you should have it IMO within 14 days of shipment. Most of my stuff is delivered in 4 days of shipment. So I would send the vendor a message at 14 days and let them know you did not get it. They can track it with the DCN. If he does not respond or give your any grief I would put it into resolution. But give him or her the opportunity to see what is up and correct the problem.

Note, if the vendor has a DCN that shows he shipped it to you and it was returned to sender for any reason, held in customs, or it shows delivered then, it is my understanding that, SR with rule in the vendors favor and finalize the escrow.
Some vendors offer a % return in some of these situations, but that is up to them.

Now you should also be aware that if you get a bitcoin return it will ding your stats and some vendors will not deal with you if your stats show significant refunds.

It should be noted that I have only seen the vendor side of this so some of the above is speculation from what I have seen.
