Title: Re: How to build cred on SR?
Post by: Hawker on April 16, 2012, 01:49 am
There are very reputable vendors here that don't require FE even for new buyers.  Start small, treat them right (finalize when the product arrives and leave honest feedback) and go from there.

If what you're looking for requires you to FE then pick the vendor with the best stats (tenure, feedback), not necessarily the best price and stay reasonably small.  This is how you build cred.

Oh, and once you have it, don't FE anymore, escrow is for your protection, it's easy for someone to set up an account and bolt with thousands of dollars, a good rule is if it's too good to be true, it probably is.

Title: Re: How to build cred on SR?
Post by: divinechemicals on April 16, 2012, 04:50 am
It's not really that hard to build cred without screwing yourself in a scam. A lot of people tell you not to finalize early no matter what, but honestly you may have to for your first few purchases. The key is to really explore a vendor's feedback page and Rumor Mill thread before going with him. Make sure nothing seems fishy. Go with a vendor who is in the top 10% of vendors on all of the Silk Road and finalize early if they require it for new buyers. Trust me, you're almost certainly not going to get screwed by a vendor that has a 100% feedback rating with over 300 sales and a Rumor Mill thread filled with glowing reviews. Just be smart. Unless you're from the Netherlands, don't purchase from there because you're basically just asking for your package to get intercepted. Try and buy from someone in the same country/continent if possible, although if you're in the U.S., there are a number of European vendors that have no problems shipping overseas. I finalized early for my first couple of purchases without any issues using the above method. And there are vendors that I still finalize early for just as a courtesy because they're so professional and consistent. But now my buyer stats are more than enough to ensure that I can stay out of escrow if I so choose. Just don't expect that luxury when you have no purchases and could easily scam an established vendor.

Welcome to paradise. Enjoy your newfound freedom. Just remember that freedom means you need to be responsible.
