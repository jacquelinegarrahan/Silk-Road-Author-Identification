Title: Just placed my first SR order. YAY! A couple tips for other newbies!
Post by: tehjiggy3 on February 17, 2012, 09:37 pm
Hey there guys!  Just thought I'd post a little something on here for all the other newbies, like myself, who are getting ready to take the plunge into the world of bitcoins.  I know it can be daunting, especially if you are like me and want to make sure you are making the most secure purchase possible.

First of all, congrats on figuring out about the site and Tor!  While this is a big step, there are many others to take.  The most confusing for me was the process of getting bitcoins.  It all seemed so complex...so confusing.  After actually using them today, I have to say the process is actually pretty easy.  Here was my route of getting the bitcoins and moving them to my SR account. 

I made a MtGox account.  I set it up with a new email address.  (Don't use Tor if you are going to go to Mt.Gox.)  Once my Mt.Gox account was set up, I wanted to check out my available methods of funding my account so I could buy some coins.  After reading for a couple of weeks on the forums here on SR as well as on the clearweb, I decided to go with bitinstant.com.  My logic behind this was: 1) I don't need any identification.  Just go to Bitinstant, tell them how much you want, give them your mtgox account name and pick one of their banks to make a cash deposit.  No ID, nothing.  That sounded awesome to me...and the best part is that they send you the funds almost instantly.  I was able to check my email and find the Code for my funds, from my smartphone, before I even made it home from the bank.  LEGIT! 

Once you go to Mt.Gox and redeem the code under the Funding Options tab, its a simple few clicks to buy your coin and get them on their way.  Once you have the coins, its pretty straightforward from there.  Just copy the address for whatever wallet you want to send the coins to.  I used BitSpinner, a free app for Android phones that acts as a wallet for your BTC.  So, I sent them to my bitspinner address and then went onto Tor, logged into Silk Road, checked my account settings and copied the address for my Silk Road wallet.  About an hour later my funds were in my Silk Road wallet and I was able to make my orders.  :D

Like I said, I was overwhelmed at first with all of the security procedures, not to mention the buying and moving Bitcoins.  It's not as bad as it seems.  Anyway, there is a ton of information on these forums.  I would spend hours just reading up on here, making sure I knew what to do.  I suggest you do the same.  There are PGP tutorials, a buyers guide...all manner of helpful posts.  Most of all, there are people here.  Friendly people, who are most likely willing to help you out, as we were all n00bs once upon a time.  (I am still a n00b)

Anyway, I really just wanted to post on here that I had placed my first couple of orders today!  I have been excited about doing it for some time now, and now that I have done it, I feel giddy about the packages coming!  I researched a couple of vendors I thought I could trust.  Went with Tony and Dolfan.  Tony because he sells quantity and Dolfan because he is a USA vendor.  Both vendors seem to have great reviews and feedback. 

I want to thank everyone for their help, anytime I had a question it was answered quickly through messages to vendors or by people on the forums.  The community here on Silk Road is truly something to be proud of and it seems like they look after their own.  An admirable quality in a world filled with scammers and greedy, selfish bastards.  So, in closing, cheers Silk Roaders!  Wish me luck on my packages arriving!

Thanks,
Jiggy
