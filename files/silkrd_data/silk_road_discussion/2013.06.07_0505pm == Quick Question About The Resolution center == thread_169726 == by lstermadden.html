Title: Re: Quick Question About The Resolution center
Post by: Thirdplace on June 15, 2013, 05:45 pm
The idea of the resolution center is that you can work out this issue with the vendor.  There are generally two ways it's worked out.  Either a refund or a reship.  You'll have to balance the pro's and con's.  you may wait longer with a reship, but a refund will lose you some money(at least 4% as that's SR's fee - that's even if you get a 100% refund) AND it'll affect your refund rate on your account that shows up under your 'buyer statistics'.  This will make other vendor's more wary of you and less likely to want to enter a transaction with you.  The only way it won't affect your refund rate is if you ask for a refund and the vendor doesn't propose ANY resolutions, not even an extension and the extension is granted by SR staff.

You and the vendor can both post proposals as for how to resolve this.  they show up under 'proposed resolutions'.  If at the time it's due, neither of you has agreed to the other's resolutions, the SR staff will review the transaction, proposed resolutions, and the previous transaction history of both you and the vendor and make a decision based on that.

Best of luck. :)
