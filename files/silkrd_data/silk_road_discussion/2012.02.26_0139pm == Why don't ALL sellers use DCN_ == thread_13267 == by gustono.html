Title: Re: Why don't ALL sellers use DCN?

It usually also turns it into a package, which in some countries means that 'secrecy of correspondence' does not apply anymore. This makes it easier for customs to open the letter.

Title: Re: Why don't ALL sellers use DCN?
Post by: cacoethes on March 02, 2012, 04:23 am
DCN is not tracking, although some people use it that way.

As a buyer, I will never check a DCN even if I am given one.  The damn package will get here or it won't.  If it does, then great!  If it doesn't, then I'll resolve it through Silk Road and let the vendor worry about the DCN.

I can't claim that I wasn't expecting the package when I've checked the damned DCN number for it twenty times a day, or even once, for that matter, from an IP address that links me to the physical address of the delivery.  And checking through Tor could possibly get it flagged (who knows if there's any truth to that, but why take the chance?).

Plausible deniabilty can only work if the illusion of it remains seamless and no other evidence exists that can prove you knew about the delivery.  DCNs are for the vendor, not the buyer.
