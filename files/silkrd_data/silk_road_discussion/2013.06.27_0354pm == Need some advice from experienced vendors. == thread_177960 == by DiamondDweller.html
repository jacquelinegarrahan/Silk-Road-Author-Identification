Title: Re: Need some advice from experienced vendors.
Post by: MarcelKetman on June 28, 2013, 12:39 pm
Solution to your problem:

1. Devise refund rules that offer buyers some protection but are priced into your profits (and this is overall - don't just look at one product).
2. State these rules clearly on your profile
3. Mention that buyers agree to your rules when purchasing on your product page
4. Offer refunds based on these rules in the resolution centre - SR will always find in your favour if you outlined Ts & Cs in advance.
5. Always engage in active, friendly dialogue with buyers to ensure you still get good feedback even if it hasn't arrived.

But note, there will always be dicks who try to blackmail you and leave bad feedback. It's part of the SR game. Don't let them bother you and keep on doing what you're doing. Your feedback should still be high 90s and even if it's not, take consollation in the fact that some of the biggest vendors on here have terrible feedback scores. Supertrips is one who comes to mind. He makes ~ $500k a month and last I checked he had a sub 90 (just see he's actually recovered to 96) feedback score.

Basically don't sweat it too much bro :)
