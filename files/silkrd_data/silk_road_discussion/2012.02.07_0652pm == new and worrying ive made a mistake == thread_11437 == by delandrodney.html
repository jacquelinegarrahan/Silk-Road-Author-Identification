Relax :-)
It depends on from where you've ordered. If you've ordered domestically it often arrives the next day but usually within a few days. If you've ordered from another continent it will generally take at least a week.

Quote from: delandrodney on February 07, 2012, 06:52 pm
one more question being.. to my understanding all orders have to be finalized by the buyer before the bitcoins are transfered to the sellers account, so if i wasnt hearing back from sellers that ive placed orders with but havnt finalized, how would i go about reclaiming my coin?

After a few days a 'resolve' link will be enabled and you'll be able to propose a resolution (e.g. refund or resend) to the seller and a mediator (SR administrator). If the seller disputes your claim and can make it plausible that he has sent the order then you'll probably only be partially refunded (e.g. 50%); it's possible that the order 'got lost' in the mail or that the buyer tries to scam the seller.

What did you say when you contacted the vendor? There's not much the vendor can do if you chose not to encrypt your address - it's on SR now unencrypted, end of story.

But it's a minor error really. The advantage of encrypting is simply that no matter what happens (SR bust, infiltrated etc), only you and the vendor will be able to read it.

Encrypt it in future - it's an extra security step you can take to protect yourself.

Assuming SR doesn't get busted in the next couple of days, you'll be fine because your address will disappear off SR once the vendor changes your order status from 'processing' to 'in transit'.

What the vendor does with your address at his end is up to him. We assume and hope they put it on an envelope and then destroy all further evidence of it, but who knows?
