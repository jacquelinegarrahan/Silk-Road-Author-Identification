What address do I use?
Use your home address with your official name, not only does this give you plausible deniability but it also doesn't raise any flags. For example, I had a customer and the package was addressed to Brad Pitt, who knows maybe really was Brad Pitt or some guy named Brad Pitt, but the point is don't use bogus names, it will raise flags.

Should I get a P.O. Box:
You should only get a P.O. Box under special circumstances such as:
-Your roommates (parents, etc) going through your mail
-Your packages being stolen, we all know those lazy postal workers just drop the box at the front door if you're lucky you'll get a slip but most people shit bricks when they get a notice.
-Most people think getting a P.O. Box using fake credentials is safe but in fact it's a death trap, if you get caught it's an automatic felony on top of whatever you're getting arrested for.

Should I Finalize Early (FE):
Probably the most controversial issue at the moment. Ultimately it's up to you whether you want to FE or not, normally I would not FE if the vendor insists. I myself have a FE policy for international customers and have had no complaints, it can be tricky but just remember once you finalize SR won't be able to help you. That's why should build relationships with vendors you like to avoid scamming and always check the forums of any scam reports.

Is it true that checking your tracking number via TOR raises a red flag?
It's not certain whether it's true or not but that isn't the point, I don't even hand out tracking numbers to my customers and I urge other vendors to do so as well. Buyers get anxious, nervous, and scared. They'll check their package status every 30 minutes which isn't going to make the package go faster, best way to solve this is to simply not give them the tracking number unless a problem should arise.

What's a good security setup to access SR:
A lot of people and I'm just making this number up but I'm going to say 60% of SR users are on SR while doing something else (fb, chatting, etc). If you're serious about your security then I suggest you follow the following steps:
-Backup important information to an external hard drive
-Reformat all drives containing traces of any illegal activity, I can confirm that ccleaner can get the job done easily and quickly or if you're paranoid you can use something like DBan.
-Once your computer is back online with zero traces of evidence install truecrypt and encrypt a usb drive with a hidden partition.
-On the visible partition you want to put something embarrassing (pictures of your ex, gay porn, tranny porn, etc) and put a simple password like the one you use for facebook.
-On the invisible partition put a complex password with all important files you want hidden away such as TOR, portable pgp, portable ccleaner, etc. Make sure all text documents are encrypted, you never be too sure.
-Always remember to put away your usb stick and only pull out when you need to access those files.

Should I encrypt my entire hard drive using truecrypt?
In my opinion, no, you shouldn't because this will only make you seem more suspicious and will cause for a more through investigation. Keep everything simple, all passwords simple, except for the hidden partition on your usb sitck (some people go up to 26 characters)

Will my address be flagged after a package being intercepted?
This is still up for debate we have reports of address not being flagged and some are, I think if you're a high valued target you'll be flagged but if you order personal amounts I wouldn't worry about.

Is it always better to order domestic?
In terms of shipping time, yes. But it's not always the case, international vendors have very excellent quality products at cheap prices that you can't seem to find domestic (US). I would say it depends on what you're ordering, the main con is the wait time but patience is a virtue.  :D

Does writing "return to sender" on the package really work?
No. In fact it's a waste of time and a risk to even do this and leave the package in your mailbox.

What is a "love letter?"
A love letter is letter sent to you by customs stating that they confiscated a package sent to you that contained something illegal, it's simply a warning and nothing to worry about.

Should I ever sign for my package?
Sometimes. Some vendors actually require a signature and some don't. Also if you're not home to receive the package they'll leave you a notice to pick up the package and sign for it, nothing to be alarmed about just usual protocol.

When should I not sign for my package?
I would advise not to sign international packages or packages that contain large quantities.

What is a controlled delivery?
A controlled delivery is a method used by LE to catch someone red handed. Basically they intercept your package, open it up to make sure it does in fact have illegal substances, reseal it as if it was never opened and then simply wait for you to open it up.

When does a controlled delivery occur?
Rarely, I don't mean to make you guys more paranoid but if you're buying personal amounts you have nothing to worry about because remember it takes resources for LE to do a controlled delivery and you'll have to be worth those resources so they usually only occur when someone is buying large amount (lbs, kilos, etc) and consistently.

Which vendor should I pick?
Do your research, go through their vendor page, their recent feedback, search their name on the forum, etc. Sometimes higher prices doesn't always mean higher quality and vice versa. I suggest you build relationships with vendors so they know who you are and are more comfortable when dealing with you and who knows maybe they'll throw in some free goodies for being a loyal customer ;)
