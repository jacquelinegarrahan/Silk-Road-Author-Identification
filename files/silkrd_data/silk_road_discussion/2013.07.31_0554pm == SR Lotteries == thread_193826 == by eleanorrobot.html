The nature of gambling lends itself to people wishing to take advantage of others.

That being said, the transaction ID is indeed a very innovative method of running a lottery service, as it is a number provided by Silk Road and randomly generated.  I wouldn't expect any official admin endorsement of these type of lotteries, but it is at least mathematically legitimate, aside from people claiming misleading odds.

Much better than the "I'll put everyone's name in a hat after two weeks and message the winner!" scams.

However, lotteries don't have to be nefarious in nature or have misleading odds or a huge house cut.

I will also add that I don't think it is appropriate for a libertarian/freedom-oriented site to go around regulating the lotteries just because they have bad odds. The free market should solve this problem by itself, and if people want to throw their money away on something, well...I guess that's life for you. At some level to really accept the philosophy of freedom you have to admit that it's okay for people to have to suffer the consequences of their stupidity.
