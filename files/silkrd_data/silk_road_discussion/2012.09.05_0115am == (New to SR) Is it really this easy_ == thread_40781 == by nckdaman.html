Title: Re: (New to SR) Is it really this easy?
Post by: captainjojo on September 05, 2012, 01:48 am
Hi,

Using a fake name AND using an abandoned house is really not recommended.  First the vendor is not going to give you any refund whatsoever if the package is doesn't arrive or is lost.

Secondly you are greatly increasing the chances that your package will be in fact never arrive.  You also increase your risk if you actually get caught picking up the package.  You will have no reasonable way of denying that the package isn't yours.

If the package comes to your house you can always claim you have no idea who sent it and deny it is yours.

The most common method of ordering is to make a small change to your name, such as 'Franc Smith' instead of Frank Smith.  Just enough to make it believable someone sent it to you by mistake.

I am sure if you were to ask around that probably 90% of people ordering either use a safe house (a friend who is completely clean who is willing to take delivery and just hold it.  If they come to his place and it's not open he can just honestly claim he figured it was delivered to the wrong address.  Or, they have it delivered to their own address with a small change in the name (some buyers will use the name of a previous tenant - someone whom they still get occasional junk mail and the like for and simply throw away).

Make sure you read the profile of the vendor you plan to order from and make note of ALL the conditions.  You will find many state outright that if you use a fake name they will not offer any refunds.

Nobody is going to stop you from using an abandoned house or fake name for the order, but honestly, the chances of having something go wrong if you order it to your own address are pretty small and if something does go wrong, the most likely scenario is you will just lose the package, it's unlikely that anyone is going to come banging on your door with a warrant unless you try ordering pounds of weed through the mail.

I've made over 40 orders and never had a problem, only losing a couple of orders from eastern Europe.

I would like to take this opportunity to talk about shipping to a vacant house.

DONT!!!!

Shipping to a vacant house is not a new idea! Back in the 1980's and 1990's people used vacant houses as drop locations for shit that was bought on a stolen credit cards. It was wide spread enough that the post office as well as UPS/FedEx/DHL/Etc.. all watch for this type stuff.

A package showing up to a vacant house is a dead giveaway something is up. Neighbors tend to now what houses are vacant and do notice people walking up to check the mail. This is VERY BAD in a Ghost busters since of crossing the streams.

Get a PO Box, get an apartment, have it sent to your house, have it sent to a friend, or have it sent to grandma for all I care, but I would stay away from a vacant house!
