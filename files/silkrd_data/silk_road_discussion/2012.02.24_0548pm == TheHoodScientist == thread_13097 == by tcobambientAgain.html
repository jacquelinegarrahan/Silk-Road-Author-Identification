Post by: BenJesuit on February 24, 2012, 06:13 pm
I wish people would stop early finalizing. The escrow system is there for a reason. If a seller requires early finalization, move on. Even new buyers. Or a new buyer, if you really must have the product and the vendor requires early finalization, only buy a quantity you are willing to lose. Don't be greedy on your first transaction.

A vendor who is serious about selling would be well financed and doesn't need you to finalize early. Even a "trusted" vendor doesn't need you to finalize early. They can wait. Don't even do it out of "respect" to the trusted vendor. Anything can happen. Even to the trusted seller. Best thing to do with a trusted vendor is to finalize on delivery. Not after you sampled the product.
