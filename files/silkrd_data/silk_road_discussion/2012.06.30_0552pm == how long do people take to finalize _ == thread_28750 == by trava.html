Title: Re: how long do people take to finalize ?
Post by: Spedly on July 07, 2012, 04:56 pm
I finalize as soon as the package arrives and I have had a chance to open it up to quickly inspect the contents. I strongly and firmly believe that at this point the vendor has lived up to their end of our agreement and deserves to be paid for their efforts. I leave temporary feedback rating the packaging, shipping time, and appearance at that time. After I have had a chance to inspect it more closely, weigh it out, and give it a try I then update the feedback accordingly. Sometimes the updated feedback takes weeks because I don't always immediately try the product.

In my experiences the vendors here are very honest, open to feedback, and are all-around good people. I have found that the times where there has been problems, effective communication has always been the key to coming up with a good resolution. I have said this many times but the only thing that ***I*** can offer a vendor is honesty. I remember one specific incident where I ordered some low-grade weed from a respected vendor but when it arrived I found that it was his extremely expensive top shelf stuff. It would have been so easy to not say anything and be happy at my good fortune. However, before I even cut into the vacuum seal I PM'd the vendor and let him know of the mistake and offered to compensate with the additional bitcoins so that everything was square. It turned out that it wasn't a mistake - he intentionally sent me the high grade stuff because he ran out of what I ordered and didn't want to leave me high and dry. You can well imagine that I have a very good business relationship with this particular vendor now and we both know where each other stands in terms of honesty and integrity.

Being prompt with payment, not being a dick, and maintaining 100% accountability for your end of the Silk Road social contract will really go a long ways here.
