UPDATE:  Quite a few people sending deposits from mtgox.com have not received theirs because of problems on mtgox's side.  If a deposit from mtgox is not showing up, please check the block chain at:
https://blockexplorer.com/address/(youraddresshere)
to see if the deposit was even sent.  If not, you need to message mtgox about it.

CORRECTION:  Please message us on the MAIN site via the contact us link, not here on the forums.

Many of the deposits that were sent to the site this week have not been properly credited to the depositors accounts.  We don't have a good way to automatically figure out where the deposits should go, so we are having to process them manually.  If you made a deposit that is taking longer than about six hours to show up, just send us the address the deposit was sent and the amount of the deposit and we'll be able to track it down and credit your account.

Also, to be on the safe side, you should only use newly generated addresses from your account page from now on.  We generated a new address for every account, so the one currently on your account page should be good.

There have been a few strange instances where people's deposits ended up in other users' accounts, so we've disabled withdrawals and transfers until everyone has a chance to claim their deposits.  If you got a deposit that wasn't yours, please let us know so we can get it to the right account.

Cheers   8)

Here's a useful tip for how to NOT use Instawallet: a few days ago I transferred some coins from an Instawallet account to SR.  After I had clicked "Send" the page refreshed & saw my new balance (the original balance minus the coins I had just sent).

I then hit the refresh button on the browser and bam! the same amount of coins were again sent, in spite of the fact that the "Amount" field was blank & there was no receiving address.  For a few hours I worried that some of my coins had just vanished into space, 'cause you know that seems to happen with Bitcoins...  A few hours after the initial transaction had shown up the second, unintended transaction, also showed up.

Moral: don't hit the refresh button on your browser just after you've sent a few coins from Instawallet.
