Post by: phubaiblues on November 02, 2011, 07:14 pm
Yep, it's kind of hazardous lately, being a newbie, but you are ahead of most of them, by coming to the forums first, rather than buying haphazardly and getting off to a bad start or getting ripped off, which has happened a lot lately.

Mostly, just show some caution.  Latest constant scam around here is scammers trying to sell bitcoins which may or may not exist.  Because of no escrow, they usually don't.  As posted above, I'd learn to navigate get-bitcoin.com, which, if you have a Bank of America, or Wells Fargo bank branch near you, is very easy....

Second, I don't think all sellers require that newbies bypass escrow.  I don't like it, but even sellers I like and trust often require it.  They used to start threads about newbies who either wouldn't finalize or scammed, but now they just won't do escrow, which means the good suffer as well as the bad.  Mostly, just make sure it is a reputable seller, here on the forums....the feedback on the main site no longer means very much, as it is all too easy to fake: get a bunch of 'buyer's accounts and just buy from yourself

But in the forums you can tell who is good seller, who is chronically late, and who is total no-show.  Best wishes to you, and I've read thru all the posts in Security, and that is a great education, and pulpfiction's newbie guide also is great...or u can just pm people you are curious about, also...most are pretty friendly and helpful, tho asking publicly is better, so all can learn...
