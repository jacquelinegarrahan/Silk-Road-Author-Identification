Title: Re: What am I doing wrong?
Post by: Frylexa on July 27, 2013, 05:49 am
Your first mistake was messaging vendors encrypted right off the bat. Most of us don't have the time to decrypt anything but sensitive information. A quick hello and inquiry I don't think counts.  :P
Title: Re: What am I doing wrong?
Post by: findingcure on July 27, 2013, 06:11 am
Frylexa is right. Encryption is only for sensitive information. If you haven't told anything about yourself or such then there shouldn't be reason to use encryption. If you sent your (encrypted) question to some more known and reputable vendor, it probably takes time as well since they get most messages, and those without encryption pass faster naturally. No one here is not going to be without clients. Though its possible you get your answer bit later, but that's slow business for both parties. ::)
Title: Re: What am I doing wrong?
Post by: Rastaman Vibration on July 27, 2013, 06:28 am
Agree, the reason you're not getting replies is because you're using PGP off the bat. Vendors are too busy for that (unfortunately for safety conscious buyers like you). Maybe a better strategy is to message them unencrypted, without revelating anything that makes you uncomfortable, and ask them if they would mind if you communicated by PGP for things you'd rather not discuss without encryption.

Title: Re: What am I doing wrong?
Post by: Hungry ghost on July 27, 2013, 07:32 am
You shouldn't feel silly, although what everyone else says is true. All over the forums is 'use PGP use PGP' and you've obviously taken the time to educate yourself before diving in. And some vendors will like ALL coms encrypted. But yes most messages can be sent plaintext unless otherwise instructed. A good tip is to put your actual question in the subject line of a message, if its a simple one. This makes it easier for them as they can read it straight off.
        If you like the sound of those vendors try again with unencrypted. Read their vendor page carefully to check your question isn't answered. There usually isn't a huge amount to talk about, but when you're new the urge to say hi is understandable.
        DO encrypt your address when you make your order though, obviously. Enjoy your trip!
