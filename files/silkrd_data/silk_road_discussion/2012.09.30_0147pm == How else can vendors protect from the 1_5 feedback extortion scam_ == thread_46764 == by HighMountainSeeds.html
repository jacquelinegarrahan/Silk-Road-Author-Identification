Title: How else can vendors protect from the 1/5 feedback extortion scam?
Post by: HighMountainSeeds on September 30, 2012, 01:47 pm

After enjoying a sterling reputation for a couple of months, that has been shattered by someone I feel is trying to pull one over on me.

I shipped two orders to a user, both with DCNs, both say DELIVERED.

I have since deleted the PMs, but I think one of those was a re-ship because the user told me he didn't receive his order. In both instances, I even sent free seeds to try and be nice.

The user now has given me 1/5 saying he didn't receive anything.

I'm now left holding two DCNs for different addresses, in different STATES, and I didn't put two and two together until it was too late. I have my BTC (I think we're talking like 5BTC here) but now I also have ruined feedback.

I have tried to follow the rules... I delete my PM inbox every now and then, and I use DCN. I almost always communicate in PMs just to let folks know what is going on with my shipping times. I'm working really hard to be a good vendor.

I have stored the DCNs in multiple layers of encryption but now I'm starting to think I need to keep all records encrypted on my end, including PMs, just to be able to prove that I am not a scammy vendor.

I have contacted SR Admin, and they want to be patient about the whole process and suggested I contact the buyer directly. I have now done this, but I really don't want to be extorted any further. I shipped two packages and receive payment for one. The 1/5 is not deserved at all.

This must be the downside to the anonymous marketplace. Ugh!
