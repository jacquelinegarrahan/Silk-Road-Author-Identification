Title: Re: MDMA effects on middle aged people?
Post by: SmCoop on April 27, 2012, 02:16 pm
I've always thought of mdma as an older person's drug.  By older, I mean late 20s thru late 30s.  While there is some heart rate increase, pure "molly" tends to be very relaxing when dosed properly.  Us older folks (I'm 35) love the euphoria as well as the increased sociability it brings.  Given the right conditions, the sex is fantastic also!  There are much worse drugs out there for someone getting up there in age (meth/coke).

I think part of the reason that there is so much crap out there on the street (in the States anyway) is that a lot of the pressed pills and even powder "molly" is cut with speed, caffeine, or some other crap that there is an expectation that X is going to fill you with insane amounts of energy to dance the night away.  In that sense, it does become safer for the younger crowd.

On the flip side, some young adults (late teens/early 20s) are still in the later stages of brain development.  You could argue that pure mdma may have a chance of causing more permanent detrimental effects if not used responsibly.

I personally know users from 23-56 and all of them love it.  I think if you're an otherwise healthy adult, the risks are relatively low.  I think the important thing is to be smart about it and know your limits....and most of all, have fun!
