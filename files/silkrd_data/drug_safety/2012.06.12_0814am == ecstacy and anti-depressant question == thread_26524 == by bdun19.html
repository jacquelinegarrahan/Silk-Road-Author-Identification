Quote from: oscarzululondon on June 13, 2012, 12:23 am
Quote from: Vinnyg007 on June 13, 2012, 12:18 am
If you're on SSRI's than you risk the chance of acquiring serotonin syndrome from the combination and dying. Both compounds affect the reuptake of serotonin in the synapse.

That's a bit extreme, it can be possible but very unlikely. I was 2 grams a night when I was on SSRI's just to feel a weak buzz (similar to half a beer), and it never killed me. I got all the shitty negative side effects though! (Dehydration, speedyness, sleeplessness etc).

It's not that extreme - the combination is actually pretty dangerous, especially because you will feel little effect and be inclined to use even more.

Also, serotonin syndrome itself is a very tricky condition: it can progress very quickly from feeling hardly anything to breaking out in serious fever, twitching, and eventually shock, possibly resulting in death.

Even worse, there really is little treatment for the condition apart from managing symptoms are trying to remove what (combination of) drugs was causing it. Removing the cause will often quickly reduce the symptoms though, so if you are ever found in this conditions its extremely important to tell medical staff what you have been taking (if you still can) so they know whats going on, and act accordingly. The symptoms are  not very specific, and attempted treatment without such knowledge could make things worse. 
Title: Re: ecstacy and anti-depressant question
Post by: Tryptamine on June 14, 2012, 02:28 am
Paxil works in part by fucking with your serotonin metabolism, increasing its activity. MDMA works by antagonizing the effects of serotonin, removing it from its binding sites on proteins, causing it to be expelled from cells and catabolized. If you have higher serotonin activity than normal the MDMA will be less effective; this is also why 5-HTP dampens the effects of MDMA.
