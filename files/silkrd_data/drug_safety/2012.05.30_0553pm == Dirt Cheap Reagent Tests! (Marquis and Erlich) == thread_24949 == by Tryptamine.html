Title: Re: Dirt Cheap Reagent Tests! (Marquis & Erlich)
Post by: Ben on June 30, 2012, 12:28 am
Considering formaldehyde is a gas, preparing the reagent is not the easiest thing to do outside of lab conditions. To prepare the reagent on the spot its probably most practical to keep a (nearly saturated) solution of formaldehyde in methanol in stock, and mix that carefully with sulfuric acid just prior to performing the test.

Having some methanol in the marquis reagent isn't a bad thing since it will slow down reaction rate, allowing you to observe any color changes better.
