Title: Re: Using Ibogaine to Treat Opiate Addiction
Post by: l0574l0ne on August 17, 2012, 11:42 am
Honestly I wouldn't touch Iboga (or any other psychedelic) as a potential opiate 'cure'. They scare the shit out of me.

The problem that I see with Ibogain treatment is that it depends on someone really wanting to quit to be effective. If you have that deeply imbedded desire, then I guess there's a decent number of people who ride the trip and come out the other end even stronger committed to quitting and without DTs.

That said, I am kinda skeptical about how effective Ibogain can be. Three days of ball-tripping hallucinations sounds a lot worse than a tapered detox to me. Perhaps its preferable to methadone or suboxone, because god knows I've heard terrible things about those detoxes. But compared to tapering with morphine it seems worse.

If the science guys can separate out the psychedelic effects from the withdrawl-canceling stuff, then we'll really be on to something. The ideal has to be quitting without DTs or hallucinating.
Title: Re: Using Ibogaine to Treat Opiate Addiction
Post by: moonflower on August 17, 2012, 06:47 pm
ibogaine has had excellent results in the treatment of opiate addiction. but a warning: it can be very dangerous. one of the few known psychedelics that can actually be fatal. it should only be taken under the supervision of someone with a medical background, who can monitor vital signs. that being said, there are safer alternatives that have the potential to help with addiction. i'd suggest a good dose of psilocybin mushrooms, or an ayahuasca brew. some of the strongest plant medicine and much safer than ibogaine.
