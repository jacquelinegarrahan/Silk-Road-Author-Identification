Post by: frogtrap on May 18, 2012, 09:35 pm
Yeah, I'm a firm believer in the fact that pets can be a HUGE positive while under the influence of pretty much any drug.

I know for a fact that one of my cats, a "tuxedo" cat (whom we'll call Tux to protect his identity ;) ), has helped two people out of a bad trip. One of those people he didn't even know.

He can definitely tell when my friends and I are on a mind-altering substance, and he's usually curious about what we're up to and how we're feeling. He's always sitting on me or next to me unless he's eating or stalking something/someone. One time, my roommate and I shared a quarter of shrooms, and the roommate starting getting really scared and cried about how it was stupid of him to have taken the mushrooms, what if he died, etc. (this was not his first time doing shrooms :P ). I did my best to talk him out of it, but the most help came from Tux. My roommate sat on the floor and basically went into the fetal position, weeping, with me trying to talk to him about how he was going to be okay (to no avail) when Tux walked up to him and started basically making my roommate pet him. He'd meow softly and inquisitively and then burrow under his arm and make my roommate's hand slide over his head and down his back. He only had to do it a couple of times before my roommate started to pet him and respond to the meows by talking back to Tux. It took my cat between thirty seconds and a minute to bring my friend back to a totally happy place, where he could appreciate what was going on without becoming overwhelmed.

One more stupid pets & drugs story (and it is REALLY stupid). When we (me and the same roommate) were really young (like 17), we had a ton of relatively weak rolls, and we got together with a bunch of our friends at someone's house to take them one day. We brought our pitbull-mutt along, because she loves people and is one of the nicest dogs ever. Well, we were coming up on the rolls and someone asked me if I thought it would kill a dog her size to take one of the pills. I said I didn't think so, based on body weight, but other than that I had no idea, since I'd never read anything about dogs and MDMA. They responded by saying "Good, because she ate one of mine." My response: ಠ_ಠ . It turned out to actually be fine, and the dog seemed to really enjoy the experience... She just walked around with gigantic pupils rubbing up against people, loving pets, drinking water, and occasionally urinating on the floor. Can't say I'll ever administer drugs to a pet (mostly just because they can't tell me if they don't want it), but the unintended experiment worked out well, all things considered. I mean, we could have been mauled by an over-stimulated pitbull. Good thing she's a lover not a fighter. :)
