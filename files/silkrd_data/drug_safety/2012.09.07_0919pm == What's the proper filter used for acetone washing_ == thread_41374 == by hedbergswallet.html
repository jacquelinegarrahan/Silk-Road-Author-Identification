Title: Re: What's the proper filter used for acetone washing?
Post by: Ben on September 09, 2012, 01:17 am
It doesn't matter very much which type of filter you use. Something like a coffee filter will do fine at stopping large particles from getting though.

Filters are sometimes used to separate solutes depending on size, and in that case the properties do matter. If the goal is to filter out particles that do no dissolve at all, most filters will work fine. For convenience sake i'd suggest using a coffee filter, but there is no reason to assume a well folded piece of kitchen roll would perform worse.
