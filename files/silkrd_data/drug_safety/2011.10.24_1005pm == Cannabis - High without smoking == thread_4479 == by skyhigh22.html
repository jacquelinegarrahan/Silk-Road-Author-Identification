Title: Re: Cannabis - High without smoking
Post by: bjhaus on October 26, 2011, 12:51 am
I think Skyhigh22 means he is going to wrap it in some paper and swallow it as in parachuting some powder. If this is the case it won't work.  Eating bud or hash raw is extremely inefficient and a waste of product as your gastro-intestinal system is not very good at extracting THC from solid material.

Also bud/hash does not need to be 'activated', this is a total myth, a popular myth, but a myth just the same. THC can be extracted from raw material by heating, but the raw material needs to be heated in combination with a high fat content substance. Most commonly butter is used. This heating process extracts the THC from the bud/hash and transfers it into the butter. The butter can then be used in any recipe that normally uses butter but the resulting product will get you high.

An alternative way to extract the THC without heating is to soak bud/hash in high proof alcohol, but it needs to soak for a few weeks at least. The resulting liquid a.k.a 'Green Dragon' will be quite potent and needs to be drunk in moderation.

EROWID has some good recipes - http://www.erowid.org/plants/cannabis/cannabis_faq_eat.shtml

Agree with vaping. I have a glass eagle bill vaporizer which works fairly well; there is no recognizable smell. It does pretty much smell like popcorn. so i had an idea after reading about firecrackers. i like the premise of this food, it's easy to make, and sounds relatively non-disgusting. however, there are a couple drawbacks.

food storage, handling (they sound messy), and cooking odors (they say its minimal, but i need a method that is stealth).

so i thought what if you were to put the veg. oil/pnut butter/cannabis mix in a small canning jar, and then boiled the canning jar for an hour or two.

i'm assuming the heat will release the thc/boids without busting the jar (canning jars are supposed to be boiled). this also is an odorless method since the material is locked inside an airtight container under water, and it's pretty easy to spread some peanut butter on a cracker or toast. it's also relatively easy to store and keep stashed (whereas food will attract rodents).

any thoughts from the more experience cannabis cooks?

edit:: i went ahead and started a test batch. 0.283g of high grade cannabis finely chopped in a mixture of 2 parts pnut butter, 1 part veg oil (1tbs=part). I mixed them together, and put them into a 1oz. canning jar, sealed the lid, and placed into a pan of boiling hot water.

i will let the jar boil for at least 30 more minutes, place in the fridge for an hour and then try the entire contents of the jar on a piece of wheat toast.

i'll post a report later this evening.
