Title: Re: first time mdma at a rave
Post by: junjo on September 05, 2013, 02:45 pm
Quote from: OOTOMXYZOO on September 05, 2013, 02:17 pm
thank you i looked at the site you gave here. only one thing i don't get. what is the differences between pills and powder?

Pills and powder should in theory be 100% MDMA, but that's rarely true. Both can be full of adulterant (it's a common myth that powder Is pure MDMA and pills dirty; powder can be just as cut or fake as the pills). If you're buying from the SR, make sure you purchase from a trusted vendor with 100s of buyers confirming that it's actually MDMA, otherwise if it's just coming off the street, you need a marquis reagent test kit to confirm you're not getting meth or possibly something a lot more dangerous.

I'd also recommend caution with using using MDMA for the first time at a rave. For many, the come up and "body load" can be extremely overwhelming and cause you to have a panic attack, ruining your night. Do a test at home in a quiet, peaceful environment first. Just make sure to wait about a month between your uses, so if your rave is coming up immediately, you may want to reconsider using it at all. If you do though, take nothing more than a COMMON dose (100-120 mg is very standard) and do an allergy test (~20 mg) of it before the rave to make sure your body doesn't adversely react to it.

Have fun and stay safe!

"Dying friends" are colloquial hearsay, and I call bullshit on it. The severe adverse reaction incidence of MDMA is less than that of acetylsalicylic acid. That's right, simple Aspirin kills more people than MDMA. This myth of "deadly" MDMA has been perpetuated by governments and is based on bad data sets, e.g. the numbers include EVERY death where MDMA was found our mentioned without regard to the actual cause of death. (meaning, a guy rolling monday and getting hit by a bus on wednesday gets counted as MDMA-related death etc).

Then there are relatively high numbers of multi-drug users included in statistics, and the few death cases that actually come back to MDMA are usually caused by hyperthermia or hyponatremia (meaning people cook their brain by massively overheating their bodies or breaking their brain with water poisoning because they drink too much water because they are paranoid about dehydration). Again, it wasn't the drug that killed them but being stupid.

There is a very very minute fraction of the general population in western nations that is prone to getting serotonin syndrome even at small dosages, but again, that fraction is smaller than the fraction that will have a sever adverse reaction to aspirin.

So, yes, long story short, MDMA is an extremely benign substance.
