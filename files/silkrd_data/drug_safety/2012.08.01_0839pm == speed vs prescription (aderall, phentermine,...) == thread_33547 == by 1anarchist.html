Don't rely on amphetamines or their near relations for the purposes of weight loss; You may suppress your appetite & affect the metabolic system, but the weight you save in the first instance you will gain back and more when you stop taking it; This is why there are fat tweakers, because they did not fall fully into the category of wanton addicts, and end up with withered and emaciated stick figures instead. It's also the case that amp is not much good for working out and general exercise, as the added cardiac stress can be hazardous, and the vasoconstrictive effects mean less oxygen less efficiently for rapidly tiring muscles. Overall, not a good idea. Just maintain a reasonable defecit in your calorie intake vs expenditure, and incorporate resistance exercises heavily into your routine, ahead of cardio.
