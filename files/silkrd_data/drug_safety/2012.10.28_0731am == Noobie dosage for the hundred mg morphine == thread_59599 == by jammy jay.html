Title: Re: Noobie dosage for the hundred mg morphine
Post by: mercurysolid on October 29, 2012, 10:52 am
If it's MS Contin - a continuous release product - then the 100mg won't all be released at once, and will instead be meted out over time. That said, 100mg of continuous release morphine is far too much for somebody who doesn't have a decent opiate tolerance. At best you'll be semiconscious and at worst you might require hospitalisation.

A safe but effective level of effect can be achieved by using a sharp knife to split the pill into two equal halves, and then split one of the halves into halves. You can then consume one of the quarters without ill effect, with the standard disclaimer that you might be hypersensitive to opiates, and that I'm making assumptions about your weight being about average.

You could chew the quarter tablet to get a quicker onset. Do not consider consuming any more than a quarter of the pill before 12 hours has elapsed.

An example might help: I do not have an opiate tolerance. I weigh about 110 kg. If I were to take 25 mg of MS contin and then follow this 4 hours later with another 25 mg of the same, I would become unpleasantly affected by morphine and would vomit.

However, if the morphine pill you have is no continuous release and is instead an immediate release tablet then do not take the above advice as it will likely render you unconscious, although you are unlikely to die unless you lose consciousness and adopt a face-up position and then the opiate causes you to vomit into your mouth, and then aspirate the vomit.

If it is an immediate release pill and you take the whole thing it is likely that you will overdose to the extent that you require hospitalisation or at least informed medical care. If you are hypersensitive to opiates 100 mg is likely to kill you.

If you have an immediate release tablet then consider dissolving it in 100 mL (~3 flOz) of hot water and drinking 10 mL of the resulting solution. 10 mg of morphine is likely to be enough to give you a reasonable effect.
