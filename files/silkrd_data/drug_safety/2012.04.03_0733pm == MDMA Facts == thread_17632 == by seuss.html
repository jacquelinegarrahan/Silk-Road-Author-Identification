Post by: Gigles on April 04, 2012, 02:54 pm
A few tips to reduce potential damage from MDMA and make sure you don't "lose the magic":

1. Keep doses reasonable.
     The higher the dose, the more potential neurotoxicity. A lot of the misleading studies in the 90s/2000s claiming brain damage from MDMA came from administering the human equivalent of 500mg+ MDMA to rats, and it really fucked up their brains. Don't be those rats.. If you're taking more than 200mg of pure stuff in a night you're building up a tolerance and need to scale back your use. Don't redose more than once, it increases potential neurotoxicity.

2. Space out your rolls.
    Alexander Shulgin (contemporary father of MDMA) suggests you roll at most 4 times a year. So I try to stick to that. But if you wait at least a month between your rolls you probably won't run into (many) negative side effects.

3. Try to keep your body temperature relatively cool.
   If you are at a very hot & packed club/rave, and people are literally drenched in sweat, it may not be the best environment to roll in. People usually worry about your body temp in terms of the possibility of collapsing from exhaustion, but they don't realize that higher body temperature also significantly increases MDMA neurotoxicity, at least in animal studies. If you are going to be in a hot place while rolling, taking breaks from dancing periodically and drink water or sports drinks.
