http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12727693

"RESULTS:

A 25%-30% decrease in the maximal extent of dopamine stimulation of adenylyl cyclase activity was seen in the striatum (nucleus accumbens, caudate, and putamen) of the methamphetamine users. No changes were found in basal or guanylyl-imidodiphosphate-stimulated enzyme activity."

Not sure how similar mephedrone is in relation to amphetamines but DA down regulation will occur with long term use.

Just because dopamine doesn't take a long time to restore doesn't mean there aren't other negative side effects.  You can end up damaging your prefrontal cortex over time, which has a wide range of implications, one of the main ones being problems with impulsivity.  Alcoholism has this type of effect.  You might be able to restore your dopamine but you need to have a better understanding of what happens when you mess with your brain chemistry and how the brain changes over time as you do so.
