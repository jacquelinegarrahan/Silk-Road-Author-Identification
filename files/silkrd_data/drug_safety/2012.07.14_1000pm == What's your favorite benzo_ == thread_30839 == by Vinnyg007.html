Title: Re: What's your favorite benzo?
Post by: Irumi on July 22, 2012, 06:47 pm
Personally, I enjoy Clonazepam the most. It lasts long without going into the Diazepam-esque crazy half life.

Xanax is fun and seems the most euphoric benzo to me, but I'm really wary of taking Xanax long term. Due to it's extremely short half life, xanax wears off quickly compared to most other benzos and this leads to redosing periodically for a lot of people, and that ultimately can lead to benzo addiction.

I've used benzos safely for years and always make sure I cut back if I take too many in a short period of time. You do not want to become addicted to benzos!! Benzo withdrawal can be fatal. Always taper down when you are quitting benzos after taking them long term; ideally with a physician monitoring your progress.

Quote from: Irumi on July 22, 2012, 06:47 pm
Benzo withdrawal can be fatal. Always taper down when you are quitting benzos after taking them long term; ideally with a physician monitoring your progress.

I would love to see the documentation of this.  To the best of my knowledge there has been only a single fatality due to benzodiazepine withdrawal documented in medical literature. 

#####  clearnet warning     http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19465812  ######

If you read the abstract of the report linked above, you will see that there is substantial doubt whether alprazolam withdrawal actually caused this death.  The abstract does, however, mention one other benzodiazepine withdrawal fatality.

Even if I allow that both this case and the other one were indeed benzodiazepine withdrawal fatalities, I would never go spouting that benzo withdrawal can be fatal.  Why?  Because the chance of it is so incredibly remote that it isn't worth mentioning. 

"Although it is possible that the zolpidem, reportedly taken in the 12 hours before admission, masked the initial symptoms of withdrawal, the constellation of symptoms and signs at presentation are more consistent with benzodiazepine withdrawal than of zolpidem overdose."

OK, so let me paraphrase this.  Doctors hate to say something like this but I will say it for them.  "We don't know why this person died."
