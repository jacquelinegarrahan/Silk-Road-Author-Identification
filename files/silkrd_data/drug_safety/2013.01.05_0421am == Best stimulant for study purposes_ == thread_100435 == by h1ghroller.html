I have used amphetamine (speed) in low dosages (15-30 mg) orally for studying on occasions. I would however like to mention that it becomes very easy to associate amphetamine with studying. Just watch out that your use doesn't become a requirement before you can do your homework/study, because it sure is easy to pop some amphetamine and feel motivated and alert. Even though it worked really well for me and I wasn't using it for anything other than homework/studying, I do feel that the potential damage from amphetamine use is not be worth it.

If you do decide to use it as a study aid then make sure you keep up your regular eating schedule and avoid staying up for more than 1 day. Even though it might not feel like it, sleep deprivation does chip away at your cognitive abilities. As mentioned in another reply amphetamines can really make you focused to the extreme of obsession. For example, you could spend hours rewriting a paragraph or analyzing an exam question. This becomes especially prominent if you take high dosages. I would also suggest oral dosing for a longer lasting effect and to spare your nose.

Also keep in mind that if you're studying on a drug (also counts for caffeine) to also dose before taking the exam. The way the human learning process works is that your memory stores a lot more than just what you've been reading. The context and situational influences are all taken into account. For the best performance you want these to as closely match the exam setting as possible.
