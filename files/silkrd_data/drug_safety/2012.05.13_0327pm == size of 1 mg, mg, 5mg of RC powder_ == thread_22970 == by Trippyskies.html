328502E dont be sarcastic and belittle someones statement if you dont yourself understand what you are blabbering about

well 1mg is a set mass? duh... you really missed an important aspect, known as density. every chemical has a different density which means the volume the chemical takes up in space is different for the mass of each respective molecule...

1 mg of a high density substance will be extremely small, a speck that could fit within the point from the lead of a .5 mm mechanical pencil, easily

1 mg of a low density substance will be much more fluffy and take up more area, like the size within this "o" about the size of a small seed.

for example, 1 mg of rocked heroin is a few grains of sand. once crushed and powdered with a lower density, a larger pile is produced. both still 1 mg

ONE SOULD ABSOLUTELY NEVER EYEBALL SUCH A TINY MASS. IT IS NOT POSSIBLE TO DO ACCURATELY. especially for 2c-p, with such a steep dose response curve, only a couple milligrams can be the difference between not tripping, tripping enjoyably, or overdosing.

a volumetric measurement as suggested above should be used. every molecule has a different solubility in every solvent, so you cant use a blanket statement for all, but most RCs used these days seem to be fairly soluble within alcohol. This includes phenethylamines (2c-x)
