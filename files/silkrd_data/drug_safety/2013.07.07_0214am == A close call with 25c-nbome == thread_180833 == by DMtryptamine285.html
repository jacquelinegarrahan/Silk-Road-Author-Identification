Title: A close call with 25c-nbome
Post by: DMtryptamine285 on July 07, 2013, 02:14 am
A close call with 25c-nbome

Dose: 1 mg exact
Substances combined: Phenibut .5 gram, and 1 cannabis joint smoked.

Dosed at 12:00 pm, 1mg complexed blotters sublingually. I held all the juices in my mouth and tried my best not to swallow any of the liquid. I started coming up in about 45 minutes and it took about 2 hours to fully peak. While waiting for the comeup I took a walk around the block and then came back inside and chatted with some people online. First real effect I noticed was that I was typing incredibly fast. I went to go check my pupils and they where fully dilated. I was quite dissapointed that I wasn't getting any visuals and just an extreme LSD like body buzz so I decided to smoke some cannabis, really bad idea.

After lighting up I felt as though my pulse was racing uncontrollably, while I felt euphoric I also felt quite anxious and tense. I placed two fingers on my neck and could feel that my BP and HR must be elevated (although without a cuff I cannot discount that this was merely a perception). Peripheral vasoconstriction was then noted after the cannabis effects began to wane. It felt as if I was getting stabbed in the leg with a sharp knife, slight discoloration of extremities was also noted and vains seems to protrude more than usual. This made me a bit concered and I started to fall into a little bit of a bad trip.

Still no visual distortions other than very very mild breathing and shifting of images, but I got caught in a bit of a thought loop and it seemed as though I would think one thought, then go on to another, and somehow that second thought would lead back to the first one which was then repeated endlessly. Breathing became somewhat labored (may have been all in my head), and I felt as though I was beginning to overheat (I am in el paso and It is 104 degrees today, and I going between inside and outside, back and forth, so I may have actually been overheating. I started guzzling down water, got in the shower and turned it as cold as it would go. This made the vasoconstriction ten times worse (although the overheating was starting to feel better), because heat causes vasodilation I turned the water to hot and the vasoconstriction subsided but the overheating sensation returned (unsuprisingly), This hot water, cold water balance between vasoconstriction and body temperature went on for about 30-45 minutes until I finally began to feel the effects subside a tad, at which point I just went and lied on the couch until I was back to baseline. Left with a bit of a lingering headache now. Reminded me quite a bit of bromo-dragonfly (although not near that bad as far as vasoconstriction goes, bromo made my foot go completely bluish/purple, I actually had to take a vasodilator when I took bromo or I would have ended up in the ER.)
