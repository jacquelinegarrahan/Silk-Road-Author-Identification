Title: Re: Permanent Opiate Tolerance?
Post by: alanko007 on September 14, 2012, 07:54 am
As far as I know there are 2 types of a tolerance regarding opiate use. A short-term one and a long-term tolerance. Short-term tolerance is the one all users know. You take opiates regularly for some time and your brain gets used to it - you need more. But when you take opiates for a long time (months, years), your dopamine receptors get really worn out. And it takes a looong time for them to recover. That is when long-term tolerance applies and it will never feel the  same as the first few times. It can take months, years or even decades for them to recover. The side-effect of this happening is PAWS (post-accute withdrawal syndrome) when people feel depressed, anxious etc. for years after they stopped.
