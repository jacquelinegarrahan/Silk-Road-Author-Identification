Title: Re: Hey potheads: Indica or Sativa?
Post by: Jack N Hoff on August 12, 2013, 05:05 am
Quote from: Bennett4545 on August 12, 2013, 04:19 am
From a consumer standpoint, the sativa buds seem to be more hollow, easier to crush and compress with your fingers.  That has been my impression so far.

That is because sativas are almost always taller, lanky with further internode spacing than indicas.  The flowers (bud) forms at the nodes.  The further apart the nodes, the less dense the bud is.  Indiacas on the other hand have very compact internode spacing which will produce very dense buds.  Also, the more intense the light is, the closer the nodes will be.

100% of either is uncommon. Most commonly sold strains of marijuana are hybrids.

That said, strong sativas (90%+ or higher) increase your heart rate and can cause an annoying twitch in the eye due to either the constriction or dilation of the blood vessels. Strong Indicas (75%+) tend to induce couchlock and and desire to rest, or at least remain at rest. I enjoy these the most when watching documentaries or preparing to go to sleep. I fidget in bed when sober and do not when stoned on a heavy Indica.
