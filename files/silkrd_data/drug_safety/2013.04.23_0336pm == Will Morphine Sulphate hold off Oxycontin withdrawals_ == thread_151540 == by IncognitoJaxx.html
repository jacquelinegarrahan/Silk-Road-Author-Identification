Title: Re: Will Morphine Sulphate hold off Oxycontin withdrawals?
Post by: captainjojo on April 23, 2013, 05:19 pm
Yes, the Morphine Sulphate pills will most likely stave off WD's.  All these products are opiates.  They are just of varying strengths.

If you look at an opiate equivalency chart you will see that all the opiates are listed as equal in strength to a multiple of morphine. 

Oxycodone is equivalent to 1.5-2.0 times the dose of morphine.  In other words you need 2mg of Morphine to equal 1mg of Oxycodone.

I would suggest you wait until you feel slightly down and then take one of the 60mg MS Contin's and see how that works out.  You may need to take another half or whole one to equal the 100mg of Oxy you take a day.

I've taken pretty much all the different forms of opiates available.  Codeine, Morphine, Hydrocodone, Oxycodone, Methadone, Diamorphine, Heroin, Hydromorphone, Oxymorphone) and the only difference is the strength of each, although the high from Heroin is different than that from Oxy's.  They all do the job of keeping you WD' free.

Title: Re: Will Morphine Sulphate hold off Oxycontin withdrawals?
Post by: captainjojo on April 23, 2013, 08:15 pm
Yes, it is always a good idea to start with a smaller dose whenever you crossover on opiates. H can be even more dangerous.

With H you never know from one batch to the next what the exact strength will be.  That's how too many users end up overdosing.  Let's say their normal dose is 100mg two or three times a day.  They get a new batch which turns out to be two or three times as strong as their normal batch, they go ahead and IV 100 or 150mg and then next thing they know they have passed out and stopped breathing.

I always start small with a new batch.  Instead of 100mg I'll take 25mg and wait 10-15 minutes to see how it's going, then I'll take more.

It's the same with different opiates.  Something like fenatyl is 50-100 times stronger than morphine.  It's one of the strongest and most dangerous of the opiates and you better really know what you are doing before using it.
