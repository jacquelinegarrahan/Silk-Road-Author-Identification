Title: Re: Advice needed: 2C-B and 4-Aco-DIPT
Post by: DiamondSky on March 27, 2013, 01:55 pm
So 2CB you should be fine with in some liquid suspension, not sure about 4-Aco-DIPT though. Neither really benefit from the process though. 4-Aco-DIPT in particular seems to like being in a cool dark place, which 2CB gets along with pretty well with too. If you keep the powder in vials in the freezer, you'll want to be careful of condensation when you take them out. For me, amber vials on the top shelf somewhere seem to do the trick pretty well. I doubt you would destroy the stash by diluting them but for 4-Aco-DIPT at least it's a bit of an unknown so I would just avoid it or try it with small part of your supply and see how that hits after a few months... the powder in the amber vials will still be fine.

Title: Re: Advice needed: 2C-B and 4-Aco-DIPT
Post by: DiamondSky on March 27, 2013, 11:02 pm
I haven't stored it in liquid myself its just that early reports on the stuff claimed that it was extremely absorbent and reacted to moisture in the air by turning clumpy and discoloring. Just got me thinking that either the sample they had wasn't very clean or the stuff might not react well to liquid. 2CB I can drop in liquid and it just dissolves nice and clean over a couple of minutes or with some shaking. If I were going to do it, I would probably take 10-20mg and drop it in a little vodka and freeze it. If it clumps up in the liquid that would be a pretty good sign that you don't want to suspend the stuff since you need it fully dissolved to know what dose you are going to end up with.
