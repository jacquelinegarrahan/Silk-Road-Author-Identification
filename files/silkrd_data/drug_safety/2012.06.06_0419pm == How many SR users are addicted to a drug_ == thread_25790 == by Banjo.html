I can't comment on the tobacco, cos that bitch has her claws into me too, but as for growing, it doesn't have to be expensive.  I've seen hundreds of cheap-ass "ghetto grows" that have produced the goods on a shoestring budget. 

Normal household "energy saver" cfls will grow weed if you use enough of them, and are cheap as hell to buy.  They are only about half as efficient as proper hps grow lights, but they do the job for a small personal grow.

Ventilation, done properly, is the most expensive part of a grow.  PC fans will work for a very small grow with cfls, but they don't have enough ability to overcome pressure, which means that they are not viable for use with carbon filters.  Carbon filters are used to remove the smells from the exhausted air, which does nothing for the plants, but helps stop you getting busted.  If your living arrangements are secure, and you don't get unwanted guests, you may be able to get away without a proper fan/filter setup for a small grow, as I did on my early efforts (although the house did stink lol), but proper fans and filters give real peace of mind, as well as the ability to grow with hps lights and higher wattage setups in general.

Even a small grow can produce quite well if you do your homework on proper care and training, and can pave the way to bigger grows in the future, since anything you harvest is weed that you would otherwise have had to buy, and the money you save can be put back into equipment for the next run.  You can easily become self-sufficient without too much effort, and even make the grow pay for itself (and then some ;))

Good luck to you, and anyone else reading this who is in the same situation.  I've been a poor, miserable weed smoker myself, and it's a real shame, because it's not the weed that makes you miserable, just the fact that it costs so fucking much.  You would be amazed at just how cheap it is to actually grow your own, in pure cash terms.  There is some effort involved, and a bit of a learning curve, but the whole process can be a pretty fun and massively rewarding hobby.

Title: Re: How many SR users are addicted to a drug?
Post by: ZA6wUw8a on June 13, 2012, 02:34 am
Quote
As long as your not hurting anyone, Does it really matter if someone is an addict?

I don't think the questions on everybody's minds can be reduced to such a simplistic analysis.

Assuming the degree of "hurting" can be measured:
1) If the set of "anyone" excludes yourself (someone is hurting themselves but not others), I think it matters whether they consider the question of whether they have an addiction because such introspection may eventually unlock the ability to stop hurting oneself.
2) If the set of "anyone" includes yourself (someone is not hurting themselves, nor others), I think if you look at the bigger picture, it matters to consider the question nonetheless because:

It is important to think of these matters in practical, not theoretical terms. Even in the case that an addiction causes no harm to the addict or his environment, the addict does not have control over his or her mind in regards to the addiction. I find it difficult to believe that this pattern of thinking in a human being can target only one thing (call it exercise, a drug, sex, etc), and left unchecked (even if it is benign), can eventually lead to problems in other areas of life. I think it is always important to be mindful of habits (mindful, not scared).

I'm not a professional in this matter. You're right that practically the question of whether or not someone is an addict /seems/ to be less meaningful when we're talking about a daily caffeine drinker, but in conclusion I don't think that this kind of analysis considers the whole picture.
