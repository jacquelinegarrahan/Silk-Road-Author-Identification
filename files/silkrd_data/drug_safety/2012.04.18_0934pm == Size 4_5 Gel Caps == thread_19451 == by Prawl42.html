I think I paid around $150 for a set of filler plates covering size 5-00 from china. Size 4 is actually my preferred capsule size. Using 0 and 00 Caps looks unprofessional....and makes people feel like they are being scammed..Smaller colored capsules, and using specific colors for specific compounds works best for me on compounds that Ive too little movement to bother tableting.

If you are unable to procure or are unwilling to spend the initial money for an encapsulator.....you can easily cap #4 DIY. Take a sheet of cardboard. Use a BIC pen to press through the cardboard between two ribs....press just until the smaller removable section of the writing nib has penetrated fully...do not push until the pen color indicator portion has penetrated. Repeat this action 10 times then skipping one cardboard rib repeat the series of 10....until you have the desired number of capsules.

Assuming you have 1 gram of active material....1000mg....100 holes will give you 10mg.....50 holes 20mg etc.
Press the small side of each capsule into a hole. Then without measure or concern for uniformity....dump your active onto the cardboard and scrape it all into capsules....this obviously is not what you want at the end.....once your cardboard no longer has active material available to fill the capsules....spoon some powdered sugar (cornstarch can actually inhibit the uptake of some compounds) Level off the sugar until every capsule is full.

Put your cardboard with improperly filled but full capsules into a ziplock bag. Flip the whole assembly over and carefully tap the capsules backside with a pen until all material has fallen out into the bag. If any capsules fall out just reinsert them into their holes when emptied.

Thoroughly mix you active and sugar. Put the mixed powder back into the capsules. Then carefully press the capsules up from the backside exposing the capsules walls....place the larger capsule halves on top of the capsules....then once they are all covered begin squeezing them one at a time until the capsules "click" engages.

Post by: N2DEEP on April 23, 2012, 12:00 pm
APPA:

homogeneous mixture is relatively easy. As for settling...that takes time....I imagine if one were to place a few capsules in a fixed orientation and applied vibration....you might very well have some substances separate....but once closed its in the capsule...so no real concern. The greater risk, in my experience and opinion, for dose variability lies not in the risk of uneven mixture...not that it is impossible to poorly mix but assuming one is thorough...but rather the uniformity of pack density. It is common practice to "tamp" down capsules add additional powder and tamp again. I do not bother with this and never have. A uniform mixture spread quickly and evenly should yield fairly even fills. I DO, however, like to take my completed batch.... Weigh it as a whole, then I weight 2-3 decreasing quantities (ie whole batch 100 caps, 2nd weigh 50, 3rd weigh 10).....provided the results of my weighing are consistent, I then randomly select a small percent for individual weighing.

If you were SERIOUSLY concerned about the uniformity of their compounding...I guess you could select a filler with a good solubility in a similar liquid as your active ingredient..one could dissolve both filler and active then evaporate off the solvent. I dont really think its necessary though.
