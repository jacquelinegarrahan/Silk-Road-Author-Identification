Title: Re: Xanax AFTER drinking
Post by: Ben on June 05, 2012, 01:24 am
Combining benzo's with alcohol is generally recommended against because of the risk of respiratory depression.

Realistically, if you are in good health, drinking  4 beers followed by half a milligram of xanax is not likely to kill you though. If you are not used to benzo's at all there is a fair chance of retrograde amnesia back to the point you started drinking though.  The extent of that effect is hard to predict, but i would suggest doing this only when some friends are present - not just to get you home safely, but also to tell you what happened in case you do forget completely.

Transient amnesia doesn't sound like that bad a thing until you actually experience it. Having no clue at all about what happened the night before can be pretty upsetting. It's unpleasant to not have a clue what you did when you were somewhat sober, very different from being too drunk to remember details.
