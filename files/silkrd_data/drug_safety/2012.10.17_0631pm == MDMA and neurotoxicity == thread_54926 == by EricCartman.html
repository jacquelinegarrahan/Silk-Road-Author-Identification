Title: Re: MDMA and neurotoxicity
Post by: microRNA on October 17, 2012, 07:20 pm
all amphetamines are slightly neurotoxic. mdma and meth may be particularly harmful due to depletion of serotonergic stores which causes dopamine to be accidentally taken up which may produce reactive free radicals which are especially damaging. but how significant the damage is and how much it effects an individual overall are still in question. occasional use will most likely not have any significant or noticeable effect. moderation is key, its when you start really enjoying the drug or using it to cope with down states that it becomes a serious issue

i will say that during my time of excessive mdma use (very very heavy) i found critical thinking processes and memory to be hindered noticeably. especially complex mathematics such as calculus work became much more difficult, but upon cessation and some time the understanding and ability to perform calculations improved significantly. though still even to this day i am much more emotionally unstable and prone to serious depression possibly exacerbated from my use and stress on my serotonergic system
