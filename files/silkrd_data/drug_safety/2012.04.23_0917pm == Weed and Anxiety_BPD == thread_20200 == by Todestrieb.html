Title: Re: Weed and Anxiety/BPD
Post by: frogger on April 24, 2012, 12:03 am
I guess it all depends on the type of therapy the Dr (You mentioned clinician??) is providing as well as the medication(s) they'll prescribe.

I'd keep your stash, and wait a week or so, and tell them you've gone cold-turkey in order to get treated.   If they ask, tell them you're serious about therapy, and want to get well, and that was your motivation for stopping (Sorry, but I think pot is absolutely harmless) .

Look up the meds they prescribe and.or talk to people on the forum about possible interactions.

Naturally (I assume) the psychiatrist is not going to recommend you continuing illicit drugs while under their care (Assuming your not in a a weed-legal state), therefore they can track your progress in relation to others.

I saw a psychiatrist for a number of years, and found no ill effects or 'springboarding' affects of other medications while in treatment.  If anything it reduced my smoking.

I also found that I didn't have the desire to drink that I had before treatment.

If you decide to go with the Dr., Research the medication and it's side effects thoroughly.  My Doc didn't mention some of the things I experienced, and we went thru a trial and error of meds at the beginning of my treatment.

One last thing:  Did you check out/investigate the Dr you are contemplating to see whether they're board certified to treat the diagnosis you are seeing them for?  And also, I'd never see a GP, or psychcologist; only a psychiatrist.
