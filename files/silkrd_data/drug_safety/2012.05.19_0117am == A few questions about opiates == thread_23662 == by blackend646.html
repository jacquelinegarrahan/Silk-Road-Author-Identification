Title: Re: A few questions about opiates
Post by: n0n00dz4u on May 19, 2012, 01:22 am
There are a variety of opiates available on the market today.

Prescription ones are the most commonly used for recreational purposes. The most popular ones are oxycodone (oxycontin, roxicodone, etc...) and hydrocodone (vicodin, lortab, etc...).

For a new user 10 mg of hydrocodone will be more than enough to introduce you to opiate affects. For oxycodone I would recommend between 5 and 10 mgs for your first time.

Heroin is also very popular but is harder to dose because it is not regulated. I'd save heroin for once you develop a tolerance and are more experienced. Also snorting instead of injecting is a common practice for beginner heroin users.
Title: Re: A few questions about opiates
Post by: spd on May 19, 2012, 05:42 am
1 - the active ingredient in Vicodin is hydrocodone, it's very similar to oxycodone, but slightly different and not quite as strong per mg.  Oxycontin is a pill that releases oxycodone over a prolonged period of time, while roxicodone releases the full amount into your system instantly.  Oxycodone is also the active ingredients in percocets.

2 - I'd recommend slightly higher amounts than the above poster for a really good high, but the amount he recommended will introduce you to the effects.  You can always take another if you like what you're feeling and want a stronger buzz though.

3.  The prices are very high - i love opiates, and have a hard time justifying paying them myself.  Definitely more of an occasional splurge than even a semi-regular thing.  I also know people who have taken vics or percs after getting a script and deciding to try getting a buzz off them and HATING it...calling me after and saying "you LIKE that feeling?" (and luckily, they'd sell me the leftovers dirt cheap!) - so it is very subjective.

Also be careful, if you do try it and like it, keep it to occasional use, tolerance and dependence can go up quickly
