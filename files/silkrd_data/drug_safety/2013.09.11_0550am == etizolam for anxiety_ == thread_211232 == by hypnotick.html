Post by: Ben on September 12, 2013, 12:46 am
As for dose equation for anxiety, 1 mg alprazolam should roughly be as effective as 1.5 mg or etizolaam or at least around that figure.

The legal status of etizolam is a bit of a tricky one. Technically it is not a benzodiazpine, and in most countries it is not a controlled substance in any way. It is, however, a prescription medication in most countries and not available over the counter without a script from a doctor. Prices on SR do not seem that high to me (under $1/tablet).

Personally i've found this drug interesting for treating anxiety: things like xanax suppress the anxiety response very well, but if they fail to do so they are more or less useless. Etizolam, for me, doesnt really block the anxiety, but it reduces the physical response (increased heart rate and respiration) to some degree. 

I think it is a substance worth exploring if you are suffering from anxiety problems - if it doesn't work as well as you want it to there is always the option of true benzos.
