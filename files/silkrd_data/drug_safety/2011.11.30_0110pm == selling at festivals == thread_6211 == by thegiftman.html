Title: Re: selling at festivals
Post by: hyruleantoker on May 27, 2012, 04:24 am
I don't have experience with buying or selling, but I do have a little bit of experience bumming. And it's harder to freeload than it is to buy.
I agree with JohnnyFried on his points, and would like to add a few of my own.

First and foremost, enjoy the fest. Don't just stand still being sketch looking for people, that's the easiest way to scare off potential buyers.
Move around the crowd! Dance around and enjoy the music, while keeping an eye out for someone who's got that look in their eye. Like they're having a good time, but they're kind of looking for a better time. If someone makes eye contact with you and they're smiling, smile back and start a conversation. Ask if they're having a good time, and if they seem right, ask if they're looking for something. Wording can be really important. Don't just go up to someone asking if they're looking for acid or ecstasy. Ease into it, and see if they'll say what they're looking for. Personally, I can find someone who's cool and ask them if they know where I can find some molly.
People will often ask, if they're looking. If you wait long enough, people will come to you.
Try to gauge people's behavior around you. Look around the crowd and find the people who just feel right. Maybe some people can't tell the way I can, but I can just be able to watch someone for a while to determine if they're on something, if they're wishing they were on something, or if they are up-tight. Maybe I'd make a good distributor at medium sized local shows... heh.
When making a deal, don't try to sneak off to a corner. Be in the crowd with people on all sides of you. Make sure there aren't any stern faces around (potential LE), and be discreet. If you need to ask how to be discreet, maybe you shouldn't be considering selling.
After making a deal with someone, tell them to tell their friends. You get a good bit of business from word of mouth. Don't ever underestimate it.

While dealing at large shows can definitely be risky, someone in the crowd is doing it. If they can pull it off, so can you.
Just BE CAREFUL! And make sure if you have people you're with, they're keeping an eye out.

Good luck with whatever you do,
~Toker
