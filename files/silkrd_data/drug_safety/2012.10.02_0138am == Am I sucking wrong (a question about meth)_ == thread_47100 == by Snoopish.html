Post by: goldfibre1 on October 06, 2012, 12:53 pm
Just thought I'd share my meth experiences...

I started out on foil and then found this vacuum ehrlenmeyer flask that works great!  It's pyrex of course and of a medium wall thickness (1/4" or so).  I stuck a chopstick into the vacuum end which sticks out perpendicular to the flask and serves as a handle.  On top of the flask, I placed a two hole stopper.  One hole I use as a carb (alternately placing finger over hole or just leave open) and the other hole I inserted a flexi-straw through down near the bottom so the end of the straw is about 1/4" from the bottom of the flask.  This creates better suction.  I haven't noticed any melting of the straw or stopper. 

I drop in a small rock about 15mg.  I've noticed any bigger and it doesn't vaporize as well.  I use a small tealight to heat the flask near the rock.  The two hole stopper keeps most of the vapor inside the flask.  I wait for the rock to liquefy and for a small amount of vapor to appear before inhaling through the straw.  I found that you shouldn't hold the flask closer than 1/2" or so from the flame or the meth will burn (carbonize).  (Note: Meth boiling point is 216 degrees Celsius and a candle flame can get up to 1,400 degrees Celsius). 

With gentle heating (as soon as you see vapor back off from flame a 1/4" or so), the entire rock will disappear into vapor with very little loss as one would get with foil.  The residual meth condensate on the sides of the flask can be gently heated as well from the outside and inhaled.  So I would say generally this is a fairly efficient way of smoking meth.  The flask should be washed every 2 to 3 times as residual meth will eventually carbonize and impart an unpleasant taste to the vapor. 

As always, I recommend taking a small dose of a benzo for the tachy.

The only thing I've noticed is that meth vapor will stick to anything that is cool.  So in my little flask set-up, as the vapor collects and climbs up the wall of the flask, it starts condensing and forming a thick translucent coating.  If there was a "condenser" around the pipe on the way to your inhalation point, I think more of your ice would stick to it.  When I condenses on the sides of my flask, I just have to heat the flask where it condenses and the ice coating is revaporized. 

I've started to pre-heat the walls of the flask before heating the ice so that less vapor sticks to the sides.  But it eventually collects there but less quickly.

The massive rips are possible with this set up because the flask can heat up enough to keep the ice in vaporizing mode so that as you inhale the volume from the flask, more vapor is being produced.  I've been able to exhale large clouds, which now I try to hold in as long as possible to allow more time for absorption.  I've noticed the longer you hold, the smaller the exhaled cloud which I'm assuming is due to the fact the cloud recondensed onto the lining of your alveoli.
