1. Well, I looked into a mirror at 350 micrograms of lsd and all I saw was my face morphing :P What I read between the lines of your question is the fear of a bad trip. Tripping on LSD usually is a quite exhausting experience. Get enough sleep before you drop, I prefer dropping in the morning so I can sleep 12 hours later... My first trip was after a hard day of work at 9pm, and boy, did I want it to stop and let me sleep... Get a friend to watch you. There is no time you need a hug that badly then when you dropped LSD. Trust me. Otherwise you will find yourself roaming the streets for some stranger to hug...
Too main routes a LSD experience can take, and I experienced them myself, are getting reborn and/or dying. Getting reborn lets you experience the whole process from fertilization to birth. The fetus swimming in a warm, friendly environment. Cosy. Birth is nasty. It is a fight between life and death, usually combined with very strong, sometimes horrible feelings. Imagine a little baby, safe for 9 months, suddenly being squeezed through a narrow channel... And that is where your sitter comes in handy. Newborn babies definitly need some attention from their mother or other people. It really sucks being alone at this point. Trust me. See the points above...
Dying was really interesting. What happens is that your ego dissolves, and that is practically the same thing as dying. Go with the flow and die, accept your death and everything will be fine. Again, dying with a sitter nearby to hold your hand makes the thing a lot more enjoyable.

2. no idea about that :P

good luck on your travels

P.S.: I definitly recommend you reading something about your inner child before tripping. Google it, most of our mental and every day problems root in troubles in our childhood. LSD is a powerful tool to explore these wounds and help to cure them.
