Well, it has properties that make it suitable as a substance to slip to someone in a drink fairly easily. Being liquid helps to do so quickly, and despite its salty taste, people may just think that their wine, beer or cocktail tastes a bit odd, but won't think to much of it as 'salty' isn't generally recognized as dangerous.

Temazepam has suffered the same faith though. Usually its in tablet form, but the gel caps containing dissolved temazepam would be easy to mix into a drink. Considering it causes anterograde amnesia especially in combination with alcohol, i suppose this would actually be more viable then ghb for such purposes.

For legitimate purposes none of this matters, apart from these substances being more actively looked for, driving up prices and making them more difficult to transport.

I've used temazepam as a sleep aid in the past, and its fairly effective. Considering the difficulty obtaining it nowadays i'd suggest going for nitrazepam instead, which also has a decently short halflife. Valium has an extremely long halflife due to its metabolites, so its not really suitable as a sleep inducing drug, unless you want to be sleepy the next day (or two) too. Clonazepam (klonopin) has the same problem, perhaps to a slightly lesser degree.

Xanax isn't all that hypnotic, its more of an anxiolytic benzo, and tolerance against its hypnotic effects will develop very quickly.
