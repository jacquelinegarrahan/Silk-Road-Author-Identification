Post by: GunsoftheNavarone86 on June 14, 2012, 05:42 am
Good thing everything turned out alright for you. I only even inject in my hands if I have no other choice, and I make sure I use a brand new 29-31 gauge needle so I don't bruise the delicate areas. Also, in any injection scenario, the moment the needle goes under the surface of your skin, pull the plunger back just a little bit -- this will create a vacuum in the chamber and the second you've entered a vein you will see the register and you know to stop pushing the needle deeper. This technique results in much less piercing through the vein, and PLEASE don't ever dig for a vein (move the needle back and forth, up and down etc.), always pull the needle completely out and try again normally. I know it hurts a little more to stick yourself again, but the digging does MASSIVE damage people don't realize.
