The thing about it is, if you know a bit about neurochemistry, activation sites, and such, you can generally ( and I use generally extremely liberally here ) figure out to a certain extent the safety profile of a drug based on other drugs in its class. But there are drugs that are completely different and do need to be used with caution. For example, 6-APB. It has effects similar to MDA, but it is not an amphetamine or even a cathinone. It's a benzofuran, and we know VERY VERY little about the effects that benzofuran compounds can have in the long term. Tests have proven that the neurotoxicity in the short term is low, but who's to say it isn't like a chemical such as ketamine, which we are just in the past ten or twenty years or so finding out first-hand can cause EXTREME damage to your kidneys if used daily. And ketamine has been known for ages! It isn't even a research chemical!

The point i'm trying to make is that even drugs we know lots about can eventually prove to be more dangerous than we could have ever imagined. RCs are generally created from the same desire to find novel chemicals that Sasha Shulgin had; or in many cases to find medical treatment to problems we haven't figured out yet. The 25x series of chemicals were made to find a way to map the 5ht pathways in the brain because they are extremely potent agonists of extremely specific parts of serotonin. But when you make these chems and present your findings to committees who decide whether these chemicals get researched or not, you publish information on how to synthesize them. All it takes is for one synth to get leaked and wham, you have an entire world of profiteers trying to make money off of a novel chemical.

RC's aren't made to destroy lives or just to be shit drugs to circumvent laws; that's eventually how they are used, but usually they are developed with truly benign purposes in mind. It's just people with gleams in their eye trying to make money that ruin them for the mainstream.
