Title: Re: Questions regarding "American Weigh Gemini-20 Portable Milligram Scale"
Post by: SandyDamage on November 27, 2012, 11:50 pm
At OP, I love this scale and I got it about 3 weeks ago. The plate that it comes with it, like everyone says, doesn't have a fit/snug position, so you can get varied weights if not using it properly. The weight varies by adding about 3 or 4 mg's to what you are weighing, if the plate is not in the proper position. If you have the scale in front of you at eye level, you can balance the tray straight for the most part, but there is no locking position that it sits in, so it is easy to bump the tray slightly, giving you a different weight.
I got the scale to read the 10 gram calibration weight as 10.000 grams, 5 out of  ten times doing a weight testing. Before each time I did not change the position of the tray, which more than likely has to do with the varied weights. The other five readings were 9.999, 10.002, 10.001, 10.003, 10.001.

I then did another separate test using the given tray,  and weighed the 10gram calibration weight 10 separate times again. Each time, before and after each weigh, I made sure the plate was straight ( according to my eyes.) Note the differences in weight are not as much.
My readings were 10.000, 9.999, 9.998, 9.999,9.999,10.000, 10.000, 10.000, 9.998, 9.999 grams. I would rate it a 10 out of 10 simply for the price and ease of use. *Before ingesting anything I was sure to use the calibration weight numerous times on both trips.*

I have used it successfully to weigh out 36 mgs of 2C-E that I ingested recently. (Weighed out 18mgs two times, so you can say the dose was between 36-42 mgs.)
I also used it to weigh out 26 mg of 2C-E 2 weeks prior to that trip. I now use the small glass vial method suggested above to weigh my stuff and noticed only a 1 or 2mg difference, so it does work slightly better and is more convenient than the tray.
The tiny glass jar I have fits perfectly where the tray is supposed to go, and empty weighed 2.849 or 2.850 grams each time I weighed it. This was done 15 times. I think this is very reliable for the money, but wouldn't buy it if the substances you need to weigh have a very steep response curve. 

* I am fully aware that 2C-E's response curve is considered steep.
