Post by: JoeHartley on March 31, 2013, 02:19 pm
To the OP.
Yeah, I have the same problem with coke. I start off a night with a line or two and always feel disappointed, like I'm not quite "there" yet. I never drink alcohol, but whilst doing coke I love to have a few beers because of the warm buzz that accompanies the coke so well. It just takes the edge off the jittery feeling from the coke and makes the comedown easier too in my opinion. I'm a smoker, and like alcohol, nicotine gives a nice buzz that goes with the coke all too well. Plus, I love numbing my gums with the coke and then smoking, the feeling is hard to describe but it's just so nice and smooth.

The problem, I think, stems from the fact that cocaine severely inhibits one's willpower, and that it is so moreish. I'm always left grasping for more of that smooth euphoria, and so more coke goes up my nose. But doing coke on its own doesn't give me the same feeling it used to, and so I resort to alcohol, tobacco and weed to get a better buzz. A chemical named cocaethylene is produced in the liver when coke and alcohol are taken together and it's more potent than either of them alone, and also more toxic. This is why these two go so well together. I guess after a while, everybody feels that straight coke doesn't cut it anymore (get it? ;) )

So far my problem hasn't been too bad; it only starts once I start snorting blow. The only way I've been able to help myself is by only picking up a gram at a time, and starting a session after at least 11pm with a couple of beers at hand. This way, after a couple of hours have passed or when my coke / booze runs out I can't be fucked to go out and pick up any more, and so I just go to sleep.

Basically, I feel for you bro, and I hope you can help yourself. Limiting the amount available to you is in my opinion the way to go. I know you'll finish a session wanting more, but just remind yourself, "The last time I did more, did I really feel that much better? Was it really worth my time / money / braincells?" Hopefully the answer to these questions is, "No" and you'll gradually be able to shorten the length of your binges and the amount that you indulge in. I'm not a pharmacologist, doctor or therapist, but I do love coke, and this method helped me cut back from about using about 4g a week to never more than 1.

Good luck,
- JH
