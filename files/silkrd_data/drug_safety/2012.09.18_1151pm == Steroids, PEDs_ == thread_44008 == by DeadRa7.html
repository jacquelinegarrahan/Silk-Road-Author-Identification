Title: Re: Steroids, PEDs?
Post by: simantecort on September 19, 2012, 12:05 am
if you have health problems that prevent you from exercising, doing steroids is definitely not for you. steroids will cause massive hormonal changes in your body that will only be beneficial if you exercise at least once a day, every day, and eat a very nutritious diet. people who do steroids and don't exercise invariably develop large deposits of fat. all that hormone has to go somewhere, after all. if you don't exercise much when you're on steroids, your metabolism will slow and your liver will struggle to eliminate the harmful chemicals (if you choose the oral route of administration, that is). injected steroid has a host of other problems that comes with it. you should really specify what your health condition is that prevents you from exercising. most bodybuilders will not recommend even the lightest prehormone to someone that does not already exercise daily. taking prehormones and steroids without training for at least a month beforehand can cause asymmetrical muscle development and exaggerated bone growth, and the rapid increase of weight is likely to damage your connective tissue. i strongly recommend you train beforehand using only protein and perhaps creatine + glutamine to supplement before even considering taking prohormones or steroids. i cannot, in good faith, recommend any specific chemicals to someone with no weight-lifting experience as it is likely to cause damage. i have seen it many times before, and in some cases the damage was irreversible.
