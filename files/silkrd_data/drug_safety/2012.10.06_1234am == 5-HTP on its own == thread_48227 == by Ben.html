Title: Re: 5-HTP on its own
Post by: Ben on October 09, 2012, 12:21 am
5-HTP is indeed metabolized by the liver, but it seems not to be completely eliminated in the first pass, central effects on conditions like anxiety, depression and some types of ataxia have been demonstrated in placebo controlled double blind studies. This would indicate that some of it does reach the brain when ingested, even if most of it is metabolized on the first pass through the liver.

Curiously its marketed for a number of effects as a supplement:

- sleep aid
- anti depressant / mood stabilizer
- weight loss agent / appetite suppressor

I'm not really sure what to make if it myself - i've taken some (200mg tablets) just to see what it does (not on ssri's, so figured the chance of harm would be minute) and can't say i have noticed any remarkable effects on either health claim. No adverse effects either, though. Perhaps the effects would be more clear if combined with carbidopa, but i can't obtain that currently, not am i sure that using it has no negative effects.

As far as the effects of melatonin go: I've tried some of the supplement type tablets that contain a very small dose (0.1 mg each or so). Taking 10 of those had no noticeable effect at all, but perhaps it becomes more pronounced with larger doses. As far as I understand, however, taking larger doses of melatonin (say 5 or 10 mg) is not without dangers either since it does produce side effects like lasting drowsiness, headaches and even hypothermia in such amounts. Somehow that seems like a side effect profile worse then benzo's, making it hardly worth the effort where hypnotic effects are desired. If the anti depressant and weight loss claims come though as well it is still an interesting substance however, so i'm not ready to bunk it completely.

A mood depressing effect would be paradoxical, but that is not uncommon with any medication in that application - things like ssri's often have side effects that do the exact opposite of what you were taking them for (e.g. worsening anxiety, cause insomnia) for some period of time. Generally these adverse effects will diminish if you persist in taking the medication, i'm not sure if that would apply to 5-htp too though.

Title: Re: 5-HTP on its own
Post by: Ben on October 13, 2012, 01:11 am
I suppose there is not reason why you could not IV pure 5-HTP, but i've never seen it available as a pure substance. Most tablets containing it are derived from a plant source and will contain all kinds of contaminations. It's mostly marketed as a herbal supplement, probably because it's legal status as an effective medication has not been established. The latter is likely to be the result of a lack of research then of negative research results however. After all, it is a natural substance that cannot be patented, and hence of little importance to pharma companies regardless of efficacy.
