Title: Re: Have an extra empty prescription sheet- pharmacy protocol needed
Post by: PrettyinGreen on May 25, 2012, 04:24 am
Actually, in my experience, and definitely in recent years, most controlled substances MUST be scripted, and very specifically, I might add.  I know this because I've actually had pharmacies refuse to fill my meds because my doctors' did not write them EXACTLY how the DEA wants.

They usually require name, birthdate, address, name of drug, exact dosage prescribed, including combination medications like Percocet which have to show the mg amount of each drug, for example "Percocet 10/325", also quantity prescribed, which must include a numeric and written amount on the script("30" and "thirty"), and dosing instructions.  Not to mention the DEA # and doctor's signature and contact information. 

Forging an RX is not something I recommend, especially since almost EVERY pharmacy uses cameras on the customers and employees now, and they also have to keep prescriptions for controlled substances for a very long period of time.
