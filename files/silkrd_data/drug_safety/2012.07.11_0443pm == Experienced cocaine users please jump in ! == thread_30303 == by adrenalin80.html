Post by: modest mouse on July 12, 2012, 08:06 am
id recommend nasal spray or distilled water, not tap water. when tap water is exposed to nasal membranes there is a small risk of a very serious amoeba infection which is difficult to treat since it is a eukaryotic organism as well (http://www.cdc.gov/parasites/acanthamoeba/biology.html). acanthamoeba can penetrate into the central nervous system wreaking havoc and can be very easily avoided. i would recommend just getting a bottle of nasal spray, which is basically just saline to moisturize nasal membranes and is very cheap for a large bottle... i use it when insufflating coke, pills, H, basically anything to help dissolve the powder and prevent damage as well as prevent getting clogged up as you mention. besides my fascination with the bug, it will just help prevent infections in general and is very handy.
