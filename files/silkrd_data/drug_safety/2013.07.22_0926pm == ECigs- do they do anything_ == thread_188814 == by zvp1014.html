Title: Re: ECigs- do they do anything?
Post by: Ben on July 26, 2013, 01:38 am
As a(ny) smoker can tell you, nicotine definitely has an effect.

Being deprived of nicotine for several hours will make most smokers feel craving it, uneasy, uncomfortable and what not - the effect is definitely really there.

Nicotine can be administered in different ways though. For example, with sublingual tablets, nicotine gum, and using an electronic cigarette or other evaporation device.

Things like nicotine gum, tablets and such reduce the discomfort of non smoking to some degree. But even if taken in equivalent dosages to smoking actual cigarettes they don't quite do the full trick. The physical withdrawal symptoms subside, but the cravings do not.

I suppose this is where the electronic cigarette comes in: that delivers nicotine, but using it also simulates the act of actually smoking, making it feel less like an inferior surrogate and more like actually smoking a cigarette that is just a bit different from what you are used to. Perhaps comparable to smoking rolling tobacco when you are used to factory made cigaretts or the other way around. Maybe a bit further off than that, but at least it is something that lets you inhale something that remotely tastes and feels like tobacco smoke and delivers nicotine.

Personally i'd say that i would not consider things like nicotine gum or tablets as a serious option to replace real cigarettes, while i think electronic cigarettes could actually work. I'm not looking to get rid of a nicotine addiction though, just interested in a safe route of administration that is less harmful but just as enjoyable.
