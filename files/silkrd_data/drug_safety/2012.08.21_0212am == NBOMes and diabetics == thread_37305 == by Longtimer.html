Title: Re: NBOMes and diabetics
Post by: john_snow_da_bastard on August 21, 2012, 09:13 am
Its medically impossible for an amount of sugar in tabs to effect your blood glucose to any significant degree, if at all.

The methylone was almost certainly the culprit. It's a stimulant so it mimics fight/flight response which.causes glucose to be released from.your body's reserves and put into circulation for energy.

Even if you glucose does jump up a bit during the trip it shouldnt be of any consequence.as  long as you dont feel ill and are.sure to get it back under control.after.the.trip (if you are type 2). If you.are type 1 maybe regulate more tightly.

The beauty of 25i is that you are pretty luscent throughout the trip should any issues arise
Title: Re: NBOMes and diabetics
Post by: thebacon on August 21, 2012, 09:47 am
As long as your sugar control has been fairly good for the last few months (maybe check current HBA1c?), an acute spike in sugar from a stimulant shouldn't be too much drama. If possible have a glucose meter and insulin on hand.

The big problems arise when you have had a high sugar level for an extended period of time, in which case avoid stims until your HBA1c is normal.
