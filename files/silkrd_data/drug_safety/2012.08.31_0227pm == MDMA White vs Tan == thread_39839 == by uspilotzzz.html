Title: Re: MDMA White vs Tan
Post by: AnimusVox on August 31, 2012, 05:38 pm
Typically this is the case. Whiter MDMA crystal (it'll always retain a very slight tan tinge in my experience, though) indicates the chemist went through multiple acetone washes on the final product. I've had tan MDMA that is more potent than white, and vice-versa, so as you have stated color is not an indicator of potency. Every chemist is different, and in the end it all boils down to the particular synthesis method they used and how much care was taken during the process.

Title: Re: MDMA White vs Tan
Post by: AnimusVox on August 31, 2012, 07:51 pm
Both. The chemist begins by distilling natural oils to obtain pure safrole. If the chemist starts with sassafras oil, the potency will be in the upwards of 90% safrole. From there, he then utilizes Wacker oxidation to obtain MDP2P. Another distillation will yield pure MDP2P, from where the chemist will conduct another reaction to yield MDMA oil which is then crystallized. If care and chemist finesse was in play, the product will have high potency. It really comes down to how talented the chemist is. Chemistry is a true art form.

i actually suggest washing the mdma yourself to ensure the highest quality especially if its still tan or smells like licorice, which could be residual safrole which is carcinogenic.

first weigh, then add mdma and swirl around in cold acetone using a shot glass. let settle and pour acetone off without dumping the mdma at the bottom. repeat a few times, then dump the acetone/mdma at the bottom on a glass dish or mirror to evaporate and scrape up. weigh again to see weight of the impurities you removed.
