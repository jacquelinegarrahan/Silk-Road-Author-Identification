Title: Scales = Safety
Post by: MaoZedong on January 31, 2013, 12:43 am
I'm sick of seeing posts of people buying drugs then wondering how they can measure accurate doses without a scale. This is a general thread about the benefits of owning a scale. It is also a place for people to discuss the different scales they own and what they use them for.

These are mine and If one of them ever broke, I would go buy a replacement.
1. 1000g accurate to .1 - got it because I can tare glass jars on it
2. 100g accurate to .01 - nice shape, nice tray - daily driver - gotten the most use
3. 100g accurate to .01 - looks just like a ipod nano so if I have to take a scale out of my house I'll take this one but has a really small tray.
4. 20g accurate to .001 - used for personal experimentation with minute differences in dosage - kind of a hassle to use

If you don't want to get drug residue on your scale you can always put a bag on it, tare the bag, then put your substance in the bag.

If you are weighing out more than your tray holds, just put a party cup or bowl on it and tare that.

If you own a scale, you can post accurate vendor reviews like "Product came in .6 overweight" rather than "product received as described"

You will never have to deal with people telling you that you're skimping them - likewise - you will never be skimped.

If your friend doesn't have one you can sell him yours at a slight markup and then just go buy a new one.

IF SOMEONE HAS A REASON NOT TO OWN A SCALE PLEASE SHARE BECAUSE EVERYONE NEEDS ONE!
