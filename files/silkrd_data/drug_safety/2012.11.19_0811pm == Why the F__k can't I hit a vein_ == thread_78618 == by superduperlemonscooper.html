Title: Re: Why the F**k can't I hit a vein?
Post by: st0lenman on November 20, 2012, 02:35 am
First off use a new needles if you can. Once a needle is used even a couple of times the point gets fucked up.
Try different sized gauge and or needle length. Make sure the plunger does not stick before using it.

I am seen people have issues when the rig more vertical. The more horizontal the better, rig should be almost laying on your forearm (when using arm). The points path to the vein will have a less of a slop.

Break the skin and then pull the plunger non stop as you slowly move the needle further in. Once you see blood stop and pull back more and if you see more your good to go. You may also be way off in depth, veins can look closer then they are.  Or you may have the whole needle in your arm and still push it to hit.

Try to avoid hands and feet they have smaller veins. If you still cant hit a vein maybe get blood drawn to see a pro in action. Watching videos also may help.
Title: Re: Why the F**k can't I hit a vein?
Post by: st0lenman on November 20, 2012, 02:42 am
Also

A needle that has a beveled point: ensure that the needle's point is at the bottom, i.e. you can see the sloping hole.

Be sure to properly anchor the vein. Place the thumb of your free hand below the vein and pull down lightly. This will pull the skin and vein tight preventing it from "rolling" in most cases. Do not make a "window" to anchor the vein. This technique uses the thumb below the vein and the index finger above the vein. This puts your index finger at risk for an accidental needle stick.
