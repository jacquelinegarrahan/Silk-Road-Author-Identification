We use a microgram scale to measure out 900.00mg of 25i-NBOMe.  One thing people NEED to notice with milligram scales is that air pressure (wind) affects them, as well as the built in equipment error.which could range from +/- 5-25mg.  What is printed on them is actually inaccurate especially the chinese scales.
 
The microgram scale we use is a peice of equipment that costs well over 10k USD.  And is technically accurate to 1nanogram.

We also use micro-pippetes which release a single drop of liquid which is ALWAYS the same amount.  Get about 100 of these and hook it up to a nice machine that'll drop them consistently across 100 individual tabs and repeat 9 times per print.

I assume the average person would simple use volumetric measurement and splash it across a sheet which will create hotspots.  Last time we did a GC/MS test on individual blotter by this method you can get 2000ug +/- 100ug on a single one if not prepared properly.  Please everyone exercise caution.
