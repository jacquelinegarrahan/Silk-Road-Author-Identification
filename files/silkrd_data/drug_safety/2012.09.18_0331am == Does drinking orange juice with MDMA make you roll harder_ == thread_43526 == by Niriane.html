Title: Re: Does drinking orange juice with MDMA make you roll harder?
Post by: Ben on September 19, 2012, 01:21 am
I think this story originates from drinking the juice of grapefruits rather then oranges.

Grapefruits do contain substances that will load the cyp34A metabolic pathway. This causes consuming grapefruit to inhibit the breakdown of a whole array of substances, ranging from illicit drugs to prescribed cholesterol-reducing medications.

Note that this effect is only present in grapefruit though, not in oranges.

Title: Re: Does drinking orange juice with MDMA make you roll harder?
Post by: Ben on September 24, 2012, 12:56 am
It's not about vitamin C, and grapefruits are not identical to oranges in this respect.

The concept of grapefruit (juice) altering the effect of certain medications is not some folk story, leaflets that come with a number of prescription meds (particularly against cholesterol and such) mention grapefruit specifically as something that should no be consumed when taking the medication.

Illicit drugs also rely on the same pathways however, which explains why combining grapefruit with mdma is not a good idea, but washing them down with orange juice is perfectly fine.
