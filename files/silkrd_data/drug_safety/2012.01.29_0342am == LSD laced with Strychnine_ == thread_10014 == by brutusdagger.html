Title: Re: LSD laced with Strychnine?
Post by: tordemon on February 01, 2012, 07:01 pm
There are certain reagent-based tests that can be useful in identifying substances. For LSD, the Ehrlich's reagent (available amazon: http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002C2I6Z0/ref=oh_o04_s00_i00_details) can be useful in at least ensuring that you haven't gotten a DOx or something else.

But, like everybody said, if the vendor is reputable, I wouldn't be concerned. The styrchnine is a myth.

If it is replaced with something, the effects will be very different than you expect, and I'd take note of that.
Title: Re: LSD laced with Strychnine?
Post by: Horizons on February 01, 2012, 07:41 pm
Strychnine-laced LSD is an urban myth. Blotter paper has a very low absorption capacity. LSD is active in the 100-microgram range, so it can be put on a blotter and used effectively. Strychnine is not. You'd have to eat one or two ten-strips of laced blotter to get any ill effects from strychnine poisoning. Your friend has no idea what he's talking about.
