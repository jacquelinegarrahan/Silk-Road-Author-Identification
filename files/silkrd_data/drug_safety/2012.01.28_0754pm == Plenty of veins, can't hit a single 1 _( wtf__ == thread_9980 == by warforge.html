Title: Re: Plenty of veins, can't hit a single 1 :( wtf??
Post by: cacoethes on January 30, 2012, 05:00 am
Are you registering before you push?

Tie off if you need to.  Sounds like you do.

Stay hydrated- the veins will be more pronounced.

Use a fresh spike!

Run the injection site under HOT water for a few minutes prior to injecting.

Go in as parallel to the vein as you can.  You're skinny, they won't be deep.  Make sure you're aiming towards your heart (towards you body).

As soon as the needle breaks the skin, pull back on the plunger- and slowly keep pushing the needle deeper.  When the needle goes through the vein, it will immediately register. STOP pushing the needle and start pushing the plunger.  This takes some practice, but many misses are caused because you don't hit the center of the vein, but rather the side of it.  Then the needle pierces the side of the vein and comes right back out- hence the bubble next to your vein.

Another technique is to just make a quick "stab" so that the vein is pierced before it can "roll" out of the way.  Also takes practice, but should be unnecessary if you are using a fresh spike.

Veins in the crook of your elbows tend to roll less than the ones on the back of your hands.

Veins in the back of your hands are about as shallow as you'll find, easier to see, and easier (for me) to hit.

Use alcohol on the injection site- it will help minimize inflammation and tracks.

Really, really consider if you should be injecting anything at all.  Once you become accustomed to the feel of the steel, it WILL become your preferred method of administration, with potentially life-changing ramifications.  Be safe, bro...
