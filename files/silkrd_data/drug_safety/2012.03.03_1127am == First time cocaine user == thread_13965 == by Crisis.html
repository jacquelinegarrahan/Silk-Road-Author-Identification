I'm thinking about trying coke for the first time. I've done just about everything else except opiates which I plan on staying away from until I reach at least eighty.


Opiates get a bad rap. You don't have to go straight to mainlining heroin. There is a HUGE range of fun to be had with opiates without risking addiction.

Have you never had any pain pills? You know, after the dentist or surgery or something. The pill bottle will say "Hydrocodone" (which is Vicodin) or "Oxycodone" (which is Percocet), etc...those are opiates.

They give a very nice euphoric buzz, especially if you have a little alcohol and cannabis with them while you're waiting for them to kick in. But here's the thing...after you do this for a day or two (at least, that's how long it takes me), you need like 4-5 times as many to get "high" as you did the first time. But that's bad...because of all the acetaminophen (aspirin) in them.

So, that's where you reach the crossroads. Is chasing that high worth migrating to more powerful opiates, do you can avoid damaging your liver with all the aspirin? For me, it's a no brainer. I hate the nod, GOD do I hate that nodding drowsy morass that opiates turn into rather quickly. I only like that big euphoric hit, feeling like I'm on top of the world.

That's where it ends for me. I don't buy more, I let my tolerance subside while I go back about my normal routine...

Just draw the line at low-strength pharmaceutical opiates, have some fun with those (they go GREAT with blow by the way), and never even fuck with snorting, smoking, or injecting an opiate. And then you honestly have nothing to worry about.
