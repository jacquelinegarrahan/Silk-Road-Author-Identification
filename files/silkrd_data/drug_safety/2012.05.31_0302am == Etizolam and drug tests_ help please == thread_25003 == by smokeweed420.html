Title: Re: Etizolam and drug tests? help please
Post by: Ben on June 02, 2012, 12:43 am
Most benzondiazepines can be detected in urine samples for at least a few days after use.

There are basic tests that just indicate benzo's, often featured in many drug panel tests commercially available.

Some of these tests can tell the difference between generic groups of benzodiazepines as well, often intended to find if the substance is prescribed diazepam or flutinazepam slipped into a drink.

Laboratory analysis will in most cases reveal the exact compound or its active metabolites.

While etizolam is not a traditional benzodiazepine, it may very well trigger the initial test, and be discovered during more detailed analysis.
