Post by: 00OOIlI00lO1O0 on July 10, 2012, 03:16 am
I'm not sure if it's done in practice or not, but in theory the clinic is able to test for EDDP, which is a longer acting metabolite than methadone itself, so it should show up in every methadone user's urine. A quick internet search shows that in-office tests are available for under 1 EUR.

Finding methadone with no EDDP is a huge red flag.

Finding EDDP without methadone may or may not be a red flag depending on one's metabolism, as EDDP sticks around longer.
