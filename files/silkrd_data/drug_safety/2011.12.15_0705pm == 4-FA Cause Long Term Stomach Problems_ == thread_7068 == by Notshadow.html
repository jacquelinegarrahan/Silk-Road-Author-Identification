Post by: microRNA on December 17, 2011, 09:11 am
If you are having pain that is so severe that fentanyl was needed to relieve the intensity, you should see a doctor. Pain is very clear warning that something else is seriously wrong.

I am no doctor, but I do know that a very important network controlling the activity of the gut called the enteric nervous system. Most people do not realize how extensive these neural pathways really are (approaching a second "brain") or that they provide a vital function for the body. Ninety percent of the bodies serotonin and fifty percent dopamine is utilized within the ENS. Serotonin plays a huge role in the regulation of the ENS (unfortunately I know less about dopamine's role), for example by reducing the activity of neurons that signal pain from the abdomen to the CNS. 4-FA is a potent serotonin releasing agent, as are most of the other RCs, and compounds such as 4-chloroamphetamine have been demonstrated to potentially cause damage to neurons, although the risk for 4-FA is reduced. The actual massive release of serotonin itself has been shown to not be inherently neurotoxic, so may be induced through mutations caused by reactive oxygen radicals somehow produced through metabolism as is likely the case with MDMA. Unless your usage was just ridiculously excessive you probably should not be too concerned about the risk of long-term consequences due to neurotoxicity however. I would actually be more likely to initially hypothesize that you are suffering from a depletion of vesicular serotonin which has disinhibited these "pain" signals, hopefully temporarily. Potentially you may take supplemental serotonin precursor 5-hydroxytryptophan and dopamine precursor tyrosine which will help promote the synthesis of these neurotransmitters. If I remember correctly, synthesis, vesicular loading, and axonal transport will take at least a few days before supplementation reaches the storage pools.

I am curious what you meant by stomach... if the pain is fairly high in your abdomen (when laying down, above the lowest point of your ribs), you may actually have stomach ulcers due to Helicobacter Pylori, a bacterium, which was aggravated by consumption of these chemicals. It would be wise to seek a medical professional but drugs like cimetidine and omeprazole may provide relief.

Good luck, I hope you find relief soon!
