Post by: tordemon on July 06, 2012, 10:10 pm
That seems kinda strange to me. I'm not sure how prepared you are for these experiences. I'd feel better if you were willing to post more about your previous drug experiences; only having done lsd and shrooms before bodes poorly in my opinion.

Mescaline is one of the mildest drugs you can ever take. If there ever was a drug appropriate for first-time trippers, it is mescaline. I don't mean mild as in you won't enjoy it, but rather that the come-up is very gentle and that the experience is overall enjoyable.

DMT is extremely intense when you smoke it. It is supposedly even more intense when injected, though based on the questions you're asking I don't think you should be attempting to convert the freebase to a salt. If you don't enjoy nausea, DMT may come as a surprise for you. DMT gave me the most intense nausea I've felt in my entire life, even more intense than times I actually did throw up (I didn't throw up). It completely replaced my field of vision, and when I could see again every object in existence had somehow been turned into a face. It can be extremely interesting, but I wouldn't say it's enjoyable and I don't feel like there is usually time for a whole lot of thinking during the experience. I really think you ought to consider the decision to take DMT for a while longer before just jumping in; make sure you really want to do it. The first time I did it I thought I had actually died, which was literally the scariest experience of my life.

Heroin is pretty interesting. You didn't mention what type of heroin you got, but I'm going to presume it's #4. #4 is good for eating, snorting, and injecting. I've eaten and snorted #4, and I've also smoked #3. Of those three ROAs, I enjoy smoking the most. The things to watch out for are compensating for tolerance (that can be dangerous if you overcompensate), and addiction. I haven't run into problems with either, yet, but I certainly see how it would be possible for somebody to do so.

You should probably do some more research, dude. Pretty much all the information you need to know is on websites like Erowid.com. You can find a lot of really interesting information there, particularly via the experience reports. Be careful, though.
