Sunlight/UV light and heat are the biggest issues, as the molecule is unstable. It can theoretically be kept in distilled water, but I don't know anyone who does. Usually it's in alcohol. So, removing light/heat is the key, hence wrapped up in the fridge is best. I couldn't tell you how long it'd keep like that though.... I seriously doubt it'd make that much difference if it's stored dry or in alcohol as what we are talking about is the deterioration of the molecule, but I'm willing to hear other views if anyone has any evidence...

Title: Re: Storing LSD

Thank for the info, What if I was to store the acid in distilled water/alcohol in the dark place? I would be getting blotter so would it be ok to simply put the blotter in the bottle with the water/alcohol?

First off, it needs to be dark AND cold - it's no good leaving it in a warm/dark place as it will still lead to the molecule deteriorating...

Second, if you but a blotter with LSD on a bottle of pure alcohol (as I said, I wouldn't use water) what is to stop the LSD dissolving into the liquid? Nothing. You would end up with some in the liquid, maybe some on the blotter, and you'd never know how much you're taking. If it's on a blotter, store the blotter dry, wrapped in foil, in a cold/dark place....

Post by: anothergirl on September 03, 2013, 07:50 am
Here's my method:

Get a lot of tiny ziplock baggies and a proper air-tight GLASS jar (rubber seal, mason, or whatever).

Cut up your blotter into units of whatever you expect to dose each time.

Put each dose into a single or double layer of tiny ziplock and put these in the jar. You could probably do a layer of foil outside the baggies if you worry about the fridge light.

Throw in some silica gel packs to absorb any moisture before it can seep through the plastic. They should have the color-change feature so you'll know if you ever need to change them.

Then you can just take one dose out at a time, and you don't have to expose the rest of your tabs to the elements every time you want to get a dose.

And I keep them in the fridge since my room is hot in the summer.

This method has never failed me and gives me peace of mind.
