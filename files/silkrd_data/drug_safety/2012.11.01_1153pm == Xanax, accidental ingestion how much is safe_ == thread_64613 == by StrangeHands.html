There's no way to know what is going to be a dangerous/lethal dose for any specific person without knowing that person's body chemistry. Title: Re: Xanax, accidental ingestion how much is safe?
Post by: Ben on November 03, 2012, 01:45 am
Its a good question, but one with a difficult answer.

If you look at animal tests, the LD50 (dose that kills half of the test animals) is in the order of 1 gram per kilogram of body weight. Extrapolated that means a 80 kilogram man would have a 50% chance of ingesting 80 grams of xanax - yes, that equates to 4000 bars.

These studies only look at the rate of survival though, not at how badly you are affected if you do survive. Also, they don't usually take into account factors such as being unable to get up for a week for a glass of water, resulting in death from dehydration rather than the substance itself. Combined with alcohol (which i can imagine given the scenario) can me deadly at much lower doses as a consequence of respiratory depression. In that case taking someone to hospital and using a ventilator will vastly improve chances of survival.

It's down to you calling the shots really. If someone accidentally took 10 mg of your xanax, i would suggest bringing that person to the ER for observation, especially if any alcohol was involved. If a lot of alchol was involved, i suppose it would be wise to do so after ingesting anything over 2 mg or so.

As the onset of effect is pretty fast, you would probably notice if anyone took a significant dose, resulting in symptoms like drowsiness, or lack of response in general at larger doses. This in itself is usually not life threatening as long as you seek medical attention. If you do not, there is a realistic chance someone could suffocate in their sleep shortly after.

Also, try not to keep benzo's in mis-labeled medicine bottles - you an use unmarked bottles if you want to hide them. Wrong labels can lead to incredible risks. Lets simply say that you would put 2 mg xanx tablets in a bottle marked as 25 mg benadryl. If i were to be bitten by wasp or bee (which i am allergic to) i would down 5 or 10 of those tablets to arrest anyphylactic shock caused by the sudden histamine release. Taking xanax instead of benadryl would in that case have the opposite effect, and be pretty dangerous to do.

Obviously i would usually not be impolitely nicking someones medication, but if time is of the essence, and something labeled 'this will safe your life' is within reach, i would not hesitate to take it without asking.
