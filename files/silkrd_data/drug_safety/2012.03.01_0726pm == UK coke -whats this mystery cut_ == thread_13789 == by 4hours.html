Title: Re: UK coke -whats this mystery cut?
Post by: cacoethes on March 02, 2012, 06:12 am
My gut feeling tells me it's not cut with anything, but is actually cocaine processed without potassium permanganate.  Cocaine processed without it contains many other inactive alkaloids, like cinnamoylcocaine, which block the effects of cocaine.  The coke will still look good, and even test high for purity, but it won't do jack shit psychoactively, because it hasn't been refined to remove the inactives.

Coke comes from South America, where the coca plant grows natively.  Potassium permanganate isn't manufactured in South America, so it must be imported.  Sodium permanganate can also be used, but is not as efficient.

The processing of tens of thousands of pounds of raw cocaine paste require tens of thousands of pounds of potassium permanganate... South American producers aren't likely to hold up tens of millions of dollars worth of product for lack of precursor chemicals.

Whoever controls the world supply of potassium permanganate also controls the world supply of cocaine- just do a search for "DEA cocaine potassium permanganate", and read through some of the DEA's Microgram Journals and you'll see what I mean.  Here's a good link to start:

h**p://www.justice.gov/dea/programs/forensicsci/microgram/journal_v6_num34/pg2.html

And here's a good link explaining how cocaine is produced and refined:

h**p://www.erowid.org/archive/rhodium/chemistry/cocaine.illicit.production.html#4

It's discussion of the chemistry is detailed, and not easy to plow through unless you've taken a fair amount it in college, but fascinating none the less.  When you piece the clues together, a pretty clear picture begins to emerge.
