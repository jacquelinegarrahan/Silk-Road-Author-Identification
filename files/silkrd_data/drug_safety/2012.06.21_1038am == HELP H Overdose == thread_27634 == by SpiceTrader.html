In my experience when you actually overdose and not just overdose where you are still conscious/puking/off balance/hard breathing. You won't even know it and just nod out and be totally unresponsive besides maybe a few grunts, but definitely not conscious enough to think or know what is happening. You will then stop breathing within a few minutes and start to lose coloration turning very white with a blue tinge around your lips.  It is a quick process, and an ambulance should be called immediatly once someone isn't responsive and is turning colors, or if you are close to a hospital or live in a big city where it takes 30 minutes to even talk to a 911 operator just drive as fast as you can to the hospital and if you see a cop stop them, so they can drive.

I have witnessed various stages of overdoses, and when they are really serious you don't have much time. I witnessed my friend die in front of me because I tried slapping, mouth to mouth, water, and whatever else I could for 10 minutes before I called an ambulance. Believe me you don't want to feel the remorse and regret that comes after watching your friend die and knowing you could of saved him.

If did too much and are on a slight overdose, like puking and nodding out a lot try to get cold water and drink it, don't eat in case you nod out and choke. Try to keep walking around, maybe put your head in the freezer (cold air feels good and makes you feel more safe) Have someone to talk to or ask someone to come over, so when you nod out they can get you out of it. Don't ever lay down... Ever. I also wouldn't smoke a cigarette or weed it seems to progress the high, and that is the last thing you want.

Just to let everyone know: throwing people into snow, bathtubs full of cold water, etc. is not an effective way of dealing with an actual heroin overdose.  Cold will awaken someone who is nodding really hard but it won't do anything to help someone who has lost consciousness or whose breathing is actually impaired. And while we're on the subject, shooting someone up with speed won't help them in the case of a heroin OD either.  Methamphetamine and amphetamines increase your heart rate but do very little to your breathing: opiates can slow your breathing but don't do much to your heart rate.  Shooting an ODing junkie with crystal will, at best, lead to shallow breathing and tachycardia.  Naloxone is the only thing that can reverse an opiate overdose: home remedies are likely to cause more problems than they solve.

As far as cheap strong gear goes, I wonder how well the H was cut?  I've had hot spots in bundles before: snorting one bag would barely get me feeling it while one line from another bag would have me nodding like Layne Staley. 

I just lost a good friend to heroin a couple weeks ago. If you're going to use, stay smart and stay safe.
