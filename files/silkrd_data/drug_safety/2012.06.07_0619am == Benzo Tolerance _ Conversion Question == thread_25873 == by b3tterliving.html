Title: Re: Benzo Tolerance / Conversion Question
Post by: Ben on June 08, 2012, 01:31 am
Thats true. Valium has a lot of attractive qualities if used as an anxiolytic or mild sedative. For clinical application the long half life (including metabolites) may be  a good thing, making it a drug that you only need to take once a day to remain a decently constant level.

For recreational use something like xanax is more attractive because it doesnt last that long. If you want to space out on benzo's you'll probably don't want to have that effect remain for the next day or so.

As far as tolerance/addiction goes all benzo's pose a risk, and the cross tolerance is very large too. The mechanism of all of these drugs is nearly identical. While valium may take a very long time to break down, withdrawal from valium is not much different then from xanax or clonazepam. Taking a different benzo will usually alleviate withdrawal symptoms.

I suppose the biggest risk for addiction is that you can take moderate amounts of benzo's while functioning normally. Just take some to take the edge off, and regular use just creeps up on you, resulting in eventual tolerance and dependence. Most people get addicted taking prescription doses regularly, not taking recreational doses occasionally.
