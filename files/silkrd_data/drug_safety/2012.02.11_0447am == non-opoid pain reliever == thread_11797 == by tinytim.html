Title: Re: non-opoid pain reliever
Post by: Steelydust on February 11, 2012, 04:54 am
DXM, which is dextromethorphan. It's found in cough syrup. Have her take a bit over the recommended dosage on the bottle, but make sure that DXM is the only active ingredient.

DXM is similar to ketamine and PCP. It acts as a "dissociative." To put it simply, it disconnects the signals from your brain from your body. This is why it's used as a cough suppressant, because your brain doesn't get those signals that you need to cough.  In my opinion, it works better as a pain reliever than opioids.
