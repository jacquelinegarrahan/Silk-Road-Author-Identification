Hey dude,
As someone dipping their feet into the chemical universe for the first time, here is your checklist:

Ask for advice in the right place: CHECK
Read and research as much as you can, particularly experience reports and scientific papers
Establish to the best of your resources that your drug of choice is legitimate (test kit recommended)
Gain access to an accurate set of scales and research the appropriate dosages
Try a tiny sub-active amount of the drug as an allergy test
Make sure your set, setting and general mindset is as comfortable as possible and that you will have no responsibilities whilst high (a little anxiety is normal)
Try and make the most of it! Prepare activities and an environment that will allow you to make the most of your chosen substance

If you can tick all of these boxes leading up to your experience, the chances of something going wrong will be greatly reduced. You will be a model of responsibility for the drug community, and a pioneering example on the right course for global justice.

Oh and most of all: have fun.

I second the advice about visiting Erowid for research, lots of good information on there.

Also be careful with hallucinogens like LSD and mushrooms, they are mind openers and demand respect. If you take them in the wrong surroundings, with the wrong people or have something traumatic happen while under the influence then you risk having a bad trip, which generally means having 6-8 hours of fear, panic attacks and really scary hallucinations. I recommend not smoking weed, maybe have a drink or two while tripping to increase confidence and avoid the risks of PTSD. Whether the effects are good, bad or just plain strange they can stay with you for a very long time (5+ years in my experience) so it's best to have a good time. Humans are emotional beings and you may well have an epiphany that is just wrong, discover God, develop a new phobia or have a weird feeling when you see certain objects for the next half a decade. I'm pretty sure that my tripped out vision has never fully gone away, not sure if that's permanent brain damage or if it's something that has always been there and I'm just aware of it now I know what it looks like. Whatever the case, taking too much LSD or taking it too often is not a good idea.

MDMA is a pretty good ride but don't do it too often, your risks are depression and possibly even amphetamine psychosis afterwards. It's best done at a club where everyone else is doing it, having a dance and meeting new people, not a shitty drinking club where everyone wants to fight or fuck. Either that or with your SO for a night of cuddles, massage and sex.

Post by: Gibbroni on November 21, 2012, 05:02 pm
I think it's best to really take it easy if you're under 18. Personally, as a more experienced user, and even more so since SR came along, I can't imagine being able to have the self control necessary if I didn't have a fair amount of life experience in various aspects.

Weed is great though! You're clearly on the right track!

If you feel you're ready, or if you're just going to dive in anyway (hopefully with caution), then I recommended MDMA before anything else.  The reasons for this are that MDMA is very pleasurable, fun, easygoing and relatively worry-free (both from a psychological and physical standpoint), provided the dose isn't too high (and even then, it's quite forgiving to an extent).
It will show you just what man-made chemicals are capable of!

Psychedelic wise, given that I do believe you're fairly young, I would worry about you getting into psychedelics the most.  They can be absolutely amazing and wonderful, but you are usually at their mercy, while your subconscious mind controls your experience, whether you like it or not.  It can be amazing, sometimes hit or miss, sometimes bland, often-times very mind expanding and a lot of fun!
When things go bad, they go REALLY bad.  It's terrifying.  So, I say this not because you wont be able to handle it someday, but because it'd be a shame to have a really bad early experience and be scared off such wonderful tools for life.  The experiences need adequate mental preparation and well-being beforehand. 

If you're going to start out on man-made psychedelics then i'd recommend LSD (low to medium dose, usually 1 or 2 tabs), moderate 2cb (15 to 30mg), or even MDA (similar to MDMA while being more visual and interesting).

Stay away from the NBOME drugs.  I have had my fair share of trips with them, a few fun ones, one amazing one, and one utterly terrifying one.  The experience varies, but the common link between them is the physical side-effects i've encountered repeatedly.  So much tension you feel like you're going to have a heart attack or seizure. So much tension it can get to a point where you can't tell if you're breathing or not, so you start to freak out.... it's hard to stay calm if the bad trip due to side effects is present, because you really don't know how safe the drug is, so how are you going to stay calm if you start to panic?  At least with LSD you can push the boundaries.  People have already started dying from pushing the boundaries of the NBOMEs, as well as attempting "safe" responsible doses.  That said, I will probably explore NBOMEs from my stash again, but very carefully.  It's easy to overdose, even with blotter, and not that safe.  I can't wait to get some quality LSD again.

2cb is fun and colourful and doesn't screw with your actually thinking processes too much.  You get to see all kinds of fun psychedelic things while being pleasantly stoned, slightly stimulated and generally in a good mood.  Say hello to Mr. Tracer

Maybe when you're older!
