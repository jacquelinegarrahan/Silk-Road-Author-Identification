Title: Re: Drugs that doesn't work on serotonine receptors?
Post by: gambino on February 16, 2013, 03:22 am
Lots.  Ketamine and related compounds.  Cannabis.  Opiates, but I don't recommend.  Benzodiazepines.

Also, there are compounds that work on your serotonin receptors but are not believed to have any cross tolerance with MDMA, such as the phenethylamine and tryptamine psychedelics. Also, cocaine.

Compounds to avoid would be MDA, methylone and mephedrone, as either of these is likely to have cross tolerance issues with  MDMA.

Title: Re: Drugs that doesn't work on serotonine receptors?
Post by: gambino on February 16, 2013, 07:56 pm
DMT is a serotonin receptor agonist, but I would not expect it to have any effect on MDMA tolerance, because it's not a serotonin releaser.  Cocaine is a triple reuptake inhibitor, so it inhibits reuptake of serotonin.  Again, I wouldn't expect cocaine to have any effect on MDMA tolerance.
