Title: Re: Warning use immense caution when using scales and strong drugs...
Post by: redalloverthelandguyhere on May 22, 2012, 04:33 am
You can buy bulk (like 100) cheap Chinese made scales for less than a fiver each!

I was told this by a retailer.

These scales are fine for MDMA which has a relatively big margin of error. 10mg more or less will not matter. They are fine for coke, speed, hash and bud.

People buy RCs from China and sell them online on the clear net and in real life in head shops.

Some of these RCs are active at 1mg or less!

I can see a few deaths caused by these RCs mainly because you can buy a gram of stuff that, if someone snorted a line of 50mg, they would likely be sectioned after the  hospital stay. That's if they lived!

Also, blotters used to be mainly LSD. These days anyone can buy seriously powerful hallucinogenics and create blotters. This method might be better than scales but all it needs is for some oversight and you could have some serious difference between blotters. These days, 1mg extra might well mean 'serious difference'.

With hash, the 'overdose' just means crawling to bed - I think eating 5 grams or more of good quality hash beats all this messing 1 mg amounts of substances that may be the next Thalidomide.

I used LSD which is measured in thousandths of a gram - 1 gram of pure LSD = 1000 MCG, usually 100 MCG would be the limit so I am told. You would give that to someone who had not used. Imagine trying to weight out 1000th of a gram?

Someone must have found a vial of pure LSD in a white crystal powder. A line of that at 50 mg and you just took 50000 MCG.- enough for a few hundred people!

Wow.

If you survived that trip you would have an interesting story. Or a nightmare actually.

Post by: Limetless on May 23, 2012, 02:50 am
Here is a guide to weighing small amounts properly -

Get scales out, set them up, plug them in blah blah.

Take the pan off

Turn scales on

Return pan to scale and make note of the weight of the scale

DO NOT TARE WEIGHT OF SCALE

Weigh drug on top off the weight of the scale working out the numbers with a pen and paper.

That's the safest way to do it.
