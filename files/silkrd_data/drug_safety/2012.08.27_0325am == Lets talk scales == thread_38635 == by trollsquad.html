Quote from: aginalda on August 27, 2012, 03:30 pm
Can someone suggest a straigntforward procedure for volumetric measurement?  TIA

The procedure is fairly simple:

Lets say you had 50 mg of a substance, and want to divide it into 0.5 mg dosages. The 50 mg you can reliably measure with affordable scales, the 0.5 mg you cannot measure reliably without an analytical balance that costs more then the average car.

The way to go then is to measure out the 50 mg of substance, and dissolve it into a suitable solvent. Something like methanol will work well for many drugs, but check solubility before proceeding. You use a pipet to add* 1.00 ml to the 50 mg you have weighed, and shake/stir/wait to dissolve completely.

Then you take a 10 uL pipette to produce each individual 0.5 mg dose from the solution you just made. Once the solvent evaporates you have pretty precies 0.5 dosages of something you could not possibly measure to that accuracy.


* Technically you should not add 1.00 ml of solvent, but add solvent until the solution is 1.00 ml in total volume. Practically this doesn't make that much difference if you want to get it right down to a few percent, not to ppm's.
