Ummmm... sure this forum is called drug safety.  ::)

You can get away with that with some drugs, but not all of them. Knowing how to dose your drug of choice well, brings lots of benefits. It reduces any harmful side effects, negative effects normally increase with dose. And it's cost effective, you get the most out of your stash using the amount that gets the most effects.

You don't say which substance you want to take. But If you are mentioning a scale, my guess is that you already know that this substance needs to be measured correctly. Otherwise why ask?

Volumetric, as fiveotwo, suggests is a good way of working out a dose without a scale. Still you have to know what solvent to use water etc. Still have to be careful, make sure your calculations are accurate. And better to be 1:1 with your ratio. Then any inaccuracies in the guess of the starting dose aren't magnified.

Eyeballing can work, again depends on the drug. Splitting coke into equal lines isn't going cause many problems if lines are slightly unequal. With an RC or a 2C drug, 1mg can be the difference between great and not so great.

right, 2c-x's can be eyeballed, but you need to be extremely careful. It helps to have a visual representation of what something close to 20mg looks like, and unfortunately, you really need a scale to get that.

If you're going to be experimenting with these substances, you should really just hop on to Amazon and buy a cheap AWS mg scale. They're something like $25.
