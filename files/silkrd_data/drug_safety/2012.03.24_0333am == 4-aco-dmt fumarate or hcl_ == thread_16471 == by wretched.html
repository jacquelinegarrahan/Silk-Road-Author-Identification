really it just is from which acid was used to salt out from the non-aqueous layer.

the biggest difference is the fumurate molecule has a larger molecular weight, compared to the measly hydrogen and chloride, so the actual accurate dosage should be adjusted slightly to compensate: (stolen from coolio, d-f)
 
-AcO-DMT freebase has a molar mass of 246. Fumaric acid has a molar mass of 116. Hydrochloric acid has a molar mass of 36.46.

2000mg of 4-AcO-DMT HCl is therefore equivalent to 1703mg of 4-AcO-DMT freebase.
2000mg of 4-AcO-DMT fumarate is equivalent to 1057mg of 4-AcO-DMT freebase.
2000mg of 4-AcO-DMT fumarate is equivalent to 1241mg of 4-AcO-DMT HCl.

To calculate an equipotent dosage of the Hcl salt to those who are using the 4-AcO-DMT fumarate salt you would multiply their dose by 0.62.

Also, both the salts are going to be fairly stable, and 4-aco is relatively stable overall anyway. Generally the fumarate is common due to its long term storage potential but I think both would be fine.
