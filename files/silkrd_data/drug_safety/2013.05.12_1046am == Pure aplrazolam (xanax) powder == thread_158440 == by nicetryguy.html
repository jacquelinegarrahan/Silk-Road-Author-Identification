A mg is a mg, no matter what shape it comes it - liquid, bar, tablet, powder, etc. In case of a solution of 1 mg/ml, 1 ml of that solution would be equal to a single normal 1 mg tablet, or half of a 2mg bar shaped tablet.

The kinetics may be a bit different though, i can imagine onset of effect is faster if taken as a solution in propylene glycol. I suppose this solution would have a rather unpleasant taste though: PG as such is pretty nice, but xanax is rather bitter, and i doubt the taste of PG would offset that by much, though i've never tried myself.

Using 1 mg once a week or so would carry very little risk of dependence, at least not physically. There can be psychological dependence that causes you to have more trouble confronting the anxiety inducing situation without xanax in the future. This will not cause any adverse effects (like cravings, withdrawal) when you avoid the anxiety trigger, but could cause you to be unable to confront the trigger without medication whereas you were able to work your way (in discomfort) through it before.

Title: Re: Pure aplrazolam (xanax) powder
Post by: Ben on May 16, 2013, 01:28 am
Glycerin is not a very convenient or good solvent to use at all - its too viscous at room temperature to be practical, and the kinetics of getting alprazolam dissolved in it are not favourable, although the thermodynamics are okay.

Ethanol would be a good solvent, and it doesn't have to be all that pure to work. I don't have the exact data here, but something like 100 proof spirit (50% ethanol in water) sounds like a fine solvent to make 1 mg/ml solutions. I suppose at room temperature even ordinary 80 proof vodka would be a suitable solvent too.

If for some reason high proof spirits are hard to find in your country, i would suggest using methanol-tainted alcohol as a solvent. Additives such as methanol are often added to alcohol intended for sterilizing wounds and such to discourage drinking it as booze, and methanol is quite dangerous to your health in amounts of the mixture that would actually get you drunk.

If you look at a solution in a total volume of 1 ml per dose or so, the risks are very limited. Even if your solvent was 10% methanol in 90% ethanol, ingesting 1 ml of that solvent containing only 100 uL of methanol would not pose a serious health risk from the methanol. Spirits such as scotch will often contain a small amount of methanol and/or propanol as well, and this really poses little health risk as long as the amounts are small.

Drinking methanol in the volumes you would ethanol certainly is dangerous and leads to blindness, insanity, liver failure and what not, but we are looking at less then a percent involved in that here. As methanol occurs naturally in numerous foodstuffs in small amounts we are actually able to process small amounts of it without adverse effect, and many of us do so on a daily basis without ever noticing.

Quote from: nancy723 on July 23, 2012, 04:50 am

Please use this responsibly and understand the directions before trying.

You need the following items:
- milligram accurate scale.  Look on ebay for one.
- several ml of pure alcohol.  I used 90% ethanol/10% methanol, but would have preferred something like everclear.
- paper towel or other blotter paper, cut into 1 inch strips
- A 1ml or 3ml syringe.  This can be the type with a needle pre-attached, or a "oral" one with a disposable needle.  I used a 1ml 20ga needle.
- a small glass jar or test tube, that lets you work with the needle in it without getting in the way.

1. clean your scale pan and glass with some alcohol and let any left evaporate.
2. measure 1ml of alcohol into the syringe.
3. measure 10ml of product in scale pan.
4. dump as much of product as you can into glass container.  use the alcohol in the1ml syringe to clean out any sticking bits.  put all remaining alcohol in the jar with the product.
5. put top on glass jar and shake gently.  wait several minutes until all disolved.  You'll notice the solution has taken on a slightly white solution.
6. use same syringe to suck up all the liquid.
7. apply to 1 square inch paper towel 0.05ml at a time.  You want to paper to be not saturated, but close to it.
8. wait several minutes for alcohol to evaporate.
repeat steps 7 & 8, each time will add 0.5mg of product to the paper.
9. let evaporate some more, for several hours, until paper is dry.

I consumed by placing paper under my toungue for a minute or 2 until it went mushy, and then swallowed it.  It helps to have some acidic fruit juice to get the taste out of your mouth I found.  Grapefruit juice is supposed to potentiate benzos...

wait 5-20 minutes and let the fun kick in.

I know it isn't exactly what you were looking for, but it sounds like a fun way to ingest and you wouldn't have to put it on blotter, just calculate your dosage based on your recipe and don't forget to allow for displacement.
