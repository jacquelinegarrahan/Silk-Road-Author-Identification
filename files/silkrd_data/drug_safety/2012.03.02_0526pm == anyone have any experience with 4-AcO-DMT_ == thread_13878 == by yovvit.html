Have you ever done shrooms? For me, 4-AcO-DMT is a lot like shrooms, but more mellow and less nauseating - though more nauseating than LSD, which hardly ever gives me any stomach discomfort.. It's very different from LSD. I get many more CEVs and visual distortions (mostly pulsations and colored tracers) on 4-AcO-DMT, and it also makes me kind of giddy. LSD puts me in a more serene and introspective mood, and the few visual distortions I get are of a different kind. 4-AcO-DMT causes a little bit of gas, lasts less than half the amount of time as LSD and the trip is easier to "Put on hold" in case some pressing matter requires your sober-ish attention.

All in all, I think it's a very fun psychedelic, whereas LSD is more of a serious business trip.

Title: Re: anyone have any experience with 4-AcO-DMT?
Post by: Horizons on March 03, 2012, 04:20 pm
All psychedelics carry the risk of acting as triggers for latent mental issues, including schizophrenia and epilepsy. This isn't exclusive to 4-AcO-DMT. As for specific risks of the drug, I know of none that aren't shared with mushrooms and mescaline - and I did a lot of research before doing it for the first time. Personally, I can say that I've taken up to 80mg of 4-AcO-DMT (40mg first, and another 40 after about an hour) and the only unusual side-effects were a constant feeling during the trip like I needed to move my bowels accompanied by a complete inability to do so, and insomnia for three nights after the trip. I don't usually get insomnia from psychedelics, but they do make me sleep less for a few nights after tripping (five and a half hours a night instead of my usual seven, but I feel perfectly rested when I wake up), so this insomnia is probably just a more intense version of that due to the high dose.

yovvit, if you're very worried about nausea it might help a bit to trip on an empty stomach and take one or two dramamine pills about 45-30 minutes before taking the 4-AcO-DMT. It'll help to calm your stomach. Also, if it's already been at least an hour since you dosed, you don't need to hold back on vomiting, since the substance has already left your stomach by then. Throwing up will get rid of the nausea, and sometimes it actually enhances the trip with a feeling that you're throwing out everything bad from inside you (this sensation is so common on psychedelics that many Latin-American shamans refer to ayahuasca as "la purga", which means "the purging").
