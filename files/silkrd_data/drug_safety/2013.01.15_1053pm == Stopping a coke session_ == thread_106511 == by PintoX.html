Title: Re: Stopping a coke session?
Post by: jsmithdiggity on January 16, 2013, 12:57 am
I want to start by saying you are not alone by any means.  The only thing I found that brings you down smoothly is an opiate, so if you aren't interested in another chemical, you may be screwed.  Psychology will lose to physiology in almost all cases.  Physiological responses ALWAYS precede psychological ones.    I don't care how much cognitive behavioral therapy you try to apply, you loaded your brain with dopamine and serotonin and every cell in your body felt great.  Your brain cells are physiologically in pain after stopping.   I'm not saying it can't be done, but it's hell, and it's not just "in your head" so to speak. Alcohol only helps, as you said, by making you pass out.  In this scenario, the physiological, as well as psychological, intensity of the opiate high is pretty much null, and the risk of becoming dependent is actually quite minimal if that is what you are scared of.  The opiate will simply change how your neurons respond to this pain, and therefore kill the "craving".  Seriously, just take a couple Vicoprofens with your drinks after and see how much easier it is ( stay away from anything with acetaminophen, especially with heavy drinking).
