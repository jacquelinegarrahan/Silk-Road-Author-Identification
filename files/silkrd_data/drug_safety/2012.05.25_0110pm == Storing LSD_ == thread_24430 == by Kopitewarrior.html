Title: Re: Storing LSD?
Post by: 328502E on May 25, 2012, 05:43 pm
The biggest killers of LSD are moisture, changes in temperature, light, and air.  If you can avoid all of these things your acid will be good for a long time.  Don't worry about the fridge - it's really only important for really really long periods of time.

It's a myth that LSD can be absorbed through the skin.  If you have a sheet of dry acid and you touch all of it, you aren't going to trip. 

Yes, acid from the 70s will still be potent if it was stored correctly.  It won't be it's full potency obviously, but it'll still be good enough to trip.
Title: Re: Storing LSD?
Post by: ProudCannabian on May 25, 2012, 05:48 pm
I think it depends on how much of it you have and how long you intend to store it for.
If you're handling large amounts, yes, you should wear gloves when handling if you don't want to trip balls.  If you just have a single sheet or less, no big deal - you should be able to cut a full sheet before an appreciable amount of L would soak into your skin.

Sore in a cool dry spot, wrapped in paper, then plastic.  Some folks believe since LSD is an acid it will actually react with aluminum (see project bluelight for storage tips and arguments, lol), and I've seen L residue on many a plastic baggie.

Where you CAN'T use the fridge, either find a cool-dry spot in your basement(if you have one), or for short-term storage, just pick a book off your shelf and place it somewhere in the middle of the book and squeeze the book between others to keep it sealed. Choose a book nobody will take off your shelf.  Don't ever use the freezer, due to condensation issues.

I use a hermetically sealed locking stash box filled with silica gel packs to absorb any moisture myself.

LSD through skin absorption is not a myth... sweaty skin is best.  Slap a 5-strip in your armpit or under your nutsack sometime... you'll see what I mean.  ;)
