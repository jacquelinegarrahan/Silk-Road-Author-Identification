Post by: HenryC0833 on August 29, 2013, 11:27 pm
Well, there's your first roll, and then there's every other roll.  As for every other roll, you can binge a little and still get a good roll out of it.  For me, I can roll two days in a row and still get a good experience.  Two rolls in 18 days is fine, but don't make a habit out of it.

Wonder if maybe 120-140 mg might be enough.  Easy to say here and now, a little more difficult when you're out of your mind.  All you have to do is be mindful.  Just plan your trip.  "I'm taking 100mg to start and will bump 40 in a couple of hours."  If you think about it BEFORE you start tripping, it's not too hard to stick to the plan when things start to go all wiggly. 

You're gonna be hung over after MDMA.  Just think about the things you've learned and ride it out. 

I'll repeat that, in case you missed it.  Think about the things you've learned. 

Don't throw the MDMA away!  Jeez.  There's nothing more fun than making new friends.  Give it away to people you meet who are deserving of something special.  Just package it up in "stamps" or capsules or something and bring it with.  It's  a lovely gift.  Look, it's fun to take MDMA and get all sweaty and dance your booty off.  But wait until you've experience the puppy pile.  :)  You think its beautiful to SEE a bunch of people lying on top of each other, stroking each other's hair... try being IN that pile.  Exquisite.

Well I think you need to be in an environment where people will be OK with you rolling your gonads off.  In polite company, people could potentially mistake you for an insane person. 

I understand completely if you don't want to be involved in the whole alcohol scene.  So I would focus on the music.

One of the best MDMA experiences I ever had, occorred listening to a string quartet playing in a town square.  Sitting there right next to them.  Chatting them up.  They played things for me, in response to my questions.  "How come it is, that things in a minor chord sound so sad?"  "Listen to this, and tell me what you think."  There are no words, no words to tell how exquisite that was.

So yeah.  It's not club, or bar.  It's music.  Which, to be clear, is usually, but not always, in clubs, and bars.  As long as it's interactive.  Don't be in the pews, passively listening.
