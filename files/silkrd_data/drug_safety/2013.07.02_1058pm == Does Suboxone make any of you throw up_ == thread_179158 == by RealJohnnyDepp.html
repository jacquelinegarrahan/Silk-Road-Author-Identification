Title: Re: Does Suboxone make any of you throw up?
Post by: Red Rover on July 03, 2013, 03:03 am
This is an extremely common experience.  Suboxone/Subutex can make you very sick if you don't have a tolerance to it.  So can heroin.  That's what's going on with you.  You're not a regular user, so you're more sensitive to it.  Of course, the catch 22 is, when you get to the point where it doesn't make you sick at all anymore, you are well on your way to an addiction.

I remember when I first started using heroin, I didn't get sick from it alone, but I couldn't drink with it.  Not even one drink.  If I did, I'd spend the entire next day puking my guts out.  Sometimes it was so bad, I couldn't even keep down WATER.  Then I got addicted to it, and it didn't bother me at all anymore, even when I drank with it.

Suboxone never made me sick, but I didn't start taking it until I went into treatment for the heroin addiction.  So I was already physically addicted to opiates when I started taking it.

If you want to continue using heroin or Suboxone, then go slow, and take small amounts (and DON'T drink with it, unless you've already tried and you didn't get sick from it).  But remember that the more you take it, the closer you get to an addiction.  Once you become physically addicted to opiates, your life becomes very scary. 

I was wondering if you could tell me what it feels like to take Suboxone recreationally.  Do you really get high off of it?  I've never understood why people take this stuff recreationally.  I mean, it's drug that was specifically designed NOT to get you high.  I'm amazed that anyone, even a recreational user, could get a high off of Suboxone.  Is the high really any good?  Is it anywhere near as good as heroin, or even Vicodin or Percocet? 

It just seems like it would be a "last resort" high, if you couldn't find any of the good stuff, like heroin or oxy/hydrocodone.
