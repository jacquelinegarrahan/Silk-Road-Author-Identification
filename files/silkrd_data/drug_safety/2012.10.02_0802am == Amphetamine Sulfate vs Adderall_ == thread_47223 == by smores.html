Title: Re: Amphetamine Sulfate vs Adderall?
Post by: LittleK on October 03, 2012, 04:52 pm
I had the same idea but have yet to test it out. Amphetamine sulfate includes two "stereoisomers" of the amphetamine molecule. Stereoisomers are non-superimposable mirror images of each other, just like your hands. Adderall includes 75% of the right (dextro) isomer and 25% of the left (levro) isomer. Amphetamine sulfate, I presume, is 50% of each. From what I can gather, the left isomer provides a bit more of a body speediness, whereas the right isomer provides a cerebral speediness. So, my guess is that amphetamine sulfate will affect the body a bit more. It will probably also hit harder because, unlike adderal, none of the amphetamine is in any kind of extended release form. If you take it after eating, it might create a de facto extended release and thus feel more like adderal.
