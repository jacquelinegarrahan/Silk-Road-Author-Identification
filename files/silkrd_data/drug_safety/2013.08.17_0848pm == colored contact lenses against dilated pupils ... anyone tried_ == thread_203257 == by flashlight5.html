Title: Re: colored contact lenses against dilated pupils ... anyone tried?
Post by: Ben on August 20, 2013, 01:32 am
Cannabis causes two visible symptoms that are easily visible in the eye: dilated pupils and reddening of the white part. The latter is very similar to what happens if you suffer from hayfever, and eyedrops designed to counteract it may very well work to mask the red eye situation.

Pupil dilation could be masked with color contact lenses to some degree, but those will limit the aperture of your pupils. This may be okay under daylight conditions in a office, but could potentially be quite dangerous if walking or driving in the dusk/dark afterwards.

Pupil dilation can be a direct result of a drug resulting in overexposure of your eyes to light, but also be caused by reduced light sensitivity of the retina, in which case you actually need the pupil dilation to gather enough light for proper vision.
