Title: Re: Another Quick Question About Adderall/Modafinil/Ritalin
Post by: a_blackbird on February 20, 2012, 05:06 pm
Quote from: Atticus on February 19, 2012, 09:20 pm
But basically, is there any comedown the next day after use of Adderall/Modafinil/Ritalin? Because, if I was to use either of these drugs to study then I had an exam the next day I really wouldn't want to be feeling like shit or different in any way.

Make sure you get some sleep.  There really isn't much in the way of comedown from any of these drugs, but the one thing you don't want to do is use one of them to stay up all night and cram and then go into the test the next day still popping pills to stay awake.  YMMV, of course, but in my personal experience, if I've used one of these (ritalin and adderall are worse for this than modafinil and armodafinil), once I go past about 25-26 hours of wakefulness, I can really feel the cognitive impairment.

At a previous job, the HR department decided they were going to have us all take an IQ test, and they required us to take it on the day that the announcement went out.  I had been up all night the night before (ritalin, I think) - and when I went to take the test, it was like I could feel the gears in my head just chunking and creaking along.  I got them to let me retake the following week (different questions, so it wasn't like I had memorized any answers) on a day when I was well-rested, and I scored 15 points higher. 

If you're thinking about going the modafinil route, see if you can get your hands on some armodafinil instead.  AFAIK, there's only one vendor on SR that sells it - I've never purchased from him, but his reviews look pretty good.  Off the top of my head I cannot remember the specific chemical differences between the two, but for me, it's all of the good things about modafinil without the sometimes-annoying side effects - and unlike ritalin/adderall, with either modafinil or modafinil, you should be able to go to sleep unassisted and you'll typically wake up well-rested after much less sleep than you'd normally need.  If you need to maximize your study time, that might be the way to go.
