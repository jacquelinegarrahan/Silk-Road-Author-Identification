If you look for ARUP Labs, they also have excellent information but may be more detail than you want.

"Pissing in a cup for work" varies depending on your work. Some jobs just do a standard 5 panel dip with high thresholds. Some run a 9 panel dip with varying thresholds. Some run a specialized panel with EVERYTHING confirmed by MS. Depends on your work or situation.

For the ones you mentioned, assuming 9 panel, standard immunoassay:
- Ecstasy: 3-4 days, and can be false positived by a very high methamphetamine level as well
- Meth: 4 days, acidifying your urine will result in much faster clearance. You will come up positive for amphetamine as well. There's a thread on here somewhere about methamphetamine/amphetamine testing that has more detail than you might want on the topic - go to main forum page and search for "methamphetamine testing isomers" and I bet it'll come up.
- Oxycontin / opiates: Oxycodone/morphone and "opiates" are not created equal. Gender, other drugs, and genetics influence this. See the Erowid page.
- LSD: They aren't testing for this on a work panel unless you have a weird-ass job. Need to be looking for it.
- 25i: They aren't testing for this on a work panel unless you have a weird-ass job. Need to be looking for it.

Synthetic urine: fine and dandy unless your lab is smart enough to look for the occasional white cell, etc., which should be present. Not 100%.

The blocking/interfering agents will give usually a conflicting result (such as positive immunoassay/negative confirmation). Not the same thing as a negative, at all. But maybe DD has a magic thing, I don't know.
