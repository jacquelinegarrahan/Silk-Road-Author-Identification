Title: Re: What is normal look/color of MDMA?
Post by: microRNA on May 12, 2012, 02:33 am
pure reagent grade mdma is translucent non-colored crystals which forms white and shiny powder.

even just a small percentage of color contaminant can greatly alter the visual appearance which is why the color has little indication of quality. some chemists add color to personalize their batches or for bag appeal. can be purple but the product could still be 99 percent mdma...

in other cases darker off-white or even brown coloration usually indicates the chemist did not take the extra time to further recrystallize and purify the product, and would be recommended.

a common contaminant, safrole, can be leftover frequently and is highly carcinogenic. it will produce a false positive for marquis reagent tests i believe as well... so is actually sometimes added to make batches appear as if they have mdma when they dont.

you can remove the safrole and color contaminants by washing, some using acetone or other non polar, then filter or recrystallize which will provide a much cleaner product
