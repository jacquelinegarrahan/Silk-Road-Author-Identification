Title: How to get dream-like hallucinations?
Post by: psychedelia on June 03, 2012, 05:59 am
Psychedelics produce three main types of hallucinations:

1.) geometric patterns;

2.) distortions of the appearance of objects in the world around you;

3.) vivid, photo-realistic, animated images of people, scenery, and objects. Hallucinations of this type share the qualities of dreams and visual memory. This is what I mean by a 'dream-like' hallucination. I find hallucinations of this type to be the most interesting.

I rarely get dream-like hallucinations with LSD, unless I am tired while still tripping and on the verge of falling asleep. Rather, I tend to see three-dimensional geometric patterns with my eyes closed, and distortions of space and time. Magic mushrooms seem to work better for me.

So, what psychedelic drugs, in your experience, tend to produce dream-like hallucinations?

Title: Re: How to get dream-like hallucinations?
Post by: Ben on June 04, 2012, 01:00 am
It depends on how strong/weak/persistent you want the experience to be really.

For many people combining a few benadryl (or other classic antihistamines) with a moderate amount of alcohol will initiate a state where you are prone to falling asleep and have pronounced dreams. It's not required to use illicit substance to get pleasurable effects, they can happen quite easily when taking more than the recommended dosage of antihistamines and the 'unwise' combination with alcohol.

Also, its probably best to avoid taking downers like benzodiazepines if you want to achieve such effects. You'll probably want to end up in a state resembling REM sleep, and large doses of benzo's or alcohol will delay that sleep phase.

You could consider trying to take 2 or 3 benadryl with a bottle of wine in the evening. The effects can be more interesting than you'd expect from things legally available... and if they aren't, you'll have a night of proper sleep as the alternative :D
