Title: Re: Rolling then driving the next day.
Post by: l0574l0ne on August 11, 2012, 03:37 pm
I have found the best test of if you are good to drive is this:

If you need to ask, don't do it.

I've known plenty of people (including me) who have driven high, drunk and/or withdrawing and while I don't have any horror stories as a result, I certainly wouldn't recommend it. Even being really tired is a pretty bad thing to be while behind the wheel, even more so during rush hour.

For most reasonably responsible people who wouldn't drive if they didn't feel like they were capable the risk is not so much in killing themselves or someone else, its in doing something that just a little bit dumb (running a light a half second too late or forgetting to turn your lights on) that can get you pulled over. Once you get pulled over, if you have ANY drugs in your system, you're looking at losing your license. And those little mistakes are easy enough to make when stone sober. The only times I've ever been pulled over were both when I was completely sober, once because I didn't have my lights on (i'd just come out of a very well lit carpark and didn't even notice) and the other was because I missed my turning and was now going REAL slow with my indicator on the whole time to make sure I found it this time.

Basically, be really careful. That fact that you are asking at all means you're reasonably sane about it and there probably isn't a risk of carnage, but that doesn't mean you can't end up in trouble. A DUI can wreck your life in lots of ways.
