Quote from: Spunkaroo on August 17, 2012, 10:00 am
Just curious, does anyone know how SSRIs will tone down the roll? If the reuptake of seretonin is lowered, wouldn't that mean more seretonin available to your brain, and thus a more intense experience?

No, because MDMA (and LSD, and psilocin) antagonize the effects of serotonin, and higher serotonin activity attenuates the effects of these drugs. When you inject an animal with serotonin it becomes lethargic and helpless, which is roughly opposite to the effects of the anti-serotonin psychedelics. This is also why taking SSRIs are anti-psychedelic and promote suicide.

Quote
Further, isn't there the risk of seretonin syndrome, too much seretonin to the point of toxicity?

Yes, there is, and even at non-acutely-toxic levels serotonin has a wide range of harmful effects on our physiology. I don't recommend that the average person take any 5-HTP supplements, since the proteins most people consume are already high in tryptophan.
