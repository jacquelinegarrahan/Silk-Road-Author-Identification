Title: Re: Eating and buccal/sublingual drugs
Post by: Ben on October 08, 2012, 01:22 am
Most drugs are removed from your system by the liver and kidneys. Taking someone trough the mucus membranes does not bypass that route. Sub-lingual dosing of drugs is useful because it bypasses the process of ingesting something, passing it through the stomach and wait  for absorption in the intestines.  This will speed up delivery of a drug, bit it will still be processed by the body in the same fashion of ingesting a substance.

The only process that can bypass that is inhalation - that route of administration will bypass metabolism, delivering the drug straight fom the lungs to the brain (and rest of the body) without any interference by the liver.

Title: Re: Eating and buccal/sublingual drugs
Post by: DayDreamer on October 08, 2012, 02:27 am
the effects of eating just before tripping depend on the drug you are taking:
MDMA - if you eat JUST before you take MDMA, you might throw up.
If not, due to the presence of the food, the absorption of the drug will be slower, as your stomach needs to deal with the load of the food as well.

If you swallow LSD on a full stomach, the effect will take longer to set in. I think buccal administration would be similar in effect/duration/onset to swallowing it on an empty stomach.
Never Experienced nausea on LSD.

NBOMe - these are destroyed by stomach juices and will have no effect if swallowed.

Once the drug enters your body, the effects will also depend on the rate of metabolism of the drug vs rate of absorption.
e.g.
rate of absorption >> rate of metabolism = strong effects ( something like LSD )
rate of metabolism >> rate of absorption = no effect ( DMT, if swallowed)
rate of metabolism ~= rate of absorption = little bit to no effect (??)
