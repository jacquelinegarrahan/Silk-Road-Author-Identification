@Snoopish: red and white never made yellow on my color wheel ;). the red of Mimosa-based Jungle-DMT is unrelated to yellow DMT (which can come from many different sources)

@34tuforlunch: you are thinking of the red DMT. Which was found to have a mild MAOI in it. and this doesn't really change anything about the trip other than that it makes it possible to consume the DMT/plant without an MAOI, under certain conditions


The truth is, there are several reasons that DMT can be yellow instead of white. the most common reason is from small amounts (<1%) of plant oils that make their way into the end product, because the chemist did not perform a defat during the extraction (usually considered unnecessary with mimosa).
The only time you should worry about the yellowness of a DMT is if it is accompanied by any trace of a solvent smell/taste. In this situation, it is typically a sign that the chemist used a impure solvent that doesn't evaporate cleanly. If you run into anything like this, just say no. Your lungs will thank you.
