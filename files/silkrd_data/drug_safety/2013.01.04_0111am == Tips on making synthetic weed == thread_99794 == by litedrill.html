Post by: matosago on January 04, 2013, 03:29 am
I don't know how people manage to make hotspots in their blends but this has always worked for me:

1.  Put 'noid in shot glass ( specifically I use a cordial glass that's really a double shot ).

2.  Pour a small amount of acetone in the glass.  Usually just  a quarter full. 

3.  Swirl to dissolve.  Pure 'noids will go into solution very readily when using acetone.

4.  Place desired amount of plant matter in a glass jar.  Might be packed in.

5.  Pour solution in.  Don't poor it all in the same spot (some in the middle and some towards the edges) so as to avoid saturation of the material which will cause you to lose a little bit on the side of the jar.

6.  Dump material in glass pan and stir with metal fork for at least five minutes.  Be random and use different techniques such as stirring, folding, and whatever else you can come up with.

7.  When it no longer wreaks of acetone place it in a gallon bag and shake it a bit more. 

You can heat gently to drive off the acetone faster, but having a low vapor pressure it doesn't take long in the first place.  Avoid using polymers that acetone will dissolve unless you are jonesing to smoke them.

Someone might chime in with their method (spray on method works too), but I have made tens of pounds in this manner.

*Also YouTube is your friend.  There are videos of the procedure above.
