Well a safe way is use sterile needle and not share or reuse them (this step is vital), use sterile water, a sterile spoon or container where you mix the water and the substance (you can also disinfect a clean spoon with isopropyl alcohol, should be sterile enough), disinfect the area you're going to inject, and use a 0.22 micrometer wheelfilter/syringe filter. This type of filter will remove all impurities and will even filter out bacteria, so it's pretty cool. When using a 0.22um filter, you don't necessarily need to use sterile water in the first place because bacteria get filtered out of the solution, but it's still better. It's also important not to heat the solution like you see in movies. Heating can disinfect but it doesn't disinfect so well and it makes harmful particles temporarily soluble, after they enter your veins, the temperature drops and those particles become solid again and can clog your veins, most notoriously in your lungs, which can kill you because even though you're breathing, the oxygen can't reach your blood if the vessels are clogged.

If you use the setup described above though, even missing a vein won't do much harm because it won't get infected. Sometimes, especially for things with a lot of fillers like pills, it's better to filter it one time with a cotton and then use the wheelfilter or else you can clog it. The 0.45 um wheelfilters are good too but they don't filter bacteria out, just particles. Using a cigarette filter or just cotton isn't a good idea, sterile cotton is somewhat better.

EDIT : to answer your other question, anything that is water soluble can be IV'd, so Ketamine, Cocaine, Amphetamine, Heroin, Morphine, oxycodone (hydrocodone and codeine can be injected but will most likely kill you because they release too much histamine), fentanyl, pretty much all opiates except those similar to codeine, some benzos like Midazolam and Loprazolam, DMT, other psychedelic salts, LSD, barbiturates, a lot of pharms in general, even GHB can be injected and is used like that in anesthesia, I can't think of anything else but pretty much anything can be injected, a member on here reported about injecting 2C-B.
IV is mostly used for opiates though, ketamine should be IM'd or taken subcutaneously because some people go to the K-hole before pulling the needle out which isn't so good.

As Tree said, once you have used a needle, throw it away and get a new one.  While it is possible to re-use a disposable needle if you can disinfect it, actually disinfecting it is difficult. Also, using a needle just one time dulls the point.  You would be amazed what the point of a used needle looks like after one use, compared to a brand new one.  The used needle will still penetrate your skin, but it will do so with much less ease.  You really don't want to get an abscess, I've had a couple and they are absolutely no fun and they really hurt. 

The first time I had an abscess I left it alone, thinking it would go away.  In three days my arm was swollen from the elbow to my wrist and there were red lines going up and down my forearm.  I ended up having to get intravenous antibiotics for 10 days.  That was after the doctor at the emergency room cut it open and damn near killed me squeezing my arm to get as much of the infection out as possible before packing it and starting the IV.  I had a 1 inch hole in my arm for a good two to three weeks until it healed.

 Also, Tree mentioned that anything water soluble can be injected and he lists a number of different types of pills.  There is one caveat however.  First, most pills have filler as he mentioned, you absolutely do not want to get any of that into your veins, so filter, filter, filter.  Secondly a large number of pills these days use anti-abuse formulas.  The new OxyContin's like the Oxy 80's gel up the minute they touch water and you shouldn't even try to filter that as you are bound to get some of the filler in the needle.  If you do go the Oxy  route, make sure you either get pills that are anti-abuse free or you look up a way to defeat the anti-abuse mechanism in the pills (which isn't easy).

You mention heroin and it leading to morphine and addiction.  Please be aware that heroin and morphine are opiates, heroin is just a stronger form.  Both are addicting.  Also included in that class are all the Oxy types such as Oxycodone, Oxymorphone, Hydrocodone, Hydromorphone and various other opiate variants.  They are ALL the same, just differing strengths. They are ALL just as addictive. 

Good luck :)
