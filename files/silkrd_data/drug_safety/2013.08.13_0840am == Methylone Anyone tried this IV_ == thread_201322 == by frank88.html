Warrants turn up more details on trio facing meth trafficking charges

Quote

New details have emerged in the case of three Raleigh men charged with methamphetamine trafficking following a search of the trio's residence.

Arrest records show Joseph Dennis Rambo, 47, Zechariah Rashad Rambo, 19 and Elijah Khalid Rambo, 17 were arrested Tuesday at 1513 Roaring Rapids Road. All three were charged with one count each of conspiracy to traffic meth. Zechariah faces additional charges, with one count of the manufacture of meth and one count of maintaining a dwelling place for keeping and selling the drug.

A search warrant for the case said the Raleigh Police Department became interested in the residence after six suspicious parcels were seized between July 21 and July 23 from a Ship on Site located at 1030-107 Rogers Lane. Inspectors traced the packages to 5500 Creek Crossing Drive in Midlothian, Va., an address registered to Zechariah. The intercepted parcels contained trafficking amounts of meth addressed to various parties throughout the country.

Police next obtained a forwarding address, coming up with the Raleigh house. The report said surveillance found Zechariah coming and going from the house, as well as a car with Virginia plates. The car and house are registered to Zina Rambo.

Police inspectors and a K-9 unit searched the house Tuesday afternoon after obtaining a search warrant. The record said officers at the scene encountered hazardous chemicals, at which point a drug expert wearing a respirator and protective gloves completed the search. Several as yet unidentified liquids, crystals, powders and plants were seized in the raid. Several Ziploc baggies were found with materials testing positive in the field for meth.

All three Rambos appeared at the Wake County Courthouse Wednesday morning. Zechariah's bond is set at $3 million while the other two men have bond set at $1 million. Their charges concern the alleged trafficking of 400 or more grams of meth, a crime which contains the state's harshest penalties for trafficking the narcotic.
