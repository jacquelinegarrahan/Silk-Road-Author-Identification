Post by: Ben on September 22, 2012, 12:43 am
Quote from: simantecort on September 21, 2012, 06:56 pm
diphenhydramine is terrible. i took large dose benadryl once and had the scariest 36+ hours of my life. i couldn't sleep all night because of frighteningly real hallucinations in my peripheral vision. i almost screamed multiple times because i was seeing animals and ghosts stare at me as i was trying to sleep. i was quite unsure as to whether i was asleep or awake the entire time. when i went to class the next morning, my eyes were visibly bloodshot and appeared to be stretched open. my peers thought i had some kind of psychotic break. in short, the people who took this drug and had good trip reports on erowid must have REALLY low standards.

Or just responded to them differently. The effects of antihistamines vary hugely between people. Some are knocked out from taking a single 25 mg dose of diphenhydramide, others are not even sleepy after 100 mg. There is some tolerance there obviously, but the effects on first time users are also highly variable. Antihistamines are commonly prescribed for things like  motion sickness too, and not all people experience any drowsiness on the recommended dose.

Taking high doses can result in odd effects though. I use cetirizine (zyrtec) as a treatment for insect bite allergy. Long before i ever knew about any effects beyond drowsiness i experienced visual hallucinations after taking about 50 mg of it, combined with a few glasses of wine.  As i am allergic to mosquito bites and got bitten several times at that point in time i figured taking a bit (5 times) more then recommended wouldn't be much of  a danger. By now i know better and choose drugs of choice more wisely if i do not want to experience such effects.

Oddly, i have never experience any hallucinations when taking diphenhydramide, even at equivalent doses to 50 mg cetirizine. The latter is marketed as 'non-drowsy' and should have less effects on the central nervous system (blocked by the blood-brain barrier), but for me personally that doesn't seem to be the case.

These differences in (side) effects would call for further research, though both substances are fine to use at the indicated dose for the intended purpose. If you are used to taking one tablet of benadryl to sleep, don't assume taking 10 will work 10 times better though - if you do, you may be in for a very strange experience. Obviously the leaflet says to seek medical attention in case you take more then recommended, but in this case that may prove to be sound advise for some people.

Stimulant--Amphetamine
Depressant/Opioid--Morphine
Phenethylamine--Mescaline
Tryptamine--LSD
Dissociative--Ketamine
Deliriant--Amanita muscaria

Those are just my personal picks based on what I've read, but really every drug is unique in some way so it's all subjective.  Even things in the same class can be violently different depending on the dose, set/setting, etc.
