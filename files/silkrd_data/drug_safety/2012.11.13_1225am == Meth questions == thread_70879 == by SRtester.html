Title: Re: Meth questions
Post by: SelfSovereignty on November 13, 2012, 12:36 am
As for second-hand smoke, I'd be absolutely amazed if there had been credible studies done.  So any answer you get is going to be just as anecdotal as my own: not unless the person has an allergy or is hypersensitive to amphetamines for whatever quirky reason of biology.  It is definitely a concern though, because you will get minute amounts of amphetamine getting in to your blood from second hand smoke... so to be safe, I'd say air the place out before your second party will be in there.  Not sure if you mean blowing it in the person's face, or just having them walk through an hour after you're finished getting your high on.

Cleaning the pipe: I have no idea.  I always smoke it when I'm out, desperate, and need to stay awake long enough to get *something* done.  It never seems to do much, but it does make me feel like I can stay awake more than 30 minutes.  Whether that's psychological or not I dunno, but it's not really fun and doesn't feel good, so I wouldn't be surprised if it's just cut with a very small proportion of residual meth.

Doesn't matter what you smoke.  It'll all melt together once you start vaping it anyway.

I try to make sure all the smoke gets in me.  I think some people blow off the smoke that they get before it melts and recrystallizes.  Presumably because they think it's pure cut.  I really can't say.  Probably healthiest, since at worst you're only losing an equal amount of meth smoke and at best not polluting yourself any further than necessary.

Hold it in as long as it's comfortable, but don't bother gasping for air over it or anything.  Your lungs don't actually "suck in" the stuff you get through your lungs.  They're a very thin layer, and your blood kind of attracts & traps stuff and whisks it away as it pumps through them.  So if you blow out a ton of smoke, chances are you just blew out a ton of your meth that didn't make contact with your lungs, and you aren't going to have much luck sucking it back up to try again.

I don't snort it.  At all.  Don't snort anything, really, so I'll let somebody who knows what they're talking about there field that one.

As for the come down... having a partner to give a hug to always helps me.  It's easier to ignore the bleak, dismal, sometimes almost suicidal feelings when I remember how lucky I really am in so many ways and how much I have to be thankful for.  And that it's okay.  That it's just a shitty feeling I have to deal with, but it'll be better soon.
