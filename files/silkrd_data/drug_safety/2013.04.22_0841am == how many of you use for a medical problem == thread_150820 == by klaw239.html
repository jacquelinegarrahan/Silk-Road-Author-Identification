Title: Re: how many of you use for a medical problem
Post by: newbottles on April 22, 2013, 09:55 pm
I don't want to trivialize anyone's position re: serious medical distress, but I find that anyone drawn to substance abuse is self-medicating to some extent.  This is a component of the 'recovery literature' that abusers and addicts should recognize.  It doesn't have to lead to the same conclusion. 

I have used alcohol, cannabis, stimulants, opioids and benzos, which are all medicating existential misery and/or anxiety to some degree.  I am fortunate to not have chronic pain which is an entirely different realm.

On the contrary, I stopped using psychedelics after initial exploration because I felt it magnified these issues.  I fault myself rather than the drugs for this.  Cannabis can blend into this territory for some.

I suppose quite a lot of people obtain substances on SR for medical rather than recreational uses.
