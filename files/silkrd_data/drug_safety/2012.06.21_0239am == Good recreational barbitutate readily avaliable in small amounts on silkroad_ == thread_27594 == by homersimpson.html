Post by: Ben on June 22, 2012, 01:21 am
I suppose they have been phased out for good reason really. The problem with barbiturates in general is that there isn't that big a difference between a safe and a dangerous dose.

As for propofol: even when pushed into a running IV the sensation is very unpleasant, a nasty stinging/burning feeling. Normally it is used to induce narcosis for surgery, and you should be out cold within about 5 seconds of beginning administration. It also has amnetic effects, so you may not even remember that much about it being administered.

While it is very effective to induce complete anesthesia, i would recommend against trying it recreationally. In a hospital setting you are already connected to heartrate, blood pressure and ventilation before it is administered. This makes is much easier to detect the effects of too high a dose, and also makes stimulant and defibrillator access much more rapid if anything were to wrong.

Things like GHB aren't without danger either, but at least they have the benefit that you can slowly self-administer them, preventing you from taking even more after a large doses passes you out. This may seem like a bit of an odd argument, but it has some merit. Try drinking yourself to death in one go: that will not work when you pour yourself a shotglass at a time, because you will be passed out on the floor before you can ingest a fatal dose. GHB as a similar limitation if slowly ingested as the liquid, whereas something like propofol is irreversible after injecting it and could result in death with no means to do anything about it.
