Hey all! So, my drug experience up until now has been with mdma and the psychedelics. But I've always been curious about ketamine, with the k-hole sounding particularly interesting to explore. So I got myself a gram to try.

Being a cautious sort I snorted a small bump of about 30mg to start with. Now this is the first time in my life I've done ketamine, or indeed snorted anything up my nose, but I think I did it right. I had another bump about 20 minutes later... and the predominant feeling is drunkenness without the nausea.

Like, the room is utterly spinning, my fingertips are numb typing on the keyboard, and I'm feeling very floaty. It's lacking any mood uplift, mental change, or trippyness that I recognise from psychedelics, and is really just a sort of mild confusion mixed with being very very drunk and a bit numb.

This normal for a low dose of K?

If so, awesome... means I've got K and am snorting it right. Though slightly disappointing there's no depth to it at this level. There anything interesting about ketamine except for the k-hole? Do people enjoy it at low doses just for the drunk-without-nausea effect or does it do more for other people than it's doing for me?
