I'd be more inclined to say that, whilst yes you are correct on the face of it regarding THC being the thing that gets you high, CBD mellowing - but I'd certainly say the choice of strain, particularly whether it was an Indica, Sativa, or combination of the two - Hybrid - that has the greatest effect on the mental state, regarding paranoia and schizophrenia.

Can I ask, if you don't mind answering, what's your age? Schizo - like symptoms are more likely at earlier ages when exposed to cannabis use. However, having said this, it's also important to note that Cannabis and Schizophrenia don't have a positive correlation - ie - increase in number of cannabis users has not lead to an increase in number of schizophrenic patients.

If you want something to chill you out, I'd certainly recommend an Indica - Dominant Hybrid. I've recently had some Purple Kush and that's a 100% Indica, giving extreme couchlock, munchies and puts you to bed. You won't really want to do much with that strain.
On the other hand, Jack Herer, something else I've had recently (both coincidentally off Canadian seller, MrOuid) is almost 100% Sativa I believe, which is very clear-headed, thought provoking and euphoric.

It's also important to note that the effects from individual strains can differ depending on the quality of the grow, conditions, etc.

See, there's a lot to consider from a technical and scientific standpoint. But I'd definitely try something that's Indica leaning and report back.
