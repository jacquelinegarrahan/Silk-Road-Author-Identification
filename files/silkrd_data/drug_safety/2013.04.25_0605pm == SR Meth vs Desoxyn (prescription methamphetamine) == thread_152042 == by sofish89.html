Title: Re: SR Meth vs Desoxyn (prescription methamphetamine)
Post by: zipstyle on April 26, 2013, 03:22 pm
I would say that that means that because clandestine chemists don't have the same resources, equipment, and expertise as pharmaceutical chemists, even if you get uncut product there will inherently be some impurities in your street drug. Some drugs are cut more often than others (read: cocaine, heroin). Some drugs are harder to cut, like methamphetamine (although it is still just as much a possibility) due to it coming in crystal form usually. MDMA is another example. If you get it on SR from a reputable vendor, chances are you're gonna be getting 80-85% pure MDMA HCl. The percentages will fluctuate when dealing with street drugs just because purity standards are either lower than pharmaceutical companies or are non-existent. I mean, if the street chemist makes the drug and people can take the supposed active dose and get high, then they're gonna sell it as-is.

TL;DR - Not all street drugs are cut. Street drugs are usually less pure than pharmaceuticals because they don't have the same quality control.
