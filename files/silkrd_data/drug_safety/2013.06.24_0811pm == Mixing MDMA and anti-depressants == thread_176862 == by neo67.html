Title: Re: Mixing MDMA and anti-depressants
Post by: thyme on June 25, 2013, 05:07 am
1) Your SSRI will likely block most of the subjective/pleasurable effect of the MDMA. Taking more MDMA to try to override that would be a Bad Idea.

2) From review, I'm in respectful disagreement with some other people here on this, but I'd say there's some risk of serotonin toxicity. Not a universal certainty, but definitely possible.  I'm basing that on reported cases of serotonin toxicity with MDMA and reasonable-dose SSRIs, including citalopram.

Assorted serotonin tox links:
hxxp://www.psychotropical.com/index.php/serotonin-toxicity

This article is also long, but discusses some of the MDMA/rx issues.
hxxp://jat.oxfordjournals.org/content/35/4/219.full.pdf

SSRI pharmacology in general:
edited: links break, look up Preskorn and SSRIs

Anyway - For me, the combination of increased risk and blocked drug effect would be a deterrent. I'd think citalopram is less of a risk than some of the other SSRIs ... but would still not on my list of combinations to take. I'll combine lots of other things that are frowned on, but not these.

I'd hold the citalopram for five half-lives of the drug (35 hours is half-life for citalopram) if you're set on the MDMA [likewise for LSD.]

Bear in mind that MDMA, while it has its benefits, also can exact a cost, depression-wise, as hypnotick wrote above.

If you can't be without your citalopram for 7-8 days,  then might need to look at the timing of the MDMA and push it out to another time.
