According to Erowid, cannabis is considered to be a psychedelic. Because of this, I like to keep in mind Tim Leary's "set and setting" approach when it comes to the usage of marijuana. That is, if there are other things on going on in your life which may cause these negative thoughts to come up, marijuana could possibly cause you to think of them in a manner which cannot be easily controlled.

I know of a few smokers who have stopped due to their inability to control their negative thoughts (they say it's paranoia). I haven't experienced this yet but then again I am very careful with my usage and I try not to exceed my threshold for getting "too high." In my opinion smoking more after already being high is a waste.

THC is the main contributing factor to the psychedelic effects of marijuana. Try smoking a strong indica?

Title: Re: The so called "bad trips" - with weed?
Post by: Shifty on August 16, 2012, 03:57 am
The only time I've ever had a bad experience with cannabis was the first time I smoked all those years ago. It happened to be a very potent strain and I was simply too high. People tend to think the effects of marijuana are static, as in they are the same every time. They forget that it is still possible to get "too high." I'm sure many of you know this feeling, and it is quite unpleasant. My advice is to remember that you dont have to smoke to excess every time. Try to only take a few hits now and again. Savor the pleasurable feelings Cannabis can produce for you and don't take it for granted.
