Good question.

Over the years I've mixed ina  few circles, from poor to pretty rich. The drug I find is pretty acceptable to most people aside from weed or hash is actually cocaine. That is seen as a 'normal' drug and one that does not give the user any real risk of losing the plot, tripping or hallucinating.

People who don't do drugs, usually means they drink. I've seen many people at parties get drunk then get curious about other drugs. I usually advise anyone drunk to avoid coke, (mixing the two is not wise) but weed is cool, its good for annoying drunks as it soon gets them green in the face, dizzy and dazed.

At one time coke was a 'serious' drug. but these days I'd say that its a drug many will just have a go of for a bit of change.

MDMA is too overbearing for most normal folk who don't get high.

Heroin is a no go. Most people into it regret using for that first time. I'd always advise anyone to strike that off the list of drugs to try because its the one drug you can try once and end up wanting it everyday, morning, noon and night. Coke can be addictive, but most people do it as a special treat, never in work, hopefully once a week tops because that way you keep a good tolerance for the stuff.

RCs, younger people might be reckless enough to try any but anyone over the age of 30 is likely to be wary of new untested and unproven drugs that might leave you ok whilst turning any kids you have into vegatables.

After cannabis and hash I think coke is the second most common drug of choice and therefore likely to be something many would try out, if it was on the table!

Mushrooms, its not common for people to jump in at the deep end. I love them but its not an indoors drug for me. Need to be in the great outdoors, wrapped in a blanket, with a stick, with a flourescent armband or waistcoat. (we trip in the country and its pitch black. The flourescent shit makes us feel safe, seeing the glow of fellow trippers, its reassuring when your seeing trees grow arms and the sky looking like it always does when your tripping. Bloody wierd! Amazing! Sometimes scary!

So shrroms are out. You don't pop that in your non drug using buddies cup.  ;D

LSD for the no drug user type is like giving a bottle of whiskey to a teetotaller.

Benzos are not really drugs people take for the experience. Its more just a safety net for stimulant sleeplessness. Ease any come downs. Not a drug I'd take just to get high on as it puts me to sleep.

I guess amphetamine is in there also. A drug that presents no danger of losing a sense of being! Makes your aunties clean the ornaments. Sadly, itys a drug that I find to have too many downsides to justify using it. 12 hours of fun followed by a week of feeling like shit. Still, its a drug a lot wil try the once. Its not too bad if your young and healthy.

The older you get the more you tend to dislike speed and in my circles most have given that up and would use coke instead because its pretty easy to wake up on Monday at 7.00am and go to work anjd sometimes actually do some work. Using coke in work is silly and waste of good coke. Coke is atmosphere dependent. You need a party to appreciate it.

So, my vote is for cocaine.

Title: Re: Besides weed, what drug are people most willing to try at least once?
Post by: flyinghigh1660 on August 09, 2013, 07:23 am
Good question, probably because the answer I think is complex.

It all depends on the current social norms. In the 60-70s most people would have tried LSD, but not now. People generally follow their peer group. The UK government keeps saying drug use is decreasing, and it is, but only in the drugs that have been around for years. All the new stuff is being ignored.

Unfortunately people equate legal with OK to take. Legal high use has shot up;

Nitrous Oxide was used by 350,000 people last year in the UK

Cocaine is the most popular drug after weed. Thus is makes sense that people will take that because others have taken it in their peer group.

Type of drug use also depends on other factors such as income, environment and even education. For example. Those earning less than 10,000 GBP a year take the most Amphetamines. While those earning 40-50K are more likely to take MDMA.

Thus if you have little experience with drugs, you will follow your peer group and social norms around you. which makes the answer complex.

But generally across all demographics, coke is the most popular, tus the drug that people are lkely to come into contact with after Alcohol.

Check out http://www.theguardian.com/news/datablog/2013/jul/25/britons-illegal-drugs-who-they [clearnet]

That gives you some idea of the trends and what is being used.
