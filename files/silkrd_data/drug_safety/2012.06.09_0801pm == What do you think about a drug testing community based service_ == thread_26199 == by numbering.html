Title: Re: What do you think about a drug testing community based service?
Post by: Ben on June 10, 2012, 12:54 am
The downside is that quantitative testing is often rather elaborous. There are plenty of test kits on the market that can demonstrate if a sample contains things like mdma, opiates, benzo's and what not.

Analyzing a tablet to prove, for example, which benzodiazepine it contains and in what exact concentration is more difficult. The best analytical method to determine the exact substance would probably be GC/MS, with concentration testing against a control.

As a chemist i wouldn't say this was difficult to do at all, but the equipment required can be very expensive - count on $100k to a million for a decent quality, second hand gc/ms system. On top of that come the operating costs of running that - helium gas supply, column replacement etc.

Title: Re: What do you think about a drug testing community based service?
Post by: Ben on June 11, 2012, 12:01 am
Scammers aside, i just don't think it would be feasible on a reasonable budget.

Surely test kits of all kinds of drugs are available, and many of the are actually low cost and pretty reliable to prove the presence of a substance. The downside is that they tell absolutely nothing about the concentration of active substances. A 10 mg MDMA tablet would test just as positive as a 200 mg one. The reaction may be slower, but that also varies greatly on temperature (and mixing, etc), and for most drugs its so fast that reaction rate isn't reliable at all.

Doing these tests would be able to identify bogus products like fake valium tablets. There would be no way to tell if someone was selling 2 mg valiums as 10 mg ones, or even if they sold a similar benzo as valium.

For some drugs you may be able to determine concentration using extraction and titration at a much lower budget. This can be done for several drugs on a few thousand worth of equipment and chemicals, but it would probably be sensitive to manipulation (ie the seller including some compound that gives the same response as the active substance, but which is not effective/illegal and much cheaper).
