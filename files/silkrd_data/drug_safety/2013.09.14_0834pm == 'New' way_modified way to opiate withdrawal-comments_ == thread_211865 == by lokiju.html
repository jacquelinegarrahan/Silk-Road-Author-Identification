The first time, after about 24 hrs, I quit and started back up the subutex, but eventually I made it through the 3 days and the hydro's started to work.
So years pass, and eventually I found the Silk Road. Week after week I'd look at the heroin up there and drool. I've heard that if you are still high on your DOC you can get 'precipitated withdrawals', which are supposed to be agonizing (had a friend go through them, he said it he had had a gun he would have shot himself) if you just take subutex or subuxone. I found several articles you can check out.
The first is a study where they tried to get precipitated withdrawals in order to study what did it.

The second article is about OWS or COWS, which is about a objective (that's the intention, anyway) way to measure how badly someone is 'kicking'. It stands for Clinical Opiate Withdrawl Syndrome.
In order to be safe from precipitated withdrawls you need to have 26 or more points while filling out the OWS.
CLEARNET WARNING  http://international.drugabuse.gov/educational-opportunities/certificate-programs/methadone-research-web-guide/part-c/question-2-how-op

The third article is about taking small doses of subutex, about 1/2 mg. every hour and a half during subutex 'induction'. I know my doctor just gave me 1/2 of a 8 mg. subutex and then after 1/2 hour another full tablet, and that was the $300 induction from the doctor. Lately I've found that there are big advantages to doing it differently, but these different ways aren't usual for doctors because they involve several hours of supervision (I didn't need 'supervision' BTW, it's as simple as falling off a ladder). The idea is to make sure that each previous dose of subutex is going directly onto the opiate receptors in your brain and although it takes longer, you end up with a lower dose of subutex that you take every day. They said a majority of people get 'stabilized' at 2 mg.
