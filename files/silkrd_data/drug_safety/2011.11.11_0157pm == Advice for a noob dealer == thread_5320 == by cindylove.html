As someone else posted, the IRL market for marijuana is extremely flooded right now, even more so on college campuses. You're going to have to offer superior product at a lower price to break into the game. You're going to need people to buy from you, and you're going to want those people to tell all their friends how bomb and cheap your weed is. Weigh everything out in front of your customers and give extra on every sale. People remember stuff like this and if your product is good they will come back to you.

Don't deal on campus. I'm not familiar with college life, but I assume that there are dorms on campus, and that someone may ask you to deal weed to them in the dorms. Do not do it. Always meet somewhere safe, like a house.

Don't smoke all your 'sale' weed. Do the math for how much profit you want to make, and weigh your stash out separately from the main stash. I can't tell you how many times I've seen weed dealers smoke themselves into debt.

I think I can give some advice, from personal experience. I've already graduated college, but only recently started slangin herb. I don't like the term dealer or slangin though, so I tell people I'm in Cannabis Supply Chain Management. (I run things with class.)

Good luck,

Post by: PumpkinYeti on November 29, 2011, 02:34 am
A few rules my friend lives by, most of which have been mentioned though...

1) Never ever let them know where you live. Eventually you WILL get robbed if you do. Or worse. Ideally you need a place that's public enough to not get robbed in broad daylight, but private enough for the exchange.

2) Get a nickname. If you tell them your real name, and then they tell you their name is "Big Smoke", then they're one up on you.

3) Don't deal with people that are extremely paranoid. They're more likely to get caught than anybody.

4) Don't get high off your own supply. Every dealer I know basically smokes for free but makes very little. Ask yourself whether you're in it for free smoke or the MONEY. Plan accordingly.

5) Don't deal in amounts so small that it's not worth the risk. It's far better to sell halves and make 40 each deal than to sell 4 eighths and make 10 each deal, because its only one transaction. If you slang grams for 5 bucks profit each, your risk/reward ratio is way off.

6) Trust your instinct. If you're feeling weird about someone, don't sell to them. If you're feeling weird about a certain deal, don't do it. Ever wonder why when people get caught  they always say "I knew this was a setup."? Because they DID.

7) Know your local laws. If your local laws say 8oz and under is a misdemeanor and over 8 is a felony, try not to buy more than 8 at a time. Try not to keep more than 8 at any one location at a time. See if dealing within so many feet of a school is a felony - if so, don't do it. And if you needed a reason not to, dealing to minors is nearly always a felony.

8) Give extra when you weigh it out. My only reasoning for that one is because they always weigh less when you weigh them later. That's been my friend's experience. I assume its from further drying but I'm not sure.

9) Build an inventory of both product and cash. Running out of product is bad for business. And cash because you'll never know when your dealer has something special or on sale you need to get ahold of.

10) Believe in karma. Do it with morals, etiquette, and professionalism and you'll be rewarded. Rip people off and it will come back to bite you.

11) Prepare for success at the start, and for failure when you get rolling. Know your rights if worse comes to worse. Know the best local drug attorney (if cash really gets rolling, think about a retainer - its a investment into your business).

12) People don't sell drugs. Drugs sell themselves. So make sure its the best price/quality ratio you can get.
