Title: 300ug+ blotters bad for acid market
Post by: ConcernedCitizen on April 08, 2012, 02:08 am
In my experience with acid, most blotters tend to be around 100ug (though many claiming as such are often less). Since there is no easy way to find out how much acid is in a blotter, my understanding of this trend is to make it easier to estimate doses with unknown tabs. With some chemists laying their blotters at 300ug+, unsuspecting consumers of 2 or more tabs could very well end up with a 24hr bad trip. I know that for myself, the ~300ug Dalai Lama tabs going around kick my ass with just one, so much so that I would prefer if they were a little less potent. Since 100ug is still a pretty decent trip for most people, it makes more sense to dose blotters at that level. This way, finer control of dosages is allowed, as cutting a blotter in more than 2 even pieces is pretty tough.

So essentially, it comes down to these points in favor of standard 100ug blotters:
(i) Easier to estimate dosage for consumers, since not everyone buys directly from people who know the true dosage
(ii) Less likely for people to unknowingly take massive dosages
(iii) Finer control over dosage
(iv) one tab is a good amount for first timers (as opposed to 300+ ug)

Obviously, it is a free and unregulated market, so no one can force anyone to modify their product. But I post this in hopes of appealing to the blotter layers out there to promote a safer acid market. What does the SR community think of my rationale?
