Title: Re: Has anyone smoked DMT extracted from Phalaris arundinacea ?
Post by: BlackIris on July 04, 2013, 06:21 pm
The problem with Phalaris, apart gramine, is that ALL (and I mean all) the reports I've read about DMT extracts from the plant seems much more like a 5-Meo-DMT experience than a pure N-N-DMT one. In my opinion phalaris contains a lot of other alkaloids that make the trip substantially different from an usual DMT trip.

Naturally this can also be something you may like or purposely want to research upon, but if you want to use Phalaris to obtain an extract of pure N-N-DMT because you like that particular experience then I would stay away from Phalaris (or be extremely cautious and meticulous on the extract part). Yes, it is everywhere, but there's a reason why it is not used so much nowadays even while being so easy to find; it was used much more for a short time, when the book "Psychedelic Shamanism" came out and the author pointed out that Phalaris was a very cheap way to obtain DMT (for smoking, so gramine was not so much of an issue there), but by and by it got in disuse just because people started to acknowledge the fact that what you obtain is not the usual DMT trip in its pure form (but it's also true that many of these people didn't surely research on a properly extract, so if you do it you can actually have different results).

If you have problems importing Mimosa where you live you can try Acacia species instead, but if you really have nothing better then go with Phalaris; just try to make the cleanest extract you can, especially on the defat part and on the washing. I would also do a mandative re-crystallization with Phalaris, so as to try to remove as much impurities as you can.
