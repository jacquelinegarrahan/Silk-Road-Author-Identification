The most common pre-employment tests, unless they've changed recently (in USA) are:


Five-Panel Drug Test

Most employers request a standard drug screen, which tests for the five most common street drugs. The five-panel test screens for signs of marijuana, cocaine, phencyclidine, amphetamines and opiates. Amphetamines include illegal drugs such as methamphetamines, speed, crank and ecstasy. Opiates include heroin, opium and codeine. The test also screens for crack cocaine and the marijuana byproduct hash. Phencyclidine refers to PCP, also known as angel dust.


Ten-Panel Drug Test

The 10-panel test screens for the five most common types of drugs and five additional drugs. A 10-panel test also detects barbiturates, benzodiazepines, methadone, methaqualone and propoxphene. Barbiturates include phenobarbital, butalbital and downers. Benzodiazepines include Valium, Librium and Xanax. Methaqualone refers to the illegal drug known as quaaludes. Propozphene refers to Darvon. Doctors use methadone to treat individuals addicted to heroin.
