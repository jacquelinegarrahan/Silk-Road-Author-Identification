Title: Re: How to measure out...
Post by: Appa on April 17, 2012, 05:12 am
All the advice so far is exactly right: for accurate dosing of just 10mg, volumetric measuring is your best bet.  Simply weighing out 10mg on most scales would be unwise, since the error of the scale could easily have you ingesting twice what you intended.

I would get a moderately-priced milligram scale (measures in 0.001 grams), and measure out a medium portion of the gram, say 200mg (0.200g).  Then, add this to an appropriate solvent (water or alcohol, depending on the chemical), and measure out the proper volume.  I would use the ratio l1llykins suggested (10mg:1mL), as well as an oral syringe for measuring.

So, using the above numbers, you would weigh out 200mg and dilute it in 20mL of solvent.  To dose, draw out 1mL with a syringe, squirt it into a glass of your favorite juice, and enjoy!

Save the other 800mg as a powder away from oxygen, light, and excessive heat (room temperature is usually okay, refrigerator is fine too) until you need it again.  Many of these chemicals tend to be at least a bit more stable in the long-term if stored as a powder than in solution, assuming they're stored properly.
