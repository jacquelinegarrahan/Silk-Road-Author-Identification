isn't the hot cook a lot harder to control than the cold cook birch reaction?

No, it's the other way around.  Reduction of ephed/pseudo with lithium via Birch is a very clean synth if done correctly.  Reductive aminiation of P2P is not as clean and requires more purification after the reaction.  The other big difference is that reductive aminiation of P2P results in a racemic product, i.e. d,l-meth.  Any method using ephed/pseudo as a starting material results in chirally pure d-meth, which is the more active isomer and therefore twice as potent.
