Title: Re: best drugs to mix with opiates
Post by: microRNA on December 12, 2011, 01:45 pm
All CNS depressants synergize with opiates to combine the effects making one more intoxicated... and overdose much more likely. Obviously any drug that increases dopamine release will intensify the pleasurable effects as well.

The true gems with opiates however are the potentiating compounds. These actually serve to intensify the effects pharmacologically, rather than just blending the effects together... it is like the difference between 3+3=6 and 3x3=9, where the mechanisms of the drug are actually interacting and producing an enhanced experience. Therefore, you will achieve the same subjective high while less of the opiate is required (2x3=6). This can be dangerous for obvious reasons.

Potentiating chemicals include first generation anti-histamines like promethazine and hydroxyzine among many others, H-2 antagonist cimetidine, alpha-2 adrenergic autoreceptor agonist clonidine, anti-cholinergics like hyoscamine and dicyclomine, drug efflux pump inhibitors such as omeprazole, other opioids such as loperamide, and catabolic enzyme inhibitors such as compounds in grapefruit juice. Plenty of others I am sure I am forgetting... like DXM, naproxen and quinine. A high fat meal has also been reported to increase bioavailability. Depending on the class of opioid ingested some potentiators have varying effect fyi.
