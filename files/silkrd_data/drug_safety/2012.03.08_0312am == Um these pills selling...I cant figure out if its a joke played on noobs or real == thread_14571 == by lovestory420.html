Title: Re: Um these pills selling...I cant figure out if its a joke played on noobs or real
Post by: quinone on March 08, 2012, 03:53 am
Most nootropics don't really have much research done to back up any of their claims, and my personal experience with them is less then spectacular.

Their claims are mostly that they increase bloodflow to the brain and between the two hemispheres of the brain and many are packed basically with chemical precursor's to the enzymes involved in serotogenic, dopiminergic and cholinergic systems in the brain.

It definitely doesn't hurt to take nootropics, but I would not really consider 'minfood', 'thinkdeep', or 'chill' nootropics and would be mildly concerned at their name alone as theres a wealth of nootropics out there with legitimate names.  You should go to a health food store and buy the chemical, eg. piracetam or whatever nootropic mixture whose ingredients you can actually read.  Don't expect anything huge out of them though, other then a hole in your wallet.
