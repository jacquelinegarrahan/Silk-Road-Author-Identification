Post by: mdpro on June 02, 2013, 09:33 am
What the OP experienced was, in fact, a needlestick through the vein rather than into it.  Depending on which vein you go for in that area and your body structure, it is actually quite likely that this would lead to a puncture in the artery.  The brachial artery runs just below the vein on the pinkie side of the AC fossa and travels a pretty similar path to the vein.  As a general rule, the named blood vessels in the body travel in pairs of arteries and veins running right next to each other.  Think about that before you inject.  Seriously, use clean gear and (it may sound totally stupid) plan your injection before you inject.  Take the extra 10 second pause.  It just may stop you from missing a safety step or having a bad situation happen from being hasty.  Take care of yourself and do everything in your power to be safe!
