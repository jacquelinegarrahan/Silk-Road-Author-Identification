Post by: jpisbetterthanme on March 12, 2012, 02:18 am
Quote from: tordemon on March 09, 2012, 01:24 am
It really didn't add anything to the work I was reading, but more than anything else I thought it was cool that my brain could subconsciously do that fantastic work. It kinda made me wonder if I can't train my brain to automatically do other cool things.

Oh yeah. You can teach your brain to do all sorts of neat things. You may already know this but the phenomenon of crossed senses is called synesthesia. It happens a lot on psychedelics but there are also people who have it all the time. I've met a fair number of them (I sought them out for reasons I'd rather not go into) and believe it or not a lot of them can experience it as distressing, or at the very least distracting. This is especially the case when it is something that starts later in life, vs. something that they are born with.

The phenomenon you are describing is actually a relatively common one.

There are tons of ways to train your brain to do amazing things but you just touched on a good place to start - do some research on synesthesia, specifically what people do to try to get rid of it. Then do that in reverse! It's way more complicated than that, of course, but it can be done - I wouldn't be able to read music if I hadn't trained my brain to read color and sound at the same time on a musical staff.
