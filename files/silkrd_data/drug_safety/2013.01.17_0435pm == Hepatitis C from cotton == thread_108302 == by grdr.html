Just get tested for it - the whole story is just too vague exclude infection. The chances aren't terribly large at all, but if you managed to get a hep c infection its important to get proper treatment for that. While hep c is a disease that is very difficult to cure completely, with medication it is manageable and you could live out a normal lifespan.

Things like briefly boiling a substance before injection are NOT a foolproof way to kill the hep c virus. It does reduce the chance of transmission surely, but the concept that quickly boiling something will kill all viruses in it is just wrong.

Go and get tested anyway, even if you don't care about what happens to you.
