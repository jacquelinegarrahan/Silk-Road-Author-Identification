Title: Re: Storing and saving shrooms
Post by: philter3 on May 13, 2012, 07:25 pm
I would be careful of ziplock bags as oxygen can migrate through the plastic. Best bet is O2 proof glass container with an oxygen absorber.  Put them in a brown paper bag, add an oxygen absorber packet, then seal in a mason jar, lid very tight and stick in the freezer.
Title: Re: Storing and saving shrooms
Post by: ArmTrax on May 13, 2012, 10:14 pm

A very good way that will keep your fungus good for months is to put them in a brown paper bag with a hand full of white rice (uncooked, raw, dry of course). I wouldn't use the freezer, its a moist environment. Instead, put them in a cool dry place.

The method above isn't wrong, just expensive and a little over the top in my opinion. Mushrooms will keep for a long time without going to any form if extreme measures.

Title: Re: Storing and saving shrooms
Post by: Knomo on May 15, 2012, 12:59 am
I recon the shrooms are already dried? Or are they fresh...? If they are fresh I'd suggest drying them first.
Easiest way to do this is to lay them over your heater on a piece of paper. When completely dried of moisture they can be stored for months as long they are kept out of sunlight. I read that sunlight depletes psilocybin so you'd wanna watch out with that :P

If they are in fact already dried you can grind them to powder, let the powder dry as well as it might still contain moisture, and put it all in a zip bag on the top shelf of your drawer.
