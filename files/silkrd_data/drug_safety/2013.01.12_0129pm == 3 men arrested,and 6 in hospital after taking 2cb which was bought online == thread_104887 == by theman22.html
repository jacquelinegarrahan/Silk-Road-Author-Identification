Title: Re: 3 men arrested,and 6 in hospital after taking 2cb which was bought online
Post by: Ben on January 15, 2013, 02:35 am
That certainly is part of the problem. If these drugs came as legal tablets including a leaflet that specifies dosages for certain effects, such overdose events would be far less likely to occur.

Such problems aren't limited to illicit substances however. People often confuse similar substance names, for example paracetamol and propanolol. While both are perfectly legal, taking a gram dose of propanolol would end rather badly while a gram of paracetamol would be fine.

Perhaps it would be a good idea to start supplying information leaflets with illicit drugs sold through SR as well. The information doesn't have to extensive, but a simple indication of what amount is used in recreational use would be helpful. Especially with substances sold in powder form, doing so could prevent a lot of potential harm.

For example, a popular product on SR is a bag of 1 gram of pure xanax. Nothing wrong with that, but whoever receives that bag and uses the drug should realize that it is the equivalent of 500 bars of xanax concentrated into something you could easily swallow in a single go.

I'd suggest that on every shipment there should be a declaration of the amount of substance (in total, or per tablet if its in tablet form) and the IUPAC name of the chemical. Using those two pieces of information it would be easy enough to create an online system that provides common dosage, side effects, risks and such for each substance.
