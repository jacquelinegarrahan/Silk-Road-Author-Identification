When I take LSD, if I take 2 hits or less no visual effects really occur other than colors seeming brighter, contrasts stronger, and the edges and details of objects appearing in higher resolution and greater quantity (for example, looking at my face). All of these things happen very lightly if at all. If I stare at a textured ceiling and let my eyes unfocus, the ceiling will tend to drift or swirl around as if it were a fluid.

LSD is something that doesn't really affect me— I affect it. I have to get out and do something, or begin a conversation or do an art of mine before it I really know I'm on LSD. Perhaps you could be the same way. I think particularly good analytical, abstract, or mathematical thinkers are more likely to have "only head trips" (thoughts/creative capacity-based) than most people.
