Title: Help with injecting tar (BTH), please
Post by: kleenbill47 on March 27, 2013, 08:56 pm
Okay, normally I buy #4 heroin, and I have no trouble at all injecting it.  However, occasionally I'll purchase tar (whether it's because the price to quality ratio was a great deal, there weren't any #4 options available for the right price or I didn't want to wait more than a day for delivery of #4, etc.).  As far as prepping the BTH, that's easy.  I use a shotglass, instead of a spoon (I have my reasons), use cold or room temp. water (I don't heat it or use a lighter--again, I have my reasons), and if it doesn't completely dissolve, I'll remove the plunger from the needle, and use it to stir the solution or mash the chunks along the bottom until it dissolves.  Then I take a small piece of cotton as a filter and draw up the solution into the syringe.  So far none of this is difficult.

The problem I have now when dealing with BTH is when trying to make sure I'm in the vein properly.  With #4, the solution in the syringe is at least translucent or clear, so it's obvious you are in the vein when you register by pulling back on the plunger and seeing blood flow in.  If you're not in the vein right, blood doesn't flow in, and you can adjust until you are.  With BTH, the heroin solution in the needle is usually dark and opaque, so it's very tough to see blood flow in when registering.  As a result, I sometimes miss the vein somewhat or completely, and the shot is partially wasted until I feel it going in wrong.

So my question is if anyone has any techniques to insure you are in the vein completely when you can't visibly register the blood flowing in?  Yeah, you can feel it hitting the vein sometimes.  Also, you can try to judge how easily the plunger is pulled back when trying to register (if you are completely outside the vein, it's difficult to pull back on the plunger, or air flows in).  Nonetheless, none of these quasi-techniques are nearly as effective as simply registering the blood flowing in when injecting #4.  So does anyone have a method of registering when injecting tar that I haven't thought of?  I'm sure if my regular type was tar, I'd have just developed an intangible feel for it--but I rarely do tar, so I need a more clear-cut method for making sure I'm in the vein properly.  I'd be eternally grateful to anyone who can enlighten me to a way that will work every time I shoot tar.

Thanks to anyone who has any suggestions.
