If you want to explore in the "in-between" state then it's better Changa or either DMT-N-Oxide. N-N-DMT effect is such that the effect changes completely after a certain threshold (the so-called breakthrough) to the point that the experience before seems almost completely another substance.

For sub-breakthrough doses, depending on the individual, the effects can range from high-doses shrooms/acid to nice visuals and nothing much more. The thing changes completely at breakthrough doses when the experience can be even too intense for many people (and along this you can also not remember anything of what happened or just in lapses). To approach DMT well you should use mediation before (so as to recollect most of the experience after) and go for a breakthrough dose and being prepared for it.

If instead you like more the sub-breakthrough states then it is much better Changa or DMT-N-Oxide that gives you the ability to do so without problems. With DMT-N-Oxide it's very difficult to reach a breakthrough, while for Changa even if it happens the experience is anyway smoother, meaning that the shift in gear (so to speak) is much less noticeable.

What you say indeed happens to many people that approach DMT for the first time and are not so accustomed to the control of their own subconscious mind (as with precedent working with meditation or magic). For these people it is probably much better to start off with Changa and then, after, pass to DMT-N-Oxide and lastly to N-N-DMT (if interested then on researching on a breakthrough to full speed).

Title: Re: DMT, What did I do wrong?
Post by: Tryptamine on August 04, 2013, 04:06 am
DMT, being an endogenous metabolite, is very quickly metabolized by enzymes like monoamine oxidase upon ingestion. This is why it lacks oral activity. For enough of it to reach your brain intact, you need to saturate the enzymes that would break it down on the way there; that is to say, you need to take it all in one (or at most two) hits. There is no other way.
Smoking (or using a lightbulb) limits the amount you can comfortably take, and is less efficient than a vaporizer.
