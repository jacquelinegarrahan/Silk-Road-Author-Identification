Oh man. Firstly, H is quite a subtle buzz compared to many drugs. You wont feel WD at all if you use for less than 3 days. It took me ages to realize I was physically addicted - I just thought I was a bit ill and therefore the reasons to score again were more justified. The WD's only really become apparent when you can't sleep because of them.

I could go on. All I will say is you shouldn't underestimate the pain you feel and how addictive temporary relief from said pain can be. WD's are terrible but they last 3-5 days and won't kill you, most go through them many times yet still return to H.  Whilst this is all pretty negative stuff, when I first got into H I was a heavy crack addict with many issues. Since then I have abstained from the stone, got a degree, a means of supporting myself and haven't been arrested since then. There a worse problems than H addiction and even the terrible WD's.

Title: Re: Heroin question for a H noob
Post by: radioheadfan5 on August 01, 2012, 11:34 pm
Just to echo what a lot of the other posters here have said, your experience with H and how addicting psychologically/physically it is to you is going to depend on you as an individual. Snorting one line won't get you immediately physically addicted - far from it. You'd have to snort on a daily basis for 4+ days to feel withdrawal symptoms, and even then they probably wouldn't be too bad.

Moreso what you have to worry about is that if you do start chipping, you're going to start craving it. If you can control yourself and remain a chipper (which, despite what some will tell you, can be done if you have the will power) then great, more power to you. However, at least from my personal experience, you need to stay away from IVing. I've been an occasional recreational user for a few years now and while sometimes I do have a tolerance from snorting (and which is a reminder to me that I need to tone it down), I know that if I IV even one time, that will be my turning point and will put me in a hole I can't crawl out of. I've been successful up until this point at resisting the urge to shoot up, and because of it I've remained in control of my life.

I personally believe that heroin doesn't have to be a negative force in someone's life. If you can control yourself, if you can take responsibility for when and how much you do, and if you remain constantly vigilant, then using heroin can be a beautiful experience that can enrich your life. Whether it becomes the bane of your existence is up to you, so please always be mindful. Good luck :)
