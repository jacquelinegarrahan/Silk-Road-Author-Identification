Title: Re: Is it safe to take roxi's to help ease through benzo withdrawal's?
Post by: maf on August 10, 2013, 03:58 am
Quote from: tree on August 09, 2013, 11:11 pm
If you wanna be clean though, why would you take the valium when it gets there?

Diazepam is generally considered the ideal benzo to use for tapering down. I think this is partly because of its long half-life and comparatively low euphoric effects at equivalent doses, and it's generally easier to titrate the dosage in the physical form it's delivered in. If you sense that you're withdrawing from benzos, it may not be something you can just stop at the drop of a hat. The risk of seizures, as in alcohol withdrawal, is not to be taken lightly.

OP, I don't believe the opiates would be harmful unless you were to combine them with the Valium, although they could lower your seizure threshold slightly (probably not to a worrisome level, but it's something to keep in mind). The only relief they would give would be psychological, though--the main problem is going to be your GABA system being all fucked up, and that's a physiological problem that only GABAergic drugs like barbiturates, alcohol, GHB, etc. can help treat (of course, you don't want to end up addicted to something else either). But going back to the opiates, from my own experience, I think they might actually make you feel worse as you're withdrawing just because of how confusing/nauseating the experience is on its own.
