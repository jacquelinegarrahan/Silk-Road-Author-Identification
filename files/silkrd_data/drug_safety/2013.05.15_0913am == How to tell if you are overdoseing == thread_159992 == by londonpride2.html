Title: Re: How to tell if you are overdoseing
Post by: PerPETualMOtion on May 15, 2013, 06:59 pm
Some medical professionals deem "overdose" as any experience that is unpleasant. This is too broad in the sense of recreational drug use, I think.

An overdose is the point at which your body goes into shock: drop in BP, elevated HR (>160-180 BPM), seizure, loss of consciousness, confusion--beyond the effects of hallucinogens... stay away from Datura products, or unexplained and unwanted symptoms.

Alcohol is the best teaching tool for understanding an overdose.

Drink too much = pass out/black out = overdose.

Drink too much = vomit = overdose.

Drink too much = stomach ache = overdose.

Drink too much = become aggressive/violent = overdose.

Drink too much = Don't drink too much!
