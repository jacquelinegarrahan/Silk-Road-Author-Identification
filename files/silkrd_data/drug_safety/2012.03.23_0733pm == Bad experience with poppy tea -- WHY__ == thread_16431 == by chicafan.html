Title: Re: Bad experience with poppy tea -- WHY??
Post by: ChaxChax on March 23, 2012, 08:02 pm
Poppy Tea, or Doda is a fairly safe way to enjoy a mild-medium opioid buzz with very few side effects provided you respect the plant (treat it's addiction profile like you would any other strong opioid).

A few things to consider:
Dosage: The dosage of the Papaver Somniferum is completely dependent on the variety/strain ( Hens & Chicks, Giganticum, Afghan, Turkish White etc).  The amount of raw opium in each pod and straw (don't dispose of the straw it is almost as potent as the pods!) depends entirely on the type of poppy. Your vendor should have told you what they were.  A good start is 4-5 crushed pods of the Hens&Chicks, or maybe one less if Turkish white, or Tasmanian/afghan.  2-3 Giganticum is plenty. Weigh that out and use the same amount of straw + .5 again in weight.

Pesticides: Some unscrupulous growers will use growing hormones, or even pesticides to improve their yield. Humans can be especially susceptible to side effects of these in the form of allergies.

Lastly: Mold and fungus grow very easily on poppy pods and straw. They are usually benign, but some aren't cant can cause many of the effects you desribe.
