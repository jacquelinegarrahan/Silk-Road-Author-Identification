From http://bitcoinmagazine.com/fincen-bitcoin-users-not-regulated-exchanges-are/

"a person that creates units of convertible virtual currency and sells those units to another person for real currency or its equivalent is engaged in transmission to another location and is a money transmitter. In addition, a person is an exchanger and a money transmitter if the person accepts such de – centralized convertible virtual currency from one person and transmits it to another person as part of the acceptance and transfer of currency, funds, or other value that substitutes for currency."

Localbitcoins.com may sooner come under scrutiny than individual sellers, but I'm not quite sure as they take commission in the form of BTC, and only deal with BTC, so they don't actually deal with real currency or any conversion.  The seller is the one who gets cash for btc.

Title: Re: Are there any laws which might apply to selling bitcoins for cash? [update: yes]
Post by: bluedev1 on August 06, 2013, 11:07 am
This is what FinCEN said: "a person that creates units of convertible virtual currency and sells those units to another person for real currency or its equivalent is engaged in transmission to another location and is a money transmitter. In addition, a person is an exchanger and a money transmitter if the person accepts such de – centralized convertible virtual currency from one person and transmits it to another person as part of the acceptance and transfer of currency, funds, or other value that substitutes for currency."

Regardless of the fact that trading money for bitcoins in and by itself is not illegal (in other words, buying bitcoins from a licensed exchange is NOT illegal) , I'm talking about what it means to act as a money transmitter/exchanger.  There are laws around that and they are not specific to bitcoin, and I guess this is something that will vary state by state.  I suggest researching what your state DFI has to say about bitcoins if anything.  You could call or write them and try to get some clarification I suppose, it wouldn't hurt.

As an analogy to make this clearer, buying and selling pharmaceutical drugs is by itself not illegal -- there is no law that says 'Prozac is illegal.' - but you need to have a license to sell it legally.
