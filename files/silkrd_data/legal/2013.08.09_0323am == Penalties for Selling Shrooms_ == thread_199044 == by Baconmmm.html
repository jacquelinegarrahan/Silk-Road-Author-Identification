Post by: NickNack on August 09, 2013, 03:53 am
In most cases, steep.  It's a schedule 1...  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Controlled_Substances_Act#Schedule_I_controlled_substances


Each state has a slightly different Controlled Substances Act so you'd have to look up your state.  But to use Illinois as an example since it pops up in startpage:
Quote
Any substance containing a substance or analog of substance classified in Schedule I or II, but not listed here

Amount    Felony status    Prison Term    Fine

50-199 gm    Class 1    4-15 years    Up to $250,000

200+ gm     Class X    6-30 years    Up to $500,000
http://commonsense.uchicago.edu/page/illinois-trafficking-penalties-controlled-substances


Federal penalties can be found in the menu on the left... these are mandatory minimum sentencing.


Quote
Federal guidelines, regulations, and penalties

The Anti-Drug Abuse Act of 1986 and 1988 set forth federal mandatory minimum sentencing guidelines. According to the Federal Trafficking Penalties, first offense penalties for anyone who manufactures, dispenses, distributes, or possesses psilocybin is imprisonment for up to 20 years. If a death or serious injury is involved there is a mandatory minimum sentence of not less than 20 years, but not to exceed a life sentence. Individuals can also be fined up to one million dollars. In the case of a second offense, offenders can receive up to a 30-year prison sentence. If a death or serious injury is involved, there is a mandatory minimum life sentence. Individuals can also be fined up to two million dollars.
